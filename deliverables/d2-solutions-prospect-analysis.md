# D2 Solutions: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work would be D2 Solutions and, by extension, its health-system specialty pharmacy clients, who are the parties whose operational and clinical data could support outcomes research.

## 1. NASP presence

D2 Solutions has a deep, multi-channel relationship with NASP that extends well beyond ordinary attendance. The relationship is confirmed across the corporate membership directory, the exhibitor and sponsor lists, committee leadership, and individual volunteer roles.

- **Sponsor and exhibitor.** D2 Solutions is listed as a **Topaz Sponsor** and as an exhibitor for the 2025 NASP Annual Meeting & Expo ([NASP 2025 Sponsors & Exhibitors](https://naspnet.org/2025-annual-meeting-expo-sponsors-exhibitors)). D2's own event page records Booth #911 at the Gaylord Rockies (Denver), September 14-17, 2025, and identifies D2 as the **exclusive sponsor of NASP's Accreditation Workshop**, with its ComplySuite platform as the featured product ([D2 NASP 2025 page](https://d2rx.com/nasp-2025)).
- **Corporate member.** D2 Solutions appears in the NASP corporate member directory at the **Nickel** membership tier ([NASP Corporate Member Directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)).
- **Committee leadership.** Dean Erhardt, D2 Solutions Founder & CEO, is listed as a **Co-Chair of the NASP Technology Committee** ([NASP Technology Committee](https://naspnet.org/about/committees/technology)). This is a formal leadership role within NASP governance, not merely membership.
- **Annual meeting volunteer.** Dean Erhardt is listed on the NASP 2025 Annual Meeting & Expo volunteer roster as "President & CEO, D2 Solutions" ([NASP 2025 Volunteers](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)). The three other named D2 attendees (Quintin Jesse, David Suchanek, Eirik Olsen) do not appear on the volunteer list ([D2 NASP 2025 page](https://d2rx.com/nasp-2025)).
- **Speaking and media.** Dean Erhardt was a guest on the NASP Specialty Pharmacy Podcast on a "Technology to Drive Efficiency" theme ([NASP Podcast recap, D2 blog](https://blog.d2rx.com/d2-insights/dean-erhard-nasp-pharmacy-podcast)). The Accreditation Workshop D2 sponsors is described by D2 as featuring "roundtable, panel discussion, and speaker-led sessions" ([D2 NASP 2025 page](https://d2rx.com/nasp-2025)); the specific identity of D2 personnel as podium speakers in NASP general-session education programming is [unverified].
- **Tenure.** D2 states it has been "attending and exhibiting at NASP for over 15 years" ([D2 NASP 2025 page](https://d2rx.com/nasp-2025)). The exact start year is [unverified] against an independent NASP source.

**Assessment: confirmed-strong.** D2 holds simultaneous sponsor, exhibitor, corporate-member, committee-leadership (Technology Committee Co-Chair), and volunteer roles within NASP. This is one of the deepest organizational footprints encountered in this screening, but it is a *commercial and governance* footprint, not a *research* footprint.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

The distinction between commercial presence and scientific output is decisive for this screen, and the two diverge sharply for D2.

- **Local NASP poster archive (2019-2025).** A search of the parsed NASP poster archive (489 markdown files covering abstracts and posters 2019-2025) for "D2 Solutions," "D2Rx," "D2 Pharma," and "D2 Consulting" returned **zero matches**. The literal token "D2" was excluded as a confound because it is a common abbreviation. The archive is treated as a strong proxy for NASP poster authorship.
- **2025 NASP research abstracts (peer-reviewed compilation).** The 2025 NASP research presentation abstracts are published in Current Medical Research and Opinion ([Taylor & Francis, 2025 NASP abstracts](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2025.2529096)). Direct programmatic retrieval of this page returned HTTP 403 (publisher access control), so author-level confirmation against this single source is [unverified]; however, no D2-authored abstract surfaced in targeted web searches combining "D2 Solutions" or "Dean Erhardt" with "poster," "abstract," or "research presentation."
- **General literature.** No peer-reviewed outcomes studies, conference posters, or published evaluations authored or sponsored by D2 Solutions were identified in this review. D2's own site references unnamed "case studies" on its health-systems page but provides no quantified, attributed client outcomes in visible content ([D2 Health Systems](https://d2rx.com/health-systems)).

**Footprint: zero confirmed posters or published studies; characterization = whitespace (infrastructure-and-governance presence without any scientific output).** D2 is highly visible at NASP as a vendor and committee leader yet has, to the extent verifiable, never presented a research poster or published an outcomes study. This is precisely the asymmetry the screen seeks: established credibility and access with no competing evidence portfolio.

## 3. Business model and data assets

D2 Solutions (also branded D2 and D2Rx) was founded in 2008 and is based in Chesterfield, Missouri ([Who We Are](https://www.d2rx.com/about-us/); [silicon review profile](https://thesiliconreview.com/magazine/profile/d2-solutions-optimizing-healthcare-delivery-system)). It positions itself as an end-to-end consulting and technology partner serving pharmaceutical manufacturers, hospitals and integrated delivery networks (IDNs), pharmacies, payers, and PBMs ([D2 home](https://d2rx.com/)). Leadership is anchored by Founder & CEO Dean Erhardt, whose background includes Express Scripts, Cardinal Health, and U.S. Healthcare ([About Dean Erhardt](https://d2rx.com/dean-erhardt)).

The health-system specialty pharmacy line of business is organized in three phases ([D2 Health Systems](https://d2rx.com/health-systems)):

1. **Strategy and financial blueprinting:** ROI analysis, operational design, physical build-out, 340B optimization, and payer contracting support.
2. **Compliance and accreditation:** federal and state licensing, regulatory compliance management (RegComply), and accreditation support across URAC, ACHC, NABP, and TJC.
3. **Operations and patient access:** prior-authorization workflows (UltraTouch Verify), patient engagement and onboarding (UltraTouch Engage), and KPI dashboard development.

D2 reports having supported **over 120 specialty pharmacies** since 2008 and **over 40 accreditations** ([D2 Health Systems](https://d2rx.com/health-systems)). The "120+ specialty pharmacies built/supported" premise is therefore consistent with D2's own published claim; an independent third-party confirmation of the count is [unverified].

Technology platforms named by D2:
- **UltraTouch Verify** (prior-authorization optimization, framed around "first-submission success")
- **UltraTouch Engage** (patient onboarding, adherence, and engagement)
- **ComplySuite** (accreditation, licensing, and regulatory compliance management)
- **RegComply** (automated regulatory alert triage and compliance documentation)
([D2 NASP 2025 page](https://d2rx.com/nasp-2025); [D2 Health Systems](https://d2rx.com/health-systems))

**Data-asset assessment.** D2 is a consulting-plus-software firm, not a dispensing pharmacy. It does not own a longitudinal dispensing or claims dataset in the way a pharmacy operator would. The relevant data assets are of two kinds:
1. **Platform-generated operational data** from UltraTouch Verify and Engage (prior-authorization submission and approval rates, turnaround times, onboarding completion, adherence/engagement touchpoints) and from ComplySuite/RegComply (accreditation readiness, compliance status). This is process and operational metric data that D2's software produces or aggregates across deployments.
2. **Client clinical and dispensing data** held by the 120+ health-system specialty pharmacies D2 has supported. D2 does not own this data, but it has trusted-vendor access and an established relationship that could underpin client-permissioned analyses.

The honest characterization is that D2 itself holds **operational and process data with cross-client breadth but limited native longitudinal clinical/outcomes depth**; clinical outcome data sits with its health-system clients.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

Research fit is **moderate to high**, with the qualification that the most rigorous clinical-outcomes work would require client data partnerships rather than D2's data alone.

D2's strategic problem is a classic specialty-pharmacy sales problem: persuading hospital and IDN leadership that an in-house or D2-supported specialty pharmacy delivers measurable financial and patient-access returns. That is fundamentally an outcomes-and-ROI evidence question, and D2 currently markets it with unnamed, unquantified case studies ([D2 Health Systems](https://d2rx.com/health-systems)). Converting that into NASP-presented, methodologically rigorous evidence directly serves D2's sales motion to prospective health-system customers.

What D2 can credibly support:
- **Process-outcome studies from its own platform data** (for example, prior-authorization first-submission approval rates and turnaround-time reductions attributable to UltraTouch Verify; patient onboarding completion and engagement metrics from UltraTouch Engage). These draw on data D2 plausibly controls across multiple deployments and require no patient-level clinical linkage.
- **Accreditation and compliance benchmarking** across the 40+ accreditations and 120+ pharmacies D2 has supported, which is differentiated content well aligned with D2's Technology Committee Co-Chair platform and its Accreditation Workshop sponsorship.
- **Client-partnered outcomes and ROI studies** (clinical adherence, time-to-fill, financial contribution of a health-system specialty pharmacy build), which require the client health systems as data owners and co-authors. D2's vendor relationships make these realistic but contingent on client participation and governance.

Constraints to state plainly: D2 does not own longitudinal clinical outcomes data; multi-site aggregation raises data-use and de-identification considerations; and the strongest clinical claims (adherence, persistence, total cost of care) depend on client-held dispensing and EHR data. The lowest-friction, highest-certainty studies are those built on D2's own platform operational metrics.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

**Verdict: STRONG.**

Rationale: D2 combines (a) confirmed deep NASP standing (Topaz sponsor, exhibitor, Nickel corporate member, Technology Committee Co-Chair, annual-meeting volunteer) with (b) a confirmed absence of any research poster or published-evidence footprint, and (c) a business model whose central sales challenge (proving ROI and outcomes to prospective health-system customers) is exactly what rigorous outcomes research solves. The credibility-access plus evidence-whitespace combination is the ideal target profile for this screen. The principal caveat is data ownership: the most rigorous clinical studies require client partnerships, so the engagement should be scoped to lead with D2's own platform data and expand into client-partnered studies.

Recommended outreach angle: Approach D2 as an organization with maximal NASP visibility but no scientific output, and position the firm as the methodology partner that converts D2's operational platform data and client relationships into NASP-grade posters that strengthen D2's health-system sales narrative. Route the introduction through, or reference, Dean Erhardt's Technology Committee Co-Chair role, framing the work as advancing the committee's evidence agenda on technology-driven specialty-pharmacy efficiency.

Candidate study ideas:
1. **Prior-authorization performance evaluation (UltraTouch Verify):** a multi-deployment analysis of first-submission approval rates and turnaround-time reduction. Audience: prospective health-system customers evaluating D2's technology; also relevant to manufacturer partners.
2. **Health-system specialty pharmacy ROI and access model:** a client-partnered case-series quantifying financial contribution, capture rate, and patient time-to-therapy for D2-supported builds. Audience: hospital and IDN executive decision-makers (the core sales target).
3. **Accreditation readiness benchmarking (ComplySuite/RegComply):** a descriptive analysis of accreditation timelines and compliance outcomes across the 40+ accreditations D2 has supported. Audience: NASP accreditation-workshop attendees and pharmacies pursuing URAC/ACHC accreditation; reinforces D2's workshop sponsorship.
4. **Patient engagement and onboarding outcomes (UltraTouch Engage):** an evaluation of onboarding completion and adherence-engagement metrics across deployments. Audience: health systems and manufacturers prioritizing patient-experience and adherence outcomes.
