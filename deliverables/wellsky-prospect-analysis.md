# WellSky: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work is WellSky, and secondarily the specialty-pharmacy and home-infusion buyers it must convince of its products' operational value.

## 1. NASP presence

WellSky has a confirmed, multi-year institutional relationship with NASP, though one centered on commercial participation rather than research presentation.

- WellSky is a current NASP corporate member, listed at the Nickel tier in the [NASP corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory).
- WellSky appears on the [2022 NASP exhibitors and sponsors list](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors), confirming the premise that it exhibited at the 2022 Annual Meeting. The listing carries no descriptive text or sponsorship-tier annotation on that page.
- WellSky does not appear among the named organizations on the [NASP 2025 Annual Meeting and Expo volunteers list](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers), so there is no evidence of volunteer participation in 2025.

Summary: confirmed exhibitor (2022) and confirmed corporate member (Nickel tier). No evidence of speaking, faculty, volunteer, or research-presentation roles.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

WellSky has no identifiable poster or research-authorship footprint at NASP.

- The local parsed archive of NASP posters and abstracts spanning 2019 to 2025 (489 markdown files) contains zero matches for "WellSky," "CareTend," or "CPR+," searched as either an author affiliation or a named data-collection tool. This archive is treated as a strong proxy for NASP poster authorship across the period.
- A search of the published [2025 NASP Annual Meeting research presentation abstracts supplement](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2025.2529096) (Current Medical Research and Opinion, Vol. 41, No. sup2, 2025; 116 accepted abstracts) returned no mention of WellSky, CareTend, or CPR+ as an author affiliation or a software tool. (The publisher site returned an access restriction on direct retrieval; this finding rests on indexed search results and the local archive, and the absence is flagged as [partially verified] for 2025 because the full supplement text could not be retrieved directly.)

Characterization: whitespace. WellSky is present at NASP as a vendor, not as a producer of peer-reviewed or poster-form evidence. There is no detectable instance of WellSky as a poster author, and no detectable instance of its software being named as a data-capture tool inside another organization's poster within the local archive. This is the desired profile for a product-outcomes evidence engagement.

## 3. Business model and data assets

WellSky is a health and community care technology company whose specialty-pharmacy and home-infusion offering is the CareTend platform, the successor to the legacy CPR+ system. CPR+ reached end of service in December 2024, and WellSky reports that nearly 70% of CareTend clients migrated from CPR+ ([WellSky CareTend transition materials](https://info.wellsky.com/2023-CPR-to-CareTend-demo.html)).

Product scope. CareTend is described as a cloud-based, end-to-end operational platform for home infusion, specialty pharmacy, and HME/DME providers. Documented workflow coverage includes patient intake and referral capture, clinical documentation with digital signatures linked to billing, pharmacy and inventory management (lot tracking, expiration, compounding logs, compliance alerts), dispensing, shipping and delivery logistics, billing and revenue-cycle management, scheduling, electronic visit verification, and reporting and analytics dashboards covering revenue trends, payment timelines, and referral metrics ([Solum Health CareTend overview](https://getsolum.com/glossary/wellsky-caretend); [WellSky CareTend transition materials](https://info.wellsky.com/2023-CPR-to-CareTend-demo.html)). WellSky also markets adherence-supporting components such as eVoucherRx for at-home specialty patients ([WellSky blog: eVoucherRx](https://wellsky.com/blog/evoucherrxtm-supporting-medication-adherence-for-at-home-specialty-patients/)). Public secondary descriptions do not consistently confirm dedicated prior-authorization or formal therapy-management modules, so those specific capabilities are flagged [unverified].

Customers and scale. WellSky positions CareTend as serving more home infusion and specialty pharmacy organizations than any other software provider, and states that more than 10,000 people use its home infusion technologies daily ([WellSky home infusion software](https://wellsky.com/home-infusion-software/), as summarized in indexed results; the page returned an access restriction on direct retrieval and is flagged [partially verified]). At the corporate level, WellSky reports serving more than 15,000 client sites worldwide across hospital systems, home health and hospice, blood and lab services, government, and human services ([WellSky CarePort acquisition release](https://wellsky.com/wellsky-to-acquire-careport-health-from-allscripts-to-enhance-care-coordination-across-acute-post-acute-continuum/)). Its broader data network includes the CarePort care-coordination assets acquired from Allscripts in 2020 for approximately $1.35 billion, connecting roughly 1,000 hospitals and health systems and approximately 110,000 post-acute provider locations ([HIT Consultant](https://hitconsultant.net/2020/10/14/wellsky-acquires-careport-health/); [Hospice News](https://hospicenews.com/2020/10/15/hospice-tech-provider-wellsky-to-acquire-careport-health-for-1-35-billion/)).

Aggregate data potential. Across its CareTend specialty-pharmacy and infusion customer base, WellSky's platform is the system of record for referral and intake timestamps, dispensing and delivery events, inventory and compounding records, billing and claims data, and clinical documentation. In aggregate, and subject to customer data-ownership and consent terms, this footprint could in principle support de-identified, multi-site operational benchmarking. The exact size of the specialty-pharmacy and infusion customer segment specifically (as distinct from the 15,000 enterprise-wide client sites) is [unverified].

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

The research fit for a product-outcomes angle is high. CareTend captures the operational event data that map directly to the outcomes specialty-pharmacy buyers care about, and which can be framed as evidence that the software improves measurable performance:

- Time-to-therapy and time-to-first-fill, derived from intake-to-dispense and intake-to-delivery timestamps.
- Prior-authorization turnaround, if PA milestones are captured (capability [unverified]; would require confirmation of whether CareTend timestamps PA submission and approval).
- Prescription abandonment and fill-completion rates, derived from order and dispensing records.
- Medication adherence and persistence proxies (proportion of days covered, refill timeliness), derivable from refill and delivery records and supportable by adherence components such as eVoucherRx.
- Operational throughput and workflow efficiency (orders not falling through real-time queues, documentation-to-billing cycle time), supporting time-saved and revenue-cycle claims.

A product-outcomes study design would compare before-and-after or cross-site metrics tied to platform adoption or specific feature use, with the explicit purpose of demonstrating that the software improves measurable specialty-pharmacy outcomes. This is the type of evidence WellSky can use in sales and renewal conversations with specialty pharmacies, health systems, and infusion providers.

Constraints to address honestly:

- Data ownership and consent. The underlying operational and clinical data belong to WellSky's customers, not to WellSky. Any aggregate or multi-site analysis requires contractual data-use rights, de-identification, and likely customer consent. This is the primary feasibility gating factor and should be addressed at the outset of any engagement.
- Single-vendor framing. Product-outcomes research authored or sponsored by the software vendor carries inherent conflict-of-interest perception. Methodological rigor (pre-specified endpoints, appropriate comparators, transparent limitations) and, where possible, customer co-authorship are needed for credibility at a venue like NASP.
- Capability verification. PA-timestamp and formal therapy-management capabilities could not be confirmed from public sources and would need direct confirmation before a PA-turnaround or therapy-management study is proposed.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

Verdict: strong.

Rationale: WellSky combines an established NASP commercial relationship (2022 exhibitor, current Nickel-tier corporate member), a complete absence of NASP poster or research-authorship footprint (whitespace), a large operational data footprint across specialty-pharmacy and infusion customers, and event-level data assets that map cleanly to the outcomes specialty-pharmacy buyers prioritize. The combination of an existing channel into the conference plus no existing evidence portfolio is the target profile for a product-outcomes engagement.

Recommended outreach angle: Position the firm as the methodological partner that converts CareTend's operational event data into conference-ready, peer-reviewable evidence that WellSky's platform improves measurable specialty-pharmacy outcomes. Lead with the contrast between WellSky's strong NASP commercial presence and its empty research shelf, and propose a first study that doubles as a marketing and renewal asset. Frame the data-rights and conflict-of-interest constraints up front as problems the firm is equipped to manage (de-identification, multi-site consent design, pre-specified rigorous methods, customer co-authorship).

Candidate study ideas:

1. Time-to-therapy improvement after CareTend adoption. Multi-site, de-identified comparison of intake-to-first-dose intervals before and after platform or feature adoption. Audience: specialty-pharmacy and infusion operations leaders evaluating or renewing CareTend.
2. Prescription abandonment and fill-completion benchmarking. De-identified cross-site rates of order completion and abandonment, with platform-feature correlates. Audience: health-system specialty pharmacies and manufacturers' channel teams assessing fulfillment performance.
3. Adherence and persistence on the WellSky platform. Refill-timeliness and proportion-of-days-covered analysis among at-home specialty patients, with eVoucherRx and delivery-tracking features as covariates. Audience: payers, manufacturers, and pharmacy clients building adherence narratives.
4. Workflow-efficiency and documentation-to-billing cycle time. Operational throughput study quantifying time saved and revenue-cycle acceleration attributable to real-time queue management. Audience: specialty-pharmacy and infusion executives making build-versus-buy and renewal decisions.
