# Cervey: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work is Cervey, LLC, and the buyers it must convince are the health-system pharmacies, specialty pharmacies, 340B covered entities, and PBMs that purchase its software.

## 1. NASP presence

Cervey is confirmed as an exhibitor at the NASP 2022 Annual Meeting & Expo. The company name appears verbatim in the published 2022 exhibitor list, alphabetically positioned between "Cassian Solutions" and "Citus Health" ([NASP, Exhibitors & Sponsors](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors)). This corroborates the premise that Cervey was among NASP 2022 exhibitors.

Beyond that single exhibitor listing, no further NASP affiliation was found:

- Corporate membership: Cervey does not appear in the NASP corporate member directory across the listed tiers (Palladium, Platinum, Gold, Silver, Bronze, Nickel, Copper) ([NASP, Corporate Member Directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)). Not confirmed as a corporate member.
- Volunteers: Cervey does not appear among the approximately 380 named volunteers and their affiliations for the NASP 2025 Annual Meeting & Expo ([NASP, 2025 Annual Meeting & Expo Volunteers](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)). Not confirmed as a 2025 volunteer.
- Sponsorship tier: The 2022 page lists sponsors by tier (Diamond, Ruby, Sapphire, Topaz, Onyx). Cervey was identified in the exhibitor portion, not in a named sponsor tier; a paid sponsorship beyond exhibiting is [unverified].
- Presenter/author: No evidence of Cervey as a presenter or poster author at NASP was found (see Section 2).

Summary: Cervey has a confirmed but minimal NASP footprint, limited to a single 2022 exhibitor appearance. Its presence in 2023, 2024, and 2025 is [unverified]; the sources reviewed do not establish recurring exhibition, membership, or volunteer involvement.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

The local parsed archive of NASP posters and abstracts spanning 2019 to 2025 (489 markdown files) was searched for "Cervey" and "cervey" and returned zero matches. This archive is treated as a strong proxy for NASP poster authorship over that period.

Web searches against NASP abstract and poster pages and the associated journal supplements (published in Current Medical Research & Opinion) returned no Cervey-authored abstract or poster ([NASP, 2023 Abstracts and Poster Presentations](https://naspnet.org/2023-abstracts-and-poster-presentations); [NASP, 2025 Abstracts and Poster Presentations](https://naspnet.org/2025-abstracts-and-poster-presentations)). A targeted search for Cervey as an author of peer-reviewed or conference abstracts in specialty-pharmacy venues, including the Journal of Managed Care & Specialty Pharmacy (AMCP), returned no Cervey-authored work.

Characterization: whitespace. Cervey has no identifiable poster or published-evidence footprint as an author at NASP or comparable venues. The distinction between Cervey-as-software-data-tool and Cervey-as-author is relevant: Cervey's Catalyst platform automates "collection, documentation and reporting of key patient data to meet accreditation requirements" for its pharmacy clients ([Cervey, Catalyst](https://cervey.com/specialty-pharmacy/)), meaning Cervey software may sit beneath data that client pharmacies present, but no evidence places Cervey itself in an author or co-author role on any poster or study. Counted footprint as author: zero.

## 3. Business model and data assets

Corporate entity. Cervey, LLC is a pharmacy technology (software) company headquartered in Shreveport, Louisiana. It was rebranded from New Tech Computer Systems, LLC in March 2020 and is a wholly owned subsidiary within the Morris & Dickson family of brands (M&D Holding Company) ([PR Newswire, Morris & Dickson Announces Cervey, LLC](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/morris--dickson-announces-cervey-llc-premier-hospital-tech-provider-301023289.html); [Cervey, About](https://cervey.com/about-cervey/)). The name is uncommon and resolves cleanly to this single pharmacy-technology entity; no unrelated business of the same name was identified.

Business model. Cervey sells software and consultative services to health-system and hospital pharmacies, specialty pharmacies, 340B covered entities, and pharmacy benefit managers ([Cervey, About](https://cervey.com/about-cervey/)). It is a technology vendor, not a dispensing specialty pharmacy and not primarily a hub or patient-engagement services firm, although it has added patient-engagement capability through a 2025 partnership with PharmaCentra for contact-center services integrated into its platforms ([PRLog, Cervey and PharmaCentra Strategic Partnership](https://www.prlog.org/13101303-cervey-llc-and-pharmacentra-llc-announce-strategic-partnership-to-expand-pharmacy-technology-support-across-specialty-pharmacy-and-pbm-services.html)).

Product portfolio ([Cervey, About](https://cervey.com/about-cervey/)):
- Catalyst: specialty pharmacy operations and workflow software.
- 340B Navigator (with RetailNAV, SplitNAV, ReferralNav, and Rebate Readiness Hub): 340B program management and compliance.
- Adjudicator: PBM claims adjudication.
- Passport: patient assistance program (PAP) management.
- Tracker: DSCSA track-and-trace.

Company size and viability. Third-party aggregators report Cervey at roughly 37 to 63 employees and approximately $9.3 million in annual revenue, with one source citing about 51 employees as of May 2025 ([ZoomInfo](https://www.zoominfo.com/c/cervey-llc/482859467); [RocketReach](https://rocketreach.co/cervey-profile_b42ac018fe0187da); [LeadIQ](https://leadiq.com/c/cervey/5f0b51abde5d70046c736179)). These figures are aggregator estimates and should be treated as approximate [unverified]. Backing by the Morris & Dickson organization is a stabilizing factor for viability relative to an unaffiliated startup of similar headcount.

Data assets. The relevant data does not belong to Cervey. Catalyst captures patient eligibility and benefits, customizable clinical protocols and assessments, prescription and order information, real-time HL7/FHIR EHR integration data, and accreditation-oriented documentation ([Cervey, Catalyst](https://cervey.com/specialty-pharmacy/)). This is operational, clinical, and dispensing data generated within client pharmacies' instances of the software. Cervey is the platform operator, not the data owner; any research use would require client data, client consent, and likely de-identification or aggregation. The public product pages do not advertise analytics dashboards, outcomes measurement, or research tooling, which is a gap and an opportunity rather than a disqualifier.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

Moderate fit, contingent on a defined data pathway.

Arguments for fit:
- Cervey's platforms sit at the operational center of specialty pharmacy workflow (patient identification, benefits investigation, prior authorization, financial assistance, dispensing, and accreditation reporting). These are exactly the process measures that translate into conference-ready outcomes: time-to-therapy, prior-authorization turnaround, abandonment and capture rates, financial-assistance dollars secured, and adherence proxies.
- The company sells to a NASP-aligned buyer base (health-system and specialty pharmacies, 340B entities). Evidence demonstrating that pharmacies using Catalyst, 340B Navigator, or Passport achieve measurable improvements would be directly usable as sales and differentiation material to that same audience.
- Whitespace is the strategic asset here. With zero prior poster footprint, a first credible NASP poster would be net-new visibility and would position Cervey alongside peer vendors that already exhibit and publish.

Constraints and honest caveats:
- Data ownership. Cervey does not own the patient-level data; client pharmacies do. Any study requires a willing client site (or a multi-site aggregate) plus a data-use agreement and de-identification. This is the central feasibility gating item.
- Vendor-attribution risk. Outcomes observed in client pharmacies reflect the combined effect of the pharmacy's staff and the software. Methodologically rigorous design (pre/post with controls, or benchmarked cohorts) is needed to avoid overclaiming software-attributable effect, which is precisely where an HEOR consultancy adds value.
- Company scale. At roughly 40 to 60 employees and approximately $9 million revenue, Cervey is small. Budget for a research engagement may be modest, and there is no in-house research or HEOR function evident, which argues for a turnkey, low-burden engagement model. Morris & Dickson affiliation may expand budget capacity but is [unverified] as a funding source for this purpose.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

Verdict: moderate (leaning toward the stronger end of moderate).

Rationale: Cervey is a confirmed, real, NASP-adjacent company with a verified 2022 exhibitor presence, a buyer base that overlaps the NASP audience, and complete whitespace as a poster author. It operates platforms that generate the operational and clinical data classes from which conference-ready outcomes can be built. It is downgraded from "strong" by two factors: it does not own the underlying data (a client-site or multi-site data pathway must be arranged), and it is a small company with no evident in-house research function, which constrains budget and requires a low-burden engagement design.

Outreach angle: Position the firm as the turnkey research partner that converts data already flowing through Cervey's platforms (with one or more consenting client pharmacies) into a methodologically rigorous, NASP-ready poster that Cervey can use as differentiation in a crowded specialty-pharmacy-technology market where peer vendors already exhibit and publish. Lead with the whitespace point: a first credible NASP poster is high-leverage, low-cost visibility, and emphasize a design that isolates software-attributable effect to avoid overclaiming.

Candidate study ideas:
1. Time-to-therapy and prior-authorization turnaround in a health-system specialty pharmacy using Catalyst (pre/post implementation or benchmarked cohort). Audience: health-system pharmacy directors and specialty pharmacy operations leaders, the core Catalyst buyer.
2. Prescription capture and abandonment-rate reduction attributable to integrated benefits investigation and financial-assistance workflow (Catalyst plus Passport). Audience: specialty pharmacy and 340B program leaders focused on revenue retention and access.
3. Financial-assistance dollars secured and patient out-of-pocket reduction via PAP automation (Passport). Audience: patient-access and financial-counseling leaders, and accreditation stakeholders (URAC/ACHC).
4. 340B compliance and savings-capture outcomes using 340B Navigator across contract-pharmacy and split-bill workflows. Audience: 340B program managers and hospital pharmacy finance leaders.
