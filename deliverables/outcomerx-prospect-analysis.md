# OutcomeRx: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work is OutcomeRx; the audience it must convince comprises the payors, plan sponsors, manufacturers, and risk-bearing intermediaries that buy or distribute its cell and gene therapy (CGT) insurance and warranty products.

## 1. NASP presence

OutcomeRx has a confirmed, multi-signal NASP footprint.

- **Corporate membership.** OutcomeRx is listed in the NASP corporate member directory at the Copper tier ([NASP corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)) and has a dedicated NASP corporate member page ([naspnet.org/corp-member/outcomerx](https://naspnet.org/corp-member/outcomerx)). The corporate member page carries a publication date of October 3, 2023, and the directory logo asset is dated September 2023, indicating membership since at least Q3 2023 ([NASP corporate member page](https://naspnet.org/corp-member/outcomerx)).
- **Volunteer participation.** Two OutcomeRx executives appear on the NASP annual meeting volunteers page: Joseph Morse, listed as CEO, and Kirby Eng, BS Pharm, listed as Chief Clinical Officer ([NASP annual meeting volunteers](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)). This confirms active individual engagement, not only a passive directory listing.
- **2026 inSPire event.** NASP has rebranded its annual meeting as inSPire, scheduled for September 22-25, 2026, at the Gaylord National Resort and Convention Center, National Harbor, Maryland ([NASP annual meeting](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting); [inSPire2026 registration](https://events.mma-inc.com/event/inSPire2026/home)). A direct OutcomeRx sponsor, exhibitor, or speaker role at inSPire 2026 **could not be confirmed** from the public registration shell, which does not publish a sponsor or exhibitor list at the time of review [unverified]. The premise of a "2026 inSPire event signal" is therefore consistent with OutcomeRx's standing membership and volunteer activity but is not independently verifiable as a confirmed 2026 sponsorship.

Net assessment: NASP presence is confirmed-strong on membership and volunteer dimensions; the specific 2026 inSPire role remains unverified.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

The poster and peer-reviewed evidence footprint is effectively whitespace.

- **NASP poster archive (2019-2025).** A local parsed archive of 489 NASP poster and abstract files (2019-2025) returns zero matches for OutcomeRx, OutcomesRx, or OutcomeRx Insurance Management Services on a case-insensitive search. This archive is treated as a strong proxy for NASP poster authorship.
- **Comparable venues.** Targeted searches of AMCP, the Journal of Managed Care and Specialty Pharmacy (JMCP), ISPOR, and PubMed returned no peer-reviewed publications or conference posters authored by OutcomeRx or its named staff ([JMCP meeting abstracts index](https://www.jmcp.org/supplements/meeting-abstracts)). OutcomeRx's own collateral cites third-party JMCP articles on CGT pricing but does not present original OutcomeRx-authored evidence ([OutcomeRx PACCT FAQ](https://outcomerx.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/01/OutcomeRx-PACCT-FAQ-Sheet.pdf)).

Characterization: infrastructure participant with no published-evidence output. Count of identified posters or peer-reviewed studies: 0.

## 3. Business model and data assets

OutcomeRx is a CGT insurance and warranty business, not a dispensing specialty pharmacy.

- **Corporate entity and ownership.** The canonical operating entity is OutcomeRx Insurance Management Services, LLC ([outcomerx.com](https://outcomerx.com/)). It was acquired by CGT Solutions from CareMetx on May 15, 2023; CareMetx retains a minority interest, and CareMetx co-founder Mark Hansan sits on the CGT Solutions board ([CareMetx press release](https://www.caremetx.com/press-release/cgt-solutions-acquires-outcomerx)). Joseph (Joe) Morse is CEO of CGT Solutions and is the OutcomeRx CEO listed by NASP ([CareMetx press release](https://www.caremetx.com/press-release/cgt-solutions-acquires-outcomerx); [NASP annual meeting volunteers](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)). This is distinct from the unrelated pharmacy-technology company "Outcomes" (outcomes.com).
- **What it does.** OutcomeRx provides "reinsurance solution to payors and other risk bearing entities" and "warranties to align clinical and financial outcomes" for high-cost cell and gene therapies ([CareMetx press release](https://www.caremetx.com/press-release/cgt-solutions-acquires-outcomerx)). Its product set includes a Specialty Therapy Warranty / Patient Access to Costly and Curative Therapies (PACCT) program, tiered coverage offerings (full coverage, risk sharing, services only), care navigation to a network described as "100+ Centers of Excellence," claims and utilization management, and a CAR-T and gene therapy targeted stop-loss insurance product launched in May 2026 ([OutcomeRx](https://outcomerx.com/); [BusinessWire, May 12, 2026](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20260512126746/en/OutcomeRx-Launches-Innovative-Targeted-Stop-Loss-Insurance-Product-Covering-CAR-T-Cell-and-Gene-Therapies)).
- **Customers.** The buyers are insurance brokers and agents, employers and plan sponsors, payors and carriers, CGT manufacturers, and intermediaries (TPAs, captives, MGUs) ([outcomerx.com](https://outcomerx.com/)). The company positions itself as "an independent, payor-agnostic partner." Specialty pharmacies are a channel and clinical-network counterpart, not the paying customer.
- **Data assets.** OutcomeRx describes "data insights that capture essential quality measures" and, by function, accrues CGT claims data, utilization patterns, warranty-trigger and durability data, and outcomes tracked against warranty terms ([outcomerx.com](https://outcomerx.com/); [Association for Cell and Gene Therapy Solutions](https://cgtsolutions.outcomerx.com/)). The granularity, volume, and consent status of this data are **not publicly specified** [unverified]; warranty-linked outcomes data is plausibly thin per therapy given small CGT patient counts.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

Moderate. The fit is conceptually strong but constrained by data maturity.

- **Argument for fit.** OutcomeRx sells on the premise that warranties "align clinical and financial outcomes." Evidence quantifying warranty performance (for example, durability rates, warranty-trigger frequency, total cost of care versus list price, or financial protection delivered to plan sponsors) would directly substantiate its core sales claim to payors and employers. The company has no published evidence today, so any rigorous, conference-grade output would be differentiating and would address an audience (payors, brokers, CGT manufacturers) that values quantified financial risk-mitigation data.
- **Constraints.** Cell and gene therapy cohorts are small, and warranty programs are recent (PACCT collateral dated 2024; stop-loss product launched May 2026), so durable outcomes data may be immature and counts may be too low for robust inference [unverified]. Much of the relevant data is claims and actuarial rather than clinical-dispensing data, which differs from the typical NASP HSSP poster (adherence, time-to-fill, clinical interventions). Methodologically rigorous design (de-identification, appropriate denominators, sensitivity to small-n) would be essential.
- **Alignment with firm value proposition.** The firm's strongest pitch applies when an organization can show outcomes to ITS customers. OutcomeRx's customers are payors, employers, and manufacturers, and its product literally promises outcomes alignment, so evidence generation maps cleanly onto its commercial need. The gap is whether OutcomeRx holds enough mature, analyzable data to support it now.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

**Verdict: moderate.**

Rationale: OutcomeRx is a confirmed, engaged NASP corporate member with named executive volunteers and zero competing poster footprint, which is the desired whitespace profile. Its business model creates a genuine and unmet need for outcomes evidence aimed at payors, employers, and manufacturers. The reservation is data maturity: small CGT cohorts and recently launched products may limit what can be analyzed rigorously in the near term, and the data is more actuarial than clinical. This is a credible target, not a top-tier certainty.

Recommended outreach angle: Approach Joseph Morse (CEO) and Kirby Eng (Chief Clinical Officer), both already visible in NASP volunteer activity, with a capabilities-led conversation framed around converting warranty and claims data into payer-facing and employer-facing evidence. Lead with a data-readiness assessment rather than a fixed study, given the unverified maturity of their outcomes dataset. Position the firm as the methodological partner that lets OutcomeRx be the first in its category to publish quantified outcomes evidence at inSPire.

Candidate study ideas:
1. Warranty performance and financial protection analysis: warranty-trigger frequency, refunds or credits issued, and net cost-of-care versus list price across the CGT warranty book. Audience: payors, employers, and plan sponsors evaluating financial risk mitigation.
2. Real-world durability and outcomes tracking against warranty terms for a specific therapy class (for example, gene therapy or CAR-T): observed durability versus warranty thresholds. Audience: CGT manufacturers and payors negotiating outcomes-based contracts.
3. Total-cost-of-care and site-of-care analysis for members routed through Centers of Excellence versus a comparator: cost, complication, and access metrics. Audience: payors and employers assessing the value of managed CGT access.
4. Methods and framework poster: a reproducible design for measuring outcomes-based-contract performance in small-n CGT populations. Audience: the broader NASP and managed-care community, positioning OutcomeRx as a thought leader where head-to-head clinical data is scarce.
