# Visante: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP (National Association of Specialty Pharmacy) outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed engagement would be Visante and, by extension, the health-system specialty pharmacy clients it serves.

## 1. NASP presence

Visante's affiliation with NASP is confirmed across multiple categories of participation.

- **Corporate member (Platinum tier).** Visante is listed in the NASP corporate member directory under the Platinum membership category, linked to visanteinc.com ([NASP corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)).
- **2025 sponsor (Topaz tier) and exhibitor.** Visante appears on the NASP 2025 Annual Meeting & Expo sponsors and exhibitors page in the Topaz sponsor tier and in the exhibitor listing ([NASP 2025 sponsors and exhibitors](https://naspnet.org/2025-annual-meeting-expo-sponsors-exhibitors)).
- **2025 volunteer.** One Visante staff member, Angie Amado, PharmD, Senior Director, is listed as a NASP 2025 Annual Meeting & Expo volunteer under the affiliation "Visante Consulting LLC" ([NASP 2025 volunteers](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)).

Both premises in the screening brief are verified: Visante is a NASP corporate member, and it appears on a NASP volunteer list. The relationship extends further than the premise stated, encompassing paid sponsorship and exhibition. This indicates an active, multi-channel commercial relationship with NASP and recurring physical presence at the Annual Meeting.

What is **not** confirmed: any record of Visante as a **presenter of peer-reviewed research** at NASP. Sponsorship, exhibition, and volunteering are commercial and organizational forms of participation; they are distinct from authoring an accepted abstract or poster. See Section 2.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

The poster and peer-reviewed evidence footprint is characterized as **whitespace**: no conference research authorship was identified.

- **Local NASP poster archive (2019-2025).** A case-insensitive search of the parsed archive of NASP abstracts and posters covering 2019 through 2025 (489 markdown files) returned zero matches for "Visante." This archive serves as a strong proxy for NASP poster authorship over the period.
- **NASP published abstract program.** A review of the NASP abstract program literature found no occurrence of "Visante" in author affiliations ([The NASP Abstract Program, PMC6764371](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6764371/)). The article notes that NASP abstract authors typically represent independent specialty pharmacies, manufacturers, group purchasing organizations, integrated delivery systems and health plans, technology and data-management vendors, and practicing pharmacists; pharmacy consultancies are not a prominent author category.
- **NASP annual abstract compilations (2023, 2024, 2025).** Targeted searches of the NASP Annual Meeting research-presentation abstract compilations, published in Current Medical Research & Opinion, did not surface a Visante author affiliation ([NASP 2025 abstracts](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2025.2529096); [NASP 2024 abstracts](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2024.2386874); [NASP 2023 abstracts](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2023.2236891)). The 2025 compilation itself was not directly retrievable (HTTP 403); the negative finding rests on the local archive and search-index results [partial-access].
- **Other venues (AMCP, ASHP, HOPA).** Searches for Visante author affiliations on posters or abstracts at AMCP, ASHP, and HOPA returned no specific Visante-authored research. One indirect academic-literature association was identified: a Visante-affiliated pharmacist served as a committee co-chair for a Pharmacy Society of Wisconsin workforce summit reported in the American Journal of Health-System Pharmacy ([AJHP article](https://academic.oup.com/ajhp/article/83/6/312/8299817)). This is a workforce-strategy report, not specialty-pharmacy outcomes research, and reflects an individual's professional service rather than a Visante research program.

**Consulting content versus research.** Visante publishes white papers, blogs, videos, and client case studies through its website and sponsored-content channels (for example, [Visante case studies](https://www.visante.com/insights/case-studies/) and [a sponsored-content feature on 340B Report](https://340breport.com/top-10-visantes-insights-for-the-biggest-issues-in-health-system-pharmacy-in-2025-sponcon-visante/)). The published case studies state qualitative goals (patient access, scalability, financial performance) and do not present quantitative, methodologically described outcomes on the public pages reviewed. This body of marketing and thought-leadership content is distinct from peer-reviewed or conference-vetted research and does not constitute an evidence footprint in the sense used here.

Estimated peer-reviewed or conference poster count attributable to Visante as an institutional author at NASP or comparable venues: **0 confirmed**.

## 3. Business model and data assets

Visante is a pharmacy consulting firm founded in 1999 and now led by CEO Amy Amick, with Steve Rough as Chairman ([Visante About page](https://www.visante.com/about/)). Its service lines target hospitals and health systems and include specialty pharmacy program development, integrated specialty pharmacy and infusion solutions, 340B program performance, pharmacy revenue cycle management, medication access services, and pharmacy supply chain optimization ([Visante solutions](https://www.visante.com/solutions/); [specialty pharmacy](https://www.visante.com/solutions/specialty-pharmacy/); [infusion](https://www.visante.com/solutions/infusion-pharmacy-solutions/); [340B](https://www.visante.com/solutions/340b-program-performance/)).

**Customers.** The primary customer base is health systems and hospitals. Public case studies name health-system clients including Skagit Regional Health and Benefis Health System and reference a pediatric specialty pharmacy engagement ([Visante case studies](https://www.visante.com/insights/case-studies/)).

**Data assets.** As a consultancy, Visante's relationship to data differs from that of an operating specialty pharmacy. Its public materials describe using "data and onsite assessments" during the diagnostic phase of engagements ([specialty pharmacy page](https://www.visante.com/solutions/specialty-pharmacy/)) but do not describe ownership of longitudinal patient-level, dispensing, or claims datasets, nor an internal analytics or research infrastructure ([Visante About page](https://www.visante.com/about/)). The realistic data position is:

- Visante does **not** appear to own longitudinal patient outcomes data of its own [inferred from absence of any such claim in public sources; not independently confirmable].
- Through engagements, Visante has **access to client health systems' operational, financial, dispensing, and program-performance data** for the duration and scope of each project. Any research use of those data would require the client health system as data owner and the relevant data-use and privacy agreements.
- Visante accumulates **cross-client operational benchmarking knowledge** (capture rates, time-to-fill, revenue cycle metrics, program ramp timelines) that, if systematically aggregated and de-identified with client consent, could support multi-site descriptive or benchmarking studies.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

Overall research fit is assessed as **moderate**, with the qualifier that feasibility depends on client data access rather than on data Visante owns outright.

Arguments for fit:

- Visante's value proposition (helping health systems stand up and optimize specialty pharmacy, infusion, and 340B operations) maps directly onto the kinds of operational and financial outcomes that NASP audiences value: prescription capture rate, time-to-first-fill, medication access turnaround, adherence proxies, revenue cycle performance, and program growth trajectories.
- Visante engages multiple health systems, which creates the raw material for **multi-site benchmarking and before/after implementation studies**, a study design well suited to a consultancy and difficult for any single operating pharmacy to replicate.
- A conference-vetted poster would convert Visante's existing case-study and white-paper content into independently credible evidence, differentiating it from competitors whose claims remain in marketing form. The whitespace in Visante's research footprint (Section 2) is precisely the gap that conference-ready outcomes research would fill.

Constraints on fit:

- Visante likely does not hold longitudinal patient outcomes data and would depend on client health systems as data owners; this introduces data-use agreement, IRB or determination-of-non-research, and attribution complexity. Posters would typically be co-authored with the client health system.
- Much of the natural endpoint set is operational and financial rather than clinical. This is acceptable and common at NASP, but it limits claims about patient clinical outcomes unless a client contributes adherence or clinical data.
- As a vendor, Visante must manage the perception of marketing-driven evidence; rigorous, pre-specified methods and client co-authorship mitigate this.

Net assessment: Visante can realistically produce conference-ready outcomes research, but the most credible path routes through aggregated, de-identified, client-consented operational data and co-authorship with health-system clients, rather than through proprietary patient-level data it owns.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

**Verdict: strong.**

Rationale: Visante combines an active and multi-tiered NASP relationship (Platinum corporate member, Topaz sponsor, exhibitor, and volunteer) with a confirmed absence of any peer-reviewed or conference research footprint. It is already paying to be present at the venue where research credibility is conferred but is not yet participating in the research channel. It serves multiple health systems and therefore has access (through engagements) to the operational data needed for benchmarking and implementation studies. This is the profile of a high-intent prospect with clear whitespace to fill.

Recommended outreach angle: Position the engagement as converting Visante's existing operational track record and case-study content into NASP-vetted, co-authored evidence that demonstrates capability and ROI to prospective health-system customers. Lead with the observation that Visante invests in NASP presence (sponsorship, exhibition, volunteering) but has no poster footprint, and that a small portfolio of methodologically rigorous, client co-authored posters would close that gap and strengthen the sales narrative at the exact venue where its buyers convene. Frame data-access and co-authorship logistics up front to address the consultancy-data constraint honestly.

Candidate study ideas:

1. **Multi-site specialty pharmacy implementation benchmarking study.** Aggregate de-identified program-launch metrics (time-to-go-live, prescription capture rate, time-to-first-fill, access turnaround) across several Visante-supported health systems. Audience: prospective health-system buyers evaluating whether to build internal specialty pharmacy and what performance to expect.
2. **Before/after revenue cycle and capture-rate study at a single anchor client.** A focused, co-authored poster with one health-system client quantifying capture-rate and revenue cycle improvement after a Visante engagement. Audience: health-system pharmacy and finance leaders weighing the ROI of consulting-led optimization.
3. **340B program performance and integrity study.** Describe compliance and performance outcomes across 340B engagements, an area of acute, current regulatory attention. Audience: 340B-covered entities and health-system compliance leaders.
4. **Infusion and site-of-care optimization outcomes study.** Quantify operational and access outcomes from integrated specialty pharmacy plus infusion launches. Audience: health systems evaluating in-house infusion build versus outsourcing.
