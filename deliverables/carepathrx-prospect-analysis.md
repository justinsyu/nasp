# CarepathRx: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work is CarepathRx (corporate entity CarepathRx LLC) and, by extension, its health-system clients, who are the audience for any outcomes evidence produced.

## 1. NASP presence

CarepathRx has a confirmed, recurring presence at the NASP Annual Meeting & Expo as an exhibitor, listed under its technology unit "CarepathRx Technology Solutions."

- The premise that CarepathRx was a NASP 2022 exhibitor is verified. The entity "CarepathRx Technology Solutions" appears in the 2022 exhibitor list ([NASP exhibitors and sponsors](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors)).
- The same entity appears in the 2025 exhibitor list, where the listing links to dromosrx.com, the domain of its DromosPTM software product ([NASP 2025 sponsors and exhibitors](https://naspnet.org/2025-annual-meeting-expo-sponsors-exhibitors)).
- CarepathRx's own events calendar lists the NASP Annual Meeting & Expo of September 18-21, 2023, as a past event, which is consistent with attendance or exhibition that year, though the page does not state the participation type ([CarepathRx NASP event page](https://carepathrxllc.com/event/nasp-annual-meeting-expo/)).

Two negatives are worth recording:

- CarepathRx does not appear in the NASP corporate member directory under any spelling variant ([NASP corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)). It is therefore an exhibitor, not a dues-paying corporate member, on the available evidence.
- No CarepathRx involvement as a sponsor, volunteer, or presenter was confirmed. Its NASP listings are in the exhibitor tier, not the sponsor tiers, in both 2022 and 2025. Volunteer participation is [unverified]; the 2025 volunteer roster was not retrieved for this analysis.

Summary: confirmed-strong as an exhibitor (2022 and 2025 confirmed by name; 2023 attendance indicated by CarepathRx's own calendar). No evidence of corporate membership, sponsorship at named tiers, or presenter status.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

CarepathRx has essentially no poster footprint at NASP and a thin published-evidence footprint overall. This is whitespace.

- Local NASP poster archive (2019-2025, 489 parsed files): no CarepathRx authorship. The only string matches ("CarePathway," "Care Pathway") are generic clinical terms in unrelated posters (a newborn HIV continuity poster, an inflammatory bowel disease citation, and a care-pathway diagram), not the company. Treating this archive as a strong proxy for NASP poster authorship, the count is zero.
- No CarepathRx-affiliated abstract was surfaced for the NASP 2024 research presentation program in targeted web searching. Direct confirmation against the 2024 abstract supplement was [unverified] because the publisher page returned an access error (HTTP 403) ([NASP 2024 abstracts, publisher](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2024.2390294)). No positive evidence of a CarepathRx 2024 NASP poster exists.
- One CarepathRx-authored study was located: a retrospective quality-improvement study of pharmacist-directed vancomycin dosing among adult home infusion patients, with all four authors affiliated with CarepathRx ([NHIA study summary](https://nhia.org/a-retrospective-quality-improvement-study-of-pharmacist-directed-vancomycin-dosing-among-adult-home-infusion-patients/)). Its methodology is thin by design: a single-arm retrospective chart review of 31 participants treated in 2021, with no comparison group; 18 of 31 (58.06%) achieved infection resolution without therapy extension and 2 of 31 (6.5%) experienced vancomycin-related acute kidney injury. The authors note the small sample and EHR-limited data collection as limitations.

Characterization: whitespace at NASP (zero posters) combined with a thin existing evidence base elsewhere (one small, single-arm, descriptive study without denominators large enough for inference or any comparison group). This matches the firm's preferred target profile.

## 3. Business model and data assets

CarepathRx operates an end-to-end hospital pharmacy care-delivery model for health systems and hospitals, positioning pharmacy as a clinical and financial asset rather than a cost center.

- Customers are institutional: health systems and hospitals. The company reports serving more than 20 health systems and 600 hospitals, with more than 2,000 employees, and states it dispenses over 8 million prescriptions annually across partnerships with 600 hospital systems, 450 payers, and risk-bearing provider partners ([CarepathRx LLC search summary, business reach](https://carepathrxllc.com/specialty-pharmacy/)).
- Service lines span specialty pharmacy, hospital and home infusion, transition-of-care management, 340B optimization, telepharmacy, prior authorization, patient financial assistance, supply-chain management, and managed-care contracting support ([CarepathRx specialty pharmacy](https://carepathrxllc.com/specialty-pharmacy/); [CarepathRx Health System Solutions launch](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/carepathrx-launches-health-system-solutions-business-301199178.html)). The Health System Solutions release cites over 20 million patient-related interactions annually across more than 600 hospitals.
- The company has grown by acquisition and partnership: a $400M landmark transaction with UPMC in December 2020, under which CarepathRx acquired the management services organization overseeing Chartwell (a Pittsburgh-based home infusion and specialty pharmacy operator) and UPMC became a strategic investor ([UPMC partnership summary](https://www.paragonventures.com/market-pulse-posts/carepathrx-and-upmc-announce-landmark-partnership-in-400mm-deal/)). It also divested BioPlus specialty pharmacy to Elevance Health in 2022 ([Elevance to acquire BioPlus](https://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20221108006310/en/Elevance-Health-to-Acquire-BioPlus)).
- Data-generating technology: DromosPTM, a cloud-based patient-therapy-management platform acquired via Semita in 2020 and originally built at Henry Ford Health. It records and reports treatment, counseling, adherence, and outcomes data per participant and generates dispensing, inventory, and patient-status reports for limited-distribution-drug contracts ([DromosPTM, Henry Ford expansion](https://accessmarketintell.com/2022/10/25/dromosptm-a-carepathrx-company-developed-by-henry-ford-health-expands-nationally/); [Dromos Rx](https://dromosrx.com/)). The CarepathRx NASP exhibitor presence is anchored on this technology unit.

As a byproduct of these services, CarepathRx accumulates dispensing records, adherence tracking, prior-authorization and access outcomes, infusion clinical monitoring data, transition-of-care touchpoints, and 340B financial data across a large, multi-health-system footprint.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

Research fit is high. CarepathRx has the three ingredients that make outcomes research feasible and credible.

- Denominators: a defined operational footprint (20+ health systems, 600+ hospitals, 8M prescriptions/year, 20M patient interactions/year) provides large, countable populations for rate-based and cohort analyses ([CarepathRx business reach](https://carepathrxllc.com/specialty-pharmacy/); [Health System Solutions launch](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/carepathrx-launches-health-system-solutions-business-301199178.html)).
- Longitudinal data: DromosPTM captures per-participant treatment, adherence, counseling, and outcomes over time, and the home-infusion and transition-of-care lines generate repeated clinical contact, enabling follow-up windows ([DromosPTM](https://dromosrx.com/)).
- Outcomes worth studying: adherence and persistence, time-to-therapy and prior-authorization turnaround, infusion safety (for example acute kidney injury and adverse drug reactions, already piloted in the vancomycin study), 30-day readmission avoidance through transition-of-care, and 340B-linked financial performance. The existing vancomycin study demonstrates both the relevance of these endpoints and the methodological gap: a comparison group and adequate sample size are absent and could be supplied by a properly designed study ([NHIA vancomycin study](https://nhia.org/a-retrospective-quality-improvement-study-of-pharmacist-directed-vancomycin-dosing-among-adult-home-infusion-patients/)).

The strategic motivation aligns with the data. CarepathRx sells the proposition that integrated pharmacy converts a cost center into a quality and revenue asset, a claim that is currently supported by capability statements rather than published, denominator-anchored outcomes ([Health System Solutions launch](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/carepathrx-launches-health-system-solutions-business-301199178.html)). Conference-ready evidence would directly serve its sales motion to prospective health-system customers.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

Verdict: strong.

Rationale: CarepathRx is a confirmed recurring NASP exhibitor (2022 and 2025 by name) with effectively zero NASP poster footprint and only a single small, single-arm published study. It simultaneously operates one of the larger health-system pharmacy footprints in the market, generating large-denominator longitudinal data through DromosPTM and its infusion and transition-of-care lines. The combination of NASP whitespace, thin existing evidence, abundant proprietary data, and a sales narrative that currently lacks published outcomes makes it a high-priority target.

Outreach angle: position the firm as the partner that converts CarepathRx's operational and clinical data into NASP-ready, methodologically defensible outcomes that its health-system clients and prospects can cite. Lead with the gap between its strong capability claims and its absence of denominator-anchored, comparison-grouped published evidence, and reference the vancomycin study as proof the data and clinical questions already exist but warrant stronger study design.

Candidate study ideas:

1. Adherence and persistence on limited-distribution specialty drugs managed through DromosPTM, reported as proportion-of-days-covered with a benchmark or matched comparison, denominator drawn from the multi-health-system dispensing population. Audience: prospective health-system pharmacy directors evaluating CarepathRx's clinical management.
2. Transition-of-care and home-infusion safety and 30-day readmission outcomes, expanding the vancomycin pilot into a larger, comparison-grouped cohort with infection resolution, acute kidney injury, and readmission endpoints. Audience: hospital C-suite and quality leaders weighing the clinical case for outsourced infusion.
3. Access and speed-to-therapy metrics (prior-authorization turnaround, time-to-first-fill, financial-assistance capture) across the 600-hospital network. Audience: health-system revenue-cycle and access leaders, and drug manufacturers granting limited-distribution contracts.
4. 340B and pharmacy financial-performance outcomes framing pharmacy as a revenue and margin asset, paired with quality endpoints to avoid a purely financial narrative. Audience: health-system finance and executive decision-makers in CarepathRx's sales pipeline.
