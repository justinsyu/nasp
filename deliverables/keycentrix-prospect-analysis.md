# Keycentrix: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP (National Association of Specialty Pharmacy) outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work is Keycentrix, and the secondary audience is the specialty-pharmacy buyers Keycentrix must convince that its software produces measurable operational and clinical outcomes.

## 1. NASP presence

Keycentrix has a confirmed, multi-year, and structurally deep relationship with NASP. The relationship is among the strongest observed for any software vendor in this screening exercise.

- Keycentrix is listed in the NASP corporate member directory under the Nickel Members tier, with an asterisk and a "Founding member" annotation indicating it was a founding member of the association ([NASP corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)).
- Keycentrix is named on the NASP 2022 exhibitor and sponsor roster ([NASP exhibitors and sponsors page](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors)). This confirms the premise's claim that Keycentrix was among NASP 2022 exhibitors.
- Keycentrix was a Ruby Sponsor at NASP 2025, exhibiting at booth 703 at the Gaylord Rockies in Denver, September 14-17, and describes itself on its own announcement page as "a proud founding member" of NASP ([Keycentrix NASP 2025 page](https://keycentrix.com/nasp-2025/)).
- Independent trade coverage describes Keycentrix as "a founding member of the National Association of Specialty Pharmacy" ([Pharmacy Times profile](https://www.pharmacytimes.com/view/keycentrix-the-key-to-innovative-specialty-pharmacy-technology)).

The company's NASP role is that of a sponsor, exhibitor, and founding corporate member. No evidence was found that Keycentrix staff served as NASP 2025 annual-meeting volunteers; the volunteers page does not mention Keycentrix or its staff ([NASP 2025 volunteers page](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)). Activities at the booth are commercial (product demonstrations of Newleaf, giveaways) rather than scientific ([Keycentrix NASP 2025 page](https://keycentrix.com/nasp-2025/)).

Summary: confirmed-strong commercial and membership presence, confirmed-absent scientific or presenter presence.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

Keycentrix has no identifiable footprint as a poster or abstract author at NASP. This is the desired condition (whitespace) for a poster-development engagement.

- The local parsed archive of NASP posters and abstracts spanning 2019-2025 (489 markdown files) returns zero matches for "Keycentrix," "Newleaf," or "BestRx."
- The NASP 2022 abstracts and poster list contains no mention of Keycentrix, Newleaf, BestRx, or Sendkey as authors, affiliations, or in any title ([2022 abstracts and poster presentations](https://naspnet.org/2022-abstracts-and-poster-presentations)).
- The NASP 2024 abstracts and poster list (104 presentations) contains no mention of Keycentrix, Newleaf, BestRx, or Sendkey ([2024 abstracts and poster presentations](https://naspnet.org/2024-abstracts-and-poster-presentations)).
- The NASP 2025 abstracts and poster list contains no mention of Keycentrix, Newleaf, BestRx, or Sendkey ([2025 abstracts and poster presentations](https://naspnet.org/2025-abstracts-and-poster-presentations)).
- The NASP 2023 research-abstract compilation published in Current Medical Research and Opinion (Taylor & Francis) could not be retrieved (HTTP 403, paywalled). The 2023 abstract year is therefore [unverified] by direct page inspection; however, the 2019-2025 local archive covers this period and shows no footprint, so the residual risk is low. This single gap should be noted.

Characterization: whitespace. Keycentrix appears at NASP exclusively as a commercial exhibitor and sponsor, not as a research author. The metrics the company publishes (see Section 3) are marketing figures on its own website, not peer-reviewed or conference-presented evidence. There is no "infrastructure-only" contamination either: the local archive search found no instances of Keycentrix software being named as a data tool inside another author's poster. The whitespace is therefore total and clean.

## 3. Business model and data assets

Keycentrix is a pharmacy management software vendor headquartered in Wichita, Kansas, founded in 1970 according to its own site and described elsewhere as founded in 1973 under the parent entity Computer, Inc. ([Keycentrix company page](https://keycentrix.com/company/); [Specialty Pharmacy Continuum corporate profile](https://www.specialtypharmacycontinuum.com/spccp/1215/content/CProfiles_022a.html)). The exact founding year is therefore inconsistent across sources and is [unverified]; both sources agree the company has operated for roughly 50 years.

Products:

- Newleaf, a pharmacy management platform for single and multi-unit specialty, retail, mail-order, and direct-to-consumer pharmacies, managing workflow, communications, integrations, and patient care ([Newleaf product page](https://keycentrix.com/newleaf/)).
- Sendkey, a patient and prescriber engagement platform (SMS, email, fax, voice) ([Keycentrix homepage](https://keycentrix.com/)).
- Flextrax, a point-of-sale system with PCI and DEA compliance ([Keycentrix homepage](https://keycentrix.com/)).

The premise lists BestRx among Keycentrix products. BestRx was not confirmed on the Keycentrix website or in the corporate profiles reviewed; the named product family is Newleaf, Sendkey, and Flextrax (historically RxKey). The BestRx association is therefore [unverified] from the sources inspected, and the relationship between Keycentrix and BestRx, if any, could not be confirmed.

Customers: specialty, retail, mail-order, infusion, and direct-to-consumer pharmacies, plus some health-system pharmacies. Named or cited customers include Carle Specialty Pharmacy, CHOC Pharmacy, TwelveStone, SMP Pharmacy Solutions, HealthWarehouse.com, RxOutreach, Schraft's 2.0, and Sterling Specialty ([Keycentrix homepage](https://keycentrix.com/); [customer reviews page](https://keycentrix.com/customer-reviews/)).

Company scale: a third-party aggregator reports approximately 58 employees, the 51-100 employee band, and roughly $6 million in annual revenue ([RocketReach profile](https://rocketreach.co/keycentrixtm-profile_b5d012ccf42e478e)). These third-party figures are approximate and not company-confirmed, but they are directionally consistent with a small, privately held vendor. Keycentrix announced a Wichita headquarters expansion in 2020 ([KWCH coverage](https://www.kwch.com/2020/07/30/software-solutions-company-expanding-headquarters-in-wichita/)).

Aggregate data assets: Keycentrix states that its customers have processed 84.5 million scripts on its platform ([Keycentrix homepage](https://keycentrix.com/)). As the pharmacy management system of record for its customer pharmacies, Keycentrix's software touches dispensing workflow, prior-authorization handling, patient and prescriber communications (via Sendkey), point-of-sale transactions, and fill/refill timing. This positions the platform to capture operational process metrics (intake-to-fill timing, prior-authorization turnaround, exception handling, refill adherence proxies) across a multi-pharmacy customer base. The data are transactional and operational rather than clinical outcomes data; the platform is not a claims warehouse or an EHR.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

Research fit is moderate, with a clear and credible product-outcomes angle constrained chiefly by data ownership and company size.

The product-outcomes thesis is strong on its face. Keycentrix already advertises quantitative impact claims that map directly onto NASP-relevant operational endpoints: an 80 percent reduction in prescription errors, a 30 percent reduction in prior-authorization wait times, and an 11 percent improvement in medication adherence in fertility ([Keycentrix homepage](https://keycentrix.com/)). These figures are currently presented as unsourced marketing statistics. Converting even one of them into a methodologically defensible, conference-presented analysis would materially strengthen Keycentrix's commercial position with specialty-pharmacy buyers, who increasingly expect operational metrics (time-to-therapy, prior-authorization turnaround, abandonment, adherence) to be demonstrated rather than asserted. The gap between what Keycentrix claims and what it has published is precisely the whitespace an HEOR engagement fills.

Feasibility considerations:

- Data type fit: high. Prior-authorization turnaround, intake-to-dispense (time-to-therapy), prescription-error or exception rates, and refill-based adherence proxies are all derivable from a pharmacy management system and are standard, well-received NASP poster topics.
- Data ownership and consent: this is the central constraint. The transactional data are generated by customer pharmacies, not owned outright by Keycentrix. Any aggregate or multi-site analysis requires customer participation and data-use agreements, and patient-level analyses require de-identification and appropriate consent or HIPAA-compliant handling. A pragmatic path is a single-site or small-multi-site study co-authored with one or more cooperative customer pharmacies (for example, a named specialty-pharmacy customer), with Keycentrix providing the platform-derived metrics and analytic framing. This sidesteps the need for firm-wide data rights.
- Company size: small (approximately 58 employees, roughly $6 million revenue per third-party estimate). This is large enough to fund a focused poster or single study and clearly motivated to differentiate in a competitive software market, but likely too small to sustain a large, multi-study research program. Scope should be calibrated to one or two high-impact posters rather than a sustained pipeline.
- Defensibility: the existing marketing numbers (80 percent, 30 percent, 11 percent) must be treated as hypotheses to be re-derived under transparent methods, not as findings to be retrofitted. Independence of analysis is a selling point to the buyer audience.

Net: the operational data and the unmet evidence need are real and well-matched to NASP, the membership relationship lowers access friction, and the company is motivated. The constraints (customer-owned data, small scale) shape scope toward a focused, customer-partnered product-outcomes study rather than an enterprise-wide research engagement.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

Verdict: strong.

Rationale: Keycentrix combines (a) a confirmed, deep, multi-year NASP relationship as a founding corporate member, repeat exhibitor, and 2025 Ruby Sponsor; (b) complete and clean poster whitespace across 2019-2025 (zero authored abstracts, no infrastructure-only mentions); (c) a clear commercial motive to convert unsourced marketing metrics into credible, conference-presented evidence; and (d) operational data assets that map directly onto high-value NASP endpoints (prior-authorization turnaround, time-to-therapy, adherence, error reduction). The principal caveats are data ownership (customer-generated data requiring partner pharmacies and data-use agreements) and small company size (scope toward one or two focused posters, not a standing program).

Recommended outreach angle: lead with the credibility gap. Keycentrix already markets specific impact numbers (30 percent reduction in prior-authorization wait times; 80 percent reduction in prescription errors; 11 percent adherence improvement in fertility) but has never presented a NASP poster substantiating them. Offer to convert one of these claims into a methodologically rigorous, independently analyzed poster, co-authored with a willing customer pharmacy, for the next NASP Annual Meeting, turning a marketing assertion into defensible evidence the sales team can put in front of prospects.

Candidate study ideas:

1. Prior-authorization turnaround before and after Newleaf adoption at a partner specialty pharmacy. Audience: specialty-pharmacy operations leaders and the payers and manufacturers who scrutinize time-to-therapy; substantiates the existing 30 percent claim.
2. Time-to-therapy (referral-intake to first-dispense) across Newleaf workflow stages, with exception-handling drivers. Audience: prospective specialty-pharmacy buyers evaluating workflow software; differentiates Keycentrix on speed-to-fill.
3. Prescription-error and exception-rate analysis under order-based, exception-driven processing. Audience: pharmacy quality and compliance leaders; substantiates the existing 80 percent error-reduction claim under transparent methods.
4. Refill-based medication-adherence proxy in a chronic or fertility cohort managed through Newleaf and Sendkey patient engagement. Audience: specialty-pharmacy clinical leaders and manufacturer partners interested in adherence; substantiates and extends the existing 11 percent fertility-adherence claim.
