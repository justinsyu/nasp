# Liberty Software: NASP Outreach Prospect Analysis

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR consulting firm screening NASP (National Association of Specialty Pharmacy) outreach targets. The customer of record for any proposed work would be Liberty Software, and the specialty-pharmacy buyers it must convince to adopt or expand use of its software.

## 1. NASP presence

Liberty Software is listed by name among the exhibitors on the NASP "Sponsoring & Exhibiting" page, which corresponds to the 2022 Annual Meeting & Expo ([naspnet.org exhibitor/sponsor list](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors)). This confirms the premise that Liberty Software was a NASP 2022 exhibitor. The company appears in a 72-name exhibitor list alongside other technology and specialty-pharmacy vendors (for example Keycentrix, Micro Merchant Systems, Inovalon, WellSky, Clearway Health, and Shields Health Solutions).

Beyond the 2022 exhibitor listing, the following could not be confirmed:

- Corporate membership: Liberty Software, "RXQ," and "RxQ" are not present in the NASP corporate member directory ([naspnet.org corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)). The directory lists technology vendors such as Omnicell, IQVIA, Inovalon, WellSky, D2, Eversana, Mandolin, and Tabz, but not Liberty Software.
- Volunteer roles: Liberty Software and its product brands do not appear among the listed NASP 2025 Annual Meeting volunteers ([naspnet.org 2025 volunteers](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers)).
- Sponsorship tier: The exhibitor/sponsor page references sponsor tiers (Diamond, Ruby, Sapphire, Topaz, Onyx) but does not render the named sponsors in retrievable text, so whether Liberty Software was a paid sponsor (versus exhibit-only) is [unverified].

A general web search initially surfaced a Liberty Software exhibit at PDS Supercon 2022, a separate independent-pharmacy conference, which is distinct from the NASP Annual Meeting and should not be conflated with NASP presence. The NASP exhibitor presence is established only by the NASP page itself, cited above.

Summary: confirmed-weak. Liberty Software has documented NASP presence as a 2022 exhibitor only, with no confirmed sponsorship, corporate membership, volunteer participation, or presenter role.

## 2. Poster and published-evidence footprint

In the local NASP poster archive (2019-2025, 489 parsed files), "Liberty Software" / "RXQ Liberty Software" appears exactly once, and not as an author or sponsor. It is named as a data-collection tool inside another organization's poster: a Clearway Health study at NASP 2025 lists "Data Source: Pharmacy software with integrated tools for data collection, RXQ Liberty Software" (local archive file `1163-OPR27-OR-Poster-AM25_2025.md`). That poster is a single-center retrospective study of specialty medication prescriptions sent to Signature Healthcare pharmacy (June 1, 2024 to December 31, 2024), measuring days between prescribing and pharmacy verification. Liberty Software is the underlying software of record for the data, but is not credited as an author, sponsor, or research collaborator.

Searches for Liberty Software or RXQ as an author of NASP or comparable outcomes research returned no matches; the NASP 2024 abstract compilation in Current Medical Research and Opinion was surfaced but did not attribute any abstract to Liberty Software or RXQ ([NASP 2024 abstracts, Tandfonline](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/03007995.2024.2390294)). No vendor-authored peer-reviewed outcomes study attributed to Liberty Software was located; this is reported as [unverified] rather than as a confirmed absence, because trade-press case studies may exist outside indexed sources.

Characterization: whitespace. Footprint is zero author-credited posters or studies and a single appearance as named infrastructure inside a customer's poster. This is the target profile: a vendor embedded in specialty-pharmacy workflows whose data feeds others' evidence, but who has not yet produced outcomes evidence of its own.

## 3. Business model and data assets

Liberty Software is a pharmacy management software company headquartered in Southlake, Texas, founded in 1987, that develops software "by pharmacists, for pharmacists" ([libertysoftware.com](https://libertysoftware.com/); [ComputerTalk buyers guide](https://www.computertalk.com/buyers-guide/liberty-software/)). Third-party business profiles report a company of roughly 100 employees with annual revenue in the low single-digit millions ([RocketReach profile](https://rocketreach.co/liberty-software-profile_b5e85cc8f42e877c)); these figures are third-party estimates and should be treated as approximate. The ComputerTalk interview describes a family-owned firm with President Jeremy Manchester and "almost 20 pharmacists" on staff across development, implementation, training, and support ([ComputerTalk buyers guide](https://www.computertalk.com/buyers-guide/liberty-software/)).

Product line and brand relationship: "RXQ" and "Liberty Software" are the same vendor, not two different companies. RXQ is the brand name of Liberty Software's pharmacy management system ([RXinsider profile](https://rxinsider.com/booth/liberty-software-3/)). The product family includes the RXQ pharmacy workstation (workflow, refilling, e-prescribing, technician and pharmacist verification, deliveries, customizable tasks), BZQ point-of-sale (front-end inventory, signature capture, card processing, IIAS/PCI compliant), and RXQOne, a cloud-based multistore/chain management platform. The company has also marketed a "PharmacyOne" platform brand on its current website; whether "PharmacyOne" is a rebrand of RXQ or a parallel brand is not explicitly stated by the company and is [unverified], though the same product screenshots are reused across both names, indicating they are the same underlying system or a brand transition rather than distinct products ([libertysoftware.com PharmacyOne](https://libertysoftware.com/pharmacy/)).

Pharmacy settings served: sources are not fully consistent. The NCPA vendor listing states the software is "used by community, specialty, LTC, compounding, 340B, and mail-order pharmacies" ([NCPA listing](https://ncpa.org/ow-liberty-software)). The company's own website and some trade profiles describe the focus as community/independent and small-chain retail pharmacy, without emphasizing specialty ([libertysoftware.com](https://libertysoftware.com/); [RXinsider profile](https://rxinsider.com/booth/liberty-software-3/)). The Clearway Health poster confirms at least one health-system specialty pharmacy (Signature Healthcare) operating on RXQ Liberty Software (local archive `1163-OPR27-OR-Poster-AM25_2025.md`). Net read: Liberty is a community/independent-pharmacy-rooted vendor with a real but secondary presence in specialty and LTC settings.

Data assets: integrations include CoverMyMeds for electronic prior authorization, Facts & Comparisons for clinical content, and Surescripts-class NCPDP SCRIPT e-prescribing ([RXinsider profile](https://rxinsider.com/booth/liberty-software-3/)). The platform captures dispensing records, fill timestamps, prescriber communications, adherence indicators (graphical patient adherence displays, MedSync), prior-authorization submission events, and offers a "Data View" reporting layer with a customizable report designer ([libertysoftware.com PharmacyOne](https://libertysoftware.com/pharmacy/); [RXinsider profile](https://rxinsider.com/booth/liberty-software-3/)). Aggregate data theoretically accessible across the installed base would include fill volumes, time-to-fill and time-to-verification intervals, PA submission-to-approval timing (via the CoverMyMeds integration), adherence/MedSync enrollment and persistence, and abandonment proxies (unclaimed/owe prescriptions). A material constraint: this data is owned by the individual pharmacy customers, not by Liberty, and any aggregate or de-identified use would require contractual permissions, BAAs, and likely an opt-in program. Liberty does not appear to operate a centralized data warehouse or analytics service comparable to larger vendors.

## 4. Research fit: can their data produce evidence that helps them?

The research angle is product-outcomes, not patient-outcomes: evidence that the software itself improves operational and clinical metrics, intended to help Liberty sell into and retain specialty and health-system pharmacy customers.

Feasible and well-matched study endpoints, given the data the platform plausibly captures:

- Time-to-therapy and time-to-verification: the Clearway Health/Signature Healthcare poster already used RXQ data to measure days from prescribing to pharmacy verification, demonstrating that the fields exist and are extractable for this exact endpoint (local archive `1163-OPR27-OR-Poster-AM25_2025.md`).
- Prior-authorization turnaround: submission-to-decision intervals via the CoverMyMeds integration ([RXinsider profile](https://rxinsider.com/booth/liberty-software-3/)).
- Adherence and persistence: MedSync enrollment effects and graphical adherence tracking ([libertysoftware.com PharmacyOne](https://libertysoftware.com/pharmacy/)).
- Prescription abandonment: unclaimed/owe-prescription rates as an abandonment proxy.
- Data-collection quality: completeness and timeliness of structured data capture, relevant to specialty-pharmacy accreditation (URAC, ACHC) reporting burdens.

Honest constraints and risks:

- Data ownership: the customer pharmacies own the operational data; Liberty cannot unilaterally pool it. Any multi-site study requires recruited, consenting pharmacy partners and data-use agreements. This raises study cost and timeline.
- Specialty depth: Liberty's installed base is predominantly community/independent; the number of health-system or specialty pharmacies on the platform that would make a credible specialty-focused dataset is uncertain and possibly small ([RXinsider profile](https://rxinsider.com/booth/liberty-software-3/); [NCPA listing](https://ncpa.org/ow-liberty-software)). A single-site or few-site study (the Signature Healthcare model) is the realistic near-term design.
- Company size and budget: a roughly 100-person, low-single-digit-million-revenue firm has limited discretionary research budget ([RocketReach profile](https://rocketreach.co/liberty-software-profile_b5e85cc8f42e877c)). Engagements would need to be small, fixed-scope, and tied to a clear sales or retention payoff.
- Confounding: product-outcomes claims (for example, "RXQ reduces time-to-therapy") are vulnerable to selection bias and site heterogeneity; defensible designs require pre/post or matched-comparator framing, which the firm can provide methodologically.

Overall, research fit is moderate. The endpoints are real and already demonstrated in one NASP poster, and the product-outcomes narrative is coherent, but data-ownership friction, uncertain specialty-segment depth, and limited budget cap the near-term feasibility to small, partner-dependent studies rather than large multi-site analyses.

## 5. Verdict and recommended outreach

Verdict: low-to-moderate (lean low for near-term paid engagement; moderate as a relationship to seed).

Rationale: Liberty Software has confirmed NASP 2022 exhibitor presence and a genuine, documented footprint inside a specialty-pharmacy workflow (the Clearway Health/Signature Healthcare poster used RXQ as its data source), which makes it a legitimate whitespace target: a vendor whose data already feeds others' evidence but who has authored none of its own. However, its core market is community/independent rather than specialty pharmacy, its specialty installed base is of uncertain size, the operational data is owned by customers rather than by Liberty, and the company is small with limited research budget. These factors reduce both the likely deal size and the feasibility of a large dataset.

Recommended outreach angle: lead with the Clearway Health/Signature Healthcare NASP 2025 poster as proof that RXQ data already produces conference-grade evidence, and propose that Liberty convert that incidental appearance into Liberty-authored or Liberty-co-authored product-outcomes evidence it can use as a sales and retention asset with specialty and health-system pharmacy buyers. Frame the first engagement as a small, single- or few-site study using one willing customer pharmacy, with the firm supplying the methodological rigor (study design, statistics, accreditation-aligned framing) that Liberty lacks in-house.

Candidate study ideas:

1. Time-to-verification / time-to-therapy benchmark at a single RXQ specialty site. Replicate and extend the Signature Healthcare design with a pre/post or matched comparison around an RXQ workflow feature. Audience: health-system and specialty pharmacy operations leaders evaluating dispensing software.
2. Prior-authorization turnaround analysis using the CoverMyMeds integration data. Quantify submission-to-decision time and its effect on time-to-first-fill. Audience: specialty pharmacy and access/hub teams; payers as a secondary audience.
3. Adherence and persistence with MedSync on RXQ for specialty or chronic high-cost therapies. Audience: manufacturers' patient-services teams and accreditation reviewers who weigh adherence reporting.
4. Data-collection completeness and accreditation-readiness study. Measure structured-data capture quality in RXQ against URAC/ACHC reporting requirements. Audience: pharmacies pursuing or maintaining specialty accreditation, for whom reporting burden is a purchase driver.
