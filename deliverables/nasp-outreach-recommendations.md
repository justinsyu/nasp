# NASP Outreach Targets: Screening Summary and Recommendations

Prepared for internal use by an HEOR (health economics and outcomes research) consulting firm. The firm serves health-system specialty pharmacy (HSSP) organizations and adjacent specialty-pharmacy companies, converting their operational, clinical, and claims data into methodologically rigorous, conference-ready research (posters and studies) those companies can use to demonstrate outcomes and capability to prospective customers. This document summarizes a screen of ten candidate organizations identified as "Omnicell-like" prospects and gives a tiered outreach recommendation. The customer of record for any proposed study is the prospect organization itself (and, where applicable, its health-system clients), not payers, PBMs, or manufacturers.

## 1. Scope and method

Each organization was investigated by a separate subagent against three screening questions:

1. **NASP presence.** Is the organization a confirmed NASP attendee, exhibitor, sponsor, corporate member, volunteer, or committee member? Confirmed against the [NASP corporate member directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory), the [2022 exhibitor and sponsor list](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors), the [2025 sponsors and exhibitors list](https://naspnet.org/2025-annual-meeting-expo-sponsors-exhibitors), the [2025 annual meeting volunteers list](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers), and organization-specific pages.
2. **Poster and published-evidence footprint.** How many research posters or published outcomes studies has the organization authored at NASP or comparable venues (AMCP, ASHP)? The objective is to find organizations with little or no poster footprint (whitespace), or whose existing evidence is methodologically thin. Footprint was checked against a parsed local archive of NASP posters 2019 to 2025 (489 markdown files in `nasp_abstracts_posters_pdfs_2019_2025`), treated as a proxy for NASP poster authorship, plus targeted web searches.
3. **Research fit.** Does the organization's business model generate data, and does it have a sales reason, such that conducting outcomes research would help it (demonstrate outcomes to prospective customers, prove return on investment, support sales)?

Nine candidates were newly investigated in this round. Four organizations from the original candidate list (Shields Health Solutions, CPS Solutions/Trellis Rx, Clearway Health, and VytlOne/Maxor) already have detailed opportunity briefs in this folder and are addressed separately in Section 6, because they are confirmed poster authors rather than whitespace targets. Each newly investigated organization has a full analysis file linked in the table below.

## 3. Tier 1 recommendations: highest priority

These three combine confirmed strong NASP standing, complete poster whitespace, and the strongest data-ownership position among the new candidates. They control data directly and have a clear commercial reason to publish.

**CarepathRx.** CarepathRx Technology Solutions is confirmed on the NASP [2022](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting/exhibitors-sponsors) and [2025](https://naspnet.org/2025-annual-meeting-expo-sponsors-exhibitors) exhibitor lists, yet has no NASP poster footprint and only a single thin published study identified. As an operator of specialty pharmacy, home infusion, and 340B services across more than 20 health systems, it owns dispensing and longitudinal adherence data at scale (its DromosPTM patient-therapy-management platform), which is the most complete native data position of the new set. The asymmetry between strong capability claims and absent denominator-anchored evidence is the lead hook. See the [CarepathRx analysis](carepathrx-prospect-analysis.md).

**WellSky.** WellSky is confirmed as a NASP 2022 exhibitor and a current Nickel-tier corporate member ([directory](https://naspnet.org/about/corporate-member-directory)), with zero poster footprint as either author or named data tool. Its CareTend platform captures intake-to-dispense and delivery timestamps, refill/adherence, and order-completion data that map directly to time-to-therapy, abandonment, and adherence endpoints. This is a product-outcomes engagement: studies that show the software improves measurable specialty-pharmacy outcomes. Data-rights and vendor conflict-of-interest framing should be addressed at the outset. See the [WellSky analysis](wellsky-prospect-analysis.md).

**D2 Solutions.** D2 holds the deepest NASP governance footprint of any candidate: Topaz sponsor, Nickel corporate member, exclusive sponsor of the NASP Accreditation Workshop, and Founder/CEO Dean Erhardt is Co-Chair of the [NASP Technology Committee](https://naspnet.org/about/committees/technology) and a [2025 volunteer](https://naspnet.org/annual-meeting-expo-volunteers). Despite this, it has no research poster or published study. It can support process-outcome studies from its own platform data (prior-authorization performance, onboarding) immediately, and client-partnered ROI studies thereafter. The committee-leadership relationship is a warm, high-credibility entry point. See the [D2 Solutions analysis](d2-solutions-prospect-analysis.md).

## 4. Tier 2 recommendations: strong, with a structural caveat

Strong NASP presence and clean whitespace, but the engagement structure differs because the organization either does not own patient-level data or is small.

**Visante.** A Platinum corporate member that paid for 2025 Topaz sponsorship and exhibition and supplied a volunteer ([2025 sponsors and exhibitors](https://naspnet.org/2025-annual-meeting-expo-sponsors-exhibitors)), Visante has no peer-reviewed or conference poster footprint; its published content is consulting white papers and case studies. As a consultancy it does not own longitudinal patient data, so credible studies route through client-owned operational data with co-authorship and data-use agreements. The hook is direct: it invests in being present at NASP but is absent from the research channel where its health-system buyers evaluate evidence. See the [Visante analysis](visante-prospect-analysis.md).

**Keycentrix.** A NASP founding corporate member, 2022 exhibitor, and 2025 Ruby sponsor with no poster footprint. It markets specific unsourced impact figures (for example, a 30% reduction in prior-authorization wait time) that have never been substantiated at NASP. The natural engagement is to convert one such claim into a rigorous, independently analyzed, customer-co-authored poster. Constraints are company scale (scope to one or two posters rather than a standing research program) and customer-owned data. See the [Keycentrix analysis](keycentrix-prospect-analysis.md).

## 5. Tier 3 recommendations: qualified, opportunistic

Engaged or present at NASP and clean whitespace, but with a material caveat on data, venue fit, or commitment depth. Pursue opportunistically or after Tiers 1 and 2.

**OutcomeRx.** A Copper-tier corporate member with two executives on the NASP volunteer list, and ideal whitespace. Its product (outcomes-based warranties for cell and gene therapies) maps cleanly to an evidence need, but cell and gene therapy cohorts are small and the products recent, so data maturity is uncertain. Approach with a data-readiness-first conversation. The specific 2026 inSPire sponsor role is unverified. See the [OutcomeRx analysis](outcomerx-prospect-analysis.md).

**Surescripts.** Confirmed on the 2022 exhibitor list with one volunteer, but not a corporate member, and present in posters only as a cited data source. Its transaction data can sustain rigorous designs, but only the specialty slice fits NASP; broader benefit and adherence work points toward AMCP. A useful hook is that the strongest published evidence about a Surescripts tool was authored by a health system and a PBM, not by Surescripts. See the [Surescripts analysis](surescripts-prospect-analysis.md).

**Cervey.** A confirmed 2022 exhibitor (not a member or volunteer), subsidiary of Morris & Dickson, with complete author whitespace and a NASP-aligned buyer base. Data is client-owned and the company is small, so a turnkey, low-burden single-poster engagement through one consenting client site is the realistic entry. See the [Cervey analysis](cervey-prospect-analysis.md).

## Tier 4: low priority

**Liberty Software.** Confirmed only as a 2022 exhibitor, appearing in the archive solely as a named data tool (RXQ) inside a Clearway Health/Signature Healthcare poster. Customer-owned data, an uncertain specialty installed base, and small budget cap near-term feasibility. Best treated as a relationship to seed (using that incidental poster appearance as proof its data already produces conference-grade evidence) rather than a near-term paid engagement. See the [Liberty Software analysis](liberty-software-prospect-analysis.md).

## 6. Already-qualified organizations (existing briefs)

Four organizations from the original candidate list are confirmed NASP poster authors and already have detailed opportunity briefs in this folder. They are not whitespace targets; the opportunity with them is methodological upgrade and re-pointing of existing evidence, not first-time poster creation.

- **Shields Health Solutions** ([brief](shields-opportunity-brief.md)): the most evidence-mature prospect; heavy poster footprint across at least eight disease states. Opportunity is causal-inference upgrades and re-pointing payer-framed analyses to health-system decisions.
- **CPS Solutions / Trellis Rx** ([brief](cps-opportunity-brief.md)): heavy poster footprint; opportunity in methods, governance, and benchmarking.
- **Clearway Health** ([brief](clearway-opportunity-brief.md)): already presents; public evidence is case-study-heavy, so the opportunity is rigor.
- **VytlOne / Maxor** ([brief](vytlone-maxor-opportunity-brief.md)): some footprint exists but methods are thin; an upgrade target.

A [cross-organization synthesis](cross-org-synthesis.md) covering these four is also on file.

## 7. Cross-cutting finding

Eight of the nine newly screened organizations are confirmed to invest in NASP commercial presence (exhibition, sponsorship, corporate membership, and in D2's case committee leadership) while having zero research-poster output. This recurring asymmetry, commercial presence without scientific evidence, is the common sales hook across the prospect set: these organizations are already paying to be visible at the venue where their buyers evaluate evidence, but are absent from the evidence channel itself. The differentiator our firm offers is the methodological rigor (denominators, comparison groups, defensible designs) that converts their operational data into credible, citable outcomes.

## 8. Recommended sequencing

1. **Open with Tier 1** (CarepathRx, WellSky, D2 Solutions): strongest combination of NASP credibility, whitespace, and owned data; lowest-friction first studies use data the organization already controls.
2. **Pursue Tier 2 in parallel** (Visante, Keycentrix), scoping engagements around client co-authorship (Visante) or single high-value posters that substantiate existing marketing claims (Keycentrix).
3. **Treat Tier 3 opportunistically** (OutcomeRx, Surescripts, Cervey), leading with data-readiness and venue-fit conversations.
4. **Seed Tier 4** (Liberty Software) as a longer-term relationship.
5. **Engage the already-qualified four** (Shields, CPS, Clearway, VytlOne/Maxor) on a separate methodological-upgrade track per their existing briefs.

## 9. Verification gaps to note

Across the screen, several publisher abstract pages (Taylor & Francis / Current Medical Research and Opinion 2025 NASP abstracts, and some organization sites) returned HTTP 403 on automated retrieval, so the absence of a poster for a given organization in those single sources is flagged as unverified in the per-organization files. In every case the zero-footprint finding is corroborated by the local 2019-2025 archive and by targeted web searches, but a confirmatory manual check of the 2024 and 2025 NASP abstract supplements is advisable before any outreach that asserts an organization has never presented. Specific unverified items (for example, OutcomeRx's 2026 inSPire sponsor role, and the RXQ/PharmacyOne branding relationship for Liberty Software) are documented in the relevant files.
