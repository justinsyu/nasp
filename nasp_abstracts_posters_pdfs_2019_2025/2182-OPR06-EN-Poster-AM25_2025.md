# Once-Daily Valbenazine for Tardive Dyskinesia or Huntington’s Disease Chorea in Patients With Dysphagia or Pill Aversion

Mello Hebert, Roland Jimenez, Linda Rees, Alexander Mar, Ali Bristow, Kira Aldrich, Jamie Hamill, Richard Moore, Khodayar Farahmand

Neurocrine Biosciences, Inc., San Diego, CA

## BACKGROUND

■ Valbenazine is a uniquely selective, once-daily medication approved for tardive dyskinesia (TD) and chorea associated with Huntington’s disease (HD)<sup>1</sup>

■ For patients with TD and HD, swallowing whole medications may be difficult due to their motor symptoms and primary condition<sup>2,3</sup>

■ Pill aversion (or non-physiologic dysphagia) can arise from psychological or emotional factors, such as fear of choking in patients with HD chorea<sup>2</sup>

■ Dysphagia may be particularly problematic in elderly patients, due to anatomical and physiologic changes associated with aging<sup>4</sup>

* The presence of comorbid conditions (e.g., gastrointestinal reflux, Alzheimer’s disease) and side effects (e.g., dry mouth) can further exacerbate problems with swallowing medication

■ Alternative modes for medication administration are needed for patients with dysphagia or pill aversion

■ Three in vitro studies assessed the acceptability of crushing the contents of valbenazine capsules<sup>5</sup>

* Study 1: established the dissolution profile of crushed capsule contents

* Study 2: tested compatibility of crushed capsule contents with soft foods and liquids

* Study 3: evaluated the suitability of delivering crushed capsule contents via gastrostomy tube (G-tube)

■ In addition, a new and recently approved valbenazine granules formulation was evaluated in 2 studies:

* Study 4 (in vitro): tested compatibility of valbenazine granules with soft foods

* Study 5 (phase 1): established bioequivalence between valbenazine granules and capsules containing compacted powder plug and between fed and fasted states for granules

## OBJECTIVE

■ To evaluate the acceptability of various modes of administration for the 2 approved valbenazine formulations: capsule with compacted powder-plug content and sprinkle capsule with granules content

## METHODS

■ Methods for the 4 in vitro studies are summarized in **Table 1**

■ The phase 1 trial was an open-label, 2-cohort study with healthy adults (**Table 2**)

* Cohort 1 (bioequivalence cohort): 3-sequence, 3-period crossover design with 24 participants

* Cohort 2 (food effect cohort): 2-sequence, 2-period crossover design with 14 participants

* Pharmacokinetic (PK) parameters for valbenazine and its primary metabolite, [+]-α-dihydrotetrabenazine ([+]-α-HTBZ), were used to determine bioequivalence: maximum plasma concentration (C<sub>max</sub>) and area under the curve from 0 hours to the last measurable concentration (AUC<sub>0-tlast</sub>) and to infinity (AUC<sub>0-∞</sub>)

* Bioequivalence between valbenazine granules and compacted powder capsule (Cohort 1) and between fed and fasted states for granules sprinkled on applesauce (Cohort 2) was defined as the 90% CI for the geometric mean ratios of PK parameters falling between 80% to 125%

## RESULTS

■ Studies 1-3 with the crushed contents of valbenazine capsules demonstrated the following<sup>2</sup>:

* Study 1: dissolution profile of crushed capsule contents was similar to whole intact capsules; rapid and complete valbenazine release was observed regardless of preparation (crushed contents or whole capsule) and dose (40 mg or 80 mg)

* Study 2: valbenazine recovery was acceptable within 2 hours after capsule contents were added to soft foods, buffer solutions, and fed-state simulated gastric fluid (FeSSGF); on the basis of pH and use, other acceptable foods and liquids include: cottage cheese, hummus, jelly/jam, mashed fruits, mashed potatoes, and orange and cranberry juice

* Study 3: G-tube administration was suitable when crushed valbenazine contents were added to room-temperature water and stored in water for up to 2.5 hours

■ Study 4 showed valbenazine granules to be compatible with applesauce, yogurt, and pudding (**Figure 1**)

* Valbenazine recovery was also acceptable for buffer solutions (pH 1.2 and pH 4.5) and FeSSGF, indicating compatibility with a wide range of acidic to slightly acidic soft foods (e.g., cottage cheese, hummus, jelly/jam, mashed fruits, and mashed potatoes)

## CONCLUSIONS

■ Results from 3 in vitro studies indicate the acceptability of crushing the compacted powder contents of valbenazine capsules for mixing with soft foods/liquids or dispersing in water for delivery via G-tube

■ Results from an in vitro study of the new valbenazine granules formulation indicate compatibility with a wide range of acidic to slightly acidic soft foods (below pH 6.8)

■ A phase 1 study demonstrated the bioequivalence between valbenazine formulations (granules vs compacted powder), with no food effects for valbenazine granules (fed vs fasted state)

■ To date, valbenazine is the only VMAT2 inhibitor approved for TD and HD that offers a once-daily formulation with multiple routes of administration

■ With 2 approved and available formulations, valbenazine offers alternative modes of administration that are suitable for patients with swallowing difficulties, including dysphagia and pill aversion
