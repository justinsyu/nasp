# Impact of a neurology specialty clinical pharmacist on migraine management outcomes: A prospective observational cohort study

Bethany Franz, PharmD, BCACP; Heather Dalton, PharmD, MS; Megan E. Fleischman, PharmD, BCACP; Kama Thomas, PharmD, BCACP

FROEDTERT & THE MEDICAL COLLEGE OF WISCONSIN | FROEDTERT HOSPITAL | MILWAUKEE, WI

Froedtert & MEDICAL COLLEGE of WISCONSIN logo

## BACKGROUND

❖ Chronic migraine affects approximately 2% of the world population<sup>1</sup>

❖ Migraines present with a significant burden of illness as they impair a patient’s functional ability at school, work and usual daily activities<sup>2</sup>

❖ Calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) antagonists (erenumab, fremanezumab, and galcanezumab) are monoclonal antibodies targeting the calcitonin gene-related peptide ligand or its receptor used in the treatment of chronic migraine prevention<sup>3</sup>

❖ CGRP antagonists are dispensed at Froedtert Specialty Pharmacy due to the high cost, initial monitoring, handling and complex care coordination

❖ Neurology specialty pharmacists at Froedtert Health provide comprehensive migraine medication management, handling everything from prior authorizations and financial assistance to drug interaction reviews, administration counseling, coordinated refills and personalized support as part of an integrated care team

>925 Neurology patients

>650 Migraine patients

## PURPOSE

Evaluate the clinical impact of enhanced pharmacist-patient engagement in a neurology specialty pharmacy program via pharmacist interventions on migraine-related outcomes over six months following CGRP monoclonal antibody initiation

## OBJECTIVES

**Primary objective:** Analyze the impact of increased pharmacist–patient contact within a neurology specialty pharmacy program by measuring interventions made in the first 6 months of CGRP monoclonal antibody therapy.

**Secondary objective:** Evaluate the impact on migraine frequency and usual daily activities from baseline to six months after CGRP monoclonal antibody initiation.

## METHODS

**Study Design**
Prospective, single center, observational cohort study

**Inclusion Criteria:**
* Patient new to CGRP monoclonal antibodies
* Prescription for one of the following subcutaneous migraine medications: erenumab, fremanezumab and galcanezumab
* Prescribed by a Froedtert Hospital Neurology Clinic Provider
* Migraine ICD-10 code G43.XXX in electronic medical record
* Patient age of 18 years or older

## OUTCOMES/ENDPOINTS

**Primary outcome:**
* Number of interventions made from the initial evaluation through the six-month follow-up call

**Secondary outcomes:**
* Number of migraine days compared at time of Specialty pharmacy enrollment (Initial assessment), month 3 and month 6
* Number of missed days of school or work per month at initial assessment, month 3 and month 6
* Average adherence as measured by proportion of days covered (PDC)
* Amount of cost assistance provided by a pharmacist
* Number of patient-outreaches completed by pharmacist
* Types of pharmacist interventions based on available interventions in the medication therapy problem (MTP) report
* Number of acute migraine-related visits needed (clinic visit, Emergency Department visit)
