# Patient Reported Outcomes, and Adherence among Patients with Multiple Sclerosis Treated with Disease Modifying Therapies at an Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy

Bisni Narayanan, PharmD, MBA, MS; Michele Riccardi, PharmD, BCPS; Terri Sue Rubino, PharmD, CSP; Tina Do, PharmD, MS, BCPS
Department of Pharmacy, Yale New Haven Health

NASP Annual Meeting & Expo 2025 logo

## Background

* Disease modifying therapies (DMTs) reduce relapse and disability progression rates among patients with Multiple Sclerosis (pwMS).

* Patient reported outcomes (PRO) can measure health-related quality of life, disease symptoms and burden, and health behaviors.

* Most DMTs are specialty medications requiring an extensive patient management program with close and consistent follow up.

* Adherence can be challenging in this patient population due to the high economic and social burden of MS.

* In May 2023, the integrated HSSP migrated to a new disease specific patient management platform.

* PwMS were enrolled in the MS disease program, with specific activities and disease management support tools.

## Objective

* To evaluate the patient reported outcomes, and adherence among pwMS on self-administered DMTs.

## Methods

Chart review icon This is a retrospective chart review of completed activities by pharmacy staff between October 1, 2023, to September 30, 2024, for pwMS being treated with DMTs

Patient inclusion icon PwMS who filled their self-administered DMT (oral or injectable) at the HSSP and had at least one clinical assessment were included in the analysis

Primary outcome icon The primary outcome included evaluating adherence of DMTs

The total number of clinical assessments and PROs were evaluated as secondary outcomes

## Discussion

* Literature reviews have cited varying MPR and PDC for DMTs. A systematic review reported MPR/PDC of 83.3%/ 76.5% for oral DMTs. Other studies have reported MPR of 69% for self injectable DMTs and 68% for oral DMTs.

* The MPR/PDC remains high at the integrated HSSP exceeding the benchmark goal for both measures.

* Non-adherent patients are more likely to discontinue treatment and report missed doses. Patients at the HSSP reporting zero missed doses varied from 88% to 92% during reassessments and monthly refill outreach.

* The close monitoring and follow up by the clinical team as evidenced by clinical services resulted in favorable patient reported outcomes.

* Studies have reported work difficulties, stigma, and poor quality of life even in a population with low physical disability among pwMS.

* The integrated specialty pharmacy model enables pharmacists to provide targeted interventions, enhances medication access, and improves coordination of care all of which contribute to better patient outcomes.

## Limitations

* This review includes pwMS of all types, patients may experience higher levels of impairment and disability based on age of diagnosis, number of relapses etc.

* Varying MPR/PDC reported in literature can be due to different methods of calculation.

* PROs are based on just patients reached for reassessments.

## Conclusion

Specialty clinical pharmacists are uniquely positioned to improve patient outcomes and adherence among pwMS.
