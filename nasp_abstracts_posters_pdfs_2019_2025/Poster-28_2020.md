# University Hospitals Specialty Pharmacy (UHSP)

* Part of an integrated healthcare delivery network model within University Hospitals Health System (UHHS)

* HCV Patient Management Program

    * Procurement of specialty medications, prior authorizations, refills, foundation and grant support, appeals, patient adherence and monitoring

    * Onsite: pharmacists; patient support advocates; pharmacy technicians

    * Clinic-based ambulatory pharmacists

# OBJECTIVES

* The objective of this study is to describe the impact and success of UHSP in managing patients with hepatitis C and to compare outcomes of patients who have filled hepatitis C medications at external specialty pharmacies or were not dispensed through UHSP.

### PRIMARY OUTCOMES | SECONDARY OUTCOMES

* Documented sustained virological response (SVR) | * Prescription turnaround time

* Documented monitoring and adherence throughout therapy | * Financial assistance implementation

* Total out-of-pocket treatment cost

# METHODS

### Study Design

* Retrospective chart analysis of University Hospitals Health Systems HCV patients

* Patient captured using the UHHS Ambulatory Electronic Medical Record (AEMR)

* Timeframe: January 1<sup>st</sup> 2018 – June 30<sup>th</sup> 2018

### INCLUSION CRITERIA | EXCLUSION CRITERIA

* Adult patients (≥18 years old) seen in the UH Hepatology Clinic/Gastroenterology Clinics | * Patients who never started therapy for HCV at UH

* Documented chronic HCV infection present | * Patients unwilling to comply with insurance pre-HCV treatment requirements

* Prescription written for HCV treatment with direct-acting antivirals (DAA) and sent to UHSP | * Patients who discontinued therapy early due to non-compliance or left UHHS

* Prescription written between January 1, 2018 and June 30, 2018 | * Pregnant patients

* Patients <18 years old

# DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSIONS

### Success of UHSP HCV Patient Management Program

* Documented UHSP SVR12 🡪 100%

* Documented adherence 🡪 Proportion of days covered (PDC) 🡪 91.6%

### Common Barriers to HCV Treatment

* External 🡪 social issues; high cost; drug procurement; insurance approval

* Internal 🡪 treatment adherence; drug-drug interactions; follow-up appointments and labs; co-morbid conditions; HCV progression

| Metric                        | WITH Clinic-based pharmacist | WITHOUT clinic-based pharmacist |
| ----------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Documented patient monitoring | 92%                          | 58%                             |
| Patients lost in follow-up    | 5.9%                         | 25%                             |
| Average Rx turnaround time    | *18* days                    | *24* days                       |


### STRENGTHS | LIMITATIONS

* Access to medical record, insurance information, copay information | * Small sample size

* Specialty pharmacy 🡪 integrated in the health system | * Single-centered study

* UHHS patient characteristics reflect general HCV population | * Short duration (6 months)

* Clinic-based pharmacist managed patients vs. no clinic-based pharmacist | * Retrospective

* PDC measures possession versus administration (global limitation)
