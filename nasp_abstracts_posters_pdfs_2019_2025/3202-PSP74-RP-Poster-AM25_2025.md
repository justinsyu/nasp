## Background

▪ Delivery of temperature-sensitive medications is a high-stakes, high-cost, and high-volume endeavor for:

* patients

* pharmacies

* environment

▪ Conventional packaging relies heavily on:

* expanded polystyrene (EPS)

* plastic

▪ Purpose of packaging:

* protect medications in range of climates and conditions

## Methodology

▪ Design: prospective, multi-center, controlled trial; patient survey

▪ Setting: Ambulatory Care

▪ Population:

* inclusion criteria:

- medications delivered in cold chain packaging (January 2024 – present)

* exclusion criteria:

- medications delivered in ambient temperature packaging

▪ Primary outcome:

* performance: temperature excursion (proportion of units, time to event)

▪ Secondary outcomes:

* non-biodegradable waste volume

* total consumer waste volume

* recyclable waste volume

* costs (e.g., acquisition, medication waste)

* patient satisfaction, terminal destination for cold packaging (landfill), recycling (rate)

▪ Statistical Analysis Plan:

* Fisher’s exact test (categorical data)

* Student’s t-test (normally distributed data)

* Mann-Whitney U (non-normally distributed data)

* Multivariate linear and logistic regression

## Discussion

▪ Value Proposition

* sustainability initiatives align with operational and financial mission

* perceived value with patients and stakeholders (e.g., trust and purchase intention)

* requires transparency and accountability

▪ Environmental Impact:

* *** # of cold-chain units and # of patients served 🡪 units per patient (for scale)

* ***difference in CO2 emissions

▪ Future directions

* improve delivery service logistics (e.g., lockers)

* explore options to re-use packaging (e.g., exchange program)

* evaluate multi-capacity coolers to optimize payload and reduce waste

* utilize smaller coolers for small-volume shipments
