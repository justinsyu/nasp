# Impact Of Race On Baseline Asthma Severity and Response To Biologic Therapy In The First 12 Months Post Biologic Initiation

QR Code Vanderbilt University Medical Center logo

Monica Littlejohn, PharmD, MHA<sup>1</sup>, Autumn D. Zuckerman, PharmD, BCPS, CSP<sup>1</sup>, Katie Cruchelow, PhD<sup>1</sup>, Nicholas Gargurevich, MS<sup>2</sup>, Leena Choi, PhD<sup>2</sup>
<sup>1</sup>Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy, <sup>2</sup>Department of Biostatistics, Vanderbilt University Medical Center, <sup>3</sup>

### HIGHLIGHTS

* At biologic initiation, **Black or African American/Other** patients had **worse lung function** than White patients.

* However, the difference narrowed between these two groups significantly over the first year of treatment.

* Interventions to reduce treatment gaps and promote pharmacoequity in patients with moderate-to-severe asthma are needed.

### PURPOSE

The purpose of the study was to identify racial and socioeconomic factors that may impact outcomes in patients with moderate-to-severe asthma before and within 12 months of biologic initiation.

### METHODS

**Design**
* Retrospective cohort study
* Enrollment: 1/1/2019-6/30/2022 with a 12-month follow up through 6/30/2023

**Sample**
* Inclusion criteria: Patients prescribed a new biologic (benralizumab, dupilumab, mepolizumab, and omalizumab) between 1/1/2019 and 6/30/2022
* Exclusion criteria: Indication other than moderate to severe asthma; Biologic not prescribed by a Vanderbilt Allergy/ Immunology/ Pulmonary Clinic provider

**Analysis**
* Primary Outcome: Race-neutral ppFEV<sub>1</sub> (forced expiratory volume in one second, percent predicted) calculated using pre-bronchodilator FEV<sub>1</sub> measured approximately within the first year post biologic initiation as a measure of efficacy/response to biologic therapy in the first year of follow-up.
* Secondary Outcomes: Biologic medication adherence and gap days. Adherence was calculated using proportion of days covered (PDC). Gap days were defined as time from medication exhaustion from one fill until the next fill, and cumulative gap days were calculated as the sum of gap days within the 1st year of treatment.
* Regression analysis covariates: smoking status, COVID period in which measurement took place, insurance type, and average distance to care in patients' geographical area

### RESULTS

#### Figure 1. Study Attrition
* 215 patients in cohort at baseline (White n=148, Black or African American/Other n=67)
* 185 patients initiated biologic (White n=126, Black or African American/Other n=59)
* 161 patients with baseline ppFEV<sub>1</sub> (White n=110, Black or African American/Other n=51)
* 43 patients with follow-up ppFEV<sub>1</sub> (White n=26, Black or African American/Other n=17)

#### Figure 2. ppFEV<sub>1</sub> by Smoking Status Before and After Biologic Initiation

| Smoking Status                  | Baseline | Follow-up |
| ------------------------------- | -------- | --------- |
| Never smoked or exposed/unknown | 73       | 84        |
| Exposure only                   | 76       | 80        |
| Previously smoked               | 65       | 64        |
| Currently smoke                 | 44       | 48        |


#### Smoking Status
#### Figure 3. Smoking Status by Social Deprivation Index
#### Figure 4. Smoking Status by Race

| Smoking Status                  | Median Social Deprivation Index |
| ------------------------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Currently smoke                 | 0.52                            |
| Previously smoked               | 0.42                            |
| Exposure only                   | 0.40                            |
| Never smoked or exposed/unknown | 0.41                            |


| Race                              | Never smoked or exposed/unknown | Exposure only | Previously smoked | Currently smoke |
| --------------------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------- | ----------------- | --------------- |
| White                             | 58%                             | 7%            | 24%               | 10%             |
| Black or African American + Other | 64%                             | 15%           | 10%               | 10%             |


* Previous (p=0.02) and current smokers (p=0.01) had significantly lower estimated ppFEV<sub>1</sub> at baseline.
* Current smokers in this study were associated with higher social deprivation index scores, indicating worse socioeconomic status.
* 20% of Black or African American/Other patients and 34% of White patients were previous or current smokers.

#### Figure 5. ppFEV<sub>1</sub> by Race

| Race                              | Baseline Median (IQR) | Follow-up Median (IQR) | Median % Change (IQR) |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------- | ---------------------- | --------------------- |
| White                             | 74 (57, 87)           | 82 (70, 93)            | 3% (-2, 16)           |
| Black or African American + Other | 63 (54, 79)           | 72 (63, 95)            | 6% (-2, 48)           |


Highlight icon
**BASELINE:**
Black or African American/Other: ppFEV<sub>1</sub> 6.87 less than White patients (p=0.06) at baseline

**FOLLOW-UP:**
Race was not significantly associated with ppFEV<sub>1</sub> (p=0.71)

#### Adherence and Gap Days

#### Figure 6. Adherence (PDC)

| Race                              | Median PDC | IQR          |
| --------------------------------- | ---------- | ------------ |
| White                             | 0.97       | (0.91, 1)    |
| Black or African American + Other | 0.95       | (0.74, 0.99) |


Median biologic adherence for all patients was 0.96 (IQR 0.88, 1) within the first year. There were no significant differences in PDC by race.

#### Figure 7. Gap Days

| Race                              | Patients with gap days >16% of the year |
| --------------------------------- | --------------------------------------- |
| White                             | 19%                                     |
| Black or African American + Other | 32%                                     |


Non-White patients were more likely to have greater cumulative gaps in treatment. Black/AA/Other patients had an average of 44 gaps days (SD 56) compared to 26 (SD 41) for White patients. This was not a statistically significant difference.
