# Evaluating Appeal Outcomes for Insurance Non-Preferred Dermatology Specialty Medications

Northwestern Medicine logo

Elna Shibu, PharmD; Kelly Dvorak, PharmD, BCPS

Northwestern Memorial Hospital – Chicago, IL

## Background

* The appeal process for prior authorization (PA) denied on specialty medications is time consuming and can result in significant patient care delays and access to medication

* At Northwestern Medicine Specialty Pharmacy (NMSP), appeal submission is often required due to the medication being excluded on the insurance company's formulary

* A study showed that over half of PA requests for complex dermatology medications are initially denied, with an average approval delays of around two weeks, worsening patient outcomes

* The administrative burden and follow-up associated with appeals are significant and impact both healthcare providers and patient care

## Purpose

* Evaluate the success rate of appeals submitted by NMSP for dermatology specialty medications not included on an insurance formulary preference list

## Methods

### Study Design:

* System-wide, retrospective observational study

* Analysis of dermatology specialty prescriptions submitted to NMSP between January 1, 2024, and August 1, 2024, requiring submission of at least one appeal

## Results: Primary Endpoint (N=41)

* Number of appeals overturned by insurance after 1<sup>st</sup>, 2<sup>nd</sup>, or external level appeal for dermatology specialty medication where a PA was initially denied due to an insurance formulary preference
    

    - **22/41** appeals were approved
    

    - **19/41** appeals were denied

| Category   | Percentage |
| ---------- | ---------- |
| Overturned | 46         |
| Upheld     | 54         |


Figure 2a: Appeal outcome

## Results: Secondary Endpoints

* Number of patients required to seek financial assistance (FA) or alternative medication due to an upheld appeal
    

    - **8 (42.1%)** switched to alternate medication
    

    - **4 (21.1%)** obtained medication through patient assistance program
    

    - **4 (21.1%)** lost to follow up or unknown
    

    - **3 (15.7%)** other reasons

* Number of appeals submitted
    

    - 1<sup>st</sup> level appeal: **40**; 2<sup>nd</sup> level appeal: **4**; External appeal: **6**

* Appeal success rate by insurance type

| Insurance Type | Success Rate (%) |
| -------------- | ---------------- |
| Commercial     | 50               |
| Medicare       | 60               |
| Medicaid       | 57.1             |

## Conclusion

* Appeal process proved to be an effective strategy for overturning PA denials; Majority of these approvals were granted after the first level of appeal

* Despite the overall effectiveness, some patients still faced barriers, requiring alternative solutions such as switching medications or seeking FA

* The findings emphasize the importance of a structured and persistent appeal process

## Limitations

* Retrospective study & small sample size (N=41)

* Documentation differences in appeal letters were not studied

* Utilized pharmacy technician’s notes to determine appeal dates when letters were missing in electronic health record
