# Elacestrant real-world progression-free survival (rwPFS) of adult patients with ER+/HER2-, advanced breast cancer: a retrospective analysis using insurance claims in the United States

Elyse Swallow<sup>1</sup>, Jessica Maitland<sup>2</sup>, Kirthana Sarathy<sup>3</sup>, Ellen Sears<sup>3</sup>, Yasir Nagarwala<sup>4</sup>, Janelle DePalantino<sup>4</sup>, Eric Kruep<sup>4</sup>, Corey Pelletier<sup>4</sup>, Sebastian Kloss<sup>5</sup>, Tomer Wasserman<sup>5</sup>

<sup>1</sup>Analysis Group, Boston, US; <sup>2</sup>Analysis Group, Montreal, Canada; <sup>3</sup>Analysis Group, NY, US; <sup>4</sup>Menarini Group, New York, NY, USA; <sup>5</sup>Menarini Group, Florence, Italy

This poster was originally presented at SABCS 2024.

## BACKGROUND

▪ Endocrine therapy (ET) + CDK4/6i is the standard-of-care (SOC) for 1<sup>st</sup>-line treatment of ER+/HER2- advanced or metastatic breast cancer (mBC).<sup>1-3</sup>

▪ In current practice, sequential endocrine monotherapy or combination therapies are used in the 2<sup>nd</sup>-line post ET + CDK4/6i as recommended by guidelines.<sup>2,4,5</sup> Combination regimens in ≥2<sup>nd</sup>-line mBC can be associated with significant toxicities and high discontinuation rates.<sup>6-12</sup>

▪ *ESR1* mutations represent a type of acquired resistance that eventually emerges in up to 50% of patients after 1<sup>st</sup>-line treatment for ER+/HER2- mBC.<sup>13-20</sup> Current guidelines recommend testing using liquid biopsy for the emergence of *ESR1* mutations at each disease progression.<sup>21</sup>

▪ In the EMERALD trial, patients with *ESR1*-mutated tumors receiving single-agent elacestrant had a 45% reduction in risk of progression or death vs SOC ET; median progression-free survival (mPFS) was 3.8 months with elacestrant vs 1.9 months with SOC ET (HR=0.55; 95% CI: 0.39-0.77; P=0.0005).<sup>22</sup>

– In patients with ≥12 months of prior ET + CDK4/6i, mPFS with elacestrant was 8.6 months vs 1.9 months with SOC (HR=0.41; 95% CI: 0.26-0.63)<sup>23</sup>

▪ Based on the EMERALD trial, elacestrant was approved by the FDA in January 2023 for the treatment of postmenopausal ER+/HER2- *ESR1*-mutated advanced or metastatic breast cancer with disease progression following at least one line of ET.

▪ Sufficient time has passed since approval to characterize the real-world use of elacestrant in the current treatment landscape.

▪ This study aims to describe the real-world PFS (rwPFS) of elacestrant overall, as well as in patients with no prior exposure to fulvestrant, only 1-2 lines of ET, ≥3 lines of ET, and/or visceral metastasis.

## METHODS

| Database                              | Komodo Research Database (KRD+)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| ------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Study population                      | ▪ Adults with diagnosed ER+/HER2- mBC who initiated elacestrant in a real-world setting were identified between January 2023 and May 2024<br/>▪ Elacestrant is indicated for the treatment of postmenopausal women and men with ER+/HER2- ESR1-mutated advanced or mBC with disease progression following at least one line of ET. ESR1-mutation status was not available in the KRD+ at the time of analysis, but it was assumed patients received treatments according to their approved use |
| Index date                            | Time of first administration of elacestrant                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Demographic information at index date | ▪ Metastatic sites identified during the 6-month baseline period prior to the index date<br/>▪ Prior treatments received in mBC setting                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| Definition of rwPFS                   | Time from index date until earliest outcome (start of next line of therapy for mBC to disease progression or death) assessed using time-to-event and Kaplan-Meier methods                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Censoring rule                        | Patients were censored at first occurrence of:<br/>▪ End of data availability, OR<br/>▪ End of health plan enrollment                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Limitations                           | ▪ Real-world differences with routine clinical care versus prospective trials may be due to multiple factors impacting patient selection: treatment heterogeneity, tumor burden, genomic profile, and follow-up duration from the time of first elacestrant treatment. Variations in patient populations and baseline characteristics might require further research.                                                                                                                          |

## CONCLUSIONS

▪ In a real-world setting, elacestrant showed a consistent rwPFS benefit among all ER+/HER2- mBC patients and across clinically relevant subgroups:

| mPFS (months)       | Patients with no prior fulvestrant (n=106) | All patients (n=276) |
| ------------------- | ------------------------------------------ | -------------------- |
| Overall mPFS        | 9.4                                        | 6.8                  |
| 1-2 lines of ET     | 9.4                                        | 8.0                  |
| Visceral metastasis | 7.3                                        | 6.1                  |


▪ The rwPFS with elacestrant exceeded the overall mPFS reported in the EMERALD study.

▪ The rwPFS with elacestrant is longer when patients are treated in earlier lines.

▪ Future analysis of additional factors such as coexisting mutations (ie, PI3K/AKT1/PTEN alterations) and other clinically relevant subgroups will provide meaningful insights to help inform real-world clinical decision-making.
