# PERSISTENCE, DISCONTINUATION AND SPECIALTY PHARMACIST INTERVENTIONS IN PATIENTS RECEIVING DROXIDOPA FOR THE MANAGEMENT OF ORTHOSTATIC HYPOTENSION

SABRINA N. LIVEZEY, PHARMD, CSP<sup>1</sup> | NISHA B. SHAH, PHARMD<sup>1</sup> | JOSH DECLERCQ, MS<sup>2</sup> | LEENA CHOI, PHD<sup>2</sup> | AUTUMN D. ZUCKERMAN, PHARMD, CSP<sup>1</sup> | JESSICA DANIELL, PHARMD<sup>3</sup>
VANDERBILT UNIVERSITY MEDICAL CENTER
<sup>1</sup>VANDERBILT SPECIALTY PHARMACY, VANDERBILT UNIVERSITY MEDICAL CENTER, <sup>2</sup>DEPARTMENT OF BIOSTATISTICS, VANDERBILT UNIVERSITY MEDICAL CENTER, <sup>3</sup>YALE NEW HAVEN HOSPITAL

## BACKGROUND

* Droxidopa improves blood pressure and reduces the number of falls in patients with neurogenic orthostatic hypotension (OH).<sup>1</sup>

* Reported 6-month discontinuation rates are between 6 and 25%, most commonly due to adverse events or reduction in benefit.<sup>2,3,4</sup>

* Integrated neurology specialty pharmacists play a crucial role in management of droxidopa therapy by providing education and clinical interventions.

## OBJECTIVES

**PRIMARY**: Evaluate persistence, discontinuation and reasons for discontinuation for patients receiving droxidopa
**SECONDARY**: Describe number and types of pharmacist interventions

## METHODS

**DESIGN**: Single-center, retrospective cohort analysis
**INCLUSION**: Adult patients prescribed droxidopa by center’s outpatient neurology and cardiology clinics with ≥ 3 fills through center’s specialty pharmacy from May 2017- April 2020
**EXCLUSION**: Patients who did not start droxidopa therapy or passed away within 90 days of initiation
**PERSISTENCE**: Measured as time to the first non-persistent event, defined as a coverage gap exceeding 60 days

Neurology specialty pharmacists performed 64 interventions via telephone for 34 patients. As droxidopa therapy was frequently titrated, most interventions were dosage review.

## CONCLUSIONS

* This study found a high probability of persistence within an integrated care model. The discontinuation rate was similar to what is reported in literature<sup>2,3,4</sup>.

* Specialty pharmacists perform an important role in reviewing medication dosing and drug interactions. Our findings support the integrated specialty pharmacist role in ongoing treatment monitoring.
