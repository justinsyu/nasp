# Barriers and Facilitators of Specialty Pharmacy Prescription Turnaround Time

*Kotschevar C, Gabriel M, Campbell PJ, Mastrangelo V*
*Pharmacy Quality Alliance (PQA)*

## Background & Rationale

* Specialty medication use has grown in recent decades, as has the growth in specialty pharmacies serving patients.

* Prompt treatment initiation is necessary for optimal management of complex conditions.

* Reducing turnaround time (TAT) is crucial for specialty pharmacies to reduce time to treatment initiation and improve patient care.

* Studies demonstrate that best practices can be utilized to reduce turnaround time.

* There is currently limited published information about barriers and facilitators of specialty pharmacy prescription turnaround time.

## Objectives

* To determine factors that may influence specialty pharmacy turnaround time.

* To identify best practices that specialty pharmacies utilize to improve turnaround time.

## Methods

* Cross-sectional survey conducted, informed by previous focus groups

* Distribution occurred to:

    * National Association of Specialty Pharmacy members

    * Pharmacy Quality Alliance specialty pharmacy members

* Distribution: April-May 2021

* Requested one response per pharmacy location

* Survey question areas included:

    * Barriers and facilitators of turnaround time

    * Perceptions of turnaround time measurement

* Demographic information collected included:

    * Specialty pharmacy type (payer, independent, integrated delivery network, chain, other)

    * Geographic region the pharmacy served

    * Rurality status

    * State in which the pharmacy is located

* Statistical analyses included descriptive statistics of survey responses

## Limitations

* Survey design, while robust, does not capture actual turnaround time.

* Additional research is needed to determine impact of facilitator implementation on turnaround time.

## Conclusions

* Respondents indicated that specialty pharmacy turnaround time is inconsistently measured and needs to be standardized.

* Turnaround time measurement was indicated to be important for patient care and satisfaction.

* While prior authorization was consistently cited as a barrier, other barriers and facilitators varied, pointing to the need to align quality improvement initiatives with barriers encountered by the specialty pharmacy.
