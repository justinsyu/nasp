# Exchanging Genomics Reports Between Pathology Labs and Medical Centers Using the mCODE FHIR Implementation Guide

## BACKGROUND

The automated transfer of discrete molecular data into the EHR facilitates clinical decision making. Existing EHR data standards lack treatment insight fields (medications, trial eligibility). mCODE defines a consensus oncology data standard supporting genomic data and lightweight FHIR transmission. Pilots explored exchange between Tempus (pathology lab) and Vanderbilt/CHLA (medical centers).

## METHODS

Validation icon

Validation of mCODE FHIR genomic elements performed in CodeX accelerator (2021–2022).

Elements icon

Elements included: lab facility, order info, pathologist, demographics, specimen, molecular studies, therapeutic implications.

Transformation icon

Synthetic tumor reports generated by Tempus were transformed into mCODE FHIR and transferred to academic sites.

## RESULTS

## OBJECTIVE

Evaluate feasibility of mapping and exchanging molecular pathology reports into mCODE-conformant FHIR data for clinical use and standards refinement.

## FUTURE DIRECTION

* Expand testing with additional labs and cancer centers

* Incorporate real-world (non-synthetic) genomic data exchange

* Continue HL7/CodeX refinements for standardization and adoption

## CONCLUSION

* mCODE supports practical genomic data exchange aligned with HL7 standards

* Mapping required only nominal changes to existing pathology reports

* Collaboration between pathology labs and academic centers is critical for adoption and cross-domain interoperability
