# Specialty Technician Training Program Development

Diaramalys Rivera, BA, CPhT; Alijah Kosarko, BA, CPhT; Vanessa Batista, CPhT; Brittany Dasher, BS, CPhT; Sharee Parker, CPhT; Vinay Sawant, RPh, MPH, MBA
Yale New Haven Health, Department of Pharmacy, New Haven, CT

NASP National Association of Specialty Pharmacy logo

## Background

* The Covid-19 pandemic worsened a nationwide technician shortage with negative impacts on pharmacy workflow and patient care.

* Our hiring requirement for specialty pharmacy technicians (SPT) included one year of experience with advanced certification. With our growing business, the ability to hire SPT’s has been a challenge.

* Currently, Yale New Haven Health (YNHH) offers an ASHP/ACPE accredited Pharmacy Technician Training Program that prepares individuals for: a pharmacy career in all practice settings, the Pharmacy Technician Certification Board (PTCB) exam, with the opportunity for practical experience in multiple practice settings, including specialty pharmacy. The twenty-three weeklong program is equivalent to one year of pharmacy experience for the purpose of hire within the organization. The YNHH health system specialty pharmacy (HSSP) collaborated with the YNHH Pharmacy Technician Training Program to create a specialty pharmacy focused rotation track. While our legacy programs experiential rotations focused on multiple practice settings our new program is largely focused on specialty pharmacy.

## Objectives

* Creation and implementation of a specialty pharmacy focused pharmacy technician training program to increase the available candidate pool.

## Discussion

* In total, the development of the specialty pharmacy focused track of the YNHH Pharmacy Technician Training Program took five months.

* Since 2016, one student was hired from the original YNHH Pharmacy Technician Training Program as SPT.

* The specialty pharmacy focused track was first available to students on March 28, 2022. To date, two students have enrolled and completed the specialty pharmacy focused track and are currently being retained as full time SPTs.

* The hiring rate into the health system of the new Specialty Pharmacy Technician Training Track program is 100% which contributes to the overall program hire rate of 87%

## Conclusions

* A specialty focused pharmacy technician training program can be a novel strategy to address HSSP pharmacy technician staffing shortage.

## Barriers / Limitations

* Recruiting Students for the Specialty track

* Scheduling training with other ancillary departments (MAP, PA, Med B, etc.)

* Scheduling training with call outs and scheduled PTO.

* Utilizing and building off an already established accredited training program.

## Future Directions

* Recruit and increase enrollment into the Specialty focused pharmacy technician training program rotation track.

* Maintain a high percentage of hiring and retaining students from the program

* Reassignment of current rotation for sterile compounding and IV admixtures to our Home Infusion Pharmacy.
