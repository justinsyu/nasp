# Implementing a health-system specialty pharmacist-led pharmacogenomic protocol for DPYD genetic testing to optimize capecitabine safety

Diana Nguyen, PharmD; Andrew Wash, PharmD, PhD; Ana Lopez Medina, PharmD, PhD; Casey Fitzpatrick, PharmD; Abbey Hunter, PharmD, CSP; Katie Jo Cash, PharmD, CSP; Kristin Hutchinson, PharmD, CSP; Jessica Mourani, PharmD

CPS logo

## Background

* DPYD gene variants can lead to a deficiency in dihydropyrimidine dehydrogenase, an enzyme involved in the breakdown of fluoropyrimidine-based chemotherapy agents (e.g., capecitabine), and increase the risk of severe adverse events 16-fold.<sup>1</sup>

* Preemptive pharmacogenomic testing can mitigate these risks and is recommended to identify high-risk patients prior to therapy initiation.<sup>2</sup>

* Adoption is limited in the US due to challenges such as cost and reimbursement, inadequate healthcare training, and conflicting recommendations in clinical guidelines.<sup>3</sup>

* Health-system specialty pharmacy (HSSP) teams can play a key role in ensuring medication safety and optimal outcomes by confirming that pharmacogenomic testing was completed, when appropriate, prior to initiating medication.

## Objective

To describe an HSSP's role in a multidisciplinary protocol for preemptive pharmacogenomic testing of DPYD metabolizer status prior to capecitabine initiation.

## Description

### Quality Improvement Initiative

An ongoing quality improvement initiative was launched in 2024 at a health system whose specialty pharmacy services are managed by CPS Solutions, LLC (CPS).

This initiative adopted a multidisciplinary approach to ensure that proactive pharmacogenomic testing for DPYD metabolizer status was performed prior to initiating capecitabine.

### Evaluation Measures

* Number of patients evaluated for DPYD genetic testing prior to capecitabine initiation

* Number of patients at increased risk of toxicity

* Recommendations made by pharmacists

* Outcomes of pharmacist recommendations

### Data Analysis

* Descriptive statistics were used to present all measures. Data through February 2025 was included for analysis.

## Description

Figure 1: Description of Quality Improvement Initiative

### Part 1: Provider Education

Providers were educated on the importance of DPYD genetic testing when prescribing capecitabine, including the most recent evidence-based recommendations regarding testing.

Down arrow icon

### Part 2: Pharmacist Confirmation of Testing

Pharmacists documented test results in the pharmacy patient management system. If testing had not been completed, pharmacists notified the prescriber and requested that testing be performed.

Down arrow icon

### Part 3: Pharmacist Recommendations

For patients identified as intermediate or poor metabolizers, pharmacists recommended dose adjustments or therapy changes based on Clinical Pharmacogenetics Implementation Consortium (CPIC) guidelines.

Down arrow icon

### Part 4: Clinical Decision Support

After approximately one month, the health system implemented additional clinical decision support within the electronic health record at the point of order entry to prompt prescribers to order genetic testing.

## Evaluation

To date, 30 patients were evaluated by the HSSP team for DPYD genetic testing prior to initiating capecitabine. Pharmacists recommended dose adjustments for high-risk patients to mitigate adverse effects, while also providing patients with supportive medications and education on the increased risks.

**10%** Three (10%) patients were identified as intermediate metabolizers and at a significantly increased risk of severe toxicity.

**100%** All recommendations were communicated to the oncologists, documented in the patient management system, and accepted by the providers.

## Discussion & Conclusion

* HSSP teams are valuable in the implementation of pharmacogenomic protocols into clinical workflows due to their expertise in medication management and precision medicine.

* HSSP pharmacists can play a crucial role in optimizing chemotherapy regimens, reducing toxicities, and improving patient outcomes.

### Next Steps

* The next steps in this initiative will focus on evaluating how this protocol can be adapted and scaled to other health systems who manage patients on capecitabine.

* Future initiatives will consider other areas where pharmacogenomic testing could be a valuable part of patient management protocols within HSSPs.
