# PERSISTENCE TO DISEASE-MODIFYING THERAPY IN PATIENTS WITH RELAPSING MULTIPLE SCLEROSIS

## BACKGROUND

* Patients with relapsing multiple sclerosis (RMS) are maintained on disease-modifying therapy (DMT) to help slow disease progression, reduce the number of disease relapses, and limit new disease activity.<sup>1</sup>

* Reported persistence rates to DMTs are concerningly low.<sup>2,3</sup>

## OBJECTIVES

* Evaluate DMT persistence over 3 years

* Assess rate and reasons for DMT discontinuation

## METHODS

* **Design & Setting**:
    - Retrospective review; May 2017 – Oct 2020
    - Academic tertiary health system with integrated specialty pharmacy

* **Sample**:
    - ≥ 3 fills of DMT (index) in May – October 2017
    - Relapsing form of MS (ICD10 of G35)
    - DMT prescribed by MS Clinic provider

* **Outcomes**:
    - Persistence to index DMT over 3 years
    - Reason for first DMT discontinuation
    - Pharmacist role in first DMT discontinuation

* 193 patients (36%) remained on index DMT
* Of patients that were non-persistent, most switched to an alternate DMT (25%)
* 17% transferred care, likely due to payer requirements
* Discontinuations (17%) occurred steadily over time

* Dimethyl fumarate accounted for the most discontinuations (33%)
* Side effects were the most common discontinuation reason (32%), particularly for dimethyl fumarate
* 13% of patients discontinued due to stable disease, often while on interferon

* Long-term persistence to one specific DMT over 3 years is low
* Patients with RMS commonly discontinue their DMT due to side effects or stable disease

## SPECIALTY PHARMACIST INVOLVEMENT IN DMT DISCONTINUATIONS

* Icon: Lightbulb Identify the need for and initiate discontinuation due to lymphopenia, liver enzyme elevation, & pregnancy
* Icon: Phone Communicate with the patient and prescriber
* Icon: Clipboard Coordinate follow-up care, including additional lab monitoring
* Icon: Speech bubbles Recommend new DMT option

## CONCLUSIONS

* DMT treatment changes and patient transitions occur frequently and integrated specialty pharmacists play an important role in this process
