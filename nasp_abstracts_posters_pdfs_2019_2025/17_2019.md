# Real-World Assessment of the Trellis Rx Integrated Clinical Model on Oral Hepatitis C Therapy at Southern Ohio Medical Center

Robert Ferraro, PharmD, AAHIVP
Stephen Quillen, PharmD, CACP, PA-C, CAQ Psychiatry
Rory Phillips, BSPh, MBA, RPh
Brandon Newman, PharmD, MMHC, CSP

## BACKGROUND

* Recent developments in therapies to treat Hepatitis C virus (HCV) infection can lead to high cure rates (>95%).<sup>1</sup>

* These therapies are only effective if patients take their oral medication as prescribed, yet studies have shown that 50% of patients do not take the medication as prescribed.<sup>2</sup>

* Pharmacists can play a big role in improving adherence to HCV therapies to achieve the desired clinical response.<sup>3</sup>

* Studies have shown that a specialty pharmacy model integrated within a health system decreases the time it takes for patients to start treatment after being prescribed their medication.<sup>4</sup>

## PURPOSE

To assess the impact of a Trellis Rx partnership on medication access, affordability, and clinical outcomes in a real-world setting for patients with HCV.

## METHODS

**IRB-approved Study**

**Inclusion Criteria**

Patients:

* ≥18yrs

* Received HCV regimen written by a Southern Ohio Medical Center provider, with Trellis Rx integrated services

* Received HCV regimen after 01-01-2018

* Completed therapy before 12-31-2018

## ENDPOINTS

* SVR12 rates stratified by regimen

* Rates of patient return for SVR12 labs

* Out-of-pocket costs for Southern Ohio Medical Center patients using Trellis Rx integrated specialty services

## CONCLUSIONS

Southern Ohio Medical Center partnered with Trellis Rx to deliver comprehensive, coordinated specialty pharmacy care to patients with HCV. Findings from this integrated clinical model include:

| Providers expressed high satisfaction rates, as evidenced by positive survey results | Medication access was not a barrier to patients beginning therapy, as evidenced by:<br/>• 100% access rate<br/>• Affordable average co-pays | Opportunities exist to improve the percentage of patients who return for SVR12 labs after therapy completion | Patients experienced positive clinical outcomes that were equal to or better than clinical trials |
