# Specialty Clinic Pharmacist Collaborative Model

Natasha Stroedecke, PharmD; Jenna Lee, PharmD, BCPS, BCACP; Kimhouy Tong, PharmD, BCPS; Kimberly Boothe, PharmD, MHA<sup>1</sup>;
Steph Luon, PharmD, BCPS, BCACP; Martha Stutsky, PharmD, BCPS; Esther Eom, PharmD<sup>2</sup>; Vinay Sawant, RPh, MPH, MBA; Marie Renauer, PharmD, MBA, BCACP
Yale New Haven Health, Department of Pharmacy, New Haven, CT; The Kimber Boothe Group, LLC, Cincinnati, OH<sup>1</sup>; Specialty Home Infusion Services, Charlottesville, VA<sup>2</sup>

## Background

* Yale New Haven Health System (YNHHS) has a growing ambulatory care pharmacy presence

* One area of opportunity is expansion of fully integrated ambulatory care pharmacists into specialty disease state clinics and closer partnership with the health system pharmacy

* Patients in these clinics are often prescribed specialty medications and require a higher level of coordination for dispensing and patient education

* A similar model showed that integration of a pharmacist decreased clinician burden, decreased time to medication initiation, and improved continuity of care for patients<sup>1</sup>

## Objectives

* Expand health system specialty pharmacy prescription clinical continuity (determined by the number of prescriptions sent to health system pharmacy) and support by implementing a decentralized specialty practice integrated model for clinical services in target sites.

## Discussion

* Limited data is available in the pharmacy community about the implementation of pharmacists into specialty clinics

* This model has been proven to be successful and can be adapted to other health systems with specialty pharmacies

* The name of the dedicated ambulatory pharmacy clinic chosen was Medication Management Clinic (MMC) to tie in with national recommendations for reference to pharmacy services and to avoid confusion with the dispensing pharmacy

* The results of this model align with other reports showing that ambulatory care pharmacists can help decrease clinician burden and improve patient outcomes

## Conclusions

* Expansion of integrated decentralized pharmacists into target practices has increased clinical continuity by 23% and number of pharmacist referrals exceeded target at 165%

* Clinicians have received pharmacists as vital members of the team

## Barriers / Limitations

* Clinicians’ full understanding of an ambulatory care pharmacist’s role

* Existing CPA regulations required monthly documentation

* Model only developed in limited number of adult ambulatory care clinics

* Limited data due to time since implementation

## Future Directions

* Continued expansion into additional specialty disease state clinics and review of endpoints

* Integration of advanced pharmacy technician roles

* Continued optimization of workflow
