# Swallowing Safety With Riluzole Oral Film

James Wymer<sup>1</sup>; Stephen Apple<sup>2</sup>; Antoinette Harrison<sup>2</sup>; Bryan Alan Hill<sup>2</sup>

<sup>1</sup>University of Florida, Gainesville, FL, USA; <sup>2</sup>Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma America, Inc., Jersey City, New Jersey, USA

Mitsubishi Tanabe Pharma America logo

## BACKGROUND

* Dysphagia is highly prevalent in patients with ALS<sup>1</sup>

* Riluzole is a US Food and Drug Administration–approved treatment with survival benefits in ALS<sup>2</sup>

* Current riluzole administration routes require swallowing a tablet or liquid form that can be physically challenging and burdensome to patients with ALS

* Riluzole oral film (ROF; Exservan™) contains riluzole in a polymer-based film matrix that is applied and dissolved onto the tongue<sup>3</sup>

## OBJECTIVE

* To describe the ROF bioavailability and pharmacokinetic data

## METHODS

**2 clinical trials were conducted**
**Study 162020**

* Pivotal bioavailability and pharmacokinetics (PK) study

* Phase 1, open-label, randomized, single-dose, 5-period, replicate crossover, comparative bioavailability study in 32 healthy volunteers (Figure 1)

* Compared riluzole 50 mg ROF and Rilutek® 50 mg tablets under fasting conditions with evaluation of food effect

    — Treatment A: ROF 50 mg (fasting)

    — Treatment B: Rilutek 50 mg (fasting)

    — Treatment C: ROF 50 mg (fatty meal)

**Figure 1. Study 162020 Study Design**

|             | Period 1    | Period 2    | Period 3    | Period 4    | Period 5   |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ---------- |
| 32 subjects | A (ROF)     | B (Rilutek) | A (ROF)     | B (Rilutek) | C (ROF)    |
|             | B (Rilutek) | A (ROF)     | B (Rilutek) | A (ROF)     | C (ROF)    |
| Fasting     |             |             |             |             | Fatty meal |


**Study 17MOIR-0012**

* Phase 2, single-center study to evaluate the effect of ROF on swallowing safety in individuals with ALS

* This videofluoroscopic swallowing study was conducted with 9 patients with ALS before and after receiving a single dose of 50 mg ROF

* The primary outcome was change on penetration-aspiration scale (PAS) scores from pre- to post-dose

* Inclusion Criteria

    — 18-80 years; diagnosis of probable/definite ALS (revised El-Escorial criteria); consuming oral diet (Functional Oral Intake Scale ≥3); no known allergy to barium sulfate, riluzole, or inactive ingredients in ROF

* Videofluoroscopic Swallowing Study

    — The Videofluoroscopic Swallowing Study (VFSS) is considered the gold standard for studying swallowing and its dysfunction<sup>4</sup>

    — VFSS permits the visualization of bolus flow in relation to structural movement throughout the upper aerodigestive tract in real-time<sup>5</sup>

    — The VFSS also permits detection of the presence and timing of aspiration<sup>5</sup>

    — With the VFSS, clinicians can observe the effects of various bolus volumes, bolus textures, and compensatory strategies on swallowing physiology<sup>5</sup>

* Participants attended a single visit and underwent the following procedures (Figure 2):

    — Baseline VFSS (×11 bolus trials)

    — Single dose ROF 50 mg placed on the median sulcus of the tongue

    — Post-ROF VFSS (×11 bolus trials)

**Figure 2. Experimental Timeline of Procedures During Single Visit. Participants Underwent 2 Identical Vfsss Before and After Administration of ROF**

**Safety Data (Table 2)**

* A total of 44 treatment-emergent adverse events (TEAEs) were reported in the study; all were mild in severity (Table 2)

* No serious AEs were observed, and no subjects discontinued due to AEs

* Among 32 subjects who received at least 1 dose of the ROF in Study 162020, 17 events in 14 subjects were characterized as oral or pharyngeal hypoesthesia

    — 17 events

* 16 events were coded as oral hypoesthesia and 1 event as pharyngeal hypoesthesia

* 14 occurred following ROF in the fasted condition

* 3 occurred following ROF in the fed condition

* In the swallowing study, 55.6% of patients were able to swallow safely by PAS standards both pre- and post-dose

* Very little numerical or categorical change was observed following the dose of ROF

* No evidence of deterioration of swallowing function was observed post-dose

* There was no correlation between baseline ALSFRS-R score and changes in PAS scores (Figure 5)

* Similar findings were obtained for ALSFRS-R bulbar domain scores vs PAS scores and for EAT-10 scores vs PAS scores

## DISCUSSION

* The ROF was bioequivalent to Rilutek tablets and was well tolerated in healthy adults

* A single dose of 50 mg ROF had no detrimental effect on swallowing, indicating that ROF represents an alternative administration route in patients with ALS who have dysphagia
