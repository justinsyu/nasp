# Real-World Adherence and Treatment Outcomes to EGFR-Targeted Therapies in NSCLC: An Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy Analysis

Bisni Narayanan, PharmD, MBA, MS; Micheal Zummo, PharmD, CSP; Christopher Territo, PharmD; Terri Sue Rubino, PharmD, CSP; Tina Do, PharmD, MS, BCPS
Department of Pharmacy, Yale New Haven Health

NASP Annual Meeting & Expo 2025 logo

## Background

* In Western populations epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) mutations occur in approximately 10% -15% of patients with non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC).

* EGFR targeted tyrosine kinase inhibitors (TKIs) for NSCLC have revolutionized the treatment paradigm.

* Specialty clinical pharmacists play a crucial role in patient adherence to EGFR targeted TKI therapy.

* Patients with NSCLC at the integrated health system specialty pharmacy (HSSP) were enrolled in the lung cancer disease program with specific clinical services and disease management support tools.

## Objective

* To describe the adherence, mutation patterns and treatment discontinuation rates among patients with NSCLC on EGFR targeted TKIs.

## Methods

Retrospective chart review icon This is a retrospective chart review of completed activities between January 1st, 2024, to December 31st, 2024, for patients on EGFR TKIs as primary or subsequent treatment.

Primary outcome icon The primary outcome included evaluating the adherence (MPR and PDC) of TKIs for patients filling with the integrated HSSP.

Secondary outcomes icon Treatment discontinuation rates were evaluated for secondary outcomes.

Demographic data icon Demographic data, presence of mutations, prescribing patterns of first-, second- and third generation TKIs were also evaluated.

## Discussion

* The average age in this analysis was 74.5 years, consistent with previously reported literature.<sup>1</sup>

* Among never-smokers, women are disproportionately affected- a trend observed in our cohort.<sup>2</sup>

* In patients with NSCLC, varying TKI discontinuation rates (7.7% to 18%) due to adverse events have been reported.<sup>3,4</sup>

* In our analysis, 4% of patients discontinued EGFR TKIs due to adverse effects which is lower than the FLAURA trial. In the FLAURA trial, 13% of patients on osimertinib, and 18% of patients on standard EGFR TKIs discontinued therapy due to adverse events.<sup>3</sup>

* In 2024, on average specialty clinical pharmacists at the integrated HSSP had about 5.5 direct phone interactions per patient for assessments or initial education.

* The integrated HSSP model with enhanced patient engagement through frequent touchpoints along with therapy specific education increased medication adherence above benchmark as evidenced by MPR/PDC of 0.96/0.94.

## Limitations

* Clinical trials enroll patients with well-defined characteristics. The methodology for reporting treatment discontinuation rates may vary among studies.

* Discontinuation rates observed in this analysis may not be directly comparable to clinical trials.

## Conclusion

Integrated HSSPs are ideally positioned to track patient outcomes in NSCLC.
