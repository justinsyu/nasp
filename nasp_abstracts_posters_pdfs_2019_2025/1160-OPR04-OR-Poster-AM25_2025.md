# Costs and Lost Time for Employees whose Spouses Have Dementia and/or Alzheimer’s Disease and Patient Direct Costs

Richard A. Brook, MS, MBA<sup>1</sup>, Stephen A. Schramm, MA<sup>2</sup>, Alek A. Drnach, MS<sup>2</sup>, Ian A. Beren, BS<sup>3</sup>, Eric M. Rosenberg, MA<sup>2</sup>

<sup>1</sup>Better Health Worldwide / NPRT, Newfoundland, NJ, USA, <sup>2</sup>Workpartners LLC, Pittsburgh, PA, USA, <sup>3</sup>WorkPartners, LLC, Denver, CO, USA

## Background

* In the United States,<sup>1</sup> nearly 7 million people are living with Alzheimer's disease (AD), the most common cause of dementia (DEM).

    - \>11m Americans provide unpaid care for people with AD or DEM, estimated as 8.4b hours and ~$350b in 2023

    - Some unpaid care comes from spouses who are Caregivers (CGs) using Discretionary Incidental Time-off (DITO) and Family Medical Leaves (FMLs) for care of a spouse patient (PAT)

    - Caregivers’ health and costs are impacted after becoming a CG.

* The Society of Actuaries 2024 survey reported:<sup>2</sup>

    - 13% of male and female pre-retirees provide care or have provided care to loved ones who are not minor children, while 38% say they are unprepared to offer any help for a family member’s medical emergency or health issue

    - Caregivers are most concerned about the emotional and/or physical toll for male and female pre-retirees (36% and 26% respectively)

## Objectives

* Examine annual medical, prescription (Rx), FML, and DITO trends for caregiver (care partner) employees of spouses with Alzheimer's disease (AD), dementia (DEM), or BOTH.

* Examine medical and Rx claims for patients with AD, DEM, or BOTH.

## Study Population

* The Workpartners Research Reference Database (RRDb)<sup>3-8</sup> contains

    - Health plan medical and Rx claims

    - Enhanced demographics (including race, marital status, salary, job type, full/part-time status, exempt/non-exempt status)

    - Absence and disability (short- and long-term), Workers’ Compensation claims (with durations and payments)

    - FML durations

* The RRDb includes over 5.5 million employees and dependents in all 50 states, from 2001 to present. Employees are linked to their dependents.

* The RRDb has been used in prior employee<sup>3-4</sup> and caregiver<sup>5-8</sup> research.

## Methods

* Retrospective analysis of US patients in the RRDb (2018—2022).

* Patients were identified by ICD-10 codes:

    - G30, G300, G301, G308, or G309 for Alzheimer’s disease

    - F01, F015, F0150, F0151, F02, F028, F0280, F0281, F03, F039, G310, G3109, G3183 for dementia

    - For Alzheimer’s disease OR dementia for BOTH

* Patients and care partners had ≥ 1 year continuous data following patient’s initial Dx (Index Year) and were analyzed based on Index Year.

* The following outcomes were reported based on the Index Year:

    - Caregiver: FML claims filed, days missed under DITO and FML; per member per month (PMPM) medical, prescription, and total costs

    - Patient: The % and trends of PMPM medical, prescription, and total costs

* Per patient per year (PPPY) costs adjusted to December 2023 US dollars.

## Results

* We identified 2851 care partner and patient pairs with (AD=23.8%, DEM=79.2%) 10.2% were Dx'd with either (BOTH).

* Patient age decreased from 63.9 to 62.6 for those with Alzheimer’s disease, from 56.1 to 54.3 for those with Dementia, and 56.7 to 54.5 for those with BOTH.

* All cohorts had increases in the percent of female patients during the observation period.

## Conclusions

* As the diagnosis age for patients with dementia and Alzheimer’s disease decreases, more employees will be impacted, either as patients or by assuming the role of a caregiver.

* Employees may require time off to manage their affected partners, or themselves.

* Although medical and Rx costs have declined since 2018, there has been

    - A significant increase in Family Medical Leave Absences (FMLA), leading to a loss of more than two weeks of worker productivity in 2021, 2022

    - An upward (though less linear) trend in DITO days

    - And may changes in costs for their covered lives (PAT+CG) and the impact of these conditions on employee absences

## Limitations

* While this research was focused on employees caring for spouses, it was unknown if other support persons were available.

* Diagnoses were based on claims and could not be verified.
