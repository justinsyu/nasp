# Efficacy and Safety of Lebrikizumab in Adult and Adolescent Patients With Skin of Color and Moderate-to-Severe Atopic Dermatitis: An Interim Analysis of the Open-Label Phase 3b Trial, ADmirable

Andrew Alexis,<sup>1</sup> Ali Moiin,<sup>2</sup> Jill Waibel,<sup>3</sup> Paul Wallace,<sup>4</sup> David Cohen,<sup>5</sup> Pearl Kwong,<sup>6</sup> Amber Reck Atwater,<sup>7</sup> Cynthia Harris,<sup>7</sup> Jennifer Proper,<sup>7</sup> Evangeline Pierce,<sup>7</sup> Angela Moore<sup>8</sup>, Sonya Raikar (Non-author presenter)<sup>7</sup>
<sup>1</sup>Weill Cornell Medicine, New York, NY, USA; <sup>2</sup>Comprehensive Dermatology Center, Detroit, MI, USA; <sup>3</sup>Miami Dermatology and Laser Institute, Miami, FL, USA; <sup>4</sup>Wallace Skin Research Center, Los Angeles, CA, USA; <sup>5</sup>Skin Care Physicians of Georgia, Macon, GA, USA; <sup>6</sup>Suncoast Skin Solutions, Inc., Jacksonville, FL, USA; <sup>7</sup>Eli Lilly and Company, Indianapolis, IN, USA; <sup>8</sup>Baylor University Medical Center, Dallas, TX, USA; Arlington Research Center, Arlington, TX, USA; Arlington Center for Dermatology, Arlington, TX, USA

## BACKGROUND

* Lebrikizumab is a novel monoclonal antibody that binds with high affinity and slow off-rate to IL-13, thereby blocking the downstream effects of IL-13 with high potency<sup>1</sup>

* The efficacy and safety of lebrikizumab to treat moderate-to-severe AD have been established in Phase 3 studies, including subset analyses by race and ethnicity<sup>2-8</sup>

* There is a paucity of data to guide the treatment of moderate-to-severe AD in populations traditionally under-represented in clinical trials, including patients with skin of color<sup>9</sup>

## OBJECTIVE

* To present interim 16-week results from Admirable (NCT05372419), the first Phase 3b, open-label, 24-week study to evaluate the safety and efficacy of lebrikizumab in adult and adolescent patients with skin of color and moderate-to-severe AD

* To describe innovative objective measures of pigment, erythema, and post-inflammatory hyper- and hypo-pigmentation

## CONCLUSIONS

* This is the first Phase 3b clinical trial of lebrikizumab for patients with moderate-to-severe AD

    - With skin of color (80% Black or African American) and Fitzpatrick Phototype IV-VI

    - Using objective and subjective tools and scales to evaluate signs and symptoms that matter to patients

* Lebrikizumab improved AD signs and symptoms in patients with skin of color and moderate-to-severe AD after 16 weeks of treatment, with

    - EASI 75 achieved in 68% of patients

    - IGA (0,1) with ≥2-point improvement achieved in 39% of patients

    - Pruritus NRS ≥4-point improvement achieved in 56% of patients

    - No serious adverse events<sup>a</sup>

* The innovative objective measure PDCA-Derm<sup>TM,b</sup> identified improvement in post-inflammatory hyperpigmented lesions in 12/21 patients and improvement to normal skin tone in 6/21 patients at Week 16

<sup>a</sup> Results from an interim analysis; full data will be reported when study is complete; <sup>b</sup> A scale used to compare post-inflammatory lesions to unaffected adjacent normal skin

### Outcomes and Statistical Analysis

#### Week 16 Outcomes

* EASI,<sup>a,b</sup> IGA (0,1),<sup>b,c</sup> and Pruritus NRS<sup>d</sup>

* PDCA-Derm<sup>TM,e</sup>

* PO-SCORAD, Mexameter (melanin), Scarletred (erythema), biomarkers, and microbiome<sup>f</sup>

#### Statistical Analyses

* Interim analysis includes patients who enrolled by June 29, 2023, and completed 16 weeks of lebrikizumab therapy or discontinued treatment by Week 16

* Primary analysis: All data were summarized as observed

* Supplemental analysis: Missing data due to lack of efficacy were imputed with NRI; other missing data were imputed with MI

<sup>a</sup> A composite index with scores ranging from 0 to 72, with higher values indicating more severe and/or extensive disease; <sup>b</sup> Investigators received training on accurate assessment of EASI and IGA for patients with skin of color; <sup>c</sup> A 5-point scale that provides a global clinical assessment of AD severity ranging from 0 to 4, where 0 indicates clear, 2 is mild, 3 is moderate, and 4 indicates severe AD; <sup>d</sup> A patient-reported, single-item, 11-point scale that is used daily to rate worst itch severity over the past 24 hours (0 indicates "no itch"; 10 indicates "worst itch imaginable"); <sup>e</sup> A scale used to compare post-inflammatory lesions to unaffected, adjacent normal skin; <sup>f</sup> These outcomes are not included in this analysis.

### Key Eligibility Criteria

* Adults (≥18 years) and adolescents (≥12 to <18 years; weight ≥40 kg)

* Self-reported race, including Black or African American, American Indian or Alaska Native, Asian, and Native Hawaiian or Other Pacific Islander

* Fitzpatrick Phototype IV, V, or VI

* Chronic AD (according to AAD Consensus Criteria<sup>10</sup>) for ≥1 year

* Moderate-to-severe AD, including baseline:

    - EASI ≥16
    - IGA ≥3
    - BSA involvement ≥10%

* History of inadequate response to topical medications

* Naive to biologics indicated for the treatment of AD
