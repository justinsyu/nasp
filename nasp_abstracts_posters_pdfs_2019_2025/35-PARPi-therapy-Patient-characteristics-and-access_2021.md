<sup>1</sup>Department of Pharmacology, Vanderbilt University, <sup>2</sup>Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy, Vanderbilt University Medical Center, <sup>3</sup>Department of Biostatistics, Vanderbilt University Medical Center

## BACKGROUND

* PolyADP-ribose polymerase Inhibitor (PARPi) therapy is used in a variety of cancer indications but is most utilized in ovarian malignancies.<sup>1</sup>

* PARPi therapy has been used largely in patients who are female, white, and possess a breast cancer gene (BRCA) mutation.<sup>2,3</sup>

* High out-of-pocket (OOP) costs for oral cancer treatment lead to delays in starting therapy.<sup>4</sup>

* Integrated specialty pharmacy assistance for OOP costs can reduce financial toxicity.<sup>5</sup>

## OBJECTIVE

To analyze disease characteristics, time to medication approval, and out-of-pocket costs for patients receiving PARPi therapy.

## METHODS

**DESIGN**: Single-center retrospective cohort study
**INCLUSION**: Adult patients who initiated PARPi therapy through center's outpatient oncology clinic from November 2017 - December 2018
**EXCLUSION**: Patients who did not initiate PARPi therapy; Patients enrolled in a clinical trial

## CONCLUSIONS

* PARPi therapy was predominantly utilized in patients with epithelial ovarian cancer, with olaparib as the most commonly used agent.

* High insurance approval rate and low OOP costs were observed, supporting the role of an integrated specialty pharmacist in reducing financial toxicity.
