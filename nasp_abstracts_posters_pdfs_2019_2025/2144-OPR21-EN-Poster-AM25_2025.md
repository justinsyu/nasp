# Post-Launch Adverse Events Reported to FDA Adverse Event Reporting System for Long-Acting Injectable Buprenorphine

Brian J. Dawson<sup>1</sup>, Michael P. Frost<sup>2</sup>, Natalie R. Budilovsky-Kelley<sup>3</sup>, Adam Friedman<sup>3</sup>, Joshua M. Cohen<sup>3</sup>

<sup>1</sup>Ideal Option, Pasco, WA, USA; <sup>2</sup>The Frost Medical Group LLC, Conshohocken, PA, USA; <sup>3</sup>Braeburn Inc, Plymouth Meeting, PA, USA

## BACKGROUND

* Two long-acting injectable buprenorphine (LAIB) medications are approved for the treatment of moderate to severe opioid use disorder (OUD): RBP-6000 (monthly) and CAM2038 (weekly and monthly).<sup>1,2</sup>

* Randomized controlled trials have demonstrated the safety of LAIBs.<sup>3,4</sup> However, post-marketing data provide valuable insights into the safety of treatments within real-world populations.

* FDA Adverse Event Reporting System (FAERS) is a centralized public database used for post-marketing drug safety surveillance.<sup>5</sup>

* Adverse event (AE) rates from FAERS for RBP-6000 and CAM2038 have not been previously analyzed.

## METHODS

* This retrospective analysis evaluated AEs spontaneously reported to FAERS during the 12-month periods following the US commercial launches of RBP-6000 (March 1, 2018 to February 28, 2019) and CAM2038 (September 5, 2023 to September 4, 2024).

  - AEs reported in ≥5% of all case reports for each product were analyzed.

* AE reporting rates (RR) were calculated based on the total number of AEs per 1000 patients treated (RR<sub>pt</sub>) and per 1000 units sold (RR<sub>rx</sub>).

  - The total number of patients treated with/units sold of RBP-6000 and CAM2038 during these periods was estimated based on publicly-reported figures and specialty pharmacy and distributor data.

* AE terms with an RR ≥1 for at least one of the products are reported.

## RESULTS

* The estimated number of patients treated with RBP-6000 and CAM2038 during these periods was 6,464 and 24,805, respectively, with an estimated 16,770 and 94,826 units sold.

## LIMITATIONS

* There is no certainty that reported AEs were caused by RBP-6000 or CAM2038, given the spontaneous and inconsistent manner in which cases are submitted to FAERS.<sup>5</sup>

* Some AEs related to these medications may not be reported.<sup>5</sup>

* These data may be impacted by the "Weber effect," which is the tendency for increased AE reporting during the initial period following a drug's approval.<sup>6</sup>

  - However, it has been suggested that the Weber effect is not generally observed for AE reporting to FAERS.<sup>6</sup>

* The total number of patients treated with/units sold of RBP-6000 and CAM2038 in the 12-month periods following their US commercial launches was estimated using publicly-reported figures and specialty pharmacy and distributor data, which may be limited by incomplete data capture, duplication, and lack of direct linkage to drug administration.

## CONCLUSIONS

* Overall, AEs reported for both RBP-6000 and CAM2038 were infrequent during the 12-month periods following their US commercial launches.

* These data provide insights into the real-world safety profile of LAIB medications for the treatment of moderate to severe OUD and may help inform clinical decision-making.
