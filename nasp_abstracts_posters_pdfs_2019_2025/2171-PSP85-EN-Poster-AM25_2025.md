# Optimizing the timing of pre-approval information exchange (PIE) engagement for oncology products, products for rare diseases, and cell and gene therapies (CGTs)

Ben Penley, PharmD, MS<sup>1</sup>; Tasmina Hydery, PharmD, MBA, BCGP, FAMCP<sup>1</sup>; Charles M. Dragovich, BSPharm<sup>1</sup> | <sup>1</sup>Cencora, Conshohocken, PA, USA

## Background

* In 2022, the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2023, was passed and codified a permanent safe harbor for manufacturers to proactively engage in pre-approval information exchange (PIE) with healthcare decision-makers (HCDMs).<sup>1</sup>

* In past surveys, HCDMs have indicated that, for certain product categories, including oncology products, products for rare diseases, and cell and gene therapies (CGTs), early PIE is critical for decision-making.<sup>2</sup>

## Objective

* To investigate when HCDMs desire certain types of pre-approval information for oncology products, products for rare diseases, and CGTs and what impact PIE has on patient access.

## Methods

* A web-based survey was fielded with advisors from Cencora’s Managed Care Network (MCN) from May 6 to May 16, 2024.

  - MCN is a proprietary research panel of over 160 healthcare executives, medical and pharmacy directors, and other experienced individuals in managed care.

  - Participation in this survey was voluntary, and a modest honorarium was paid by Cencora to participants who completed the survey.

  - HCDMs who did not use pre-approval information in the drug evaluation process were excluded from participating in the survey.

* Descriptive statistics were used to summarize multiple-choice responses.

## Results

### Demographics

* A total of 41 HCDMs responded to the survey, representing health plans (51%), pharmacy benefit managers (32%), and integrated delivery networks (17%), with roles as pharmacy directors (66%), medical directors (24%), and pharmacy managers/trade relations (10%).

* Pharmacy and Therapeutics (P&T) committee roles included voting members (78%), presenters (49%), medical or clinical advisors (46%), and chairs or co-chairs (41%). Responsibilities included making coverage recommendations (93%), voting on coverage decisions at meetings (85%), conducting clinical reviews (80%), negotiating contracts with manufacturers (68%), and preparing or presenting drug reviews (51%).

### PIE accelerates access to products

* Of the HCDM respondents, 49% indicated that patients would gain access to a product sooner if PIE were conducted for specific product categories. Among these respondents, categories included oncology products (85%), products for rare diseases (85%), and CGTs (80%) (Figure 1).

### Timing to engage in PIE

* For all products, nearly half (47%) of HCDMs reported that the latest timeframe by which manufacturers should engage in PIE was up to 6 or 3 months before anticipated Food and Drug Administration (FDA) product approval (Figure 2).

### PIE considerations for specific product categories

* Relative to all products, HCDMs desired most types of pre-approval information earlier for products for rare diseases, CGTs, and oncology products (Figure 3).

* Types of pre-approval information most often desired >12 months before anticipated FDA approval included product information (all products: 15%; products for rare diseases: 37%; CGTs: 34%; oncology products: 29%), anticipated FDA approval timeline (19%; 27%; 34%; 20%), and indication sought (19%; 32%; 31%; 31%) (Figure 3).

## Limitations

* This study encapsulates the perspectives of a small sample of recruited HCDMs from Cencora’s MCN research panel.

* The respondent sample had greater representation from pharmacy directors at national health plans, which could affect generalizability of the results across all types of HCDMs.

## Conclusions

* Relative to all products, HCDMs desired pre-approval information sooner for oncology products, products for rare diseases, and CGTs. Pre-approval communication of phase 2 data was more highly valued for these products.

* Some HCDMs indicated that patients could gain access to these products sooner if pre-approval information were communicated.

* To facilitate patient access to products in these categories, manufacturers should consider earlier PIE engagement with HCDMs.
