# Transforming Rare Disease Management: Real-World Impact of an Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy Services

Bisni Narayanan PharmD, MBA, MS; Mitchell DelVecchio PharmD, CSP; Andrew Cadorette PharmD; Kimhouy Tong PharmD, BCPS; Terri Sue Rubino PharmD, CSP; Tina Do PharmD, MS, BCPS

Department of Pharmacy, Yale New Haven Health

NASP Annual Meeting & Expo 2025 logo

## Background

* Medications for rare diseases require close clinical monitoring and care coordination.

* Manufacturers may require risk evaluation and mitigation strategies (REMS) for medication management.

* Integrated health system specialty pharmacies (HSSP) are ideally positioned to meet the complex requirements of managing patients with rare diseases.

* In February 2025, the integrated HSSP at Yale New Haven Health achieved the Utilization Review Accreditation Commission’s (URAC) Rare Disease Certification.

* Mavacamten and trientine, penicillamine are specialty medications approved for treating obstructive hypertrophic cardiomyopathy and Wilson disease, both rare diseases.

## Objective

* To describe the impact of the integrated HSSP on turnaround time (TAT), medication assistance, and pharmacist clinical services for rare diseases.

## Methods

## Discussion

* At the integrated HSSP, specialty pharmacy liaisons assist with applying for prior authorizations, handling appeals, and processing prescriptions leading to expediated treatment initiation.

* Complex payor models, specialty drug carve outs, deductibles etc. may hinder the access of specialty medications for patients with rare diseases.

* About 22% of patients enrolled in the cardiomyopathy or Wilson disease received financial assistance at the integrated HSSP.

* Early initiation of therapy also improves treatment and patient reported outcomes<sup>1</sup>

* High touch services provided at the integrated HSSP such as clinical assessments, medication therapy interventions, and routine outreach for refills ensured patients stayed on therapy.

## Limitations

* Data elements required to calculate TAT are not standardized across specialty pharmacies resulting in differences in TAT definitions.<sup>2</sup>

* Health systems might not have access to the medications used to treat rare diseases, as most are Limited Distribution Drugs (LDD).

## Future Directions

* Patients with rare diseases need easy access to treatment and resources. Allowing integrated HSSPs access to LDD medications will improve outcomes for patients.

## Conclusion

Integrated HSSP’s high touch model is suited for navigating rare disease patient management.
