# Cation Exchange Chromatography: An Effective Means of Removing FXIa from a New 10% Intravenous Immunoglobulin Preparation

Alan Huber<sup>a</sup>, Gil Bu Kang<sup>b</sup>, Min-Jung Kim<sup>b</sup>, Seulgi Park<sup>b</sup>, Eun Bang<sup>b</sup>, Jeungwoon Hong<sup>b</sup>, Jihye Lee<sup>b</sup>

<sup>a</sup> GC Biopharma USA, 500 Frank W Burr Blvd, Suite 230, Teaneck, NJ 07666
<sup>b</sup> GCBiopharma, 107 Ihyeon-ro 30-beongil, Giheung-gu, Yongin-si, Gyeonggi-do, Republic of Korea.

## INTRODUCTION

* The presence of residual coagulation factors II, VII, IX, X and XIa in intravenous immunoglobulin (IVIG) preparations increases the risk of thrombi formation and thromboembolic events (TEEs) in patients receiving such therapy.<sup>1,2</sup>
* In 2010, a surprising increase in the number of TEEs was observed following use of a 5% IGIV product. Residual FXIa in the product was identified as the root cause.<sup>1</sup>
* IVIG manufacturing via Cohn-Oncley cold ethanol fractionation has been demonstrated to successfully remove FII, FVII, FIX, and FX.<sup>3,4</sup>
* Ethanol precipitation does not effectively separate FXIa from IVIG due to the high isoelectric point of FXIa (8.9 - 9.1).<sup>5</sup>
* FXIa can remain in intermediary products, risking the presence of procoagulant activity in the final IVIG preparation.
* The development of processes to remove residual FXIa from IVIG has been a longstanding goal with the aim of improving product safety.

## STUDY OBJECTIVES

* To develop and evaluate a process to remove FXIa from the GC Biopharma 10% IVIG (GC5107) preparation.
* To assess the robustness of the process via a spiking study.

### 6 Cation Exchange Chromatography (CEX)
* Ceramic-based resin, binds with 1.5X higher capacity to IgG compared to commonly used resins
* Functions to remove procoagulant activities

## METHODS

Procoagulant activity in intermediate and final purification products of the GC5107 manufacturing process was assessed using:
* Thrombin generation assay (TGA)
* Chromogenic FXIa assay
* Non-activated partial thromboplastin time (NaPTT)
* FXI/FXIa ELISA
* Western blot analysis

## DISCUSSION

* The GC5107 manufacturing process including CEX chromatography reduced procoagulant activity in the final product to below detection limits (Table 1) as measured by<sup>6</sup>:
    * TGA assay (< 1.56 mIU/mL)
    * Chromogenic FXIa assay (< 0.16 mIU/mL)
* The NaPTT of >250 sec in all 9 batches of GC5107 indicates very low levels of procoagulant activity (Table 1).
* Western blot analysis of intermediate samples indicated that FII, FVII, FIX, and FX were removed through cold ethanol fractionation, while FXIa was successfully reduced to undetectable levels only after CEX chromatography (Figures 1 and 2).
* In starting samples spiked with FXIa at 32.5X and 169.9X levels found in normal samples, the GC5107 manufacturing process reduced FXIa to very low levels (by ELISA) and reduced procoagulant activity to below the detection limit (by TGA) (Table 2).

## CONCLUSION

* These results demonstrate the ability of CEX chromatography to effectively remove FXIa (by Western blot), and reduce procoagulant activity to below detection limits (by TGA and chromogenic FXIa assay).
* The results of the spiking study demonstrate the robustness of the process — even for starting material containing 32X and 169.9X the FXIa content of a normal sample.
* The new manufacturing process including CEX chromatography may serve to reduce the risk of TEEs in patients receiving this therapy.
