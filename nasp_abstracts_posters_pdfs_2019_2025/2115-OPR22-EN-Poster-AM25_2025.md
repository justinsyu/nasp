## OBJECTIVE

* To evaluate dosing patterns across biologics in Crohn’s disease (CD) and ulcerative colitis (UC) in the United States (US).

## CONCLUSIONS

* This study in a US population showed dose escalation was common but occurred at varying rates between biologic treatments.

* While CD and UC are distinct diseases, the dose escalation observed for each biologic was generally similar across the two conditions.

* The discontinuation rates were higher among non-escalators, and higher rates of switching were observed among escalators who discontinued.

## BACKGROUND

* Crohn’s disease (CD) and ulcerative colitis (UC) are chronic inflammatory conditions with varying severity and a relapsing-remitting disease course.<sup>1,2</sup>

* Biologics have become mainstay treatments for moderately to severely active CD and UC; however, patients may lose responsiveness over time requiring therapy change.<sup>3</sup>

* Evaluating treatment patterns across biologics can aid decision-makers in optimizing therapeutic management for patients with CD or UC.<sup>4</sup>

## METHODS

| Study design: | A retrospective, observational study                    |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------------------------- |
| Data source:  | Merative™ MarketScan® Commercial and Medicare Databases |
| Biologics:    | Adalimumab, infliximab, ustekinumab, vedolizumab        |


| January 01, 2016 | Start of study period                 |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Index date       | Date of first claim of study biologic |
| June 30, 2023    | End of study period                   |


* **12-month baseline period**: Prior to index date
* **≥12 month follow-up period**: After index date

**Study population**:

* ≥18 years

* ≥12 months of continuous enrollment pre- and post- index date

* ≥1 medical or pharmacy claim for newly initiated study biologics

* ≥1 additional claim for the index medication during the follow-up period

### Study cohorts

| CD                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                   | UC                                                                         |
| -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| • ≥1 inpatient or non-diagnostic outpatient claim with a diagnosis of CD.                                                                                                                                                                                            | • ≥1 inpatient or non-diagnostic outpatient claim with a diagnosis of UC.  |
| • No inpatient or non-diagnostic outpatient claims with a diagnosis of UC.                                                                                                                                                                                           | • No inpatient or non-diagnostic outpatient claims with a diagnosis of CD. |
| • No evidence during the study, of other selected autoimmune conditions: ankylosing spondylitis, atopic dermatitis, axial spondyloarthritis, hidradenitis suppurativa, juvenile idiopathic arthritis, psoriasis, uveitis, psoriatic arthritis, rheumatoid arthritis. |                                                                            |


### Assessments

| Dose escalation                          | ≥20% increase in the starting maintenance dose or early administration (≥7 days early)                                 |
| ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Sensitivity analysis for dose escalation | ≥2 consecutive claims with ≥20% dose increase in the starting maintenance dose or early administration (≥7 days early) |
| Discontinuation                          | ≥90-day gap in days of supply of index biologic                                                                        |
| Switching                                | ≥1 claim for a non-index biologic or Janus kinase inhibitor among discontinuers                                        |


\* Data were reported using descriptive statistics for categorical variables (n, mean, standard deviation, frequency).
