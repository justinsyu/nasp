# Clearer Skin, Smoother Care: Dermatology Pharmacist-Provider Co-Management in Isotretinoin Therapy for Better Outcomes and Increased Provider Access

Amanda M. Popko PharmD, BCACP, Susanne Burns PharmD, MBA, Cara Ciamacco, PharmD
Geisinger, Danville, PA

## Background

* Isotretinoin is a highly effective treatment for severe acne but requires strict adherence to iPLEDGE requirements and frequent monitoring.<sup>1</sup> These demands can burden dermatology providers and limit patient access.

* Integrating pharmacists into the care model offers a promising solution to streamline care, improve adherence, and enhance provider efficiency.

## Objectives

* Evaluate the impact of pharmacist-provider co-management on:
    

    - Provider access
    

    - Patient adherence to iPLEDGE requirements
    

    - Prescription capture within the health system pharmacy
    

    - Success of dose adjustments and pharmacist-led interventions

## Process:

| Visit                     | Timeline           | Action                                                                                                                                                                      |
| ------------------------- | ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Provider                  | Decision to Start  | \* Order labs & activate iPLEDGE<br/>\* Refer to Pharmacist                                                                                                                 |
| Pharmacist                | Initial Touchpoint | \* Reviw baseline and order future labs<br/>\* Review beta-hcg and complete iPLEDGE (if applicable)<br/>\* Order Rx to Geisinger Pharmacy<br/>\* Authorization coordination |
| Treatment Initiation      |                    |                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Pharmacist                | Month 1 & 2        | \* Tolerability assessment<br/>\* Review labs<br/>\* Dose titration<br/>\* Complete iPLEDGE<br/>\* Order Rx to Geisinger Pharmacy                                           |
| Pharmacist                | Month 3 & 4        | \* Tolerability assessment<br/>\* Dose adjustment if needed<br/>\* Complete iPLEDGE<br/>\* Order Rx to Geisinger Pharmacy                                                   |
| Provider                  | Month 5            | \* Treatment completed: Pharmacist follow up month 6<br/>\* Treatment not completed: Provider follow up month 6<br/>\* Complete iPLEDGE (if applicable)                     |
| Treatment Discontinuation |                    |                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Pharmacist                | Final Visit        | \* Sign off from patient care<br/>\* Complete final documentation in iPLEDGE (if applicable)                                                                                |


## Evaluation Methods:

* Appointment access for providers referring into co-management program

* PDC for patients on isotretinoin managed through this process

* Provider and patient survey

* Prescription capture rate to health system pharmacy

## Anticipated Outcomes:

* Pharmacist-provider co-management in isotretinoin therapy significantly improves provider access by reducing required touchpoints by provider.

* Pharmacist-led coordination, counseling, and medication management enhance patient adherence and satisfaction.

* Increased prescription capture through health system pharmacy creates operational and financial value.

## Next Steps:

* Expand to include all dermatology practices in the health system

* Evaluate the interventions completed by the pharmacist

* Explore opportunity to implement similar co-management workflows in other disease states
