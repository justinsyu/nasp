# Migraine ACES: Assessing CGRP Efficacy and Safety
Trellis logo

Ashley Zupancic, PharmD, BCACP; Amanda Hickman, PharmD, MPH, MSCS; Mark Kocin, CPhT

## BACKGROUND

* The calcitonin gene-related peptide (CGRP) monoclonal antibodies used for migraine prevention and CGRP receptor antagonists for abortive migraine treatment are being more utilized for migraine management

* Though they have a similar mechanism of action, slight differences in their target may make dual therapy of these agents more effective in managing migraines with minimal concerns of increased safety risks

* Dual CGRP therapy could be a beneficial option for patients with contraindications, history of intolerance, or lack of benefit to other migraine medications

## OBJECTIVES

The aim of the study is to assess the risk of adverse drug reactions resulting in therapy discontinuation when taking preventative and abortive dual CGRP agents compared to monotherapy

## METHODS

**Study Design**: Multicenter double arm retrospective analysis
**Patient Population**: Ages 18 years and older
**Setting**: Integrated health-system specialty pharmacy
**Timeframe**: May 2019 – June 10, 2021

| Preventative | Abortive   | Exclusion   |
| ------------ | ---------- | ----------- |
| Erenumab     | Rimegepant | Eptinezumab |
| Galcanezumab | Ubrogepant |             |
| Fremanezumab |            |             |


## DATA COLLECTION AND ENDPOINTS

* Data was obtained from Arbor® specialty pharmacy technology platform which is utilized by all health system specialty pharmacies included in the study

* Data was organized into two arms including a monotherapy arm consisting of patients only ever actively on one CGRP and a dual therapy arm consisting of patients on an included preventative CGRP and one abortive CGRP agent concomitantly

* Descriptive statistics were utilized to analyze the collected data

Study endpoints diagram showing Sample Size (n = 2,523) split into Monotherapy (n = 1,814) and Dual Therapy (n = 709). Primary Endpoint: Compare discontinuation of migraine medication due to patient reported adverse drug reactions. Secondary Endpoint: Compare the rate of patient reported adverse drug reaction triggered interventions between the arms.

## DISCUSSION AND CONCLUSIONS

### CONCLUSION

* Incidence of adverse drug reactions was low between both arms
* Occurrence of adverse drug events was marginally higher in the dual therapy arm
* There was a minimal difference in adverse drug events between the arms

**LOW ADR RATE** → **DIFFERENT OCCURENCE** → **CONCLUSION**

### ADDITIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

Strengths diagram: Limited evidence in literature; Large sample size (n=2,523); National, multicenter population.

### Additional Considerations

* Further evaluation is needed to confidently conclude whether safety concerns exist when treating patients with dual CGRP therapy

* Future studies on trends and efficacy when using dual therapy are needed to further our understanding of these novel migraine treatments

## FUTURE DIRECTIONS

* Investigate reasons for differences in adverse drug reactions between the arms
* Compare total number of all abortive medications used between comparator arms
* Assess efficacy of migraine management between the dual therapy group versus monotherapy group
