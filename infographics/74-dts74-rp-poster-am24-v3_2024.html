Learning objective

Test adherence-based outreach while surfacing barriers to care

The poster positions Mind The Gap as a practical operating system for telehealth PrEP and HIV treatment continuity. Linkage Specialists use templated reports, custom scripting, and repeated outreach to help patients complete labs and attend follow-up visits.

  • Determine the efficacy of adherence-based outreach using the proprietary Mind The Gap Adherence Tool & Playbook.
  • Identify socioeconomic barriers that negatively affect adherence among observed HIV-positive and at-risk patients.
  • Support the "Treat" and "Prevent" pillars of the Ending the HIV Epidemic initiative.

Playbook workflow

Mind The Gap targets the handoffs where patients can fall out of care

Patient interested

A patient begins PrEP engagement through the telehealth pathway.

Intake submitted

The first flagged handoff is completion of the intake form.

Initial video visit

Another early risk point is attendance at the first provider encounter.

Lab kit sent

The provider sends a home lab kit to support therapy initiation or continuation.

Sample obtained

The poster marks specimen collection and lab submission as a common failure point.

Results returned

The lab analyzes the sample and sends results to the provider.

Prescription sent

The provider prescribes medication and sends it to the pharmacy.

Medication shipped

The pharmacy ships HIV treatment or PrEP medication to the patient.

Refill loop

The patient sets up the next refill, returning to the ongoing adherence cycle.

Steps marked as gaps correspond to asterisks in the extracted poster workflow.

Operational trigger

Two templated reports become two outreach queues

LAB

No Labs Call List

Identifies patients whose self-collected specimen was invalid or who did not attempt or submit specimen self-collection.

TV

No Tele-visit Call List

Identifies patients whose lab results have been received but who have no scheduled follow-up tele-visit or missed the visit.

Linkage Specialists work either queue every 10 days using the MTG scripting approach.

Results and conclusion

The poster reports the intervention design, not measured outcomes

The extracted markdown contains background, methodology, learning objectives, eligibility criteria, workflow steps, and citations, but no numerical pre/post adherence results or completed efficacy table.

Practical conclusion: the MTG tool is intended to close lab and visit gaps through compassionate, scripted outreach and barrier identification for HIV treatment and PrEP patients.

PrEP eligibility lens

Eight risk indicators define the at-risk population

01Sexual partner with HIV, especially with undetectable viral load.
02Consistent condom non-use.
03STI diagnosis in the past 6 months.
04Intravenous drug use.
05Injection partner with HIV.
06Shared needles, syringes, or injection equipment.
07Reported risk behavior.
08Multiple courses of post-exposure prophylaxis.
