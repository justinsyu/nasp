Analysis of Appropriate Storage and Stability of Oral Anticancer Agents

Cleveland Clinic specialty pharmacy built a systematic medication-use evaluation workflow to determine when oral anticancer agents can be opened, partially filled, or repackaged without compromising stability, storage requirements, or patient safety.

Operational tension Open only when evidence supports it

Partial fills can reduce waste and cost, but bottle integrity, desiccants, light, moisture, and manufacturer guidance determine whether opening is appropriate.

Objective

Build a repeatable decision workflow before opening medication packages

The poster describes a specialty pharmacy process for determining whether oral anticancer medications may be opened when partial fills are clinically or operationally useful.

Methodology

Three-step evidence workflow

Identify medications

  • Retrospective review of medications historically opened by the pharmacy.
  • Students, residents, and pharmacists compiled the medication list over time.
  • Initial sources included package inserts, medication guides, patient guides, and manufacturers.

Research and outreach

  • Requested written manufacturer correspondence when available.
  • Documented phone calls when written responses were not available.
  • Assessed desiccants, open-dish studies, and literature on storage outside original containers.

Compile findings

  • Consolidated medication-level findings into a spreadsheet.
  • Tracked storage handling, packaging, comments, historical opening, and final open-package recommendations.

Data collection

The decision record triangulates four evidence streams

Package insert

Storage and handling text, including controlled room temperature, light, moisture, and original package requirements.

Drug references

Lexi-Comp storage recommendations were abstracted alongside labeling and manufacturer details.

Desiccant status

Presence of a desiccant and whether it must remain with the product shaped recommendations.

Manufacturer correspondence

Written responses or documented calls supplied product-specific guidance beyond limited labeling.

Medication-use evaluation

Fifteen oral anticancer agents moved through approval rounds

The MUE identified more than 160 medications for review. Half of applicable medications had historically been opened, equivalent to 36% of hematology/oncology medications.

Initial 6

  • Apalutamide (Erleada)
  • Axitinib (Inlyta)
  • Dabrafenib (Tafinlar)
  • Enzalutamide (Xtandi)
  • Pacritinib (Vonjo)
  • Venetoclax (Venclexta)

Second round

Conclusion

A durable review infrastructure replaces one-off judgment

The project positions oncology pharmacists to reduce avoidable waste and cost while preserving product viability by requiring structured review before opening bottles or packages.
