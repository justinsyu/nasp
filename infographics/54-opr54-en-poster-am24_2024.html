Early Treat-to-Target Signals After 4 Weeks of Abrocitinib Monotherapy

A post hoc analysis of pooled JADE MONO-1 and JADE MONO-2 phase 3 data examined whether patients with moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis reached proposed 3- and 6-month treatment goals after only 4 weeks.

1. Target Framework

Adult moderate-to-severe AD goals combine PtGA change or absolute status with at least one disease-domain threshold.

2. Trial Pool

JADE MONO-1 and MONO-2 contributed randomized monotherapy data across placebo, 100 mg, and 200 mg arms.

3. Week 4 Lens

Only patients with Week 4 PtGA, EASI, PP-NRS, and DLQI data were included in the target-attainment analysis.

4. Response Gradient

Both abrocitinib doses exceeded placebo, with the 200 mg arm showing the largest Month 3 and Month 6 target attainment.

5. Clinical Readout

The authors conclude that proposed later treatment goals may be achieved far earlier with abrocitinib.

Conclusion

Rapid multi-domain improvement

What the poster adds

  • Substantial proportions of abrocitinib-treated patients reached proposed 3- and 6-month goals by Week 4.
  • Itch severity improved more with abrocitinib 200 mg and 100 mg than with placebo.
  • Early target assessment may help clinicians evaluate response sooner in moderate-to-severe AD.
