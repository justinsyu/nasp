NASP 2025 | 1191-OPR32-OR | PAH medication management

Pharmacist interventions helped resolve PAH treatment problems for most referred patients

MUSC Health reviewed integrated specialty pharmacist interventions for pulmonary hypertension patients and found that patient-level symptom or laboratory resolution occurred in 82.3% of documented cases.

Objective and methods

Question: do specialty pharmacist interventions resolve PAH therapy problems?

The study evaluated the impact of integrated specialty pharmacist interventions on side effect resolution and clinical management in patients with PAH.

  • Study design: single-center, retrospective descriptive review.
  • Study period: January 2024 through April 2025.
  • Outcome: resolution rate, calculated as documented symptom or laboratory resolution divided by total documented interventions or patient cases.

Specialty pharmacy utilization

Among resolved patients, most filled at MUSC Specialty Pharmacy.

Background

PAH therapies can create adverse effects that make adherence difficult for WSPH group I and IV pulmonary hypertension patients.

Practice gap

The poster notes limited literature on specialty pharmacist impact on medication adherence in PAH treatment.

Clinical focus

The tracked intervention categories map to practical management needs: electrolytes, fluid, side effects, and blood pressure.

Operational signal

Communication and lab coordination created follow-up loss for five patients, highlighting an implementation dependency.

Next question

The authors call for future work evaluating patient-reported outcomes and quality of life in PAH patients.
