Digital Patient Engagement and Improved Biosimilar Adoption Outcomes in Specialty Pharmacy

Lumicera Health Services compared a conventional telephone outreach program with a secure digital portal workflow for adalimumab biosimilar conversion. The digital pathway produced more conversions per 100 patients, larger projected savings, and less labor per successful switch.

Why this mattered

A 2023 market opening created an operational conversion challenge.

Nine adalimumab biosimilars launched in 2023, creating new treatment options and potential cost savings. The poster evaluated whether a digital assessment could identify interested patients and reduce the work required to complete successful biosimilar transitions.

Cohort 1: standard call-center conversion

Phone-first pathway for patients with copay greater than $50.

  1. Identify adalimumab reference product patients with copay above $50.
  2. Run coverage determination and copay comparison.
  3. Conduct non-clinician patient outreach for cost-saving opportunity.
  4. Use clinician outreach and education to confirm patient acceptance.
  5. Send provider fax for a new biosimilar prescription.

Cohort 2: digital patient engagement conversion

Portal-first pathway for patients showing interest digitally.

  1. Invite secure portal users to complete a biosimilar education assessment.
  2. Identify patients interested in biosimilar conversion.
  3. Run coverage determination and start provider fax outreach earlier.
  4. Use clinician education to confirm patient acceptance.
  5. Complete provider phone outreach and new biosimilar prescription screening.

Opportunity

Adalimumab biosimilar launches created new options for lower-cost biologic therapy.

Question

Could digital engagement improve adoption and reduce specialty pharmacy workload?

Design

Retrospective cohorts compared conventional outreach with a secure portal assessment.

Signal

Digital engagement yielded 7.5 conversions per 100 patients versus 3.6 by standard outreach.

Operational value

Total labor dropped from 9.6 hours to 2.0 hours per successful conversion.

Limitations

Read the signal as operational, not definitive.

The poster noted a retrospective design, limited study duration, and a small patient sample size, each of which may affect the certainty of conclusions.

Future directions

Extend the model beyond the initial adalimumab program.

Next steps included expanding to other formulary adalimumab biosimilars and applying digital engagement services to other biologic biosimilars in the pipeline.

Conclusion: Digital patient engagement in a specialty pharmacy adalimumab biosimilar adoption program was associated with higher conversion rates, projected cost savings, and reduced FTE burden compared with conventional non-digital outreach.
