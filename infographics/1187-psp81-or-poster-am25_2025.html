Driving patient outcomes with the implementation of a social determinants of health network in an imbedded community pharmacy setting

Genoa Healthcare evaluated whether linking new-to-pharmacy HIV and behavioral health patients in Clark County, Nevada to a social care platform improved medication persistence and adherence versus matched non-participants.

Study objective

Test whether social care support moved medication outcomes.

Primary objective: determine the impact of an SDOH network on participant medication persistence and adherence compared with non-participants.

Secondary objective: quantify and categorize the SDOH cases resolved among participants.

  • Participants opted in, completed SDOH screening, and had case resolution activity.
  • Controls were eligible patients who were unreachable or opted out of the program.
  • Propensity score matching balanced baseline age, first fill date, sex, and behavioral health therapy mix.

Evidence flow

From new-to-pharmacy enrollment to matched outcome comparison.

1

Identify patients

New-to-pharmacy HIV or behavioral health therapy patients at two Genoa pharmacies in Las Vegas/Clark County.

2

Route SDOH needs

Opted-in participants completed screening and were connected to community-based organizations through a social care platform.

3

Match cohorts

Propensity score matching produced balanced control and participant groups of 112 patients each.

4

Compare outcomes

Persistence used DOT and fill curves; adherence used PDC with Student's t-test and alpha set at 0.05.

Poster interpretation

The adherence measure was flat, but the persistence signal was consistent.

The poster concluded that targeted SDOH support in a patient's local area can positively affect medication-related outcomes. The clearest signal was persistence: participants remained on therapy more often at each subsequent fill.

  • Clinically meaningful improvement in days on therapy may translate to an additional fill per year.
  • Baseline characteristics were well balanced after matching, including age, sex, first fill date, and behavioral health therapy mix.
  • Generalizability is limited because the study was conducted at two Genoa pharmacies in Clark County.
