Treatment patterns of intravenous edaravone in patients with amyotrophic lateral sclerosis

A retrospective administrative claims analysis used Anthem-affiliated health plan data to compare IV edaravone use against the FDA-approved dosing schedule and a flexible real-world schedule over 12 months after treatment initiation.

Methods

Claims-based cohort construction and treatment pattern lens

Longitudinal database

Healthcare Integrated Research Database, a geographically diverse repository from Anthem-affiliated health plans representing more than 50 million US lives.

ALS diagnosis

Patients with an ALS diagnosis observed from Jan 1, 2012 through Mar 31, 2022.

IV edaravone index

First observed IV edaravone administration from Aug 8, 2017, the first US market availability date, through Mar 31, 2022.

12-month follow-up

Days covered included administration days and drug-free days in a cycle; discontinuation was at least 60 drug-free days outside a cycle.

Only a minority of patients followed the FDA-approved IV edaravone schedule, and real-world utilization varied from the approved schedule.

Conclusion and caution

Opportunity to improve adherence

While clinical reasons may explain some variability, the authors concluded that there is an opportunity to improve adherence in general.

Lack of adherence may limit the maximum potential benefit from any treatment. Claims-based findings may be affected by coding, billing, and measurement limitations and may not generalize outside commercially insured or Medicare Advantage US populations.
