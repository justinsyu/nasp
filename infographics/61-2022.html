NASP 2022 poster 61-2022 | Oral oncology adherence

Oral oncolytics: adherence, cost and utilization

Walgreens researchers tested whether discontinuation gaps in cycled oral antineoplastic therapy were associated with higher medical costs and hospitalization events. The poster frames discontinuation as an adherence metric for medications whose monthly supply and cycle timing can vary.

Cost signal

Discontinuation groups showed steeper adjusted medical cost growth.

In the repeated-measures models, the discontinued groups had higher adjusted 2019 medical costs and larger year-over-year increases than the non-discontinued groups.

Utilization signal

Hospitalization odds rose with current-year discontinuation.

Logistic regression found that 2019 discontinuation was associated with significantly higher odds of 2019 hospitalization. Longer medication coverage in the prior year reduced hospitalization odds.

Evidence flow

How the poster translated refill gaps into medical cost and hospitalization outcomes.

Claims cohort

Commercially insured MarketScan patients were observed across 2017 to 2019.

Therapy scope

Eight generic products across six oral oncology therapy classes were included as single-therapy use.

Gap metric

Discontinuation was calculated as a medication supply gap at 1.5 times the last supply in the last six months.

Filters

Exclusions removed under-18 patients, missing cancer diagnosis or enrollment, late starts, death, hospice, and transplant indicators.

Models

Repeated-measures models evaluated total medical costs; logistic regression evaluated 2019 hospitalization odds.

Therapy class mix

Objective and conclusion

The central question was economic and clinical.

Objective: Identify significant associations between cycled oncolytic medication discontinuation and total medical cost and hospitalizations.

Conclusion: Medication adherence, represented by fewer gaps in cycled oral antineoplastic coverage, was associated with lower medical costs and fewer hospitalization events in current-year and following-year outcomes.

The poster also positions the discontinuation method as a reportable annual metric when filters account for end of therapy, late starts, and product switching within therapy classes.
