NASP 2019 | Kidney transplant outcomes

Medication adherence and graft survival among kidney transplant recipients

A retrospective claims-plus-OPTN cohort analysis linked immunosuppressant refill adherence with kidney graft survival. Patients meeting the PDC threshold had substantially higher adjusted odds of a surviving graft.

Evidence flow

How the poster connected pharmacy behavior to graft status

Pharmacy claims

Single large national pharmacy chain claims were used to identify immunosuppressant fills from 2013-2016.

Recipient cohort

Adults with deceased-donor kidney-only transplant history and at least two immunosuppressant claims 150+ days apart were included.

PDC adherence

Proportion of days covered over 12 months after first fill defined adherence at a PDC threshold of 80% or higher.

OPTN follow-up

Post-transplant follow-up data through 2016 supplied the graft-survival endpoint; median follow-up was 969 days.

Adjusted model

Logistic regression estimated graft-survival odds while controlling for demographics, payor, copay, fills, assistance, pharmacy type, and interactions.

Study question

Objective and methods

Objective

Examine the association between graft survival and adherence in kidney transplant recipients.

Methods lens

  • Retrospective observational cohort using pharmacy claims and OPTN follow-up data.
  • Adherence measured as PDC for any immunosuppressant in the 12 months after first fill.
  • Graft survival defined as a surviving graft at the end of the study period.

Interpretation

Conclusion

Adherent patients were more likely to have a surviving graft than those not adherent to immunosuppressants.

The poster frames adherence as a modifiable behavior worth studying across patient populations to identify which behaviors contribute to sustained medication adherence.
