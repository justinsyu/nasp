Streamlining Hepatitis C Treatment through a specialty pharmacist-driven clinical program

CHRISTUS Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether specialty pharmacist enrollment, therapy selection, medication access, and monitoring could shorten treatment start, resolve drug interactions, and preserve strong cure outcomes for patients with chronic HCV.

Objective

Quantify enrollments and measure program impact

The poster assessed specialty pharmacist-driven enrollments in a Hepatitis C Clinical Program and summarized safety, operational, and clinical outcomes among patients prescribed or filling all-oral DAA therapy.

  • Adults had chronic HCV diagnosis code B18.2.
  • Patients were included if prescribed through CHRISTUS Gastroenterology clinics or filled at CHRISTUS Specialty Pharmacy.
  • Patients referred but not initiating therapy were excluded.

Background

HCV affects millions in the United States, and only a minority of diagnosed patients have been prescribed treatment.

Gap

Specialty pharmacists can support therapy selection and access, but operational workflow data remain limited.

Methods

Retrospective descriptive review of HCCP patients treated through gastroenterology clinics or CSP fills.

Safety

More than half of patients required SP-driven DDI resolution before or during treatment.

Outcome

High on-time completion and SVR12 rates accompanied rapid operational throughput.
