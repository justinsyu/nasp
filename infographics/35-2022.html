NASP 2022 / Poster 35-2022 / Diabetes

Pharmacy liaison-managed care model in diabetes

Baystate Health and Shields Health Solutions described an integrated pharmacy liaison model designed to reduce medication access barriers, coordinate refills, and support diabetes outcomes across adult and pediatric endocrinology clinics.

Evidence flow

Proactive liaison engagement converted diabetes medication access into a managed pathway

1

Prescription need identified

Patients requiring diabetes medication prescriptions are directed to the pharmacy liaison before the access process fragments.

2

Benefits and authorization work

The liaison investigates pharmacy and medical benefits, completes prior authorizations, and identifies financial assistance.

3

Pharmacy option alignment

Patients receive education on pharmacy options, copay, and delivery while prescribers are alerted to approved PAs and preferred pharmacy.

4

Monthly refill coordination

Medication and durable medical equipment refills are coordinated, then delivered to the home or picked up when convenient.

Objective and methods

What was evaluated

  • Objective: describe an observational analysis of a pharmacy liaison-managed care model for patients with diabetes.
  • Adult endocrinology clinic implementation began in March 2019; pediatric endocrinology clinic implementation began in September 2019.
  • Observed model outcomes were summarized from September 2020 through November 2021.
  • HbA1c change used patients on service through November 2020 and followed values for six months after enrollment.

Conclusion

What the poster concluded

  • The model was associated with high PDC, HbA1c reduction, faster therapy initiation, and lower medication copays.
  • The authors noted that a 1% HbA1c reduction may have implications for medical expenditures.
  • The care model may be adaptable to other health systems seeking to simplify diabetes care and improve outcomes.
