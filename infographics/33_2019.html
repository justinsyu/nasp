Development of a Specialty Pharmacy Productivity Benchmarking Model

UK HealthCare Specialty Pharmacy piloted an internal benchmarking model that combined task counts, task time, and staffing hours to compare productivity across specialty pharmacy service lines.

Method engine

Raw work volume plus measured time became a branch-level productivity ratio

Capture task time

Pharmacists used an Excel reporting tool to record time spent completing each KPI.

Collect task counts

Raw task numbers came from the patient management system or enrollment counts.

Add staffing hours

Median task time, raw volume, and staffing hours were combined for each branch.

Compare to benchmark

Inflammatory diseases served as the reference branch because workflows were more mature.

Time signal

Clinical assessments revealed the clearest branch difference

2x

Oncology pharmacists spent about twice as long on clinical assessments compared with other branches: 14.86 minutes versus 7.23 minutes; p<0.0001.

Intake activities tied to new prescriptions, including prior authorizations, appeals, and financial assistance, had low response rates and were dropped from the final analysis. The final comparison therefore focused on clinical activities.

Need

Specialty pharmacy lacked a validated model to evaluate productivity across workflows.

Measure

The model paired time reporting with system-derived task counts and staffing hours.

Benchmark

Inflammatory diseases anchored the comparison because of perceived maturity and workload.

Limit

Pharmacist buy-in, intake response rates, workflow variation, and EHR differences affected results.

Next

The next phase adds technicians, expands KPIs, shifts to REDCap, and extends collection to four months.

Conclusion

A pilot model proved feasible, but the model needs broader capture

The poster concluded that a novel productivity benchmarking model could aggregate and compare activity across multiple specialty pharmacy service lines.

The authors emphasized that intake work and operational differences must be better represented before the model can mature into a fuller productivity system.

Future buildout

From pilot KPIs to a larger operational panel

  • Include technician and pharmacist activities.
  • Add benefits investigation, refills, consults, cold-chain metrics, interventions, and laboratory review.
  • Use REDCap with a four-month collection window and fewer fixed KPI windows.
  • Improve participation through education, check-ins, and lower workflow burden.
