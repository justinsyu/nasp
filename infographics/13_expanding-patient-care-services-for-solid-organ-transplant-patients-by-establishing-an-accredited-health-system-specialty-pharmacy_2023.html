Objective

Move transplant pharmacy care from discharge event to longitudinal service.

The program aimed to expand patient care services for UT Southwestern solid organ transplant patients beyond hospital discharge through an accredited health-system specialty pharmacy.

Initial assessment Care plan, counseling, education, goals, adherence risks
Medication access Delivery services and payer coordination for complex therapy
Monthly refill calls Pill counts, dose confirmation, medication synchronization
Quarterly reassessment Quality of life, satisfaction, goal progression, interventions

Intervention architecture

ACHC patient care standards became the operating frame.

UTSW Specialty Pharmacy implemented a patient management program for solid organ transplant patients that combined clinical services, refill operations, and medication delivery.

Context: in 2022, UTSW reported completing more transplants than any program in North Texas for the third straight year.

Need

Transplant recipients require lifelong access to immunosuppressants, adherence support, and adverse-effect monitoring.

Program

An accredited specialty pharmacy embedded care plans, refill reminders, reassessments, interventions, and delivery.

New services

Initial care plans averaged 72 new referrals per quarter, with a 20% decline from Q4 2021 to Q4 2022.

Retention

While new enrollments declined, refill calls rose 24% and follow-up care plans rose 36% over the same period.

Capacity

Pharmacy staffing increased from 10 to 16 employees to support a growing transplant patient population.

Conclusion and future direction

Accreditation supported a transplant care program built for follow-up.

The authors concluded that UTSW Specialty Pharmacy successfully expanded patient care services for solid organ transplant patients and used ACHC accreditation to support a robust follow-up program inside the health system.

  • Extend specialty pharmacy services to all patients receiving a solid organ transplant at UTSW.
  • Expand geographic access by pursuing licensure beyond currently licensed states, TX and NM.
  • Increase pharmacy payer relationships so more patients can receive integrated specialty pharmacy services.
