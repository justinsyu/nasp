Adherence for Members Taking Oral Oncolytic Agents Undergoing Dose Modification Strategies

In a national pharmacy benefit manager cohort, dose modification was common and was associated with lower odds of optimal adherence to oral oncolytic therapy.

Objective

Measure adherence after a dosing regimen changes

The poster assessed adherence rates among adult members taking selected oral oncolytic agents who experienced a modification to their dosing regimen. Dose modification was defined as a change in daily dose from one prescription to the next.

1

Identify therapies

Palbociclib, abemaciclib, ribociclib, axitinib, lenvatinib, or cabozantinib fills from Jan. 1, 2021 to Dec. 31, 2022.

2

Apply eligibility

Adults with continuous eligibility for 180 days before and 2 years after first eligible fill were retained.

3

Stratify exposure

Members were grouped by whether their prescription sequence showed a daily-dose change.

4

Model adherence

PDC was calculated through final-fill exhaust; optimal adherence used PDC ≥0.85 and logistic regression.

Conclusion

Support around dose changes may protect adherence

The poster concluded that dose modification was associated with decreased adherence to oral oncolytic medications. Interventions that minimize avoidable dose modifications or provide additional member support during a dose modification may increase optimal adherence.
