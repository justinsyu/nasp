Standardizing Specialty Pharmacy Practices: Addressing Risk in Loading Dose and Maintenance Dose Management

A UVA Health specialty pharmacy quality-improvement project traced loading dose and maintenance dose vulnerabilities from a sentinel refill case into a structured plan for staff training, dedicated near-miss tracking, and workflow standardization.

Methodology

A PDSA-informed build: measure risk, train the team, then keep reviewing the signal.

Evaluate

Collect baseline error data and conduct a pre-training staff confidence survey.

Categorize

Create dedicated LD/MD near-miss and error categories for biweekly review.

Educate

Develop training materials, computer-based learning modules, and tutorial video support.

Adapt

Use Plan-Do-Study-Act cycles to incorporate feedback and refine the workflow.

Monitor

Continue data collection to inform future standardization and improvement work.

Objectives

Targets focused on error reduction, confidence, and durable onboarding.

  • Primary: reduce LD/MD-related near misses and errors by at least 75%.
  • Secondary: increase staff-reported confidence by at least 25% and reach at least 90% education attestation within one month.
  • Tertiary: test queue standardization feasibility and build a long-term onboarding module for new pharmacists and technicians.

Risk surfaced

A loading-dose refill sequence produced excess high-dose exposure and delayed transition to maintenance therapy.

Root vulnerability

The poster identifies inconsistent prescribing practices, absent LD/MD tracking, and unclear handoffs across intake, clinical review, and processing.

Clinical stakes

Improper LD/MD sequencing can affect clinical goals, healthcare resource use, patient-reported outcomes, and specialty therapy value.

Intervention package

Dedicated categories, education modules, tutorial video, attestation, feedback, and PDSA cycles create a standard work loop.

Expected direction

The quality-improvement endpoint is fewer LD/MD near misses, greater staff confidence, and a scalable onboarding model.

Future considerations

The poster defines escalation paths if training alone is not enough.

Restructure high-risk queues in Willow Ambulatory.

If refills remain high

Limit autofill eligibility for loading doses.
