NASP 2023 / Poster 40

Apalutamide plus relugolix maintained castrate testosterone through 1 year.

In a 12-patient Apa-RP substudy of high-risk localized prostate cancer after radical prostatectomy, oral ADT with relugolix was coadministered with apalutamide without relugolix dose escalation; all 10 patients completing 1 year maintained testosterone <50 ng/dL.

Objective 1 year

Assess maintenance of castration with apalutamide plus relugolix through 12 months of coadministration.

Evidence Flow

Run-in, combination, continuation, recovery.

Eligible high-risk localized PC

Radical prostatectomy, post-operative PSA ≤0.2 ng/mL, and high-risk features.

Relugolix run-in

360 mg at Day -14, then 120 mg daily through Day -1.

Combination start

Apalutamide 240 mg daily plus relugolix 120 mg daily from Day 1.

12-month assessment

Testosterone measured every 3 months and 30 days after discontinuation.

Testosterone Trajectory

Median T moved rapidly below the castration threshold.

Median testosterone fell from 348.5 ng/dL at Day -14 to single digits at Day 1 and remained low at 1 year, then rose after treatment discontinuation.

Castration was durable.

One-year apalutamide plus relugolix coadministration maintained castrate testosterone in all patients who completed therapy.

Recovery was rapid for most.

Eight patients recovered testosterone above 50 ng/dL within 1 month of treatment discontinuation.

Safety aligned with known profiles.

The reported safety findings were consistent with the known safety profiles of apalutamide and relugolix.
