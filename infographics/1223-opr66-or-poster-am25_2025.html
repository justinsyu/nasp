Hematological oral oncolytics: adherence, healthcare cost and utilization

A Walgreens Connected Care Oncology analysis linked discontinuation of 21 targeted oral hematological oncolytics with higher medical spending, hospital admission odds, and length of stay after adjustment for demographic, clinical, utilization, and payer factors.

Evidence flow

From refill gaps to payer outcomes

1

Select eligible patients

Commercially insured adults with cancer diagnosis evidence, continuous enrollment, and at least two fills of a targeted oral hematological oncolytic.

2

Classify discontinuation

A discontinuation event was defined as a gap exceeding 1.5 times the prior days supply between consecutive fills.

3

Risk-adjust outcomes

General linear models adjusted for demographics, region, Rx channel, comorbidity, COVID-19, provider visits, admissions, ER use, surgery, and insurance type.

4

Estimate economic impact

Predicted values were scaled to payer-eligible cancer populations to quantify inpatient utilization and cost differences.

Conclusion

Clinical management is positioned as an adherence-retention lever

The poster concludes that adherence to oral hematological oncolytics was associated with lower medical costs, lower hospitalization odds, and shorter length of stay after adjustment. Connected Care Oncology was framed as a support model intended to help patients remain on therapy.
