Assessment of a Clinical Pharmacist-Driven Appeal Process in a Dermatology Practice

Wake Forest Baptist Health evaluated whether embedding a clinical pharmacist into dermatology medication access work changed how often denied specialty medication prior authorizations moved to appeal, how quickly appeals were submitted, and how often appeals were approved.

Objective

Measure whether a pharmacist changed the appeal pathway

The study evaluated the impact of a clinic-embedded pharmacist on specialty medication appeal submission in a dermatology practice.

Secondary endpoints included appeal approval rate, number of appeals submitted, and time to appeal submission.

Dermatology appeal process workflow

Medication access specialist plus pharmacist created a clearer handoff

MAS submits PA

Specialty medications requiring prior authorization route first to the medication access specialist.

Denial triggers appeal

If the PA is denied, the embedded pharmacist prepares the appeal by phone or letter.

Provider approves

The provider reviews and approves the appeal letter before submission.

MAS follows status

The medication access specialist tracks payer response after submission.

Escalate if needed

Additional appeal levels may be pursued after another denial.

Approval rate

Submission gains were paired with a higher approval signal

Appeal approval increased from 47.6% to 65.0%, a 17.4 percentage-point rise (p=0.05).

The authors note approval success is partly outside clinic control because final determinations remain payer decisions.

Interpretation

A workflow result, not just a payer result

The clearest effect was operational: more appeals were submitted and they were submitted faster after the pharmacist took ownership of denied specialty medication PAs.

Limitations included single-center design, inconsistent pre-intervention PA documentation, and different medication access training levels between nurses and pharmacists.

Conclusion: embedding a clinical pharmacist in dermatology positively impacted appeal submission rate, appeal approval rate, and time to appeal submission for specialty medication access.
