A novel tool to monitor adherence to oral oncolytics: a pilot study

Sun W, Reeve R, Ouellette T, Stutsky M, De Jesus R, Huffer MJ, Mougalian SS

This Yale New Haven Health pilot tested whether Nomi smart bottles could capture real-time capecitabine-taking behavior and trigger text-message reminders during later treatment cycles.

Objective

Can a connected bottle monitor and intervene in real time?

  • Assess Nomi functionality for monitoring capecitabine adherence.
  • Enable text notifications after a monitored pre-intervention period.
  • Define adherence as correct doses divided by total prescribed doses.
  • Track conversion when a patient takes a dose after a text reminder.

Study schema

Monitor first, message later

Cycle 1 Smart bottle monitoring only; no patient text intervention.
Cycle 2 Monitoring continues during pre-intervention baseline.
Cycle 3 Text messaging enabled for non-adherence signals.
Cycle 4 Optional continued post-intervention observation.

Adherence phenotypes

Three response patterns emerged from pre- to post-intervention change

+>8%

Category 1: improved

N = 5; most had conversion rates above 35% and tended to live in lower-income regions (mean $58.9k).

±8%

Category 2: stable

N = 15; adherence changed within a narrow band after text messaging was enabled.

->8%

Category 3: declined

N = 4; these patients tended to live in higher-income regions (mean $91.0k), illustrating that reminders did not help everyone.

Patient feedback

Usefulness signal with practical friction

Wanted to continue using Nomi 60% of feedback survey respondents
Would recommend to others 80% of feedback survey respondents
Operational issues Bottle opening/closing, text timing after regimen changes, connectivity, and battery replacement

Limitations

Interpretation needs caution

Clinical confounds Hospitalizations, disease progression, and medication toxicity could affect adherence.
Device burden Technical difficulties with the bottle affected some participants.
Feedback capture Patient feedback was difficult to elicit during the study.

Conclusion

Nomi was feasible for real-time monitoring, with the clearest future use case in patients at high risk for non-adherence.

Overall adherence was high, and some patients appeared to benefit more from text reminders than others. The poster highlights Category 1 patients as the next focus for targeted adherence support.
