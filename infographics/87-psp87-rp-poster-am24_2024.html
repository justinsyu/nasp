Senseless and Meaningless: redesigning clinical intervention documentation

UC Davis Health specialty pharmacy replaced inconsistent intervention documentation with a standardized Epic SmartForm that connected pharmacist interventions, actions, outcomes, and self-service KPI reporting.

Core transformation

From free-text friction to KPI-ready care signals Standard categories and cascading logic turned documentation into an operational data asset.

Objective

Make intervention documentation effective for staff, leaders, and accreditation

  • Support communication and continuity of care by replacing inconsistent documentation products.
  • Reduce end-user friction through an Epic SmartForm with cascading logic and standardized categories.
  • Improve leadership output by embedding a self-service tool for intervention KPIs.

The poster frames documentation as central to care continuity, KPI trending, and accreditation standards.

Methods

A stepwise build converted cultural inertia into a documentation system

1

Assess process

Map existing documentation, workflow gaps, and adoption barriers.

2

Compare tools

Compile legacy tools and examine overlapping documentation fields.

3

Standardize

Develop intervention categories from an external medication therapy framework.

4

Crosswalk

Identify redundancies, missing fields, and reporting deficiencies.

5

Build SmartForm

Use cascading Epic logic to guide intervention, action, and outcome selection.

6

Surface KPIs

Embed a self-service reporting tool for leadership and quality tracking.

SmartForm pattern

Four documentation domains organized the clinical menu

Medication management and optimization

  • Therapy selection
  • Side effect mitigation
  • Cost-effective therapy selection
  • Dose adjustment and therapy change

Safety

  • Duplicate therapy
  • Adverse drug reaction
  • Drug-drug interaction
  • Contraindication screening

Health and social maintenance

  • Medication access
  • Order screening and monitoring
  • Immunization screening
  • Healthcare and SDOH barriers

Adherence and persistence

  • Patient education
  • Financial assistance enrollment
  • Access coordination
  • Adherence aids and follow-up

Problem

Emerging documentation tools were inconsistent and inefficient, limiting visibility into interventions and outcomes.

Build

A standardized Epic SmartForm used cascading logic to reduce redundant fields and guide category selection.

Output

FY2024 Q4 generated 783 interventions, 863 actions, and 605 outcomes in the new documentation structure.

Engagement

Staff engagement increased, with Q4 intervention volume reported as 350% higher than the prior quarterly average.

Conclusion

The collaborative redesign advanced documentation practice, aligned with accreditation needs, and supported continuous quality assurance.
