Hereditary Angioedema Medication Utilization

A specialty pharmacy retrospective study compared acute hereditary angioedema medication use 12 months before and after prophylaxis initiation, separating subcutaneous and intravenous acute-treatment cohorts.

Study design

How the retrospective comparison was built

Identify fills

Patients across the United States had an index HAE medication fill from a large specialty pharmacy.

Set index

Study patients used first prophylactic fill; controls used a randomly selected acute drug fill.

Look back and ahead

Acute drug utilization was assessed over 12 months before and after the index fill.

Stratify route

Analyses separated subcutaneous and intravenous acute-treatment cohorts.

Adjust model

Regression estimated differences in acute utilization while adjusting for confounders.

Conclusion

Poster takeaway

In this real-world specialty pharmacy cohort, initiating prophylactic HAE therapy was associated with significant reductions in acute medication utilization for both SC and IV acute-treatment cohorts, while prospective work is still needed to quantify financial and quality-of-life impact.
