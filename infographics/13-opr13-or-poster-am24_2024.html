Adherence, persistence, and molecular response patterns in CML treated with TKIs

Yale New Haven Health evaluated chronic myelogenous leukemia patients enrolled in an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy disease program to connect adherence, persistence, and molecular response patterns.

CML cohort

Eligibility lens

Included patients had at least one year of TKI therapy and completed program tasks.

Core outcomes

MPR and PDC were evaluated for OPS patients as adherence and persistence measures.

Prescribing pattern

Interpretation

Specialty pharmacist follow-up is the practical mechanism

  • Specialty clinical pharmacists assessed adverse events and missed doses 5 days after medication initiation.
  • Patients were reassessed every 6 months, or sooner when clinically needed.
  • The poster attributes benchmark-exceeding MPR and PDC to close HSSP monitoring.

Objective

Evaluate adherence, persistence, and molecular response patterns among patients with CML on TKIs.

Methods

Retrospective chart review of completed tasks from May 1, 2023 to April 30, 2024.

Population

Patients on TKI therapy for at least one year in the YNHH CML disease program.

Mutation notes

Observed mutations included T315I, F359I, CALR, ASLX1, and E255V.

Conclusion

Specialty clinical pharmacist interventions result in improved adherence and outcomes for patients with CML.
