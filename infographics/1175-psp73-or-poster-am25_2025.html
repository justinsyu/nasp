A model for expanding disease-specific specialty pharmacy patient management programs

Yale New Haven Health described a repeatable, data-driven approach for moving an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy from broad umbrella programs toward disease-specific patient management modules with evidence-based therapeutic goals.

From generalized management to disease-specific care pathways

The poster frames PMP growth as a structured clinical operations model: identify gaps, prioritize high-impact disease states, and assign measurable outcomes that fit each therapy area.

3.5x module growth from 2020 to 2025

Objective

Build a systematic approach for ongoing PMP expansion

The health system sought a formal process to develop and expand disease-specific programs within an integrated HSSP. The model was designed to align patient volume, specialty drug complexity, accreditation expectations, payer requirements, and pharmacist-led high-touch care needs.

Development model

A three-stage operating process prioritized new PMP modules

1

Current state scan

  • Survey pharmacy staff.
  • Assess specialty medication utilization.
  • Stratify module priority by historical and current volumes.
  • Use standards of care and guidelines.
2

Subsequent analysis

  • Evaluate umbrella programs and disease-state volumes.
  • Review specialty drugs in the pipeline.
  • Assess need for high-touch specialty pharmacist care.
  • Review literature for evidence-based goals and outcomes.
3

Expansion decision

  • Identify disease-state PMPs to develop.
  • Replace broad categories with tailored programs where useful.
  • Assign measurable therapeutic goals.

Analysis workflow

Specialty medication review

Novel approvals, new indications, and FDA-filed pipeline drugs helped identify emerging program needs.

Program analysis

Teams evaluated patient volume, specialty drug volume, and areas where specialty pharmacists could have the greatest impact.

Potential PMPs

Examples included hidradenitis suppurativa, IgE-mediated food allergy, and uveitis.

Interpretation

Formal evaluation converts scale into individualized care

  • Analysis identified opportunities to provide more individualized patient care for specific disease states.
  • Evaluation of broad umbrella programs created opportunities to establish customized, evidence-based PMPs.
  • Periodic review helps integrated HSSPs respond to changing accreditation requirements and payer expectations.
Conclusion Consistent, data-driven expansion

A systematic PMP development process can help integrated HSSPs expand disease-specific programs while supporting evidence-based, patient-centered specialty care.

Barrier

Barrier

Barrier
