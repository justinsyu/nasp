Outreach effectiveness

Targeted counseling showed a lower discontinuation rate and strong patient engagement.

Experimental 15% Targeted AE education plus standard counseling.
Control 30% General counseling or no targeted PREDICT counseling.

The poster reports a 15 percentage-point absolute reduction in discontinuation among model-identified patients receiving targeted counseling. The result was directionally favorable but not statistically significant in this small cohort.

Evidence flow

PREDICT translated risk scores into a clinician action path.

Claims inputs

Retrospective claims and clinical resources defined oral oncology drug cohorts and patient attributes.

Risk score

Models estimated adverse event-related discontinuation risk within the first 180 days.

Driver insight

Random forest analysis surfaced medications, comorbidities, and patient characteristics tied to risk.

Targeted counsel

High-risk patients received standard counseling plus education tailored to predicted AE drivers.

Follow-up

Pharmacists checked therapy continuation at 30 and 90 days after first fill.

Counseling targets

Medication patterns and clinical history became pharmacist talking points.

Objective

Test a hybrid of artificial intelligence and direct clinician intervention.

The poster sought to explore whether adverse events could be identified and mitigated within specialty pharmacy using machine learning plus individualized pharmacist outreach.

It also assessed whether PREDICT affected oral oncology therapy discontinuation and increased patient engagement through targeted clinician contact.

Conclusion

Clinical acumen made model output actionable.

The authors concluded that risk stratification can predict discontinuation patterns and that targeted, clinician-guided AE management may reduce discontinuation while achieving high participation.

Reported limitations included reliance on Humana Medicare Advantage Prescription Drug plan data and models limited to oral oncology medications.
