Objective and cohort

Who entered the adherence analysis?

The study sought to understand factors associated with adherence for patients starting CDK4/6 inhibitors in an HSSP-supported care model.

Methods

Five lenses were used to compare PDC

Drug

Palbociclib, ribociclib, and abemaciclib cohorts.

Age

Average age and age groups, including patients 50 years or younger.

Insurance

Commercial, Medicaid, Medicare, and unknown coverage patterns.

Region

Midwest, Northeast, South, and West comparisons.

Income

State median household income mapped to PDC tiers.

Statistics

Kruskal-Wallis and Dunn tests assessed regional drug-group differences.

PDC was defined as total days' supply divided by total possible days covered.

Results

What the poster adds

  • Average adherence remained high across drug, age, insurance type, and geographic region.
  • The South had the lowest regional PDC values for all three drugs, including ribociclib at 82%.
  • Patients in higher PDC tiers were associated with higher state median household income.

Conclusion

Care model signal plus equity signal

  • The large HSSP cohort showed high, consistent CDK4/6 inhibitor adherence.
  • Income-linked adherence observations suggest added support should be directed to vulnerable populations.
  • The authors recommended future zip-code-level income mapping to separate income groups more precisely.
