Assessment pattern

Two patient-reported measures created the analytic frame

BHLS Four self-reported health literacy items scored on a 5-point Likert scale and grouped into literacy categories.
RAPID3 RA disease activity index using physical abilities, pain, and overall health perception, scored up to 30.

Objective

Evaluate the relationship between health literacy and change in patient-reported RA disease activity after treatment initiation.

Methods

Prospective observational HSSP study using baseline BHLS and baseline plus follow-up RAPID3 PROMs.

Result

No statistically significant relationship was found between baseline BHLS and RAPID3 change.

Interpretation

The small exploratory sample limits inference, especially across marginal and limited literacy categories.

Conclusion

Future studies should use larger samples and longer follow-up while expanding BHLS-guided counseling workflows.

Future direction

Health literacy screening as counseling infrastructure

The source poster recommends incorporating BHLS during all initial counseling sessions to better understand patient literacy status.

  • Use health literacy status to guide pharmacist counseling approaches.
  • Provide formal guidance for HSSP pharmacists on counseling by literacy category.
  • Expand evaluation to a larger sample with longer follow-up to better test clinical associations.
