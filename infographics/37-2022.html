NASP 2022 / Poster 37-2022

Eco-friendly cold-chain packaging kept patient satisfaction high while reducing delivery waste.

Source poster: Patient Satisfaction of Delivery Methods and Eco-Friendly Cold Chain Packaging for Medication Deliveries at One Health System Specialty Pharmacy

A WVU Medicine Health System Specialty Pharmacy telephone survey evaluated whether patients preferred reusable cold-chain packaging over conventional insulated boxes and whether in-house courier delivery outperformed UPS experience.

Objective and Methods

A satisfaction survey compared packaging and delivery experiences.

Identify

Patients were retrospectively identified in dispensing software after receiving cold-chain specialty medication deliveries.

Classify

Deliveries were categorized by conventional packaging, reusable eco-friendly packaging, courier, and UPS exposure.

Survey

Telephone surveys captured demographics, packaging preference, reasoning, Likert satisfaction, and delivery preference.

Compare

The primary endpoint asked whether patients were more satisfied with eco-friendly cold-chain packaging.

Translate

Conclusions focused on patient satisfaction, courier delivery, environmental footprint, and potential cost savings.

Why eco-friendly won

  • Reduced waste, bulk, and hassle.
  • Easier disposal and handling.
  • Efficient medication transfer.

Why conventional persisted

  • More flexibility when a package must be directly accepted.
  • Some patients valued reusing packaging materials.

Conclusion

Patient experience can support greener cold-chain operations.

The poster recommends increasing adoption of similar eco-friendly delivery methods and using in-house couriers where feasible to support satisfaction.

It frames cold-chain innovation as a way to reduce environmental footprint while preserving patient experience, with further research needed to test whether reusable packaging also produces pharmacy cost savings.
