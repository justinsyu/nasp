Objective

Measure whether embedded specialty pharmacy changes the provider experience.

The study compared provider satisfaction before implementation, at 6 months, and at 1 year after a clinically integrated health-system specialty pharmacy was placed alongside outpatient care teams.

Problem

External specialty pharmacies can leave clinic teams with limited visibility into adherence, access, and affordability barriers.

Intervention

Pharmacists and pharmacy liaisons were embedded directly in outpatient clinics as part of the care team.

Measurement

Provider Experience Surveys and Net Promoter Score were collected before implementation, at 6 months, and at 1 year.

Signal

Experience scores rose across all domains, and NPS improved from -40 to 57 and then 87.

Meaning

The poster concluded that clinically integrated specialty pharmacy dramatically improved provider satisfaction.

Survey waves

Respondent counts increased as implementation matured.

65 Pre-implementation survey completions
87 6-month post-implementation completions
143 1-year post-implementation completions

Conclusion

The embedded model improved satisfaction and operational confidence.

Providers reported better visibility into adherence, easier referrals, and improved medication affordability support after implementation. The clearest headline was the 127-point rise in Net Promoter Score from baseline to one year.
