Objective

Build a clinic-embedded specialty pharmacy service

Develop an integrated ambulatory care pharmacy model within rheumatology clinics to facilitate patient education, specialty medication monitoring, adherence, and clinical continuity with the health-system specialty pharmacy.

Research

Use background research and stakeholder analysis to identify a rheumatology site with high utilization of self-administered specialty medications.

Develop

Create a specialty collaborative practice agreement and pharmacist visit workflow for clinic-based care.

Integrate

Recruit and credential pharmacists, allocate clinic space and exam rooms, then launch the service in clinic.

Assess

Track clinical continuity and referrals first, with PDC adherence and RAPID3 disease activity as secondary outcomes.

Discussion

Why the pilot mattered

Operational expansion Pilot clinic achievements expedited expansion of pharmacy services to additional rheumatology sites.
Care continuity More specialty prescriptions were routed through the integrated health-system specialty pharmacy.
Patient support Integrated pharmacists supported education, affordability, safety, and medication monitoring.

Limitations and future directions

Next scaling questions

Data maturity Secondary outcomes, including RAPID3 and lab adherence, were still being collected.
Reporting workflow Future work called for improved reporting to collect lab monitoring adherence efficiently.
Service spread Authors proposed expansion to all rheumatology departments and other specialties such as dermatology.

The integrated ambulatory pharmacist model was developed and implemented, exceeding referral targets while increasing clinical continuity and maintaining high medication adherence.
