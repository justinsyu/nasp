Use of Texting to Re-engage Specialty Patients

Fairview Pharmacy Services used Relay Network(TM) text outreach to reconnect specialty patients who had opted out of therapy management and route patients with higher RAPID-3 disease activity toward pharmacist follow-up.

Objective

Re-engage patients outside therapy management.

The program targeted specialty patients with rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, or ankylosing spondylitis who had no follow-up scheduled with the therapy management team but had filled a specialty medication in the previous 120 days.

Methods

A text-first workflow separated low disease activity from patients needing follow-up.

Identify

Patients had a qualifying inflammatory arthritis diagnosis, no therapy management follow-up, and a recent specialty medication fill.

Text

Relay Network(TM) sent the outreach message and linked patients to a RAPID-3 assessment.

Score

Completed RAPID-3 surveys stratified patients by disease activity from near remission through high severity.

Triage

Low disease activity patients received a thank-you message; moderate or high activity patients entered a call list.

Enroll

Pharmacists offered therapy management enrollment when follow-up assessment suggested a need.

Discussion

Texting created a new way back into care.

The response rate among delivered texts allowed the pharmacy to re-engage 342 patients who had opted out of therapy management. The team focused pharmacist calls on the moderate/high disease activity group, where adherence support, lifestyle counseling, and clinic communication could be most useful.

Future consideration

Extend the alternate follow-up channel.

The poster identified other specialty pharmacy disease states, including atopic dermatitis, Crohn's disease, and ulcerative colitis, as potential candidates for operationalizing a similar text-based follow-up method.

Conclusion: Relay text outreach gave specialty pharmacists an efficient alternate follow-up channel and reconnected patients who had previously opted out of therapy management.
