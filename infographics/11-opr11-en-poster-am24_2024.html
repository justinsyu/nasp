Pharmacist-led monitoring for patients initiating PARP inhibitor therapy

A Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy pre/post study evaluated whether tailored pharmacist monitoring during the first 90 days of PARP inhibitor therapy changed interruptions, dose changes, discontinuations, and acute-care use.

Objective

Evaluate pharmacist-led tailored monitoring on medication interruptions, dose reductions, discontinuations, ER visits, and hospitalizations.

Population

Adults initiating PARP inhibitor therapy filled through Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy or manufacturer assistance programs.

Monitoring topics

Calls assessed medication-taking, missed doses, side effects, mitigation strategies, next call timing, and escalation to physicians if needed.

Detected workload

The post-intervention model generated 181 pharmacist interventions; patient education was the dominant category.

Conclusion

Tailored pharmacist monitoring was associated with fewer and shorter interruptions and fewer hospitalizations during early PARPi treatment.

Bottom line

More frequent pharmacist contact changed the early-treatment risk profile.

The poster concludes that pharmacist-led monitoring during PARP inhibitor initiation was associated with fewer and shorter dose interruptions and fewer hospitalizations in the first 90 days, even as additional touchpoints captured more symptoms and intervention opportunities.
