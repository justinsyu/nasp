Methods at a glance

A claims-based pre/post design compared matched calendar windows.

Pre-COVID window

930

Adult multiple myeloma patients with new IV or oral medication starts from March 1 through August 31, 2019.

→

COVID window

824

Adult multiple myeloma patients with new starts from March 1 through August 31, 2020, identified through medical and pharmacy claims.

  • Objective: Assess the impact of COVID-19 on IV and oral medication prescribing patterns among patients with multiple myeloma.
  • Rate definition: New therapy starts divided by newly diagnosed patients with a first multiple myeloma indication date in the study timeframe.
  • Statistical test: Rates were compared using chi-square testing, with p-values at or below 0.05 considered statistically significant.

Interpretation

The pandemic pattern was not a simple shift away from infused therapy.

  • Both IV and oral new-start rates increased significantly during the COVID period.
  • The overall count of starts decreased, but the 22% drop in newly diagnosed patients was larger than the 11% drop in starts.
  • Authors concluded that further study is needed to understand COVID-19 effects on IV versus oral use in multiple myeloma.

Conclusion

Rate increases should be read against a shrinking diagnosis denominator.

While total new therapy starts decreased during COVID-19, the rate of starts for both IV and oral therapies increased significantly among patients newly diagnosed with multiple myeloma.

The authors noted that the increased rates may be explained by the 22% drop in newly diagnosed patients during the COVID period.
