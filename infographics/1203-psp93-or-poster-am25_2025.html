Evaluating the Specialty APPE Experience

A first-year UK Specialty Pharmacy and Infusion Services APPE program used a site coordinator, structured specialty content, disease-state discussions, and mock interviews to support student learning and preceptor workflow.

Objective

Measure satisfaction and locate program strengths

The project assessed student pharmacist and preceptor satisfaction with the UKSPIS APPE Program, identified strengths and weaknesses, explored expansion opportunities, and evaluated the impact of the newly implemented site coordinator.

The questionnaire was distributed through REDCap to APPE students after each rotation block and to preceptors after their final scheduled APPE block in the 2024-2025 academic year.

Evidence Flow

Four structured components formed the APPE support model

Streamlined onboarding

Centralized communication and orientation reduced early rotation friction for students and preceptors.

Expert-led specialty topics

Specialty Topic Series presentations were used to broaden exposure to specialty pharmacy practice.

Student-led disease discussions

Disease-state topic discussions connected rotation learning to NAPLEX preparation.

Mock application and interview

A rigorous mock process targeted future job and postgraduate training readiness.

Conclusion

Organized specialty APPE infrastructure showed early promise

The first official year of the UKSPIS APPE Program suggests that a site coordinator within an organized specialty pharmacy rotation can support operational efficiency, student learning needs, and preceptor experience.
