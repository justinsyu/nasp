Figure 1 translated

A six-stage dashboard cycle turns clinical questions into maintained analytics

  1. 1Purpose: define key metrics and end users before building the screen.
  2. 2Plan: identify the team, stakeholders, and governance needed to sustain the work.
  3. 3Data: choose software, create feeds, centralize data, and apply clear inclusion and exclusion rules.
  4. 4Build and validate: design visualizations, check calculations, and confirm the dashboard reflects the source data.
  5. 5Deploy: use dashboards to improve outcomes and demonstrate HSSP value.
  6. 6Maintain: refine based on feedback and automate updates for real-time use.

Background

Integrated HSSPs are increasingly used to improve care and reduce costs for complex specialty-medication conditions.

Objective

Create comprehensive clinical outcomes dashboards that track patient-centered measures and HSSP service value.

Methods

Combine literature review, committee input, clinical analytics work, de-identified data, validation, filters, and user-centered graphics.

Results

Comprehensive dashboards expand evaluation from operational metrics to outcomes, interventions, efficacy, and discontinuation patterns.

Conclusion

Clinical outcomes dashboards enable data-driven decision-making, patient-care optimization, and organizational performance improvement.

Bottom line

The poster argues for dashboards as specialty pharmacy infrastructure

The key contribution is a practical dashboard-development framework for HSSPs. By integrating clinical outcomes, interventions, efficacy, and discontinuation measures into validated real-time views, the model helps teams understand care quality, communicate across disciplines, support research, and demonstrate the value of integrated specialty pharmacy services.
