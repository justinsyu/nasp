NASP 2019 Annual Meeting & Expo

Evaluation of a Specialty Pharmacy Health Coaching Program

Fairview Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether adding a health coach to therapy management could address social, emotional, lifestyle, and end-of-life needs that may sit outside routine pharmacist or nurse support.

Objective

Measure whether a health coach added value beyond standard specialty pharmacy therapy management.

The poster assessed program reach, patient satisfaction and perceived health impact, change in PHQ-9 depression scores, and referrals to additional support services.

Methods

How patients moved through coaching

1

Trigger or offer

Coaching was offered during initial calls or after needs such as emotional distress, new chronic illness, life transition, coping issues, or advanced care planning.

2

Initial outreach

The health coach explained the program, confirmed interest, identified a focus area, encouraged goals, and documented baseline PHQ-9.

3

Follow-up calls

Ongoing calls focused on patient goals and documented referrals for outside services when additional support was needed.

4

Session 3 check

After the third call, patients received repeat PHQ-9 screening and a mailed satisfaction survey.

Coaching focus

Loss, grief, and emotional needs dominated the program mix.

The largest focus pattern was emotional/spiritual support with loss and grief, accounting for 38 of 82 patients, or 46%.

PHQ-9 pattern

Depression scores generally improved after three coaching sessions.

Among 54 patients with paired PHQ-9 scores, 30 patients improved overall. In patients with baseline moderate to severe depression, 70% had decreased depression scores.

Conclusion

Health coaching was associated with high satisfaction, perceived physical and mental health benefit, improved depression scores, and resource referrals.

The program ultimately shifted from PHQ-9 alone to PROMIS-2 to better monitor physical, mental, and social health domains.
