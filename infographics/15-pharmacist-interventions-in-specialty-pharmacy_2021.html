Objective

Quantify the clinical and economic signal of pharmacist interventions

The poster evaluated likely outcomes from pharmacist interventions across all disease states within health-system specialty pharmacies, then matched standardized outcomes to published cost-avoidance estimates.

  • Retrospective observational study of patients on specialty medications.
  • Clinical outcomes captured in a patient management platform.
  • Interventions supported by EMR integration, board-certified pharmacists, and clinic-embedded liaisons.

Interpretation

Why the pharmacist workflow mattered

  • Interventions were embedded in the standard clinical workflow, not treated as isolated events.
  • Provider-facing interventions focused on proactive mitigation of drug interactions, adverse events, and care-plan gaps.
  • Patient-facing interventions supported medication handling, administration, adherence, and disease-state management.
  • Economic value was estimated by pairing documented outcomes with cost-avoidance values from the literature.

Conclusion: an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy model with pharmacist-led interventions was associated with favorable clinical and economic benefits, including $15,292,883 in total cost avoidance across all disease states.
