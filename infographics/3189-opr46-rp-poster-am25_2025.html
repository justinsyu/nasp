Quantifying the Economic Value of Specialty Pharmacy Care Through Pharmacist Intervention

Study Purpose

Calculate avoided healthcare cost from pharmacist interventions

The poster asks how much economic value specialty pharmacy care can demonstrate when pharmacist interventions prevent medication waste, suboptimal therapeutic response, or avoidable adverse outcomes.

1 Direct costMedication price of a tangible product that was not wasted.
2 Indirect cost x probabilityEstimated downstream consequence cost weighted conservatively by likelihood.
= Total avoided costPrimary outcome, with secondary views by intervention, medication, and therapy category.

Secondary outcomes segment average cost avoidance by intervention type, medication, and therapeutic category. The extracted source defines these endpoints but does not include final aggregate totals.

Methods Snapshot

Patients on specialty medications with documented pharmacist interventions

Included records came from a clinical management system for patients on specialty medications between January and July 2024 when a pharmacist intervention had an associated outcome of interest.

1 Identify records Specialty medication patients with documented interventions.
2 Assign outcomes Investigators labeled potential avoided consequences.
3 Separate costs Direct medication costs versus indirect downstream costs.
4 Check agreement Interrater variability calculated by percent agreement.

Conclusion Signal

A conservative framework for documenting specialty pharmacy value

The poster positions clinical intervention documentation as a bridge between specialty pharmacy care activities and measurable economic value, aligning with recent NASP recommendations and accreditation documentation expectations.
