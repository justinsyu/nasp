NASP 2022 / Poster 05-2022

HealthBeacon smart Late SMS reminders were linked with higher adherence to late injections.

HealthBeacon evaluated whether a connected sharps bin plus a smart text reminder could recover doses that were already more than 24 hours late. Across 2,514 patients and 30,129 late-SMS-eligible drops, the reminder group recorded more than twice the late-dose drop rate.

Objective

Test whether a smart Late SMS improves adherence when a scheduled injection is already late.

The poster framed a "drop" into the smart sharps bin as an adherence signal. If no drop appeared 24 hours after the scheduled dose, the dose became eligible for a Late SMS. The analysis compared patients who received that smart reminder with patients who did not.

Evidence Flow

How the connected intervention turns a missed-window dose into a measurable adherence event.

Personal schedule

The ICMS tracks individualized injection schedules and expected drop timing.

Late-dose signal

A dose becomes eligible when no bin drop is detected 24 hours after its scheduled time.

Smart reminder

Group 1 patients receive a Late SMS, layered on top of the bin's due-dose blue light.

Adherence record

A later bin drop updates the medication schedule and contributes to the adherence score.

Conclusion

Smart late-dose prompts can support adherence for patients using injectable therapies.

The poster concludes that smart Late SMS reminders had a significant impact on adherence to doses that might otherwise have been missed. The analysis also notes that additional factors may contribute, but the intervention should be considered as part of adherence support for injectable medications.

  • Eligible-drop adherence was higher among patients receiving the Late SMS reminder.
  • Patients receiving the reminder were more than eight times as likely to make a drop.
  • Automated reminders paired with real-time monitoring created an actionable adherence signal.
