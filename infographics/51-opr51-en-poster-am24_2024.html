Study build

From broad chart review to a focused step-therapy cohort.

The single-center retrospective cohort included adults prescribed a specialty medication for atopic dermatitis or psoriasis when insurance required step-therapy before the specialty medication.

Objective and outcomes

The study asked where step-therapy redirected patients.

Objective

Evaluate the patient journey and outcomes after specialty medication prescribing for atopic dermatitis or psoriasis.

Primary

Count patients whose specialty medication was denied by insurance and who then failed step-therapy.

Secondary

Track non-start within 12 months, reasons, and time from first referral to second referral.

Conclusion

Step-therapy often delayed clinically appropriate treatment.

The poster concluded that 37 patients, or 60% of the analyzed cohort, failed step-therapy and returned to specialty pharmacy after a median 75 days from initial referral to second referral. In the authors' interpretation, these requirements often delayed specialty treatment for atopic dermatitis and psoriasis.
