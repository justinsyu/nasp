Background

Specialty medication prior authorizations can delay therapy, increase administrative work, and burden pharmacy and clinic teams.

Objective

Determine AI's impact on prior authorization approval rates and turnaround time in a health-system specialty pharmacy.

Methods

Prospective, single-center quality improvement study of specialty pharmacy PAs from OSUWMC providers.

Data sources

CoverMyMeds and Latent Health dashboards supplied active time, clinical time, and approval or denial status.

Conclusion

Latent Health implementation improved average approval rates and may scale administrative-burden reduction.

What changed operationally

AI sits inside an already electronic prior authorization workflow

  • OSUWMC primarily completed PAs electronically through CoverMyMeds using dedicated prior authorization coordinators.
  • Latent Health was evaluated as a tool to reduce manual workflow inefficiency, treatment delays, and team workload.
  • Included prescriptions required prior authorization from OSUWMC providers; prescriptions not requiring PA were excluded.

Poster bottom line

Scalable access support, with a measured approval signal

The poster concludes that Latent Health improved prior authorization approval rates, increasing the average from 80.2% without AI to 81.9% with AI.

The authors frame the findings as potential support for AI as a scalable health-system specialty pharmacy tool to reduce administrative burden and optimize medication access.
