Track total turnaround time from prescription arrival through patient-ready medication access.

Resolve coverage

Investigate benefits, submit test claims, coordinate prior authorization materials from the EMR, and follow up daily.

Remove cost barriers

Review drug and patient financials, then pursue grants, copay cards, LIS, or manufacturer free-drug programs.

Monitor experience

Review PDC, approval time, turnaround time, and satisfaction continually, with quarterly workflow and staffing review.

Financial toxicity

$15,000/month

The poster notes that after deductible, coinsurance, and coverage gap exposure, a Medicare Part D catastrophic 5% share can still leave a patient with a $750 monthly burden.

Access playbook

Technicians convert barriers into a daily follow-up queue.

The care model depends on persistent coordination: staff contact patients, collect financial information, work with prescribers on forms, and follow up until approval.

Objective

Show that measurement, review, and action plans improve the medication experience.

The poster asks how a specialty pharmacy can create an environment where high-touch care and service maximize experience and support adherence to complex therapies with financial and clinical toxicities.

Measure

PDC, turnaround time, approval time, and survey scores are monitored continually.

Review

Metrics are reviewed quarterly to identify workflow, staffing, or technology changes.

Act

Negative survey results trigger direct patient follow-up by pharmacy technicians.

Conclusion

Specialty pharmacies are positioned to maximize care while minimizing time to therapy.

AON Pharmacy argues that developing and monitoring metrics supports accreditation requirements and, more importantly, helps patients access complicated, costly prescriptions with an elevated service model.
