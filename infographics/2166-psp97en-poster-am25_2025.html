Objective

Assess current and expected savings from biosimilar use in North America.

Search

Targeted review of MEDLINE and grey literature from January 2020 to January 2025.

Pattern

Most sources found savings for payers; exceptions were limited to specific sites or stakeholders.

Scale

Reported savings range from targeted million-dollar programs to national billion-dollar estimates.

Decision point

Health economic decision-makers should consider policies that favor biosimilar use to control costs.

Conclusion: the literature base supports biosimilars as a cost-control lever in both Canada and the United States, with the strongest pattern showing that increased uptake and switching policies can unlock substantial payer savings.
