Poster 49 | NASP 2021 | Pediatric IBD

Adalimumab Dose Intensification in Pediatric Patients with IBD

A single-center Vanderbilt chart review evaluated how often pediatric IBD patients required adalimumab therapy changes, how long they remained on the original schedule, and how drug levels shifted after intensification.

  • Retrospective chart review
  • Adalimumab
  • Age <18 years
  • January 2008-February 2019

Victoria W. Reynolds, PharmD, BCACP; Dedrick Moulton, MD; Midya Yarwais, MS; Stacy Hawkins, NP; Amy Mitchell, PharmD, BCPPS, CSP; Josh DeClercq, MS; Leena Choi, PhD.

Objective and Methods

How the study defined the treatment-change question

Design Single-center retrospective chart review.
Population Patients younger than 18 years prescribed adalimumab in the MCCHV Pediatric IBD Program.
Exclusions No adalimumab levels, no baseline labs, non-prescribed use, or adalimumab initiated outside MCCHV.
Endpoints Frequency and time to therapy change, plus clinical characteristics before and after change.

Dose Intensification Signal

29 of 109 patients required a change in adalimumab therapy
Changed therapy No change shown for scale

Intensification Pattern

Frequency increase, with selective reinduction

Among patients requiring therapy change, 10 received reinduction before dose intensification: 3 full reinductions and 7 micro reinductions of 80 mg once.

Medication History

Cohort treatment context

Measure Pattern
Prior biologic therapy 32% (34) previously received Remicade; 68% (74) had no prior biologic therapy.
Standard adalimumab start 83% (90) received 160 mg day 1, 80 mg day 15, then 40 mg every 14 days.
Concomitant initiation therapy 27% (29) had MTX, AZA, or corticosteroids; 73% (80) had none.

Conclusion

What this poster adds

This study characterizes real-world pediatric IBD patients who required adalimumab dose intensification and points to a need for further work on clinical response, biochemical response, and proactive drug monitoring after waning or loss of response.
