Hepatitis C Treatment Outcomes: The Collaborative Roles of Pharmacists and Pharmacy Technicians

Clearway Health and NeighborHealth describe an integrated specialty pharmacy model that moves hepatitis C patients from centralized screening data through pharmacist triage, medication access work, treatment monitoring, and SVR lab coordination.

Objective

Assess whether an integrated pharmacy team improves the treatment pathway

The study focuses on hepatitis C treatment initiation, adherence, and outcomes when pharmacists and pharmacy technicians operate as a coordinated specialty pharmacy service.

Conclusion status

What the poster supports now

The source text supports a clear operational thesis: pairing pharmacist clinical triage and monitoring with technician-led access work may remove friction between positive hepatitis C testing and DAA treatment completion.

What remains to be quantified

The decisive measures are still endpoint percentages

To close the loop, the study needs the percent linked to care and percent with confirmed care, with supporting treatment initiation, adherence, and SVR outcome reporting if available in the final poster materials.
