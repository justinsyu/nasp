Objective and interpretation

The poster asks how early PARPi toxicity translates into medication changes.

Primary objective: Identify AE type and frequency, then quantify AE-related treatment holds, dose reductions, and discontinuations after PARPi initiation.

Secondary objective: Measure adherence to PARPi therapy.

  • Patients initiating olaparib, rucaparib, niraparib, or talazoparib for FDA-approved indications were included.
  • Clinical trial participation was excluded.
  • The prospective phase will evaluate the integrated specialty pharmacist role in AE mitigation, patient education, and supportive therapy.

Clinical issue

PARPi therapy can cause early adverse events that require active monitoring.

Study design

Retrospective review followed new-start patients for 90 days after initiation.

Observed burden

Every patient experienced at least one AE; fatigue, nausea, and anemia recurred across visits.

Management pattern

Holds and dose reductions were frequent, especially after the first month.

Bottom line

High adherence was maintained among patients who remained on therapy, despite modifications.
