Objective

Describe utilization, time-to-event, and medication changes after CBD start

The poster evaluated emergency healthcare service use, time to first emergency healthcare related utilization, and changes in anti-seizure medication use after the first 12 months of prescription cannabidiol therapy.

  • Population: patients prescribed CBD from January 2019 through April 2020.
  • Setting: outpatient neurology clinics at an academic medical center with an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy.
  • Follow-up: monthly check-ins until 12 months of therapy or discontinuation.

Conclusion signal

Discontinuation was not rare in year one

The poster's headline finding was that roughly one in five patients discontinued prescription CBD within the first 12 months. Side effects commonly led to discontinuation during the first 100 days, while suboptimal response appeared later in therapy.

Practice signal

Emergency use remained part of the early follow-up story

Emergency healthcare service utilization was common after prescription CBD initiation, with first ER and hospitalization medians occurring before month four among patients with events. Concurrent ASM utilization showed minimal median change by study close.
