NASP 2024 • OPR70-OR

Optimizing initiation for self-injectable medications with remote demonstration kits delivered via specialty pharmacy

An exploratory randomized pilot tested whether sending a remote demonstration kit with the first specialty pharmacy shipment could improve early self-injection behavior for patients new to self-injectable biologics.

Intervention model

Practice kit meets first dose Patients received standard care alone or standard care plus an RDK containing instructions for use, a high-fidelity demonstration device, and video support.

Objective

Can specialty pharmacy-delivered training accelerate confident self-injection?

The pilot evaluated the impact of providing self-injection RDKs through specialty pharmacy to patients naive to self-injection medication and using biologics on a 14-day dosing schedule.

Conclusion

Remote practice support was associated with faster initiation and stronger early refill behavior

  • Patients receiving the RDK took their first injection about 3.5 days sooner than patients receiving standard care alone.
  • The kit group was more likely to reach acceptable short-term adherence, measured by PDC above 80%.
  • Most kit recipients used the training device or video for the first self-injection and returned to the kit for later orientation.
  • Limitations include small sample size, missing first-injection survey dates for some patients, and a short pilot duration.
