Study design

Evidence flow from specialty pharmacy documentation to avoided-cost estimate

1

Identify cohort

Patients followed by CHOC specialty providers for CF, GH, IBD, or RIC who opted into specialty pharmacy clinical management.

2

Capture interventions

Pharmacist-documented clinical interventions were extracted from therapy management software for January 1 to December 31, 2023.

3

Classify severity

Naranjo, Nesbit, and CTCAE frameworks supported adverse event probability, impact, and care-intensity classification.

4

Estimate value

Published cost models and Pediatric Health Information System charge data were used to assign avoidable-cost estimates.

Objective

Quantify pharmacist clinical interventions and associated cost avoidance in pediatric ambulatory specialty clinics.

Secondary lens

Classify severity, adverse drug event prevention, and estimated pharmacist time spent.

Methods

Retrospective review of documented interventions in TherigySTM for CHOC specialty pharmacy patients.

Signal

149 of 312 interventions had quantifiable cost-avoidance implications.

Conclusion

Documented intervention value can support specialty pharmacy service expansion, while models and review committees can reduce subjectivity.

Poster caveat

Unpriced work still mattered

The poster notes that coordination of care, drug shortage management, and similar activities often could not be assigned a cost-avoidance value.

  • Coordination of care was described as a large share of clinical work without assigned avoided cost.
  • Estimated value varied by protocols, practice area, and contemporary challenges such as shortages and recalls.
  • Five adverse drug events, representing 16% of ADEs, were reported to MedWatch because of high severity.
