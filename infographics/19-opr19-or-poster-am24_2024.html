A Health-System Specialty Pharmacy Approach to Optimize Medication Regimens Among Patients Diagnosed with Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus

Seven health-system specialty pharmacies used data analytics, pharmacist review, shared decision-making, and electronic health record recommendations to identify insulin-treated adults with T2DM who might benefit from SGLT2 inhibitor, GLP-1 RA, or GIP/GLP-1 RA add-on therapy.

Initiative framework

How the HSSP model turned data into therapy access

Identify

Use data analytics tools to find patients with T2DM who may have a treatment gap.

Review and reach out

Pharmacists review profiles, contact patients, and determine clinical appropriateness.

Recommend

If appropriate, the pharmacist sends an add-on therapy recommendation through the EHR.

Facilitate access

For accepted interventions, the HSSP supports medication access and initiation.

Extend follow-up

Future evaluation may assess 6- to 12-month clinical outcomes such as A1C change.

Conclusion

Embedded pharmacists can close therapy gaps

Gaps in guideline-directed prescribing remained for patients with T2DM, and the HSSP model positioned pharmacists to recommend patient-specific add-on therapy within multidisciplinary care.

The 71% prescriber acceptance rate was the central outcome signal supporting pharmacist integration into outpatient clinical teams.

Limitations and next question

What the poster could not yet answer

  • Medication-selection rationale and patient out-of-pocket costs were not analyzed.
  • The review window was short, limiting pharmacist review for 61 identified patients.
  • Future work is planned for broader T2DM populations and 6- to 12-month outcome follow-up.
