Poster 66 | 2023 | IBD biologic adherence

Specialty pharmacy patients showed higher biologic adherence than a national claims benchmark.

This retrospective claims analysis compared biologic medication adherence among adults with ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease using 2021 specialty pharmacy data and the 2021 MarketScan commercial claims database.

Objective

Compare biologic adherence against a national claims benchmark.

The poster asks whether adults with ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease receiving biologics through a large national specialty pharmacy demonstrated different adherence than comparable patients in MarketScan.

Evidence flow

Retrospective claims design filtered to patients with enough fill history to calculate PDC.

1

Data sources

Large national specialty pharmacy claims and MarketScan commercial claims from January to December 2021.

2

Population

Adults with Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis who were prescribed a biologic medication.

3

PDC criteria

Patients needed at least two fills, more than 56 days' supply, and more than 150 days between first and last fill.

4

Analysis

Adherence was defined as PDC above 80%; t tests and chi-square tests used p < .05 for significance.

Condition pattern

Crohn's and UC moved in the same direction.

Result

Full sample favored specialty pharmacy.

Both mean PDC and adherent share were significantly higher than the MarketScan benchmark.

Crohn's disease

Two significant uplifts.

PDC and adherence each showed higher specialty pharmacy values with p < .01.

Ulcerative colitis

PDC signal remained strong.

Mean PDC was higher with p < .01; adherence was directionally higher at p = .05.

Interpretation

Pharmacy support may matter.

Authors suggest pharmacist and pharmacy team involvement may be positively correlated with adherence.

Next step

More subpopulation research.

Additional IBD biologic adherence studies could clarify links to health outcomes and targeted strategies.

Conclusion

Higher adherence was observed in specialty pharmacy claims.

Across the main PDC-eligible population, specialty pharmacy patients using biologic therapies for ulcerative colitis or Crohn's disease had higher adherence levels than patients in MarketScan.

Read with context

The commercial-only cut tempers the headline.

When the specialty pharmacy sample was restricted to commercial-only patients, Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis differences were directionally favorable but did not reach statistical significance.
