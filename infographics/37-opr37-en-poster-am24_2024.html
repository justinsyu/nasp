  • Vomiting and swallowing difficulty were common documented reasons for missed doses.
  • The only patient who did not achieve SVR reported 13 missed doses.
  • Potential DDIs involved acid-reducing agents, herbal supplements, and oxcarbazepine.
  • Pharmacist management included timing adjustment, dose/timing adjustment, or discontinuing the non-DAA.

Objective

Evaluate DAA efficacy for pediatric chronic hepatitis C patients using an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy model.

Methods

Single-center retrospective cohort of patients younger than 18 years referred for DAA initiation from January 2017 to September 2022.

Primary result

Overall SVR was 92%, with highest observed response in the 6-11 year subgroup.

Operational signal

Medication swallowing readiness required substantial pharmacist contact before some children could initiate treatment.

Conclusion

The HSSP model supported high SVR rates, manageable adherence barriers, and pharmacist-managed drug interactions.
