Objective

Optimize implementation and analysis of a patient satisfaction survey in specialty pharmacy.

The poster positions patient satisfaction measurement as an accreditation-driven quality improvement need, with the survey designed to be short, convenient to administer, and numerically actionable.

Methods

The analysis moved from survey deployment to completion-rate measurement.

Build PSAT

Therigy created a 7-item patient satisfaction survey across satisfaction, communication, and advocacy domains.

Deploy channels

Surveys were completed in office, over the phone, or through text to a patient's mobile device.

Select period

Generated surveys were reviewed from March 2020 through June 2021.

Apply criteria

Included pharmacies had been established in the Therigy system for at least one year.

Analyze use

TherigyInsights data were used to observe completion rate and mobile-enabled survey use.

Results

A broad deployment produced usable satisfaction data, but most generated activities remained incomplete.

  • 51 specialty pharmacies implemented the PSAT survey.
  • 5,539 survey activities were generated for 1,983 patients.
  • 2,376 activities were completed with a valid score.
  • 522 completed surveys were submitted from a patient's mobile device.

Interpretation

The adoption signal was strong; the participation signal was the next improvement target.

Completion reached 43%, and mobile texting contributed 22% of completions. The authors identified additional opportunity for wider pharmacy adoption and increased patient participation.

Conclusion: the 7-item Therigy PSAT survey offered specialty pharmacies a feasible, metric-driven way to measure patient satisfaction, with future work focused on broader adoption, higher response rates, and turnaround-time analysis.
