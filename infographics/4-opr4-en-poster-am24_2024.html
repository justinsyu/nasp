Objective

Describe HSSP impact on immunization screening rates and outcomes before self-administered immune modulating therapies.

Design

Prospective, observational, multi-site cohort using de-identified aggregated site data in REDCap.

Eligibility

Adults prescribed a qualifying immune modulating specialty medication for dermatology, IBD, or rheumatology from March 1 to May 31, 2023.

Finding

High intervention rates suggest vaccine gaps are common immediately before therapy initiation.

Conclusion

Screening plus vaccine administration under collaborative practice agreements may be an underused HSSP mechanism.

Poster interpretation

Operational takeaway

The study found a large screening-to-intervention signal, but only a small subset of vaccine services occurred under collaborative practice agreements.

  • Communication to patients or clinic staff was far more common than pharmacist-led ordering or administration.
  • No interventions were associated with HPV, MMR, or meningococcal vaccines in the extracted results.
  • The authors identify direct vaccine administration as a potential efficiency lever for safer therapy initiation.
