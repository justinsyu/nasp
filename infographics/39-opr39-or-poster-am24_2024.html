Implementation of the PozQoL in a specialty pharmacy setting

A two-site quality improvement pilot tested whether specialty pharmacists could use PozQoL responses to identify quality-of-life needs in people with HIV and convert those signals into targeted, accepted interventions.

Objective and methods

Measurement embedded in routine specialty pharmacy care

The pilot evaluated a quality improvement initiative implemented from July 2023 through April 2024. PozQoL was administered to eligible adults with HIV who were clinically managed by a specialty pharmacist.

Any low or moderate domain score could trigger a pharmacist intervention based on clinical judgment, followed by documentation of the action, recommendation, outcome, level of healthcare impact, and pharmacist time.

Conclusion

QoL data helped direct pharmacist support.

This pilot demonstrated that specialty pharmacists could use PozQoL information to identify unmet quality-of-life needs, make targeted recommendations, and achieve high acceptance from patients and care teams.
