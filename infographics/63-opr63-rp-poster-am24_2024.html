Multiple Interventions to Reduce Delivery Costs in a Health System Pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health's integrated health system specialty pharmacy paired staff engagement, logistics changes, and invoice auditing to shift delivery volume away from same-day courier service toward commercial overnight shipping.

Central insight

Same access, lower-cost route The project did not remove home delivery. It redirected eligible deliveries to the less expensive channel while monitoring loss, payer, and cold-chain risks.

Objective

Two operating goals guided the work

  • Convert enough same-day courier deliveries to commercial overnight carrier to reach an even split of both delivery methods.
  • Reduce average delivery cost by 25% by implementing strategies and monitoring expenses monthly before and after the initiative.
  • Use recurring review to keep savings from creating new cold-chain, failed-delivery, or payer-signature problems.

Intervention flow

What changed in the delivery system

Apr 2023

Employee rewards

Top performers received a six-month incentive program to reinforce commercial overnight selection.

Nov 2023

Longer windows

Short-turnaround courier deliveries shifted from two-hour to four-hour windows, with evening coverage expanded to 5 PM-9 PM.

Nov 2023

Invoice audits

Courier invoices were monitored to verify charges and surface preventable expense.

Feb 2024

Targeted coaching

Employees choosing courier for more than 30% of deliveries had individual barrier-resolution conversations.

Mar 2024

User-level spend review

Overnight shipping settings were reviewed, revealing $2-$4 savings opportunities per shipment for some users.

Conclusion

Staff engagement plus monitoring changed behavior

The IHSSP team trended toward the 50% commercial overnight goal while maintaining regular follow-up on failed deliveries, payer signature requirements, cold-chain risk, and shipping errors.

Next move

Hard stops for cold shipments

Future IT work was planned to prevent commercial overnight shipping for cold shipments when payer requirements or patient availability make signature failure and product loss more likely.
