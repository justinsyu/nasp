Improving Adherence in Patients Taking Cycle-directed Oral Anti-cancer Medications

A single-center quality-improvement study at Mizzou Specialty Pharmacy tested whether refill-specific treatment calendars could help patients stay aligned with complex oral anti-cancer medication cycles.

Evidence flow

How the adherence aid moved through care

1

Identify eligible cycles

Adults filling at least three cycles of selected OAMs through Mizzou Specialty Pharmacy were included.

2

Provide calendar

Each refill included a treatment calendar with start/stop dates and dose reminder check boxes.

3

Document in EMR

Calendars were scanned into the electronic medical record for provider reference.

4

Assess adherence

Staff used patient recollection, cycle-date confirmation, and pill counts at assessment.

5

Capture feedback

Patients reported whether the calendars were helpful and how they used them.

Objective

Improve adherence for complex cycle-directed regimens

  • Targeted medications included Ibrance, Inqovi, Kisqali, Lonsurf, Temodar, and Xeloda.
  • The project paired pharmacy refill assessments with one-on-one counseling and follow-up monitoring.
  • Calendars were designed to make on-treatment days, off-treatment days, and next-cycle timing visible.

Conclusion

Calendars supported most patients

  • Adherence was achieved in the majority of patients who used treatment calendars.
  • Fear of side effects and forgetting were the top reasons for non-adherence.
  • Next steps were to expand calendars across all cycle-directed therapies and deepen counseling around calendar use.

Eligibility frame

Who entered the review

Included

  • Age >= 18 years
  • Prescribed one of six selected cycle-directed OAMs
  • At least three refills through Mizzou Specialty Pharmacy

Excluded

  • Pregnant
  • Prisoner
  • Medication received for fewer than three refills or cycles during the 12-month period
