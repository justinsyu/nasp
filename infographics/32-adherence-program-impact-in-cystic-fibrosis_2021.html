Adherence Program Impact in Cystic Fibrosis

AcariaHealth evaluated a pharmacist-led specialty pharmacy adherence program for patients taking CFTR modulator therapy who were at least seven days overdue for refill.

Objective

Day 35

Find overdue CFTRm refills and intervene before gaps harden into therapy disruption.

The primary objective was to determine the percent of referrals scheduled after telephonic pharmacist intervention among patients identified as high risk for non-adherence.

Adherence outreach timeline

The program escalated from routine specialty pharmacy cadence to pharmacist intervention after the refill became overdue.

Therapy start

The refill clock began when CFTR modulator therapy was initiated or scheduled from the study site.

Standard outreach

Specialty pharmacy refill outreach began one week before the expected refill due date.

Refill due

The anticipated refill date was used as the benchmark for identifying late refills.

Clinical intervention

At seven days overdue, a pharmacist completed chart assessment and called the prescriber, patient, or both.

Referral status

Most eligible referrals were scheduled by the data extraction date.

Methods pattern

Inclusion

Results

Clinical escalation helped convert overdue referrals into scheduled refills.

  • 949 referrals met inclusion criteria across CFTRm therapies.
  • 711 referrals were scheduled, while 147 remained active and 91 were closed.
  • 289 scheduled referrals followed pharmacist consultation with a prescriber.
  • 230 patients required more than one intervention during the study period.

Conclusion

Prescriber collaboration was a key mechanism for closing refill gaps.

Integration of pharmacist-led adherence interventions helped mitigate gaps in CFTRm therapy, and direct collaboration with prescriber offices was described as successful for refill scheduling.
