Appeal Approval Rating of Specialty Pharmacists

University Hospitals Specialty Pharmacy evaluated pharmacist-written prior authorization appeals to quantify how often denied specialty medications became approved, how quickly approvals occurred, and whether patients ultimately gained access to therapy.

Objective and methods

Quantify pharmacist impact after PA denial.

The objective was to determine the appeal approval rating of UHSP clinical specialty pharmacists and describe the direct impact on medication access and patient outcomes.

The IRB-approved retrospective chart review used completed appeal-related tasks from the internal clinical monitoring program during January-December 2021.

Interpretation

Clinical expertise translated policy denials into access decisions.

  • Most denials clustered around formulary status, off-label use, qualifying criteria, and step therapy.
  • First-level pharmacist appeals drove most of the documented approval volume.
  • Template standardization and improved documentation were the poster's main future opportunities.

Bottom line

Pharmacist-led appeals were associated with rapid approvals and high medication access.

The poster concludes that appeals written by UHSP clinical specialty pharmacists were often approved with quick turnaround from denial to approval, helping patients gain access to specialty medication despite persistent PA and insurance barriers.

Future work included an appeal letter template, technician support for non-clinical appeal sections, a documentation quality improvement project, and Epic implementation.
