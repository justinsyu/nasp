Evaluating Specialty Pharmacist Impact on Failed Adalimumab Biosimilar Transitions

CHRISTUS Specialty Pharmacy reviewed real-world adalimumab biosimilar switches in rheumatology care and found that specialty pharmacists identified more than half of failed biosimilar initiations.

Evidence flow

Where specialty pharmacist monitoring changed the transition pathway

1

Formulary or biosimilar switch

Adalimumab biosimilar changes created multiple products, access hurdles, and patient confusion risk.

2

Early monitoring window

Health-system specialty pharmacists can detect flares before the next specialist visit.

3

Failure signal identified

Reported signals included clinical failure, flares, steroid prescriptions, adverse events, and lost stability.

4

Therapy redirected

After failure, patients moved to another biosimilar, the reference product, or a different therapeutic class.

Objective

Evaluate unsuccessful biosimilar adoptions.

The study aimed to evaluate the impact of specialty pharmacists on identifying and addressing unsuccessful adalimumab biosimilar adoptions and to explore related prescription characteristics.

Methods

Retrospective descriptive review.

Investigators reviewed adalimumab biosimilar switches for patients treated for rheumatology conditions at a CHRISTUS clinic. Biosimilar prescriptions and clinical data were collected from the electronic medical record.

Conclusion

Specialty pharmacist monitoring surfaced failures during biosimilar transition.

The poster concludes that specialty pharmacists are important in monitoring biosimilar transitions and identifying therapy failures. The results also support the need for more real-world evidence on biosimilar effectiveness and continued development of specialty pharmacist-driven biosimilar uptake and monitoring programs.
