Poster PSP97-OR | AM24 | 2024

The Impact of an Ambulatory Clinical Pharmacist-led Obesity Management Program to Improve Weight Loss Goals Within an Integrated Health-System Specialty Pharmacy

NYU Langone Health and Shields Health Solutions evaluated whether specialty pharmacy patients receiving GLP-1RA obesity therapy had measurable weight loss, accepted pharmacist interventions, and lower modeled annual medical expenditures after ambulatory clinical pharmacist support.

Objective

Can pharmacist-led GLP-1RA management move obesity outcomes?

The study assessed the impact of an ambulatory clinical pharmacist-led obesity management program within an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy on weight-loss goal attainment, healthcare cost savings, and pharmacist intervention activity.

Program signal

Weight loss plus cost impact Patients had at least two pharmacist encounters at least 30 days apart and documented baseline plus follow-up weight.

Methods

Evidence flow from prescription fill to modeled savings

1

Identify eligible adults

NYU Langone clinic patients age 18 or older filling obesity prescriptions for liraglutide, semaglutide, or tirzepatide.

2

Require pharmacist exposure

Patients needed at least two ACP encounters separated by at least 30 days.

3

Measure weight change

Analysis required baseline weight plus at least one subsequent weight collected after program start.

4

Summarize outcomes

Descriptive statistics captured weight-loss thresholds, interventions, and modeled annual savings from BMI reduction.

Conclusion

ACP involvement supported clinical and cost outcomes despite GLP-1RA backorders

The poster concludes that the ACP-led obesity management program demonstrated beneficial impact on weight-loss outcomes and reduced annual healthcare costs while supporting GLP-1RA use in an integrated HSSP model.
