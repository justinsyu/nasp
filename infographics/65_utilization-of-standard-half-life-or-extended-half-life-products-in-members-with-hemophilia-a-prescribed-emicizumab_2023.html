Factor fills remained common after emicizumab prophylaxis in Hemophilia A

A national payer cohort examined adult males with Hemophilia A prescribed Hemlibra® and compared post-emicizumab factor utilization, specialty pharmacy dispenses, and annualized bleeding rates across standard half-life and extended half-life factor products.

Evidence Flow

How to read the poster signal

Prophylaxis standard

Hemlibra® reduces bleeding events, but factor availability remains important for breakthrough bleed self-management.

Claims-defined cohort

Adult male members required at least 6 months of Hemlibra® use and continuous post-dose plan enrollment.

Factor fill split

Most factor utilizers filled SHL products, creating a 27-member SHL group and 11-member EHL group.

Dispenses similar

Mean SP dispenses did not differ significantly between EHL and SHL users: 3.0 vs 1.9, p=0.5.

Interpret cautiously

The observed SHL/EHL ABR difference is limited by small sample size and potential underlying confounding.

Conclusion

Key take-away

When prescribed Hemlibra®, most members filled a factor product and had low overall ABR; SHL utilizers had higher observed ABR than EHL utilizers, but interpretation should account for limited sample size and possible confounding.
