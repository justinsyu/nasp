Impact of a Pharmacist-Driven Appeals Process in an Inflammatory Bowel Disease Clinic

A clinic-embedded pharmacist took ownership of prior authorization appeals for self-administered biologics and oral small molecules, moving the process from shared provider/nurse duties to a dedicated medication-access workflow.

Interpretation

Operational gains without observed utilization shift

  • Pharmacist ownership improved appeal timeliness, submission rate, and approval outcomes.
  • Steroid initiation was not significantly different: 9.3% pre vs 11.8% post.
  • Healthcare utilization was not significantly different: 17.6% pre vs 16.3% post.
  • The authors note appeal submission was timely in both periods, which may explain the lack of downstream utilization difference.

Objective and methods

What was evaluated

Objective: evaluate the impact of a pharmacist-driven appeals process in an IBD clinic.

Population: adult patients with at least one self-administered biologic or oral small molecule prescription requiring prior authorization at the AHWFB IBD Clinic.

Time windows: April 1, 2018 to September 30, 2019 and January 1, 2020 to June 30, 2021.

Conclusion

What the poster concluded

Ownership of the appeals process by a clinic-embedded pharmacist positively impacted appeal outcomes.

Pharmacists can apply clinical knowledge and medication-access expertise to manage the appeals process effectively and efficiently.
