Impact of a specialty pharmacy clinical program on medical outcomes and costs in patients with cancer

A retrospective Medicare Part D analysis compared oncology patients using CenterWell Specialty Pharmacy's Oncology Center of Excellence with patients using non-affiliated pharmacies, then isolated the added value of engagement with dedicated clinicians.

Program Logic

High-touch oncology support is the active ingredient

The poster frames specialty pharmacy as more than fulfillment: the Oncology Center of Excellence layers disease management, side effect management, adherence coaching, social determinants review, and operational processes around cancer medication use.

Mediation Finding

Engagement carried the cost effect

Conclusion

Clinical pharmacy programs can reduce negative health events and overall medical costs in oncology care.

The largest cost reduction appeared among patients who actively engaged with the Oncology Center of Excellence.

Important limitation

The study could not account for clinical programming and engagement offered by non-affiliated pharmacies.

Next research questions

Identify drivers of Oncology Center of Excellence engagement, determine which cancer medications benefit most from clinical intensity, and extend the analysis to other specialty disease states.
