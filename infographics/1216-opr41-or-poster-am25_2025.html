NASP 2025 | OPR41-OR | Oncology specialty pharmacy

Embedded clinic pharmacists changed the pattern of HSSP interventions.

In a three-month retrospective chart review at WVU Medicine Specialty Pharmacy, oncology clinics without an embedded pharmacist had the highest share of patients needing health-system specialty pharmacy pharmacist interventions.

Conclusion Prescription volume alone undercounts specialty pharmacy workload.

Interpretation

  • Clinics without embedded pharmacists had the highest intervention proportion, suggesting a care coordination gap that centralized HSSP pharmacists help absorb.
  • Interventions were more variable than initial and follow-up care plan work, making interventions per patient a practical workload signal across staffing models.
  • The three-month analysis was preliminary; the poster calls for longer follow-up, disease-area expansion, time and complexity assessment, and cost analysis.
