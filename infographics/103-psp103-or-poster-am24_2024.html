Medication Assistance Renewal Process Optimization at an Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health specialty pharmacy redesigned its medication assistance program renewal season by separating re-enrollment work from new-patient enrollment, allowing the team to manage a larger annual renewal load while continuing intake for patients newly seeking assistance.

Objective

Make renewal season faster without closing the front door

The poster objective was to optimize the MAP workflow so the year-end renewal process could improve while the health-system specialty pharmacy continued serving new patients seeking medication assistance.

  • MAP support covers grant or foundation assistance and free drug applications for patients unable to afford medication.
  • Grant and foundation assistance was preferred because funding counts toward patients' out-of-pocket maximums.
  • Medicare out-of-pocket maximum changes in 2024 prompted a reassessment of the renewal workflow.

Core move

Split the queue Dedicated renewal staff handled existing MAP patients while a separate team protected capacity for new enrollments.

Methods

A five-step operating model turned annual renewal into parallel workstreams

1

Brainstorm

MAP team members met in July 2023 to redesign the re-enrollment process.

2

Split teams

MART handled re-enrollments while MAPT handled all new patient enrollments.

3

Pull report

The clinical management platform generated the list of patients needing renewal.

4

Segment

Excel was used to organize patients by disease state for faster grant enrollment.

5

Execute

Renewal and new-enrollment queues moved at the same time during peak season.

Team design

Renewals became a dedicated lane

Discussion pattern

Diagnosis-based sorting made fast grant access the operational lever

  • Grant applications close quickly after opening, making speed and disease-state organization important.
  • Dividing patients by diagnosis helped the team pursue grant applications more efficiently.
  • Grant assistance supports continuity of care, specialty pharmacy prescription volume, and revenue retention.
  • The clinical documentation system supported date and outcome tracking, which the authors noted may limit generalizability to other health systems.
2 lanes one renewal engine, one new-patient intake lane

The authors concluded that the two-team approach improved year-end renewal processing, converted some patients from free drug assistance to grant assistance, retained prescriptions within the health-system specialty pharmacy, and supported continuity of care.

Future direction

Next operating pressure: 2025 Medicare changes

The poster points to adapting the workflow for 2025 Medicare out-of-pocket maximum changes and expanding need-based assistance for all patients using the health-system specialty pharmacy.

  • Renewal structure can be reused as policy rules shift.
  • Need-based assistance may broaden beyond the annual renewal cohort.
  • Documentation fidelity remains central to measuring task dates and outcomes.
