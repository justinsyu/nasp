Enhancing asthma management with ACT screening and pharmacist follow-up

Yale New Haven Health specialty pharmacists used Asthma Control Test screening in an integrated health system specialty pharmacy to identify control status, adherence patterns, and barriers among patients filling asthma biologics.

Objective

Use ACT scores to evaluate asthma control in patients taking biologics.

The stated goal was to optimize therapeutic strategies by combining ACT screening, adherence assessment, and pharmacist review of barriers in an integrated specialty pharmacy program.

Patient flow

Retrospective review from May 1, 2023 to March 31, 2024.

Pharmacist follow-up signal

Clinical management was associated with ACT score improvement.

50% of intervention patients improved ACT scores after pharmacist interventions.

The poster discussion links improvement with frequent clinical management and intervention by specialty pharmacists.

Social drivers of health

No social drivers: 13 Social drivers: 9

Identified drivers included language, finance, transportation, and tobacco use.

Background need

Medication adherence in asthma is associated with better disease control and health outcomes.

Documentation shift

A May 2023 platform transition improved reporting visibility and clinical management.

ACT screen

Pharmacists categorized asthma control using ACT thresholds and reviewed very poor control for barriers.

Intervention

Counseling centered on adherence, appropriate biologic use, inhaler technique, and injection technique.

PMP opportunity

The authors recommend incorporating ACT scores into the patient management program follow-up cadence.

Conclusion

ACT-informed pharmacist interventions can improve asthma control.

The poster concludes that specialty clinical pharmacist interventions can improve ACT scores, but closer monitoring and follow-up are warranted by incorporating ACT scores into the IHSSP patient management program.

Future direction

Turn ACT status into follow-up cadence.

  • Evaluate whether ACT score should determine clinical assessment follow-up timing.
  • Analyze adherence using missed-dose reports collected during technician refill outreach.
  • Continue reviewing adherence, biologic use, technique, and written asthma action plans during interventions.
