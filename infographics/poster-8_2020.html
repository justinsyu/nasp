NASP 2020 Poster 8

Digital technology: An effective communication tool for pharmacy staff during COVID-19

Yale New Haven Health used a text-message engagement platform to deliver rapid, network-independent COVID-19 pharmacy updates to nearly 900 employees, then monitored delivery, click, and action patterns across 11 weekly messages.

Communication architecture

The campaign turned pandemic updates into a closed feedback loop.

1 Consolidate

Incident Command Pharmacy communication team organized fast-changing COVID-19 messages.

2 Deliver

Texts reached staff on and off the health system network, supplementing email and manager updates.

3 Engage

Hyperlinks opened video messages, FAQs, safety updates, operations guidance, and resource links.

4 Route back

Post-message analytics were shared with section chiefs and then with all employees.

Objective

Evaluate the utility of digital technology for pharmacy staff communication during COVID-19.

Methods

Partnered with a mobile engagement firm; required a phone master list and platform training.

Content

Initial executive video message was followed by weekly FAQs, safety, drug policy, operations, staffing, and wellness updates.

Measurement

Delivered count, clicked count, and actioned click count were collected for each message.

Interpretation

Click-through exceeded a cited 19% SMS average, but the authors flagged the remaining no-click population for study.

Conclusion

Text-based digital messaging was successful and well received by pharmacy staff.

The authors concluded that digital technology helped reach a high number of staff compared with traditional methods and was versatile enough to deliver updates in different formats.

They also recommended evaluating reasons behind non-clicks, including message timing, preference, privacy concerns, and possible confusion with telemarketing spam.

Future direction

The communication platform could extend beyond pandemic updates.

Suggested next uses included staffing notifications to find replacements and rapid information dissemination during disaster-response scenarios.

A transformation survey ranked digital messages third in the COVID-19 communication strategy, behind direct manager communication and weekly executive presentation.
