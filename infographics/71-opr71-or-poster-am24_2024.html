Real-World Evaluation of Oncolytic Biosimilar Adoption on Medication Adherence and Out-of-Pocket Costs

A national payor claims analysis compared adult members receiving oncology biosimilars with those receiving reference branded products, focusing on treatment duration, adherence, and member out-of-pocket cost.

Included adult commercial fully-insured and Medicare Advantage members with at least one physician-administered oncology biosimilar or reference product claim.

Core interpretation

Lower OOP, higher monthly fills Biosimilar recipients had more claims per month and markedly lower out-of-pocket spend, while reference branded products were used more often and had longer observed time on therapy.

Evidence flow

How the poster framed the real-world comparison

1

Policy question

Can biosimilar adoption expand access while preserving or improving member-centric outcomes?

2

Claims cohort

Adult members with oncology biosimilar or reference branded product claims during the 24-month window.

3

Comparative metrics

Claims, claims per month, time on therapy, and OOP costs were compared using t-tests or chi-squared tests.

4

Observed result

Biosimilars had higher adherence measures and lower OOP costs, but reference products were prescribed more often.

Objective

Compare treatment experience and cost

To compare treatment duration, adherence, and out-of-pocket costs between individuals receiving oncology biosimilars and those receiving oncology reference branded products.

Conclusion

Biosimilar outcomes looked member-favorable

The evaluation found shorter observed time on therapy, higher adherence, and lower OOP spend among individuals receiving oncology biosimilars, even though biosimilars were prescribed less often than reference branded products.
