Health-System Specialty Pharmacy Role in Managing Medications for Weight Loss in Patients Without Evidence of Type 2 Diabetes

A 12-health-system descriptive evaluation of an HSSP-led chronic disease management program for patients prescribed FDA-approved weight-management therapies, including GLP-1 and dual GIP/GLP-1 receptor agonists.

Objective: map HSSP support from referral to sustained therapy

The program described a high-touch pharmacy model for patients without evidence of type 2 diabetes who were prescribed weight-loss medication as part of a comprehensive plan.

1,646 prescribed targeted therapy
671 active on therapy
540 initiated in 2024

Program Design

Five-step HSSP care pathway

1

Referral

HSSP received targeted weight-loss medication referrals, including GLP-1 and dual GIP/GLP-1 agents.

2

Coverage

If patients filled with HSSP, teams navigated benefits investigation, PAs, appeals, and financial assistance.

3

Standard support

Patients were monitored for medication-related issues, including adherence concerns and side effects.

4

Clinical support

Additional counseling sessions and more frequent monitoring were offered to patients needing more support.

5

Evaluation

Enrollment, financial, and clinical outcomes were summarized using descriptive statistics.

Discussion and Conclusion

HSSP teams provided integral coverage, financial, adherence, and monitoring support

The poster concluded that the HSSP model helped patients navigate prior authorizations and financial assistance quickly, maintain high adherence, and receive monitoring for medication-related problems or therapy discontinuation while pursuing weight-loss goals within a comprehensive management plan.
