Extended vs Standard Half-life Factor VIII Therapy in Hemophilia A Prophylaxis

A specialty pharmacy retrospective study compared extended half-life (EHL) and standard half-life (SHL) FVIII products across bleed control, safety signals, factor utilization, treatment cost, and patient copay burden.

Objective

Direct real-world comparison of FVIII prophylaxis choices

The poster evaluated efficacy, safety, cost, and factor utilization for patients with hemophilia A receiving an FDA-approved SHL or EHL FVIII product as prophylactic therapy.

Data came from dispensing software, clinical patient management applications, and notes from hemophilia treatment centers, physicians, or home infusion nurses.

Conclusion

Better bleed control came with payer-facing cost tradeoffs

EHL products provided greater control over bleeds and similar factor utilization, but their average wholesale price was higher and quality-of-life outcomes were not improved. The poster emphasized individualized treatment, ongoing monitoring, and product selection, especially because Eloctate and Adynovate showed different ABR and AWP patterns when compared separately with SHL products.
