ALK+ mNSCLC therapy use through the specialty pharmacy lens

A retrospective, multi-center analysis followed adults receiving ALK tyrosine kinase inhibitors from October 2017 through April 2025, summarizing treatment-line use, time to discontinuation, and adherence across ALK inhibitor generations.

Objective

Describe utilization and persistence with ALK TKIs

The poster asked how patients receiving ALK TKIs for ALK+ metastatic non-small cell lung cancer move across treatment lines, how long therapy continues before discontinuation, and whether adherence remains high during specialty pharmacy management.

Oct 2017 Apr 2025

Patient identification

From pharmacy fills to treatment episodes

Include

Adults enrolled in integrated health system specialty pharmacy services with at least 3 qualifying ALK TKI fills after the relevant first-line approval date.

Classify

Fill records were mapped to first, second, and third line therapy, with extracted demographics, dates, insurance type, and region.

Measure

Time to discontinuation was days between first and last qualifying fill before a gap of 90 days or more; adherence used adjusted PDC.

Conclusion

Later lines showed shorter persistence, while adherence remained strong

  • Mean time to discontinuation generally decreased as treatment line increased.
  • Most patients did not move to a subsequent treatment line after first-line therapy.
  • Future analysis was recommended to evaluate utilization of third-generation ALK TKIs after first-line approval.

Context

Why this pattern matters

Guidelines recommend newer-generation ALK TKIs in first-line ALK+ mNSCLC because they offer the longest expected benefit, but clinical deterioration can limit later-line therapy. This pharmacy-fill analysis visualizes that attrition while showing consistently high adjusted PDC among patients managed by the specialty pharmacy service.
