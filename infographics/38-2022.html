NASP 2022 / Poster 38-2022

Communities of Practice turned specialty pharmacy change into structured learning.

Shields Health Solutions described a disease-state Community of Practice model that connects specialty pharmacy liaisons, patient support advocates, pharmacists, nurses, trainers, managers, and operations teams through recurring education and leadership roles.

Objective

Keep clinical operations current as specialty pharmacy changes.

The poster frames Communities of Practice as a continuous learning system for specialty teams facing new medications, disease states, guidelines, payer policies, and manufacturer resources.

CoP Lead CPhT stretch assignment guiding the disease-state community.
Training Specialist CPhT role translating topic needs into education.
PSA Partner CPhT view into financial assistance and patient support workflows.

Methods

Topics moved from operational signals into recurring education.

Observe

Leadership teams used observations, recommendations, and business analytics reports to select topics.

Convene

Monthly meetings and quarterly webinars created predictable learning touchpoints.

Specialize

Education focused on disease-state operations, patient outcomes, workflow efficiency, and manufacturer criteria.

Connect

Guest speakers and peer connections expanded learning beyond a single local team.

Improve

The model targeted PA approval, patient affordability, and medication refill coordination.

Results

Prior authorization approval increased in every supported disease-state community.

Average PA approval increase 27%

Patient affordability

Copays were maintained or reduced after CoP implementation.

Despite manufacturer, foundation, payer, and outside-pharmacy challenges, the poster reports stable or lower out-of-pocket costs in CoP-supported disease states.

Conclusion

The CoP became both a learning platform and a leadership pathway.

Shields concluded that Communities of Practice enhanced employee disease-state knowledge, promoted professional development, and created a cross-role network aimed at improved patient outcomes.
