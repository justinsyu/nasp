The poster defines full immunization as two RZV doses at least two months apart.

Intervention model

Specialty pharmacists used routine patient touchpoints to close RZV gaps

1

Find eligible patients

Adults age 19 or older with HIV on antiretroviral therapy or cancer treated with oral chemotherapy/supportive medications.

2

Check immunization status

Clinical and operations specialty pharmacists reviewed whether zero, one, or two RZV doses were documented.

3

Recommend needed doses

Pharmacists educated patients by telephone during clinical assessments or during prescription pickup.

4

Reclassify outcomes

Patients were counted as partially or fully immunized when documentation showed one dose or two appropriately spaced doses.

Objective

Evaluate the impact of specialty pharmacist education on herpes zoster vaccination rates in immunocompromised patients.

Background

A prior medication-use evaluation found only 0.74% of more than 7,300 eligible adults were fully immunized with RZV.

Methods

Patients with zero or one documented RZV dose received pharmacist recommendations for sufficient doses to become fully immunized.

Signal

All six new full immunizations occurred after clinical specialty pharmacist intervention; operations outreach produced no RZV immunizations.

Conclusion

The intervention had a modest positive impact, and longer operations follow-up may be needed to assess that workflow.

Discussion caveats

What tempers the interpretation

  • Immunization status reporting was inconsistent, which may have affected outcome capture.
  • The operations intervention was much shorter than the clinical intervention, at three weeks versus 24 weeks.
  • Because half of immunizations occurred in provider offices, the causal driver of those doses could not be isolated.
