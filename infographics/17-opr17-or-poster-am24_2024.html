Big Interventions for Little Patients

A retrospective, multicenter review of pediatric pharmacist interventions in health-system specialty pharmacies found frequent medication-related actions across a vulnerable population, with near-universal acceptance when recommendations required a response.

  • Pediatric specialty pharmacy
  • Health-system specialty pharmacy
  • Retrospective descriptive study
  • January 2019 to January 2024

Objective

Describe pediatric pharmacist interventions inside HSSP care.

  • Pediatric patients were less than 18 years old and enrolled in health-system specialty pharmacy services.
  • Interventions were documented in the patient management system by type, recommendation, and response status.
  • The analysis used descriptive statistics and calculated acceptance among interventions requiring a response.

Conclusion

High-touch pediatric specialty pharmacy created many points for useful clinical correction.

  • The HSSP model produced meaningful interventions related to therapy adjustment, medication information, monitoring, and counseling.
  • Embedded pediatric pharmacists helped streamline provider-pharmacist communication within interdisciplinary care.
  • The authors identified future work to evaluate effects on adherence, persistence, and long-term health outcomes.

Evidence flow

A pediatric HSSP intervention cycle follows the patient as growth and disease status change.

1

Diagnosis and specialty therapy

A pediatric patient starts a specialty medication for a chronic condition.

2

Pharmacist verification

The pharmacist checks weight-based dosing, administration, adherence needs, and dispensing readiness.

3

Growth changes the regimen

Age, weight, lifestyle, and disease status shift over time, creating new therapy questions.

4

Regular reassessment

Frequent touchpoints uncover regimen, lab, safety, access, and counseling opportunities.

5

Actionable intervention

Relationship-based care supports accepted recommendations and positive medication practices.
