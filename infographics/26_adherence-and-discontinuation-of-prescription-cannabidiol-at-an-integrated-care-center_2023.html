NASP 2023 / Poster 26 / Seizure Disorders

Adherence and discontinuation of prescription cannabidiol in integrated care

A single-center prospective cohort followed patients prescribed cannabidiol through a neurology clinic and integrated health-system specialty pharmacy. The central signal: patients who made it to repeat fills were highly adherent, while discontinuation clustered around tolerability and response management.

Methods

How the cohort was defined

Setting Neurology clinics at a Southeastern United States academic medical center with an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy.
Design and window Single-center prospective cohort study from January 1, 2019 through April 30, 2020.
Included Patients prescribed CBD during the study period by the outpatient neurology clinic and dispensed by the integrated specialty pharmacy.
Excluded Patients whose insurance approval or fulfillment was not managed by the center's specialty pharmacy, or who participated in a CBD clinical trial.

Interpretation

What the poster implies for pharmacy follow-up

Set response expectations

Discontinuation for sub-optimal response was as common as common side effects.

Prepare for dose changes

Education should normalize dose adjustment as a tool to balance efficacy and adverse events.

Watch tolerability early

The median stop occurred at 117 days, with the IQR beginning at 68 days.

Protect refill continuity

Among patients with repeat fills, PDC was very high and tightly distributed.

Close contact gaps

Unreachable patients, delayed starts, and outside pharmacy transfer appeared among non-adherence reasons.
