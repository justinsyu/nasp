The Impact of Re-Training Specialty Pharmacy Outreach Technicians and Optimizing Compliance and Productivity

A structured outreach technician re-training program paired compliance refreshers, patient engagement practice, and workflow coaching to test whether existing teams could improve knowledge, productivity, and enrollment into health system specialty pharmacies.

Objective Train, test, track

Assess whether re-training improved compliance knowledge, outreach productivity, and patient enrollment rates among existing specialty pharmacy outreach technicians.

Evidence architecture

Three lenses connected the classroom to operational output

Pre- and post-training assessments Used to measure compliance knowledge retention.
Outreach and enrollment data Connected technician activity with HSSP enrollment.

Conclusion and recommendation

Ongoing education translated into stronger compliance, productivity, and onboarding signals

1 Compliance expectations were reinforced

Knowledge scores rose from 82% to 92% after the structured program.

2 Patient engagement became more productive

Enrollment to health system specialty pharmacies increased from 27.3% to 34.3%.

3 Training remained an operational investment

The authors recommend continued education and development to sustain outreach effectiveness and regulatory compliance.
