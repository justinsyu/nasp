Comparing turnaround time, adherence, and persistence at Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy, an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy, was compared with external specialty pharmacies across access, adherence, and persistence outcomes for oncology, inflammatory, and multiple sclerosis specialty prescriptions.

Design

Retrospective cohort

Patients with a specialty medication prescription from June 1, 2021 to May 31, 2022 were evaluated.

Setting

Three clinic areas

Vanderbilt oncology, inflammatory conditions, and multiple sclerosis clinics formed the care settings.

Classification

Fill pathway

Patients were categorized as HSSP, non-HSSP, or never filled; adherence classified HSSP when at least 50% of fills were at HSSP.

Analysis

Access and continuity

Wilcoxon rank sum tested TAT and adherence, while Pearson chi-square tested persistence.

Lengthy turnaround drivers

Delays clustered around logistics, insurance, and visibility gaps.

Bars use the highest observed percentage across HSSP or non-HSSP for each reason.

Conclusion extracted from poster

HSSP performance was strongest where access and continuity were measured together.

  • Patients who filled with the HSSP had higher adherence.
  • Patients with inflammatory conditions who filled with the HSSP had shorter turnaround time and higher persistence than those filling at non-HSSP.
  • Patients with multiple sclerosis who filled with the HSSP had higher adherence.
