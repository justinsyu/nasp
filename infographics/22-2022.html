Specialty Technician Training Program Development

Yale New Haven Health built a specialty pharmacy technician rotation track inside its accredited technician training program to widen the candidate pool during a pandemic-era staffing shortage.

Hiring-rate pattern

Specialty exposure changed the conversion curve

The original program produced limited specialty technician hiring despite offering broad pharmacy exposure. The new track centered rotations around specialty pharmacy teams and retained both initial completers as full-time SPTs.

Evidence flow

From staffing problem to live rotation in five months

Set criteria

Met with the technician training program coordinator and mapped requirements for a specialty track.

Map roles

Outlined specialty pharmacy technician positions and reviewed objectives against ASHP accreditation standards.

Build tools

Created the student binder, competency checklists, objective assignments, and preceptor preparation materials.

Align leaders

Introduced the track to leadership and specialty pharmacy staff, including preceptor expectations.

Launch rotation

The first student began the on-site specialty pharmacy rotation portion of the track.

Objective

Increase the available SPT candidate pool

The poster framed the track as a response to technician shortages that worsened during Covid-19 and strained specialty pharmacy workflow and patient care.

YNHH repurposed an established 23-week technician training program, treating completion as equivalent to one year of pharmacy experience for organizational hiring.

Training architecture

A rotating specialty curriculum replaced generic exposure

W9Hospital inpatient pharmacy scheduling

Rotation pattern

The schedule emphasized the operational work of specialty pharmacy

Access and intake Multi-specialty intake, oncology intake, MAP, prior authorization, Med B, 340B.
Fulfillment operations Dispensing, shipping, inventory, customer service, safety and compliance.
Disease-state workflows Inflammatory disease, transplant, oncology, MS, HIV, and Hepatitis C rotations.
Clinical support tasks Medication adherence, reassessments, titration dosing, in-baskets, patient outreach.

Conclusion

A specialty-focused training track can be a staffing strategy

The authors concluded that a specialty-focused pharmacy technician training program offers a novel approach to addressing health-system specialty pharmacy technician shortages.

Recruitment

Recruiting enough students for the specialty track remained a limitation.

Scheduling

Ancillary department training, call-outs, and PTO complicated rotation planning.

Next step

Grow enrollment and move sterile compounding / IV exposure to Home Infusion Pharmacy.
