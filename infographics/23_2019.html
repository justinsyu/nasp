Implementation of an Internal Check of Oral Oncolytics

A University of Pennsylvania specialty pharmacy quality initiative standardized pharmacist review for BSA-dosed oral chemotherapy, catching order issues before the first fill and creating a structured communication path with oncology care teams.

A survey of US cancer centers found few standard safeguards and underused pharmacist consultation for oral oncolytics.

More than 30 new oral oncolytic medications entered the market, increasing safety pressure on ambulatory dispensing workflows.

Objective

Create a standardized check process, clarify prescriptions early, and connect specialty pharmacy with the care team.

Methods

Single-center QI analysis of 22 months of specialty pharmacist i-vent documentation in Epic.

Targets

Initial implementation focused on capecitabine and temozolomide, both BSA-dosed oral oncolytics.

Result

Interventions clustered around supportive care, laboratory readiness, and drug interaction clarification.

Conclusion

The internal check promoted active communication and highlighted the resource need for oral oncolytic double checks.
