Hereditary angioedema, C1-INH replacement therapy, and coexisting autoimmune disorders

A retrospective claims analysis compared autoimmune-diagnosis visit frequency in patients with C1-INH-HAE treated with plasma-derived C1-INH versus other, non-C1-INH therapies.

Objective

Test whether replacement therapy aligns with fewer autoimmune-diagnosis visits

The poster hypothesized that C1-INH deficiency may mimic early complement-component deficiency and contribute to autoimmune disease; pdC1-INH corrects the underlying deficiency and was compared with non-C1-INH HAE therapies.

Conclusion

C1-INH replacement may modulate autoimmune disease occurrence

The authors concluded that C1-INH replacement therapy may have a modulating effect on the occurrence of autoimmune disease in patients with C1-INH-HAE.

They called for further research to clarify whether C1-INH replacement influences the severity or risk of concomitant autoimmune disorders.
