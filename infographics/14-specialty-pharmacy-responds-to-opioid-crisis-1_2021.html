Specialty Pharmacy Responds to Opioid Crisis

Shields Health Solutions described how an integrated specialty pharmacy built a comprehensive buprenorphine extended-release injection program that aligned REMS, DEA, health-system, payer, accreditation, and manufacturer requirements while supporting coordinated patient care.

Objective

Build a safe access pathway for healthcare-provider-administered buprenorphine ER injection.

The poster's objective was to describe implementation of a comprehensive program that could safely manage patients receiving buprenorphine ER injection while satisfying regulatory and manufacturer requirements specific to controlled-substance storage, dispensing, delivery, and documentation.

Regulatory controls

FDA REMS and DEA obligations were translated into specialty pharmacy workflow requirements.

Care coordination

Provider clinics, pharmacy liaisons, and pharmacists were assigned explicit handoffs.

Patient management

An internal PMP captured required data elements, outreach activity, outcomes, and custody steps.

Evidence flow

A three-lane workflow routed enrollment, benefits, clinical review, documentation, and shipment.

Figure 1 positioned the liaison as the bridge between the provider clinic and pharmacy. The pharmacy assessed prescription appropriateness, documented ETASU REMS and manufacturer elements in the PMP, adjudicated the claim, and shipped medication only to the prescriber's DEA-registered administration site.

Provider / Clinic Initiates enrollment and prescription; administers at registered location.
Liaison Confirms benefits, coordinates onboarding, and sends delivery details.
Pharmacy Verifies appropriateness, documents requirements, adjudicates, and ships.

Methods

The program was assembled by converting external obligations into internal platform fields, SOPs, and training.

Contract access

The pharmacy contacted the manufacturer in January 2018 to obtain a distribution and data contract.

Compile rules

FDA and DEA requirements were assessed for inclusion in the buprenorphine ER injection program.

Map data

Manufacturer, commercial payer, and accreditation data elements were reviewed and incorporated.

Build PMP

Requirements were embedded in an internal patient management platform used for outreach and custody tracking.

Train staff

Liaisons and pharmacists were trained on ETASU REMS, pharmacovigilance, product quality reporting, and SOPs.

SOP anchors

Four operational policies converted compliance language into dispensing guardrails.

Prescriber waiver Verify prescriber DATA-2000 waiver before dispensing.
No patient dispensing Prevent direct dispensing of the product to a patient.
DEA site match Confirm shipping location matches the DEA-registered administration site.
Provider notification Notify prescriber that medication must not be dispensed to a patient.

Public health driver

The opioid crisis requires health systems to expand access to addiction treatment while preventing misuse.

Access challenge

Buprenorphine ER injection is provider-administered, restricted-distribution, and governed by ETASU REMS.

Infrastructure answer

The pharmacy built required fields, documentation, and custody steps into its patient management platform.

Clinical response

Reported adverse events were pharmacist-managed through a multidisciplinary approach and customized plans.

Operational takeaway

Integrated specialty pharmacies are positioned to coordinate compliant dispensing and patient support.

Conclusion: the provider-liaison-pharmacist model achieved compliance with REMS, DEA, health-system, and manufacturer requirements while expanding access to buprenorphine ER injection and supporting coordinated care for patients with opioid addiction.
