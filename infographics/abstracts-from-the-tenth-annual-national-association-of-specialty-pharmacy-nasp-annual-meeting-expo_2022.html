What this supplement measures

Operational care models are treated as clinical interventions.

Across the book, the recurring comparison is not drug A versus drug B. It is integrated versus external fill, pharmacist-supported versus standard workflow, automated outreach versus telephone outreach, and faster authorization versus delayed access.

Evidence flow

How most abstracts convert pharmacy activity into outcome evidence.

1

Access friction

Prior authorization, insurance lockout, limited distribution, referral delay, or manual outreach creates a measurable wait.

2

Pharmacy intervention

Embedded pharmacists, liaisons, technicians, CPAs, dashboards, texting, or EHR tools reshape the workflow.

3

Operational metric

Studies track turnaround time, capture rate, referral-to-start time, call volume, documentation burden, or refill completion.

4

Patient metric

Evidence then moves to PDC, persistence, therapy gaps, discontinuation, PROs, AEs, disease activity, or satisfaction.

5

Value claim

The conclusion usually links faster, more integrated specialty pharmacy care to better continuity, fewer delays, or lower use/cost.

MS access

HSSP fills compressed script-to-mouth time.

7 vs 24d

In poster 1, average time to first MS DMT dispense was 7 days through integrated HSSP versus 24 days externally; new-start PDC was 0.97 versus 0.89.

Texting adherence

Digital nudges produced measurable PDC gains.

93.0%

Poster 2 included 8,048 patients; adherence texting participants had 93.0% PDC versus 90.8% for non-participants across MS, inflammatory, and oncology cohorts.

Oncology check-in

A 14-day pharmacist touchpoint reduced discontinuation.

40.4% → 29%

Poster 3 evaluated 9,414 oral anticancer patients and reported an approximately 11 percentage point drop after pharmacist-led early check-ins.

Workflow automation

Texting became capacity infrastructure.

3,593

Poster 15 expanded a specialty refill texting platform to 7 pharmacies, 8 service lines, and 189 medications, with 80% successful completions and W2H NPS of 85.

Economic signal

Integrated care shifted pharmacy expense and ER use.

$903

Poster 62 found a $903 PMPM pharmacy expense reduction for non-oncology HSSP members and lower ER visits per member per year, 0.3 versus 0.6.

Persistence

External pharmacy was linked to lower HIV PrEP persistence.

HR 2.7

Poster 64 reported that external pharmacy users were 2.7 times more likely to become non-persistent than HSSP patients in a Vanderbilt PrEP Clinic cohort.

Theme map

Five ideas recur across the supplement.

1. Specialty pharmacy as access engine

Multiple abstracts track PA turnaround, appeal approval, referral-to-start time, prescription capture, and insurance delay. The clearest pattern is that pharmacy operations are framed as time-to-therapy interventions.

2. Adherence moves beyond refill counts

PDC remains central, but abstracts also use therapy gaps, DOT, MPR, discontinuation, persistence curves, late-dose drops, and reasons for nonadherence such as memory or refill scheduling.

3. Technology is practical, not decorative

Texting, dashboards, SmartForms, CRM tickets, AI chatbots, and machine learning are positioned as ways to remove manual work, surface clinical exceptions, and reserve staff time for higher-risk patients.

4. Patient-reported data becomes pharmacy evidence

PRO-focused abstracts use MS, IBD, dermatology, asthma/allergy, rheumatology, and general specialty pharmacy settings to connect pharmacist interventions with patient function, symptoms, and satisfaction.

5. Oncology remains a stress test

Oral oncolytics appear repeatedly because they combine complex access, toxicity management, high discontinuation risk, and cost/utilization concerns. Early contact and adverse-event workflows are common solutions.

6. Economic value is tied to utilization

Cost abstracts link pharmacy model, discontinuation, or integrated care to PMPM spend, ER visits, hospitalizations, medical cost, and pharmacy expense rather than relying on satisfaction alone.

Bottom line

The 2022 NASP abstract book shows specialty pharmacy becoming a measurable care-delivery system.

The strongest throughline is not a single therapy area; it is a repeatable evidence pattern. Integrated specialty pharmacy teams identify a delay or adherence gap, insert a pharmacist, technician, technology, or documentation workflow, then measure whether access, persistence, symptoms, utilization, or cost moves in the right direction.
