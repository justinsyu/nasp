NASP 2021 poster 64

Migraine ACES: Assessing CGRP Efficacy and Safety

A multicenter specialty pharmacy review compared adverse drug reaction driven discontinuation and pharmacist interventions among patients receiving CGRP migraine monotherapy versus dual preventive plus abortive CGRP therapy.

  • Migraine prevention
  • CGRP monoclonal antibodies
  • CGRP receptor antagonists
  • Retrospective analysis

Key safety signal: ADR incidence remained low in both arms, with only marginally higher rates in the dual therapy group.

Objective

Does dual CGRP therapy add a meaningful safety burden?

The study assessed the risk of adverse drug reactions resulting in therapy discontinuation when patients used preventive and abortive CGRP agents together compared with CGRP monotherapy.

Clinical rationale: dual CGRP therapy may help patients with contraindications, intolerance, or inadequate benefit from other migraine medications, but safety evidence remains limited.

Future directions

Evidence gaps to close

  • Investigate reasons for differences in adverse drug reactions between treatment arms.
  • Compare the total number of abortive medications used between comparator arms.
  • Assess migraine management efficacy for dual therapy versus monotherapy.
