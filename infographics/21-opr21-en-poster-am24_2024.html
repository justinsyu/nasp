Real-world outcomes for oral specialty therapy in CLL/SLL

A Vanderbilt integrated health-system specialty pharmacy study followed patients receiving acalabrutinib, ibrutinib, or venetoclax for chronic lymphocytic leukemia/small lymphocytic lymphoma and summarized adherence, persistence, discontinuation, and therapy-switch patterns.

Methods

Integrated pharmacy records were used to follow therapy coverage and treatment status.

Identify eligible therapy

Patients prescribed acalabrutinib, ibrutinib, or venetoclax for CLL/SLL treatment.

Apply exclusions

Clinical trial participation, off-label use, lost follow-up, transplant, or transferred care were excluded.

Calculate outcomes

PDC, persistence, therapy discontinuation, therapy switch, and documented reasons were extracted.

Focus on 3+ fills

Adherence and persistence analyses used patients with at least 3 fills, with overlap supply carried forward.

Objective

Evaluate adherence, persistence, switching, discontinuation, and reasons for treatment changes in an IHSSP oral oncolytic cohort.

Setting

Vanderbilt Health outpatient oncology and hematology clinics using electronic medication records and specialty pharmacy management data.

Adherence signal

Mean PDC reached 92%, and 113 of 137 adherence-evaluable patients had PDC of at least 80%.

Persistence signal

Median time to non-persistence was 375 days, with probability falling to 0.35 by 42 months.

Practice implication

Adverse effects remained a major lever for specialty pharmacist monitoring, management, and mitigation.
