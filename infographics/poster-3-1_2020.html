Assessing Rates and Reasons for Treatment Lapses in Patients Treated With Denosumab

Vanderbilt University Medical Center studied real-world denosumab persistence in adults receiving at least two doses, asking how often injection gaps occurred, why they happened, and whether patient characteristics predicted lapse risk.

Objective

Measure lapse frequency, reasons, and predictors in routine care.

Denosumab is administered subcutaneously every six months for osteoporosis. The poster notes that fracture risk can rise when therapy is stopped without a reasonable cause, while some interruptions, such as dental procedures, infections, trauma-related fractures, or adverse effects, may be clinically reasonable.

Interpretation

Pharmacist prevention opportunities clustered around coordination.

The conclusion emphasized that pharmacists can coordinate care, help patients navigate insurance, and offer solutions for scheduling or transportation barriers. Those roles map directly to the largest avoidable-looking categories in the lapse table.

Evidence flow

The study moved from dispensing history to documented reasons and regression testing.

Define cohort

Adults receiving two or more doses of denosumab from January 2010 through December 2018.

Extract records

Demographics, treatment dates, and fracture dates before and after denosumab initiation were reviewed.

Identify lapses

Gaps of at least 240 days between injections were counted after prespecified exclusions.

Classify reasons

Documented causes were grouped as medical, logistical, patient decision, other, or unknown.

Test predictors

Logistic regression assessed whether patient characteristics were associated with lapse risk.

Conclusion

Lapses were common, mostly explainable, and operationally addressable.

Most documented lapses were tied to medical or health-related reasons, with logistical issues close behind. The authors proposed future work to identify patients at risk and develop pharmacist-led, patient-centered interventions to support adherence to osteoporosis therapy.
