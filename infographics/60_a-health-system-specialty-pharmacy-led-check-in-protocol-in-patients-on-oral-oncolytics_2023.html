A Health System Specialty Pharmacy-led Check-in Protocol in Patients on Oral Oncolytics

A retrospective health system specialty pharmacy study evaluated whether a pharmacist check-in 7 to 14 days after oral anticancer medication initiation could identify early toxicity, drug therapy problems, and risk for discontinuation within 60 days.

Objective and methods

Test whether an early, protocolized HSSP touchpoint finds toxicity soon enough to act.

The objective was to evaluate outcomes of a 7- to 14-day check-in protocol using Therigy to identify and address early side effects of oral anticancer medications.

This retrospective observational study included patients prescribed a new oral anticancer medication and managed by an HSSP pharmacist. Patients were excluded when therapy duration or HSSP service time was less than 60 days.

Conclusion

Early check-ins positioned pharmacists to catch toxicity before discontinuation.

The authors concluded that an HSSP pharmacist-led 7- to 14-day automated check-in can identify and address adverse drug events that could otherwise lead to early, inappropriate discontinuation of oral anticancer medications.

Clinical interpretation

Persistence risk and symptom management pointed to the same target.

Almost half of patients reported a side effect at check-in, and those patients were more likely to discontinue early. The most common early discontinuation reason was also side effects, making symptom management the clearest opportunity for pharmacist intervention.
