Safety of Deutetrabenazine Above FDA Maximum Recommended Dose in Huntington's Disease Chorea

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy reviewed adult patients with Huntington's disease chorea who received deutetrabenazine doses greater than 48 mg/day for at least 6 months, focusing on adverse events, monitoring findings, dose changes, and discontinuation.

Objective and methods

Describe adverse event and discontinuation rates after high-dose exposure.

The retrospective chart review examined dose changes, adverse events, safety monitoring, and discontinuation among adults treated at an academic medical center between April 2017 and July 2022.

Required exposure

At least 6 months of deutetrabenazine therapy at a dose greater than 48 mg/day.

Clinical setting

Huntington Disease Society of America Center of Excellence outpatient clinic.

Major exclusions

Strong CYP2D6 inhibitor use, inadequate follow-up, or previous clinical-trial high-dose exposure.

Baseline profile

Median age 55 years; 8 male patients; all included patients were White and non-Hispanic.

Safety signal

Adverse events were frequent, but only a minority triggered dose changes.

The poster reports 26 total adverse event reports. Four adverse events, or 15% of reported events, were associated with a dose change; events resolved after dose change without requiring DBZ discontinuation.

Interpretation

High-dose use required monitoring, but dose adjustment appeared sufficient for AE management.

The adverse event pattern suggests that exceeding 48 mg/day did not automatically lead to treatment cessation in this cohort. Instead, most patients stayed on therapy after monitoring and dose-management decisions.

This is a retrospective single-center chart review with a small included cohort, so the signal should be interpreted as descriptive rather than definitive.

Poster conclusion

DBZ dosed above 48 mg/day appeared safe in patients with HD chorea.

Although adverse events were common, the authors concluded that they resolved after dose change without requiring discontinuation of deutetrabenazine.
