Health Plan Cancer Concerns in 2019

A national payor survey found oncology remained a dominant medical and budgetary concern as plans faced rapid growth in targeted immunotherapies, genomic testing, CAR-T, and combination treatment pathways.

Evidence flow

Background

Targeted immunotherapies, CAR-T, tumor-agnostic drugs, and combination regimens were expanding rapidly.

Objective

Determine which oncology areas were most concerning to managed care organizations.

Method

An online interactive survey asked senior plan and PBM officers to rank cancer concerns and management approaches.

Result

Lung and breast cancer led concern rankings; oncology was the top medical and budgetary concern.

Implication

Plans need rapid benefit design, pathway, testing, and coverage adaptation as oncology innovation accelerates.

Conclusion

Oncology management had to move at the speed of innovation.

The authors concluded that oncology care management through ACOs and DMOs was increasing while cancer therapy shifted from traditional chemotherapy toward targeted immunotherapies.

The cost implications required payors, medical directors, and pharmacy directors to adapt coverage, benefit design, and pathway use as new agents became available.
