NASP 2022 / Poster 07-2022

Nurse-led outreach was associated with higher compliance for oral oncology therapy.

CVS Specialty evaluated a Compliance and Persistency program for patients initiating oral oncolytics. Over 12 months, the nurse-supported group had higher medication possession ratio and more fills than comparable pharmacy-claims controls after demographic adjustment.

Objective

Determine whether a nurse-led compliance and persistence program improves adherence.

The poster's premise is that oral oncology shifts treatment management away from infusion settings, reducing in-person touchpoints. The C&P program adds structured nurse outreach to help patients understand therapy, manage symptoms, and remain persistent with treatment.

Study group 5,771 patients

Received oral oncolytic therapy supported by nurse-led C&P outreach.

Control group 1,529 patients

Patients not supported by the nurse-led program in pharmacy claims.

Evidence Flow

Claims-based comparison linked structured nurse support to adherence at one year.

Identify initiators

Patients starting oral oncolytic therapy were identified across an August 2013-February 2022 window.

Assign exposure

Study patients had C&P support; controls did not have nurse-led outreach support in claims.

Follow 12 months

MPR was calculated as days' supply divided by days from first fill to last fill exhaust date.

Adjust and test

Logistic regression examined demographic factors associated with adherence; p<0.05 was significant.

Care gap

Oral oncolytics are convenient but can reduce in-person nurse contact compared with IV therapy.

Intervention

The C&P program uses nurse-led outreach calls aligned to the treatment plan established by the oncologist.

Measure

MPR converts refill claims into a longitudinal proxy for medication compliance and persistence.

Adjustment

Results were adjusted for demographic differences, including age and race-rate differences between groups.

Signal

Supported patients had statistically higher MPR, suggesting outreach can strengthen oral oncology adherence.

Poster Takeaway

Education plus recurring nurse contact appears to reinforce oral therapy persistence.

The extracted poster states that patients in the educational nursing program demonstrated a positive effect on overall medication compliance, reflected by a statistically significant improvement in MPR.

Conclusion

Standard care patients may benefit from nurse-led outreach programs.

The authors concluded that patients supported by a nurse-led program had better adherence to oral oncolytic therapy and that a multidisciplinary support approach contributed to improved patient outcomes.

  • Nurse-led outreach was associated with higher MPR at 12 months.
  • Supported patients averaged more fills than controls.
  • Structured education and symptom-management support are central to the model.
