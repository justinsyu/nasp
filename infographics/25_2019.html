Impact of a Specialty Pharmacy Benefit on Rheumatoid Arthritis Medication Adherence and Functional Status

A continuation study followed rheumatoid arthritis members using TNF inhibitors before and after a mandated specialty pharmacy transition, pairing pharmacy claims adherence with patient-reported HAQ-II functional status.

Objective

Did specialty benefit management improve RA adherence and function?

The study objective was to determine the impact of a specialty pharmacy benefit on rheumatoid arthritis medication adherence and functional status.

  • Included members had claims for adalimumab, etanercept, certolizumab pegol, or golimumab for RA treatment.
  • Members needed at least two fills within each study period.
  • Adherence was measured with proportion of days covered; PDC >=0.80 was treated as the clinical benefit threshold.

Group mix

Prior fill channel shaped the analysis

  • The transition increased adherence among members who had filled at non-specialty pharmacies before 2018.
  • Both groups fell to 61% adherent during the extension period.

Figure 4 signal

r=-0.200 p=0.035 | n=92

Baseline adherence and function moved in opposite directions

The poster reported a statistically significant inverse relationship between baseline HAQ-II score and pre-transition PDC among members who filled at specialty pharmacies before the transition and had completed baseline functional assessments.

Conclusions

What the authors concluded

  • PDC was significantly correlated with HAQ-II scores at baseline.
  • Adherence increased for members transitioning from non-specialty to specialty pharmacy.
  • Maintaining desired adherence rates remained a priority for continued functional status benefit.
  • More analysis was needed to determine whether HAQ-II is the appropriate RA functionality questionnaire.

Limitations

Interpret with sample-size caution

Functional assessments

Incomplete or refused HAQ-II assessments limited the sample available for functional status analysis.

Additional therapy

The poster noted insufficient data on additional rheumatoid arthritis therapy as another limitation.
