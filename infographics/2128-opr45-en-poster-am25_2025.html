Objective

Can pooled IVIG hold quality in flexible bags?

The study tested whether immune globulin intravenous, human-stwk, 10% liquid retained stability and quality after aseptic transfer from manufacturer vials into commonly used flexible containers.

Study follow-up

USP <797> Category 2 context cited in the poster: 4 days at controlled room temperature and 10 days refrigerated. This study assessed product characteristics through 14 refrigerated days.

Conclusion: pooled immune globulin intravenous, human-stwk, 10% liquid remained within release specifications for all assessed parameters for 14 days at 2°-8°C.

Why it matters

Pooling can simplify administration, but product-specific stability still matters

Pooling IVIG into one flexible container can streamline nurse workflow, support patient mobility during infusion, and reduce breakage risk. The poster emphasizes that these workflow advantages need product-level stability evidence under aseptic pooling conditions.
