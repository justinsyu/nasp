Impact of Specialty Pharmacy Integration on Time to Medication Access for Prescription Cannabidiol

A single-center Vanderbilt cohort tracked how an embedded specialty pharmacy team, with certified pharmacy technician ownership of access steps, moved patients from treatment decision to first cannabidiol shipment.

Objective

Measure the access interval after the pharmacy team is notified

The study measured days from initial specialty pharmacy patient assessment to first prescription cannabidiol shipment, with secondary intervals for benefit investigation, insurance approval, delivery, and financial-assistance effect on out-of-pocket cost.

CPhT Role Pattern

Ownership spans pre-launch, initiation, and ongoing access

Pre-launch

Coordinate with clinical staff and market access representatives to standardize the pathway and identify likely obstacles.

Initiation

Complete benefit investigations, prior authorizations, appeals, out-of-pocket evaluation, and assistance applications.

Maintenance

Renew authorizations and assistance, schedule refills, and check monthly for medication changes and seizure control.

Methods

Retrospective access-pathway measurement

  • Included all patients prescribed cannabidiol by outpatient neurology clinics from January 2019 through April 2020.
  • Excluded clinical-trial participants and cases where access and fulfillment were not handled by the integrated specialty pharmacy.
  • Data came from the electronic health record and specialty pharmacy management system.

Conclusion: integrated specialty pharmacy management ensured timely access, while CPhT ownership of key access steps let pharmacists focus on clinical patient care.
