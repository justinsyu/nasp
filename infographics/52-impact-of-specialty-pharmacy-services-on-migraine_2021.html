Impact of Health System Specialty Pharmacy Services on Migraine in Patients Prescribed CGRP Modulators

Parkview Specialty Pharmacy followed migraine patients receiving calcitonin gene-related peptide modulators and measured patient-reported migraine days, missed planned-activity days, and pharmacist interventions across an eight-month service window.

Objective

Measure migraine burden before and after specialty pharmacy enrollment.

The study evaluated monthly migraine days and days missed planned activity due to migraine using standardized refill-assessment questions asked approximately one month apart.

Clinical service model

Routine services spanned access, education, monitoring, adherence support, side effect management, refill coordination, home delivery, and interventions when therapy appeared suboptimal.

Methods

Single-group interrupted time series around specialty pharmacy enrollment.

Identify patients

Adults prescribed erenumab, galcanezumab, or fremanezumab for migraine.

Confirm service exposure

Included patients enrolled with Parkview Specialty Pharmacy for dispensing and clinical monitoring for at least two refills.

Collect monthly reports

Standardized questions captured migraine episodes, missed doses, activity loss, adverse reactions, hospitalizations, and abortive use.

Track interventions

Pharmacists completed access, adherence, safety, education, refill, outreach, and therapy-optimization activities.

Compare outcomes

Monthly outcomes before and after enrollment were examined for changes in migraine burden and activity loss.

Established therapy

Subjects averaged 187 days of CGRP-modulator therapy before specialty pharmacy enrollment.

Routine touchpoints

Monthly refill communications repeatedly captured migraine burden, adherence, activity loss, adverse reactions, and abortive use.

High-touch services

Services included access support, medication review, counseling, refill coordination, delivery, and clinical outreaches.

Outcome movement

Mean monthly migraine days moved from 3.4 in May to 1.5 in December, with the poster reporting a 55% decrease.

Functional impact

The poster reported a 47% decrease in days missed planned activities and concluded frequent touchpoints support long-term success.

Conclusion: CGRP modulators reduced migraine frequency, and the Parkview Specialty Pharmacy high-touch model added measurable patient-reported benefit through repeated monitoring and pharmacist interventions that helped optimize long-term migraine therapy.
