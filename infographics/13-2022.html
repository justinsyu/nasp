Biosimilar benefit use, adoption, and conversions

Workpartners Research Reference Database claims from 2010-2021 showed accelerating biosimilar adoption, lower biosimilar costs, and persistent loyalty to the first product selected once patients started therapy.

Objective

Use objective claims data to examine biosimilar growth, benefit use, and coverage value.

Annual specialty-drug claims and costs were classified as innovator or biosimilar, then compared by year and benefit type across marketed US biosimilars for reference products including filgrastim, infliximab, and rituximab.

Evidence flow

The analysis links benefit routing, price erosion, and adoption behavior.

Classify claims

Specialty agents were grouped as innovator/reference products or biosimilars.

Separate benefits

Claims were tracked through prescription versus medical benefit channels.

Compare costs

Annual dollars were compared by product to show biosimilar price erosion.

Track conversions

First and last drug patterns were used to assess switching behavior after biosimilars became available.

New-start adoption

By 2021, innovator starts had eroded across all three highlighted products.

The steepest shift was observed for rituximab and infliximab, where 2021 new starts moved sharply away from the innovator product compared with 2019.

Conversion pattern

Patients largely remained on the drug type they started.

Across 2010-2021, switching back to an innovator product was rare, while switching from an innovator to a biosimilar was observed in 5% to 11% of patients depending on the reference product group.

Benefit routing

Most 2021 claims were processed through the medical benefit.

The medical-benefit share was high for the highlighted innovator products and many biosimilars, reinforcing that biosimilar strategy is not only a pharmacy-benefit issue.

84% Filgrastim claims

Conclusion

Health plans appear to be recognizing biosimilar value, with adoption rising as options expand.

The poster concluded that biosimilar use is increasing and biosimilar prices erode as more options become available. Future analyses should examine individual innovator-biosimilar pairs and evaluate variation by therapeutic area, agent, insurance plan, and US region.

Authors: Richard A. Brook, Alek A. Drnach, Ian A. Beren, Nathan L. Kleinman, and Eric M. Rosenberg.

Market access premise

Biosimilars can reduce biologic specialty-drug costs and broaden access when payers support adoption.

Benefit-design lens

High medical-benefit processing means payer biosimilar strategy must address medical and pharmacy channels.

Price signal

Several biosimilars were dispensed at approximately half of innovator cost or below in 2021.

Adoption signal

New patient starts shifted away from innovator products as biosimilar availability matured.

Persistence signal

Once patients started on either an innovator or biosimilar, most remained on the initial product type.
