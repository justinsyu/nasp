Cation exchange chromatography removes FXIa from a new 10% IVIG preparation

GC Biopharma evaluated whether a ceramic-resin cation exchange chromatography step could reduce residual factor XIa and procoagulant activity in GC5107, a 10% intravenous immunoglobulin preparation.

Measurement strategy

Five evidence lenses converged on procoagulant activity

The study combined functional, chromogenic, clotting-time, protein, and immunoblot readouts across intermediates, final product, and spike-challenge material.

Manufacturing signal

CEX is the highlighted inflection point

Cold ethanol fractionation removed factors II, VII, IX, and X, while FXIa persisted until the cation exchange chromatography step. The CEX resin was described as ceramic-based, high-capacity for IgG, and intended to remove procoagulant activity.

Objective

Build robustness into a 10% IVIG purification process

  • Develop and evaluate a process to remove residual FXIa from GC5107.
  • Assess whether the process remained effective when starting material was intentionally spiked with FXIa.
  • Use multiple readouts to connect protein removal with lower procoagulant activity.

1. Risk context

Residual coagulation factors in IVIG can increase thrombus and thromboembolic-event risk.

2. Process gap

Ethanol precipitation removes several factors but does not effectively separate FXIa from IVIG.

3. CEX intervention

Cation exchange chromatography was inserted as a targeted step for procoagulant activity removal.

4. Assay confirmation

Final-product TGA and chromogenic FXIa values were below detection limits across 9 lots.

5. Conclusion

The new process may reduce TEE risk by robustly removing FXIa from GC5107.
