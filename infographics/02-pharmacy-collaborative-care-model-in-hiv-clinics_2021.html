Pharmacy Collaborative Care Model in HIV Clinics

Yale New Haven Health implemented an integrated ambulatory pharmacy service line in two HIV clinics, pairing a collaborative practice agreement with embedded pharmacist workflows, EHR referrals, and outcome tracking.

Evidence flow

From stakeholder interest to pharmacist-managed continuity

Assess need

Background research and stakeholder analysis identified clinic-specific opportunities for pharmacist support.

Authorize practice

The team drafted a specialty CPA and trained pharmacists and clinic staff on the new workflow.

Embed workflow

EHR referral pathways and pharmacist note templates connected ambulatory clinics to outpatient pharmacy services.

Track outcomes

Clinical continuity, referrals, PDC adherence, and viral suppression monitoring became the service measures.

Objective

Build a standard, clinic-integrated pharmacist service

  • Establish a collaborative practice agreement for HIV-related medication management.
  • Integrate a credentialed pharmacist into specialty clinics.
  • Create a standard process for patient education and reassessment.

The design targeted expensive, monitoring-intensive HIV medications where strict adherence and coordinated follow-up are central to treatment success.

CPA pattern

A broad scope matched therapies to monitoring obligations

HIV/AIDS therapy Attachment inhibitors, INSTIs, PIs, NNRTIs, NRTIs, entry inhibitors, CYP3A inhibitors, and cabotegravir/rilpivirine.
PrEP NRTI-based prevention with HIV antigen/antibody, viral load, CMP, hepatitis status, STI, and pregnancy monitoring.
OI prophylaxis Pneumocystis pneumonia and toxoplasma prophylaxis supported by BMP/CMP, CBC with differential, and CD4 monitoring.
Immunizations Pneumococcal, hepatitis, zoster, MMR, varicella, influenza, and HPV vaccines with relevant serology or titer review.
