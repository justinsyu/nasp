Real-world adherence and treatment outcomes to EGFR-targeted therapies in NSCLC

Yale New Haven Health reviewed integrated health system specialty pharmacy care for patients receiving EGFR-targeted TKIs, connecting mutation and treatment patterns with adherence, discontinuation, and pharmacist touchpoints.

Objective: describe adherence, mutation patterns, and discontinuation rates among patients with NSCLC receiving EGFR-targeted TKIs.

Methods flow

From chart review to treatment status

1

Retrospective review

Completed 2024 activities for EGFR TKI therapy used as primary or subsequent treatment.

2

Adherence measures

MPR and PDC were assessed for patients filling through the integrated specialty pharmacy.

3

Clinical descriptors

Demographics, disease stage, smoking status, EGFR mutation category, and TKI prescribed were summarized.

4

Secondary outcome

Treatment discontinuation reasons were evaluated among 94 patients after excluding two who transitioned care.

Conclusion

Integrated HSSPs are positioned to track oncology outcomes.

In this NSCLC cohort, frequent pharmacist engagement and therapy-specific education coincided with adherence above benchmark and relatively low discontinuation for adverse effects.
