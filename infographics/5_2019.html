NASP 2019 Annual Meeting & Expo

Medication adherence and graft survival among heart transplant recipients

A retrospective cohort linked pharmacy-chain immunosuppressant claims with OPTN follow-up data to test whether adherence, measured by proportion of days covered, was associated with heart graft survival.

  • Retrospective observational cohort
  • Heart transplant
  • Immunosuppressant adherence
  • OPTN follow-up

Conclusion

Adherence remained the central modifiable signal

Patients adherent to immunosuppressants had greater adjusted odds of a surviving graft than patients who were not adherent. A stricter PDC ≥90% sensitivity analysis was consistent, with survival odds 2.01 times higher among adherent patients.

Behavior

Future work should identify the patient behaviors that drive transplant medication adherence.

Threshold

The poster highlights uncertainty about the best PDC cutoff for transplant research.

Practice

Pharmacy claims can reveal adherence patterns before they become clinical risk signals.
