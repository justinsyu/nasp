Impact of an integrated health system specialty pharmacy liaison-managed care model within an uncontrolled diabetes population

CHRISTUS Health System and Shields Health Solutions evaluated a pharmacy liaison model for patients living with diabetes and baseline A1C greater than 9. The poster links access support, medication adherence, A1C reduction, and financial-assistance activity across income brackets.

Objective and measured impact

A pharmacy liaison model was associated with lower A1C and strong adherence.

The objective was to describe the associated impact of CHRISTUS Health System's pharmacy liaison-managed care model on HbA1c reduction and medication adherence for patients living with diabetes and baseline A1C greater than 9.

Conclusions distilled

The poster's interpretation in three signals.

  • Implementation of the liaison-led model was associated with significant A1C reduction.
  • Average PDC was higher than national adherence benchmarks cited for diabetes medications.
  • Similar A1C reduction and financial-assistance capture across income brackets suggested reduced income-related access barriers.

Economic context

A1C improvement was translated into total medical expenditure impact.

Using a cited estimate of $736 in annual diabetes-related expense reduction for each 1-point A1C decrease, the poster estimated a $721K reduction in total medical expenditures associated with the liaison-managed model.

This estimate is interpretive rather than a direct cost analysis, but it gives the clinical A1C improvement a health-system scale.
