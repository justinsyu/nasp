Impact of Expanding Belzutifan to an Integrated Health-System Specialty Pharmacy

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether gaining access to dispense belzutifan in a limited distribution network changed treatment initiation speed, treatment gaps, and 6-month adherence for patients with RCC or VHL.

Evidence flow

One medication access change, measured across initiation and 6-month continuity

1

LDN barrier

Belzutifan launched through a limited distribution network for VHL-associated tumors and later RCC use.

2

Pre-access cohort

Patients started belzutifan from Aug. 15, 2022 to Aug. 14, 2023 before VSP could dispense it.

3

VSP access

VSP gained access on Aug. 15, 2023, allowing fills through the integrated specialty pharmacy.

4

Post-access cohort

Patients starting through June 30, 2024 were followed for initiation time, gaps, and adherence.

5

Continuity lens

Gaps over 6 months were defined as more than 1 day without treatment; adherence used PDC.

Objective

Assess how expanding belzutifan access to VSP affected time to first fill, treatment gaps, and adherence.

Design

Single-center retrospective cohort study at an integrated HSSP within Vanderbilt Ingram Cancer Center.

Initiation

Median first-fill time improved from 9 days before access to 4 days after access.

Continuity

The share with any 6-month treatment gap decreased from 64% to 32%.

Conclusion

The findings support integrated HSSP access for LDN medications that require coordinated initiation and refill management.

Bottom line

Integrated access shortened initiation and reduced gaps for a complex LDN therapy.

Once VSP could dispense belzutifan, patients started faster and were less likely to experience 6-month therapy gaps. The adherence result moved in the same direction but was not statistically significant in this small cohort.
