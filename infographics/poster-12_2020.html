Interpretation

Oncology discontinuations suggest an outreach opportunity.

The authors identified therapy ineffectiveness and drug intolerance as disproportionately higher among patients receiving oral oncology specialty medications.

  • Early toxicity checks: prioritize side-effect triage before intolerance becomes a stop reason.
  • Progression and response monitoring: align outreach with windows where ineffective therapy is likely to be documented.
  • Transition support: coordinate hospice, mortality, and patient-choice pathways with oncology teams.

Objective, methods, conclusion

Condensed poster abstract

Objective: Analyze discontinuation reasons by drug and disease state to inform targeted interventions.

Methods: Multicenter retrospective observational review of adults receiving specialty medications, with discontinuations stratified by reason, medication, and diagnosis.

Conclusion: Embedded health-system specialty pharmacists may reduce discontinuation in oral oncology populations through targeted patient outreach; interventional studies are needed.
