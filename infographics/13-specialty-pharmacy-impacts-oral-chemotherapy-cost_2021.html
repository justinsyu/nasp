NASP 2021 poster 13

Specialty Pharmacy Impacts Oral Chemotherapy Cost

A University of Chicago Medicine retrospective chart review quantified how an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy avoided payer cost by holding oral chemotherapy refills until pharmacist review confirmed that continuation was appropriate.

  • Retrospective single-center review
  • Oral chemotherapy refills
  • February 1 to August 31, 2020
  • UChicago Medicine Specialty Pharmacy

Objective

Measure the payer effect of appropriate dispense holds

The study evaluated whether an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy could decrease payer cost by ensuring oral chemotherapy refills were appropriate before dispensing.

Methods

Retrospective chart review

  • Single-center review at the University of Chicago Medicine Specialty Pharmacy.
  • Included oral chemotherapy refills due between February 1 and August 31, 2020.
  • Required a pharmacist-initiated intervention before refill.
  • Excluded discontinuations from the cost avoidance analysis.

Conclusion

Integrated specialty pharmacy review prevented premature oral chemotherapy dispenses

Working alongside providers with access to the EMR positioned pharmacists to identify hold opportunities before refill calls, reducing medication waste and avoiding payer cost while preserving appropriate therapy continuation.

Limitations

Interpret with documentation limits in mind

  • Real-time dose changes or therapy changes may not have had an intervention documented.
  • Pharmacist interventions were proactive, so patient-driven refill-call interventions were not measured.
  • The proposed next step was to calculate proportion of days covered for continued-therapy patients.
