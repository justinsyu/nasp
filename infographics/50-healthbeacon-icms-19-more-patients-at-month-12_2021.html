Objective

Test whether the ICMS reduced discontinuation risk over 12 months.

The study compared persistence among HealthBeacon users on injectable rheumatological, dermatological, and gastroenterological treatments with persistence rates reported in the literature.

13.4%dermatology

Persistence improved despite a stricter proxy definition.

HealthBeacon defined persistence as time from first system use until ICMS deactivation, while the comparator literature used at least 90 days without therapy.

Methods

Persistence was measured until ICMS deactivation.

Patients were monitored for 12 months or until discontinuation on the ICMS. The analysis compared HealthBeacon persistence with a published study of therapy discontinuation in the same therapeutic areas.

Interpretation

Adherence and persistence should be treated as separate targets.

The poster notes that older age groups may show higher cross-sectional adherence in other data while also having higher discontinuation risk over time, so a single adherence metric can miss persistence loss.
