Objective

Use specialty pharmacy infrastructure to find rare disease risk earlier.

The poster describes a collaboration between an advanced heart failure program and a health system specialty pharmacy to identify patients at risk for ATTR, a rare disease associated with progressive neuropathy and cardiomyopathy.

Problem

ATTR diagnosis is often delayed because screening is difficult to execute across a health system.

Approach

Build a diagnosis-code tool, then educate PCPs and route candidates to AHFP evaluation.

Claim

HSSP can play a multidisciplinary role in identifying patients at risk for ATTR.

Methods

The report started broad, then narrowed to a high-risk clinical signature.

1 Patient older than 40 years.
2 Encounter at the institution within the prior 2 years.
3 Alive on the day of the report.
4 All four diagnosis-code patterns present.

Identified patients were targeted with PCP-directed education; the nurse coordinator initiated outreach and scheduled AHFP follow-up.

Evidence flow

Collaboration turned latent EHR clues into a managed clinical pathway.

1

Design

AHFP physicians, pharmacy leaders, a pharmacist program coordinator, a nurse coordinator, and subject matter experts designed the algorithm and outreach.

2

Find

HSSP created a diagnosis-code identification tool requiring age, recent encounter, alive status, and all four clinical code categories.

3

Educate

Patients identified by the tool were paired with educational activity directed to their primary care physicians.

4

Outreach

The nurse coordinator initiated PCP and patient outreach and scheduled AHFP follow-up for evaluation.

5

Evaluate

Twelve patients accepted evaluation, and one was diagnosed with ATTR.

Conclusion

Health system specialty pharmacies can help multidisciplinary teams identify patients at risk for ATTR.

The poster positions specialty pharmacy as a practical partner for rare-disease case finding because pharmacy teams can build data tools, coordinate education, and connect patients to specialty evaluation.

Next steps from poster

Broaden screening

Expand the algorithm and add non-diagnosis-code information.

Widen inclusion

Include non-cardiomyopathy patients to reduce reliance on a narrow cardiac signal.

Deepen partners

Partner more directly with primary care and neurology.
