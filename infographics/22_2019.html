NASP 2019 Annual Meeting & Expo

Integrating high touch specialty pharmacy services within a neurology clinic to improve medication access and affordability

This poster assessed whether an embedded specialty pharmacy services team could improve migraine medication access and affordability for CGRP receptor antagonists and onabotulinumtoxinA prescriptions routed through pharmacy benefits.

Objective

Move access work upstream

The primary objective was to minimize patient copay burden for CGRPra and Ona A prescriptions processed through pharmacy benefits. Secondary objectives tracked Ona A vial growth and the share of dispenses with $0 copay.

Embedded SPS coordinated benefit route, prior authorization, copay cards, and foundation grants.

Methods

One data source, two affordability tests

1

Population

Patients actively filling with the onsite specialty pharmacy and treated with CGRPra, Ona A, or both for migraine.

2

Exclusions

Non-health-system prescribers and records with incomplete dispensing data were removed.

3

Comparison

Ona A results compared the 6 months before SPS management, Feb-Jul 2018, with the 6 months after initiation, Aug 2018-Jan 2019.

4

Deductible check

January 2018 and January 2019 were compared to better isolate program effect from deductible timing.

Conclusion

Access coordination changed both patient and facility economics

  • High touch SPS benefited patients through prescription coordination and copay mitigation.
  • For CGRPra, SPS kept patient responsibility at 2% of medication cost with a $3 median copay per refill.
  • For Ona A, SPS increased onsite pharmacy dispensing while reducing median vial copay to $0.
  • Benefit verification helped identify preferred pharmacy versus medical benefit routing and supported projected Medicaid reimbursement gains.

Cost pressure

Specialty branded medications had increased in price by 57% since 2014, motivating targeted affordability services.

Embedded team

The neurology clinic specialty pharmacy services team identified unmet need for migraine medication assistance.

Benefit route

The team evaluated medical versus pharmacy benefit routing for office-administered medication access.

Assistance mix

Ona A financial assistance after integration came from manufacturer programs and foundation grants.

Residual limits

The study did not account for administration-related medical expenses and lacked a CGRPra control group.
