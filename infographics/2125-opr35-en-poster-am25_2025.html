Race, baseline asthma severity, and biologic response over 12 months

A Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy retrospective cohort evaluated whether racial and socioeconomic factors were associated with lung function, biologic adherence, and treatment gaps among patients initiating biologics for moderate-to-severe asthma.

Objective and design

Look for racial and socioeconomic factors affecting response

The study included patients prescribed a new biologic from January 1, 2019 through June 30, 2022, with follow-up through June 30, 2023. The primary outcome was race-neutral ppFEV1 within the first year after biologic initiation; secondary outcomes were adherence by PDC and cumulative treatment gap days.

4 Biologics included: benralizumab, dupilumab, mepolizumab, and omalizumab.
5 Regression covariates included smoking, COVID period, insurance, and distance to care.

Lung function by race

Median ppFEV1 rose in both groups

  • White baseline median74
  • White follow-up median82
  • Black/AA/Other baseline median63
  • Black/AA/Other follow-up median72

At baseline, Black/AA/Other patients had ppFEV1 estimated 6.87 lower than White patients (p=0.06). At follow-up, race was not significantly associated with ppFEV1 (p=0.71).

Starting point

Black/AA/Other patients showed worse baseline lung function before biologic treatment.

First year

The race-associated difference narrowed over 12 months after biologic initiation.

Smoking lens

Current and previous smoking were associated with lower estimated baseline ppFEV1.

Treatment gaps

Black/AA/Other patients had more average gap days and more frequent larger gaps.

Conclusion

Interventions to reduce treatment gaps and promote pharmacoequity remain needed.

Study conclusion

Biologic response may improve equity in lung-function outcomes, but access gaps still matter

  • At initiation, Black/AA/Other patients had worse lung function than White patients.
  • By follow-up, the race-associated ppFEV1 difference was no longer statistically significant.
  • Gap days and socioeconomic signals point to opportunities for specialty pharmacy interventions.
