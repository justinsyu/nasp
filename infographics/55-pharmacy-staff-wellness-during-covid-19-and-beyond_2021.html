Pharmacy Staff Wellness During COVID-19 and Beyond

Yale New Haven Health specialty pharmacy staff used recurring surveys, town halls, peer support, and tailored enrichment activities to track burnout contributors and adapt wellness resources during the pandemic.

Objective

Build sustainable tools for staff resilience

The project developed and implemented a collection of wellbeing and resilience resources for specialty pharmacy staff, targeting contributors to burnout that intensified during COVID-19.

1

Listen

Use surveys to capture mental health perceptions, service utility, and desired wellbeing topics.

2

Connect

Launch town halls and STEP OUTT peer-support sessions for pandemic, family, self-care, and gratitude topics.

3

Tailor

Revise offerings after each assessment to match evolving staff feedback and participation patterns.

Methods

Six-month pulse survey with a positive-perception score

Responses used a 6-point Likert scale coded so higher values represented the desired perception. Results were summarized as proportions and mean scores in Excel 2016.

Staff Voice

Conclusion

Survey-informed interventions can meet evolving staff needs

A recurring pharmacy wellbeing survey paired with tailored peer-focused interventions can support changing staff needs at a health system specialty pharmacy during COVID-19 and beyond.

Future directions included training additional peer-support facilitators and expanding sessions to activity- and craft-based formats.
