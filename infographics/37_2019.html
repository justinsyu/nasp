Impact of a health-system specialty pharmacy on medication adherence in post-kidney transplant patients

Yale New Haven Health reviewed one-year post-transplant medication adherence and found the lowest proportion of days covered among patients splitting prescriptions between the health-system specialty pharmacy and an outside pharmacy.

Primary signal

Adherence was lowest when patients used both the health-system specialty pharmacy and an outside pharmacy.

The poster reported a significantly lower proportion of days covered for the mixed-pharmacy group compared with either single-pharmacy pattern, p=0.045.

Need

Support

Interpret

Conclusion

The adherence story is less "which pharmacy" than "how many pharmacies."

The authors concluded that health-system specialty pharmacy use affects one-year adherence after kidney transplant, but the stronger practical signal may be that splitting fills across multiple pharmacies is associated with lower PDC.
