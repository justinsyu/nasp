Evaluating the Impact of a Clinic Embedded Specialty Pharmacy Team on POEM Scores

Summa Health Specialty Pharmacy and Trellis Rx assessed whether high-touch, clinic-embedded pharmacist and liaison support improved patient-reported eczema symptoms among patients treated for atopic dermatitis.

Evidence flow

How the embedded team turned patient-reported symptoms into intervention-ready data

Identify eligible patients

Adult patients with atopic dermatitis receiving care through Summa Health Specialty Pharmacy were reviewed.

Collect baseline POEM

Patients completed the seven-question Patient-Oriented Eczema Measure, scored from 0 to 28.

Deliver high-touch support

Pharmacists and liaisons supported access, education, monitoring, and therapy journey coordination.

Reassess and intervene

Follow-up POEM scores were tracked in Arbor, with worsening scores able to trigger pharmacist intervention.

Objective and rationale

Measure the patient experience of eczema, then act on it inside the care team

POEM captures itching, sleep disturbance, bleeding, weeping or oozing, cracking, flaking, and dryness or roughness from the patient's perspective.

The clinic-embedded specialty pharmacy model positioned pharmacists and specialty pharmacy liaisons to collect assessments consistently, coordinate access support, and escalate worsening symptoms.
