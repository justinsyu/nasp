NASP 2020 Poster 21

Improving patient experience by closing injection training gaps

Walmart Specialty Pharmacy tested a live, face-to-face video training call for patients using self-administered injectable specialty medications, pairing pharmacist counseling with real-time technique feedback and motivational interviewing.

Objective

Measure satisfaction while finding training gaps

The study evaluated whether a live, interactive injection training call could improve patient experience, identify gaps in prior training, and address patient-specific confidence and technique goals.

The rationale was practical: many patients begin costly, self-injectable specialty medications at home, where device errors can occur if training is incomplete or poorly absorbed during office visits.

Methods

Live training was embedded after pharmacist counseling

Identify eligible patients

Treatment-naive and treatment-experienced patients receiving self-administered injectable medication after initial pharmacist counseling.

Offer video training

At the pharmacist's professional judgment, patients were offered a live, interactive appointment with a specialty pharmacy clinician.

Coach in real time

The clinician observed technique and addressed confidence, device education, injection concerns, and side-effect management.

Use motivational interviewing

The call helped patients make sense of their therapy, surface barriers, and connect training to desired outcomes.

Review documentation

Clinical call records over two months were analyzed for prior training, call goals, and willingness to recommend.

Conclusion

Video-based injection training was highly acceptable and exposed unmet training needs

The authors concluded that a live injection training call with a specialty pharmacy clinician delivered high patient satisfaction and may help prevent avoidable administration questions, visits, or calls related to injection technique.

They also noted that additional analysis would be required to determine whether the service closed specific knowledge gaps or resolved defined patient and caregiver barriers.
