Medication Assistance Renewal Process Optimization at an Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health's specialty pharmacy redesigned its medication assistance program renewal season by separating year-end re-enrollments from new enrollment intake, preserving continuity for current patients while keeping access open for new patients seeking affordability support.

Operational redesign

One medication assistance team became two focused workstreams.

MART: renewal team

Three team members handled current-patient re-enrollments, using a clinical management report organized further in Excel by disease state.

MAPT: new enrollment team

Four team members maintained intake for new patients needing medication assistance while renewal work accelerated in parallel.

Evidence flow

The workflow prioritized faster access to limited grant windows.

Brainstorm

MAP team met in July 2023 to rethink the year-end renewal process.

Split teams

Existing staff were divided into renewal and new-patient enrollment groups.

Interpretation

Faster renewal processing protected specialty pharmacy continuity.

Objective: optimize the medication assistance workflow for year-end renewal processing while continuing to service new patients seeking support.

Conclusion: the two-team approach improved renewal processing, converted some patients from free drug to grant assistance, retained prescriptions at the health system specialty pharmacy, and maintained continuity of care.

Limitation: task tracking depended on the HSSP clinical documentation system, which may not translate directly to other health systems.

Future direction: adapt the workflow for 2025 Medicare out-of-pocket maximum changes and expand need-based assistance options.
