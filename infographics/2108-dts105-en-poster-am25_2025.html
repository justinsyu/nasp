Implementation of oral anticancer early monitoring using electronic questionnaires

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether portal-based early monitoring could surface adverse effects soon enough for pharmacists to intervene during new oral anticancer therapy.

Objective

Test whether early portal monitoring changes pharmacist detection of adverse effects

The study asked whether electronic questionnaires sent shortly after oral anticancer therapy initiation improved the timing and frequency of adverse-effect identification requiring pharmacist intervention, and whether downstream medication changes or clinical outcomes differed by 90 days.

Conclusion

Portal-based early monitoring appears most useful when patients respond

Questionnaire responders had higher and faster pharmacist identification and action on adverse events after oral anticancer therapy initiation.

The intervention arm showed lower repeat healthcare utilization at 90 days than usual care.

The operational bottleneck is response rate: improving completion is central to increasing impact.
