Evidence flow

HSSP staff converted migraine follow-up into an active treatment loop.

Start therapy

Adults enrolled and initiated Aimovig, Ajovy, or Emgality between October 2020 and October 2021.

Capture symptoms

Pharmacists and liaisons documented migraine data in the Arbor specialty pharmacy platform.

Check efficacy

At 56-84 days, the team classified therapy as effective, not effective, or suboptimal.

Intervene

For "no" or "suboptimal" responses, pharmacists contacted patients and providers with recommendations.

Reassess

Therapy changes, added therapies, or counseling were followed by another efficacy check or routine follow-up.

Objective

Measure HSSP value by speed to effective migraine therapy.

The primary objective assessed overall time until therapy was determined effective, plus time to effectiveness after a first "no" or "suboptimal" efficacy-check response.

Secondary objectives assessed change in monthly migraine days from baseline to most recent assessment and outcomes of pharmacist interventions.

Conclusion

Rapid checks paired with accepted interventions supported migraine control.

The poster concluded that health system specialty pharmacy services helped patients obtain rapid migraine control through therapy efficacy checks and pharmacist interventions.

More than 90% of patients were on effective therapy by study end, average time to control was less than 84 days, and most initially uncontrolled patients obtained control by the next check.

Burden

Migraine was framed as a high-cost condition, with substantial disability and productivity loss.

Need

Preventive therapies often require 1 to 6 months to judge effectiveness.

Mechanism

Onsite pharmacists and liaisons used proactive efficacy checks to spot ineffective or suboptimal therapy.

Action

Pharmacists recommended dose changes, additional therapy, discontinuation, new regimens, or adherence counseling.

Signal

Most patients reached effective control quickly, and most initially uncontrolled patients improved after intervention.
