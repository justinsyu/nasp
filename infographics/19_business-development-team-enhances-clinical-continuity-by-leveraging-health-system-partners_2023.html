Yale New Haven Health's specialty pharmacy built a multidisciplinary business development team to turn existing health system relationships into faster clinic engagement, stronger stakeholder coordination, and more continuous specialty pharmacy care.

Discussion signals

Why the team structure mattered.

  • Strong health system partner relationships let the HSSP use existing expertise in areas such as contracting.
  • Dedicated outreach and account management accelerated clinic engagement and broadened network perspectives.
  • Manufacturer relationship management and pipeline monitoring supported proactive medication access strategy.
  • Data and outcomes coordination improved value-proposition messaging for different partner audiences.

Problem

Specialty care and specialty drug fulfillment can fragment when partners, clinics, payers, and manufacturers are not coordinated.

Intervention

YNHH HSSP established a business development team to formalize engagement, outreach, contracting, and evidence work.

Execution

The team used an access strategy, pipeline monitoring, and a shared CRM tool to manage clinic and stakeholder work.

Measured reach

Among 536 eligible clinics, 34% were live or scheduled by August 1, 2023, with 66% left as future opportunities.

Outcome

Preferred-specialty-pharmacy clinics showed a +22.4% clinical continuity change from baseline after go-live.

Conclusion and future direction

A dedicated business development team can expedite continuity and stakeholder engagement.

The poster concludes that forming a business development team to leverage health system partners can expedite and increase clinical continuity and key stakeholder engagement. The future direction is to expand the team as the specialty medication portfolio and payer landscape become more complex, while strengthening internal and external partnerships.
