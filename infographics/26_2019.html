Economic Burden of Hemophilia B in the US

A systematic literature review synthesized U.S. evidence on hemophilia B costs and resource use, showing that factor IX replacement dominates annual medical cost and that extended half-life products carried higher annual factor costs despite lower unit consumption.

Rare disease, high burden

HB affects a small population but requires lifelong management to prevent bleeding and morbidity.

Claims dominated

Included studies primarily used insurance claims databases, followed by medical record analyses.

Cost concentrated

FIX therapy accounted for nearly all annual medical cost in studies reporting that decomposition.

Severity mattered

Patients with severe HB generally reported higher replacement-therapy costs than moderate or mild cohorts.

Need remained

The authors concluded that substantial costs and resource use highlight unmet needs in HB management.

Conclusion

HB management imposed substantial annual economic burden

The review found annual clotting factor costs ranging from approximately $230,000 to $577,363 for SHL treatment and $454,000 to $925,864 for EHL treatment.

The authors emphasized that rising movement from SHL to EHL FIX prophylaxis could increase payer burden, while differences in clinical profiles may explain part of the observed consumption and cost differences.
