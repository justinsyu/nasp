Objective and analytic frame

Provider specialty was treated as a real-world adoption signal.

The poster defined biosimilar adoption through APD, comparing the probability of adoption between HCP specialty pairs for each biosimilar cohort.

  • Provider practice relationships were created from real-world claims patterns.
  • NPI specialty taxonomy supported specialty and sub-specialty distribution analysis.
  • Reporting windows varied by molecule to reflect time on market and biosimilar launch timing.

Objective

Evaluate whether provider specialty drives biosimilar adoption, measured as APD.

Data source

PurpleLab HealthNexus report and analytics across claims and provider relationships.

Method

R 4.4.0 and Random Forest modeling predicted specialty-level adoption variation.

Result pattern

New biosimilar launches had lower provider counts and lower adoption.

Implication

Real-world specialty insights may help direct awareness, access strategy, and patient routing.

Conclusion: biosimilar prescribing varied by provider specialty, and adoption patterns were shaped not only by availability, but also by launch timing, switching evidence, payer policy, formulary coverage, and provider awareness.
