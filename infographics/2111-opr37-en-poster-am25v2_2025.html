The Impact of an Integrated Outpatient Specialty Neurology Pharmacist in a Non-MS Clinical Setting

A single-center retrospective review at Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy quantified the pharmacist workload, recommendation yield, acceptance rate, intervention time, and medication cost avoidance across non-MS neurology specialty clinics.

Evidence flow

How the poster connects pharmacist integration to operational and economic value

1

Embedded setting

Specialty pharmacists supported ATTR amyloidosis, autonomics, epilepsy, movement disorders, neuromuscular, and other non-MS clinics.

2

Routine capture

Interventions were extracted from the electronic health record and specialty pharmacy management system.

3

Clinical action

Recommendations most often involved therapy changes, financial assistance referrals, counseling, appointments, or safety monitoring.

4

Accepted advice

Almost all recommendations were accepted, suggesting high alignment between pharmacists, providers, and patients.

5

Cost impact

Dose changes and discontinuations generated estimated medication cost avoidance approaching $200,000.

Methods

Retrospective cohort with operational, clinical, and cost outcomes.

  • Included patients prescribed a specialty medication in non-MS neurology clinics with at least one pharmacist intervention during January-March 2023.
  • Primary outcomes were recommendation-generating interventions and pharmacist time spent on interventions.
  • Secondary outcomes included recommendation outcome, impact score, cost avoidance, accepted recommendations, and factors associated with greater intervention time.
  • Cost avoidance used medication costs linked to discontinuation, medication change, or dose change follow-up outcomes.

Conclusion

Integrated outpatient neurology pharmacists delivered measurable care and cost value.

Over 3 months, pharmacists performed 2,244 interventions in 741 patients, frequently leading to therapy-change or monitoring recommendations. The poster estimates direct medication cost avoidance at $188,570 using average wholesale price minus 20%.

Infographic readout

The operational story is not just volume; it is volume plus accepted action.

The study describes an integrated pharmacist model where routine specialty-neurology interventions produced a clear funnel: broad intervention capture, a smaller set of provider or patient recommendations, very high acceptance, and a measurable cost-avoidance estimate. The medication and recommendation tables both point to targeted opportunities: therapy optimization, financial assistance, and monitoring in therapies where dose changes can materially affect spend.
