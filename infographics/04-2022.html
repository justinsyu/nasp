Risks of low adherence in pediatric patients with cystic fibrosis

CHOC Specialty Pharmacy examined proportion of days covered across CFTR modulators, dornase alfa, and inhaled antibiotics to understand how adherence tiers aligned with pulmonary exacerbations, hospitalizations, FEV1, and BMI.

Objective

Profile adherence across CF specialty medication classes and look for clinical risk patterns.

The study stratified pediatric patients into high, medium, and low adherence groups using PDC, then explored associations with pulmonary exacerbations, hospitalizations, percent predicted FEV1, and BMI.

Clinical signals

The outcomes story was directional, not powered for firm risk correlations.

5 Hospitalizations after pulmonary exacerbations

Among 11 patients with multiple PEx, five had events that led to hospitalization.

1 Hospitalized/PEx patient on a CFTRm

Only one of those patients was receiving a CFTR modulator, Trikafta.

84.9% Dornase alfa discussion average

Lower than CFTRm adherence and close to inhaled therapy averages.

92% COLI discussion average

Inhaled antibiotic subclasses varied, but sample sizes were limited.

Background tension

CF treatment regimens are complex, and adherence may fall when CFTR modulators make patients feel better.

Measurement

PDC was used as the adherence measure because it captures the proportion of eligible days covered by medication on hand.

Class pattern

CFTRm adherence stayed consistently high, exceeding inhaled therapy adherence across the study period.

Clinical pattern

Hospitalizations were more common among patients not on CFTRm, and low adherence to other therapies did not clearly increase PEx when patients were on CFTRm.

Next study

The authors called for larger, longitudinal studies to connect adherence trajectories with clinical outcomes.

Conclusion

CFTRm adherence may have greater clinical influence than adherence to other inhaled therapies.

The poster concluded that higher adherence to CFTR modulators may have a larger impact on clinical outcomes, but the study was not powered to prove associations between risk factors and adherence level.

Limitations

Interpret findings as a local signal.

The analysis was limited by small sample size, insufficient variation across adherence tiers for correlation testing, and inability to calculate PDC for medications filled at outside pharmacies.

Authors: Allison Nguyen, PharmD; Jennifer Yen, PharmD, BCPPS; Lois Sayrs, PhD; Katie Tu, PharmD.
