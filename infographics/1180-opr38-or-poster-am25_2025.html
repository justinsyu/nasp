Methods

Cost avoidance followed a direct plus indirect model recommended for pharmacy practice studies.

Identify eligible IPF interventions

Include completed HSSP interventions for patients diagnosed with IPF and receiving IPF-related specialty therapy.

Estimate direct avoidance

Use discontinuation of unnecessary medications, trajectory-change probability, and average wholesale price differences.

Add indirect avoidance

Use harmful-consequence probabilities, trajectory-change probability, and cost data from published literature.

Interpretation

Clinical service translated into an economic signal.

HSSP teams providing care for patients with IPF performed clinically meaningful interventions, and the poster quantified potential costs avoided as a result of those services.

Limitations and next step

Labor costs were outside the estimate.

The study did not account for pharmacist labor costs. Future work should evaluate the financial impact across larger samples and additional disease states.
