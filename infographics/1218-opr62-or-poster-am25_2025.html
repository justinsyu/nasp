Earlier, denser nurse outreach was associated with stronger ponatinib continuity.

AcariaHealth compared a legacy counseling cadence with an enhanced specialty pharmacy program that moved support before first shipment and into the first 60 days of therapy.

Early touchpoints changed the risk window.

The enhanced cadence placed counseling before shipment and at days 7, 30, 45, and 60 instead of waiting until after shipment and quarterly follow-up.

Day-30 persistence improved by 23.60 percentage points.

At day 30, 80.47% of Program 2.0 patients remained on therapy versus 56.87% in Program 1.0.

Interpretation should account for indication mix.

The poster notes that addition of Ph+ ALL during Program 2.0 may limit comparability because it added a disease state with slightly lower dosing.

Conclusion

Earlier initiation of outreach was presented as an effective adherence strategy.

The authors concluded that incorporating early patient outreach can improve medication adherence and persistency, while recommending further research with variable call cadence across more comparable sample groups.
