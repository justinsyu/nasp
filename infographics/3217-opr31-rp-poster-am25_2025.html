Evaluating Efficiency through Workflow Productivity Metrics in a Health System Specialty Pharmacy

A health system specialty pharmacy project proposes reproducible time studies and monthly staffing benchmarks to identify delays, interruptions, and task-allocation opportunities across the technician workflow.

Measurement target

Turn daily work into benchmarks Stopwatch observation plus dispensing-system history becomes a repeatable view of capacity, interruptions, and staffing needs.

Objective

Assess workflow efficiency and locate delay points

The project uses time studies to analyze technician productivity, generate benchmarks, and document factors that cause delays or interruptions in specialty pharmacy operations.

Staffing benchmark pattern Monthly task completions / full-time employees typically assigned to those tasks

Operational interpretation

Why these metrics matter

Productivity metrics are positioned as tools for evaluating staff competency, identifying training needs, assessing new processes, and supporting a staffing model that maintains high-quality patient care.

Expected conclusion

Actionable insight for redesign

The project is designed to reveal task duration, interruption sources, and monthly task volume per assigned FTE, creating evidence for process improvement, task allocation, and staffing decisions.
