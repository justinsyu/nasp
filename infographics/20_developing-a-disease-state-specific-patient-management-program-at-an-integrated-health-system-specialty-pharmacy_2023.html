Objective Standardize scale

Create a repeatable HSSP patient management program development approach that can address the diversity and unique needs of specialty conditions.

Results timeline

Module development accelerated when the process became repeatable.

The poster states that modules grew during process development in 2020, 41 modules were developed in 2021, and the 2022 EHR redesign enabled disease-specific workflow and documentation customization.

Gap analysis prioritized need

  • Surveyed pharmacy staff.
  • Assessed specialty medication utilization to find patient-management gaps.
  • Stratified module priority by historical and current patient volumes.
  • Used standards of care and guidelines to frame disease-state needs.

Template design made modules reproducible

  • Defined disease overview, inclusion criteria, onboarding, education, ongoing management, interventions, goals, and references.
  • Subject matter experts created evidence-based module content.
  • Each module packaged an SOP, in-service presentation, and learning assessment.

Quality assurance kept content governed

  • Internal clinical oversight committee vetted modules.
  • Modules were reviewed and approved annually.
  • Select PMP modules were vetted with physician specialists.

Pressure

Specialty pharmacies face diverse conditions, medications, accreditation requirements, and payor expectations.

Design choice

OPS moved from a universal process toward standardized, disease-specific module development.

Build system

Gap analysis, template design, SME authorship, and committee governance created repeatable modules.

Operational embed

Modules informed EHR workflows, screening tools, care episodes, and clinical documentation.

Result

The program grew from 10 PMP modules in 2019 to coverage across 85 disease states.

Module template pattern

Common elements extracted from the poster table

  • Covers a single disease or a group of related diseases.
  • Defines patient inclusion criteria and onboarding education.
  • Documents ongoing management, intervention triggers, and escalation process.
  • States program goals, measures, references, and evidence base.
  • Supports URAC, ACHC, and third-party payor requirements.

A governed template turned specialty pharmacy complexity into scalable disease-state care infrastructure.

Conclusion

  • A standardized module-development approach grew the HSSP patient management program from 10 modules to coverage across 85 disease states.
  • The new EHR care-management build allowed disease-specific workflows, documentation, and screening tools that were previously unattainable.
  • Patients outside specific PMP scope remain supported through a standard specialty pharmacy module that meets accreditation requirements.
