Objective

Measure direct costs and caregiver work disruption together.

  • Examine annual medical, prescription, FML, and DITO trends for employee caregivers of spouses with Parkinson's disease.
  • Examine medical and pharmacy claims for the patients with Parkinson's disease.
  • Report results by index year after the spouse patient's initial Parkinson's diagnosis.

Database

Identification

US patients with Parkinson's disease were identified from 2018-2022 using ICD-10 code G20.

3

Follow-up

Patients and care partners required at least one year of continuous data after the patient's initial diagnosis.

4

Outcomes

Caregiver leave, DITO and FML days, and PMPM medical, prescription, and total costs were assessed by index year.

5

Conclusion

Costs declined, but FML and DITO showed rising productivity burden for working-age spouse caregivers.

Bottom line

The direct cost trend softened, but caregiver absence burden rose.

The poster concludes that Parkinson's disease is increasingly relevant to working-age families and employers. Although medical and prescription costs declined since 2018 in this cohort, Family Medical Leave and DITO days rose, pointing to a productivity burden that persists beyond the patient's direct claims.
