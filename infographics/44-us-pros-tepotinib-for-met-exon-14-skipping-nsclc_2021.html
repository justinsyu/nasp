NASP 2021 | VISION Cohort A | Patient-reported outcomes

US health utility in advanced NSCLC patients harboring MET exon 14 skipping mutations treated with tepotinib

A Phase II VISION analysis translated repeated EORTC QLQ-C30 and EQ-5D-5L patient-reported outcomes into US-weighted utility values for people receiving once-daily tepotinib for METex14 skipping NSCLC.

Objective

Convert trial PROs into utility evidence for HTA-relevant health states

The analysis used PRO data collected in VISION Cohort A to estimate health utilities in tepotinib-treated patients with METex14 skipping NSCLC, including baseline, pre-progression, and post-progression health states.

Methods flow

From VISION assessments to US-weighted utilities

1

Enroll MET-altered advanced NSCLC

Cohort A focused on METex14 skipping disease in an open-label, multicenter, multicohort Phase II trial.

2

Treat once daily

Tepotinib 500 mg was given until progression, intolerable toxicity, or consent withdrawal.

3

Collect PRO instruments

EORTC QLQ-C30 and EQ-5D-5L were collected repeatedly through treatment, end of treatment, and safety follow-up.

4

Model utility by health state

Linear mixed models used patient random intercepts plus fixed effects for baseline utility and progression status.

EORTC QLU-C10D utilities were derived from EORTC QLQ-C30 responses using US value sets. EQ-5D-5L data were mapped to EQ-5D-3L and scored with US weights.

Global health and VAS pattern

Mean changes stayed inside the +/-10 point meaningful-change band

EORTC QLQ-C30 global health and EQ-5D-5L VAS scores showed stable quality of life over time up to 84 weeks, with end-of-treatment values trending lower.

Instrument alignment

Matched utility instruments moved together

0.681 Pearson correlation

Matched EORTC QLU-C10D and EQ-5D utilities from the same visit were highly correlated, supporting broadly similar utility signals from both instruments.

Conclusion: VISION was reported as the first MET inhibitor trial to provide health-utility evidence in METex14 skipping NSCLC, with overall HRQoL remaining stable during tepotinib treatment and utility values generally moderate to high until progression.
