Objective

Measure accuracy through the error log

The primary objective was to determine dispensing and distribution accuracy by comparing the percentage of different reported error types.

The secondary objective focused the lens on external prescriber errors and described how those errors distributed by service line.

Dispensing Accuracy (MP 2012-06) Assesses inaccurate dispensing across drug or product, recipient, strength, dosage form, instructions, and quantity.
Distribution Accuracy (MP 2012-07) Assesses prescriptions delivered to the wrong recipient, including incorrect address or incorrect location.

Methods

From frontline reporting to accuracy measures

Capture events

Clinical interventions, near misses, and errors were reported to an intervention email outbox or physical on-site log.

Log centrally

Events were recorded in the Pharmacy Event Log on SharePoint for structured review.

Apply window

Documented errors from Oct 7, 2019 through Mar 31, 2020 were extracted and analyzed.

Classify fields

Event type, issue, resolution, outcome, service line, PA-only status, and dispensing accuracy were collected.

Exclude lab-only

Interventions that pertained to labs being ordered for treatment were excluded from the analysis.

Service-line concentration

External prescriber errors clustered in oncology

Among 264 external prescriber errors, oncology contributed 135 patients, just over half of the prescriber-error total. The next largest bucket was "Other" with 75 patients.

Conclusion signal

Event reporting identified where quality work should focus

UHSP's intervention-log review made accreditation-related accuracy measures visible and showed that the dominant opportunity was not dispensing error volume, but external prescriber error capture and service-line-specific follow-up.

53% of reported errors were external prescriber errors.
51% of external prescriber errors came from oncology.
2 URAC Safe Care measures anchored the analysis.

Action agenda

Next reporting improvements

  • Add severity of error and prescriber-of-Rx questions to the intervention log.
  • Add subcategories so event types are better defined.
  • Create a Clinical Interventions Subcommittee.
  • Hold monthly retail-pharmacist reviews of actionable items.
  • Use trend identification to launch quality improvement projects.

Limitations

Interpretation guardrails

  • Not all errors and interventions may have been forwarded or logged.
  • Error categories were not mutually exclusive.
  • The process required human interpretation.
  • Categories lacked definitions and subcategories within the intervention log.
