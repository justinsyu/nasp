Study thesis

Integrated pharmacy management created operational growth and measurable clinical signals.

The poster frames specialty medications as complex therapies that require access coordination, safety monitoring, and outcome optimization. The HSSP transition brought pharmacists into the health-system workflow with EHR visibility and dashboards.

  • Patient engagement was tracked through active managed patients, dispensed therapies, and financial assistance.
  • Clinical outcomes included A1c change for diabetes mellitus and RAPID3 capture for rheumatoid arthritis.
  • Endpoints were analyzed descriptively from operational and clinical dashboards.

Evidence flow

From HSSP transition to dashboard-tracked outcomes

1

Transition

HSSP management began in October 2022 at United Health Services Hospital.

2

Manage

Patients received clinical support, education, monitoring, and medication access services.

3

Stratify

DM patients with A1c ≥ 10 were classified as high risk and graduated when A1c was < 8.

4

Measure

Dashboards tracked active patients, dispensed therapies, assistance, A1c, and RAPID3 collection.

5

Improve

Clinical dashboards identified patients not at goal and opportunities for workflow refinement.

Best practices

Design the program around integration

  • Integrate pharmacists within the health system.
  • Use advanced technology for data collection.
  • Support medication access and financial assistance.
  • Benchmark and monitor clinical outcomes.

Improving outcomes

Make outcomes visible and actionable

  • Continuously monitor clinical outcomes.
  • Use a systematic approach to identify patients not at goal.
  • Use dashboards to find improvement opportunities.

Future direction

Scale access, transitions, and disease coverage

  • Implement pharmacist-led transitions of care.
  • Expand into additional disease states and clinics.
  • Grow the payor access network.
  • Serve patients facing payor and limited distribution barriers.

Bottom line

The HSSP model linked access infrastructure with measurable patient engagement and clinical improvement.

Across the first four months after transition, active management volume and dispensed therapies increased, financial assistance exceeded $6.1 million, RAPID3 capture reached 100% for eligible RA patients, and high-risk DM patients averaged a 4.22-point A1c reduction with all high-risk patients graduating to low risk.
