Optimizing the timing of pre-approval information exchange for high-complexity therapies

Healthcare decision-makers reported that earlier pre-approval information exchange (PIE) is especially valuable for oncology products, rare disease products, and cell and gene therapies, with many linking PIE to faster patient access.

Objective

When do healthcare decision-makers want PIE, and what does it mean for access?

The survey asked HCDMs who use pre-approval information in drug evaluation to identify preferred timing, information types, and the perceived access effect for oncology, rare disease, and cell and gene therapy products.

Respondents 41

Healthcare decision-makers completed the survey.

Methods

Survey frame and exclusions

  • Web-based survey fielded with advisors from Cencora's Managed Care Network from May 6 to May 16, 2024.
  • The MCN panel includes more than 160 healthcare executives, medical and pharmacy directors, and managed care experts.
  • HCDMs who did not use pre-approval information in drug evaluation were excluded.
  • Multiple-choice responses were summarized using descriptive statistics.

Evidence Flow

PIE supports readiness before FDA action and before coverage decisions are finalized.

1

Early product context

HCDMs wanted product information, indication, and approval-timeline signals earlier for rare disease, CGT, and oncology products than for all products.

2

Expedited pathway focus

Two-thirds rated PIE important or extremely important when products are under FDA expedited approval pathways.

3

Phase 2 data matters

Phase 2 data before approval were valued far more for conditional approval, oncology, rare disease, and CGT products than for all products.

4

Potential access gain

Some HCDMs reported patients could gain access sooner when PIE is communicated for these categories.

Conclusion: manufacturers should consider earlier PIE engagement with HCDMs to support patient access for oncology, rare disease, and cell and gene therapy products.

Limitations

Interpret with sample context

  • The study reflects a small recruited sample from Cencora's Managed Care Network research panel.
  • The sample had greater representation from pharmacy directors at national health plans, which may limit generalizability across all HCDMs.
