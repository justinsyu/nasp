Optimizing Specialty Medication Access Through Multi-Step Order Transmittal Workflows

A single academic health system evaluation found that routing complex specialty prescriptions through an EHR-based MSOT workflow shortened access time, retained more fills within the health system, and improved visibility into payer and limited distribution drug barriers.

  • Retrospective pre/post cohort
  • Neurology
  • Rheumatology
  • Cardiology
  • March 2024-April 2025
MSOT

Embedded EHR logic automatically routed complex prescriptions to a centralized pharmacy access team for benefits investigation, prior authorization, and fulfillment support.

Evidence Flow

How the evaluation connected workflow design to access outcomes.

Objective

Evaluate MSOT impact on turnaround time, capture rate, access-barrier identification, and provider satisfaction across pilot clinics.

Population

Specialty prescriptions from Neurology, Rheumatology, and Cardiology clinics at one academic medical center.

Intervention

A centralized pharmacy team managed benefits investigation, prior authorization completion, and fulfillment after EHR routing.

Sources

Epic, MSOT queue data, pharmacy dispense records, Qualtrics surveys, and manual baseline tracking supported descriptive analysis.

Conclusion

MSOT appeared scalable for faster patient access, stronger prescription capture, and earlier visibility into manufacturer and payer restrictions.

15,448 Prescriptions reviewed

The evaluation covered a high-volume access workflow, with 8,132 prescriptions before MSOT and 7,316 after implementation.

Automation gap Important limitation

Manual baseline tracking, incomplete prior authorization capture, and phased rollout limited data completeness.

Next step Program expansion

The authors point to broader MSOT adoption and real-time analytics as ways to extend value across more therapeutic areas.
