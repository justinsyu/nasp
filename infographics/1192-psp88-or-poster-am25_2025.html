Evaluating the impact of clinical pharmacist integration on patient care outcomes in a private rheumatology clinic

Objective

Measure collaboration across outcomes, satisfaction, and operational efficiency

The study assessed whether pharmacist-provider collaboration in a private rheumatology clinic affected patient care outcomes, provider satisfaction, and prior authorization turnaround time.

  • Single-center, retrospective observational cohort study.
  • Patients referred to the pharmacist from August 1, 2024 through January 31, 2025.
  • Data came from pharmacist interventions, RAPID-3 scores, PA records, and a provider survey.

Discussion signal

What changed

  • Pharmacist integration supported medication access, patient education, and treatment-related side-effect management.
  • Most recent RAPID-3 scores showed statistically significant and clinically important improvement.
  • Faster PA turnaround suggests fewer treatment delays for specialty therapies.

Interpretation limits

What to keep in view

  • Single-center private rheumatology clinic results may not generalize to broader settings.
  • An EMR vendor transition required manual retrieval of legacy data during the study window.
  • Future work should examine patient-reported outcomes, resource use, and provider administrative burden.
