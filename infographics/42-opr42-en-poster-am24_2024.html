Radicava ORS®-treated patients with ALS in a US claims database

Objective

Characterize US patients with ALS receiving oral edaravone in administrative claims.

The poster's primary aim was descriptive: define demographics, clinical characteristics, pre-index disease progression milestones, and healthcare utilization for oral edaravone-treated patients.

Methods

Claims streams were adjudicated, de-identified, then summarized descriptively.

1

Claims inputs

Medical, pharmacy, facility, laboratory, and patient enrollment records.

2

De-identify

Optum CDM data were statistically de-identified under expert determination.

3

Index

First dosing date of MTPA oral edaravone served as the index date.

4

Split cohorts

Patients were classified as IV switchers or oral initiators.

5

Describe

Counts, percentages, means, medians, SDs, and ranges were reported.

Clinical context

ALS is fatal and heterogeneous; the authors positioned real-world evidence as a supplement to randomized trials.

Therapy context

IV edaravone was approved in 2017; oral edaravone suspension was FDA approved for ALS in May 2022.

Claims lens

The Optum CDM enabled national-scale descriptive analysis across medical and pharmacy utilization.

Cohort readout

Of 375 oral edaravone-treated patients, 306 initiated orally and 69 switched after IV edaravone exposure.

Interpretation

The study was ongoing, with additional analyses expected to further inform clinicians and payers.

Conclusion

Real-world data may clarify who receives oral edaravone and how care patterns cluster around treatment.

The authors concluded that these ongoing real-world analyses may help clinicians and payers better understand demographics, clinical characteristics, and healthcare utilization among patients with ALS treated with MTPA oral edaravone.

Main limitations: commercial or Medicare Advantage coverage only, claims coding limitations, possible ALS underdiagnosis, and exclusion of patients no longer enrolled post-index.
