Optimizing HIV Pre-Exposure Prophylaxis Services in Primary Care Settings

A pharmacist-led performance improvement program uses a weekly STI laboratory feed, a monitored dashboard, and structured follow-up to identify PrEP candidates and support ongoing HIV testing, refill, and monitoring needs in primary care.

Eligible PrEP access 30%

Poster cites limited access when prescribing is concentrated in infectious disease specialty care.

HIV follow-up gap >50%

More than half of STI-positive patients lacked follow-up HIV screening in the 2023 baseline review.

PrEP linkage gap <3%

Among STI-positive, HIV-negative patients, fewer than 3% were linked to PrEP therapy.

Monitoring cadence 2-3 mo

Patients on PrEP are targeted for routine HIV testing and refill review every 2-3 months.

Baseline result

<3% linked to PrEP after STI-positive, HIV-negative result

The poster's core finding is a missed prevention opportunity after STI screening, creating the rationale for pharmacist dashboard surveillance.

Objective

Evaluate a pharmacist-led STI screening and PrEP linkage model in primary care.

The intervention is designed to surface high-risk patients from system-wide STI results, close HIV screening loops, connect eligible patients to PrEP, and reduce drop-off during ongoing prevention care.

Conclusion signal

A dashboard-supported pharmacist model is expected to improve STI-to-HIV screening follow-up, increase linkage to PrEP care, and maintain safer PrEP continuation through recurring HIV-negative lab verification.

Why primary care matters

Moving PrEP support closer to routine screening can reduce missed prevention opportunities.

Because many PrEP-eligible patients may not be connected to infectious disease specialists, engaging primary care physicians with pharmacist support can expand identification, medication access assistance, monitoring, and refill continuity.
