Denosumab showed lower fracture risk than second-line bisphosphonate therapy

A U.S. Medicare comparative effectiveness study followed treatment-experienced postmenopausal women with osteoporosis after they switched to denosumab, another oral bisphosphonate, alendronate, or zoledronic acid.

Evidence flow

Claims-based design converted treatment switching into fracture-outcome comparisons

1

Identify switchers

Prior oral BP users newly initiated denosumab, a different oral BP, alendronate, or ZA.

2

Define outcomes

MOP, hip, nonvertebral, non-hip nonvertebral, and hospitalized vertebral fractures.

3

Weight cohorts

IPTW balanced demographics, comorbidities, medication use, utilization, and fracture history.

4

Handle censoring

Doubly robust IPTCW estimated relative risk at 1, 2, 3, and 5 years.

5

Risk reductions generally grew larger with longer denosumab exposure.

Objective

Assess comparative effectiveness of denosumab versus bisphosphonates on fracture outcomes among treatment-experienced postmenopausal women.

Methods

Follow-up began at second-line initiation and ended at fracture, discontinuation, switch, disenrollment, death, data end, or 5 years.

Balance

Most characteristics were initially balanced, but denosumab patients were higher risk; all variables balanced after IPTW.

Results

Risk reductions were observed for hip, nonvertebral, non-hip nonvertebral, hospitalized vertebral, and MOP fractures.

Conclusion

Longer denosumab exposure was associated with greater fracture risk reduction in this second-line osteoporosis setting.

Interpretation guardrails

Strengths and limitations shaped the inference

  • Strengths included large scale, negative-control gating to reduce unmeasured confounding, and a validated claims-based incident fracture algorithm.
  • Residual confounding could not be ruled out because some risk factors, including BMD for the entire cohort, were missing.
  • Hospitalized vertebral fracture findings may not generalize to all vertebral fractures because many are asymptomatic and difficult to capture in claims.
