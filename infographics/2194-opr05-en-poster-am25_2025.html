NASP Annual Meeting 2025 • OPR05 • Denver, CO

Real-world therapeutic dose attainment for valbenazine and deutetrabenazine in tardive dyskinesia

A US claims analysis compared dosing trends for VMAT2 inhibitor initiators with TD. The central finding was stark: all VBZ patients began at the therapeutic threshold, while roughly half of dTBZ patients did not reach it within 6 months.

Results

Therapeutic dose attainment split sharply by starting dose

VBZ 100%

All 3,527 VBZ patients reached therapeutic dose because the starting dosage strength was clinically effective.

dTBZ BID 47.5%

1,029 of 2,166 reached 24 mg/day within 6 months; mean time among attainers was 24 days.

dTBZ XR 54.3%

177 of 326 reached 24 mg/day within 6 months; mean time among attainers was 22 days.

Pairwise comparisons versus VBZ were statistically significant for both dTBZ cohorts (P < 0.001).

Interpretation

Concise conclusion

Within 6 months, roughly half of dTBZ-treated patients did not reach therapeutic dose, while all VBZ-treated patients did.

  • More dTBZ patients experienced dose change after month 1.
  • Nearly 10% of dTBZ attainers later did not maintain therapeutic dosing.
  • The authors call for further study of tolerability, titration difficulty, effectiveness, and downstream consequences of subtherapeutic dosing.

Objective

What the poster set out to compare

The analysis compared real-world dosing trends, therapeutic dose attainment, and change in pharmacy costs among patients with TD who initiated VBZ or dTBZ.
