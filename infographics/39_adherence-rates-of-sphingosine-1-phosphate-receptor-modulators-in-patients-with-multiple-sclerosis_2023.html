Adherence Rates of Sphingosine-1-Phosphate Receptor Modulators in Patients with Multiple Sclerosis

A retrospective claims cohort asked whether oral S1P receptor modulators produced better medication adherence than other oral MS disease-modifying therapies.

Main finding: adherence was high across oral therapies, and S1PRM patients did not have significantly different PDC or adherence than OOT patients.

Evidence flow

How the poster moved from claims to conclusion

1

Population

Adults with MS in fully insured commercial or Medicare data from a large US payor.

2

Exposure

Oral S1P receptor modulators compared with other oral MS disease-modifying therapies.

3

Filters

Excluded class switchers, cladribine users, and patients with less than 6 months of eligibility.

4

Measure

Medication adherence was assessed by proportion of days covered; optimal adherence used PDC ≥ 0.8.

5

Validation

A secondary analysis repeated the comparison in newly initiated patients only.

Adjusted signal

Logistic regression showed no clear adherence advantage for S1PRM

Most selected confidence intervals cross 1.0; age per year was the narrow adjusted signal above 1.0 in the forest plot.

Conclusion

High adherence, no meaningful separation by oral therapy class

No differences in PDC or adherence were found between patients prescribed S1PRMs and those prescribed other oral therapies, and the conclusion was consistent in newly initiated patients.
