Ambulatory pharmacist management supported safe mavacamten access in an integrated HSSP model.

A retrospective review of adults with hypertrophic cardiomyopathy receiving two or more mavacamten dispenses assessed clinical interventions, REMS coordination, adherence, adverse events, and acute-care utilization from May 2022 through March 2023.

Objective

Evaluate whether an ambulatory pharmacist-led HCM program could manage clinical and quality outcomes while meeting REMS requirements.

The program centered on adult patients receiving at least two mavacamten dispenses from the health-system specialty pharmacy.

Data elements included pharmacist interventions, unplanned ED or hospital visits, adverse events, proportion of days covered, and operational metrics.

Evidence flow

The workflow kept REMS screening and reassessment on a fixed cadence.

Month 0 First dispense

Prescreen, initial assessment, first-cycle clinical assessment, and REMS screening.

Month 1 Refill screen

Monthly REMS refill screening and counseling checkpoint.

Months 2-5 Ongoing refill

Drug-interaction review, adherence monitoring, and dose coordination.

Month 6 Reassessment

Clinical reassessment aligned to REMS refill screening.

Months 7-11 Maintenance

Monthly REMS refill screening and patient counseling.

Month 12 Annual review

Annual clinical reassessment, documented every 10 months in the poster workflow.

REMS coordination

Five recurring requirements formed the coordination wheel.

5 REMS domains
  • Drug interaction screening and counseling checklist
  • Dose verification and/or adjustment
  • Patient counseling and REMS education
  • Dispense authorization and ongoing counseling
  • Adverse event reporting

Program outcomes

The early program signal combined high engagement, high adherence, and no HCM-related acute-care utilization.

100% Opt-in rate

All analyzed patients opted into the pharmacist-led HCM care model.

97% Medication adherence

Adherence was measured by proportion of days covered.

0 HCM-related ED or hospital visits

No unplanned acute-care utilization related to HCM was reported.

0 Permanent discontinuations due to systolic heart failure

The poster reported no permanent therapy discontinuation for this safety reason.

4 Adverse events reported

Events were captured through the program monitoring process.

Full REMS audit compliance

Camzyos REMS desktop audits were reported as fully compliant.

Conclusion

Integrated specialty pharmacy infrastructure can expand access to limited-distribution HCM therapy.

The authors concluded that an ambulatory pharmacist-led HCM management program demonstrated effective management of mavacamten therapy and REMS requirements within a health-system specialty pharmacy model.

This model was presented as adaptable for other HSSPs seeking to support access to mavacamten.
