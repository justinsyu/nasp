Retraining transplant pharmacy staff to reduce Medicare Part B billing errors

A single-center pre/post quality improvement project retrained certified pharmacy technicians on recurring Medicare Part B billing errors in post-transplant outpatient prescriptions, then compared error frequency and potential fine exposure before and after the training period.

Retraining content

Three common error mechanisms became the curriculum

  • Early refill or refill-too-soon processing issues.
  • Missing caller relationship to the patient or missing patient residence information on orders.
  • No remaining day supply recorded on the prescription order.

The intervention paired common-error topics with a training checklist and coaching based on technician-specific errors.

Objective

Retrain CPhTs on common monthly billing errors and evaluate changes in error rates and potential fines.

Population

Patients with at least one Medicare Part B prescription billing error in the transplant pharmacy setting.

Method

Single-center, pre/post analysis at Vanderbilt Transplant Pharmacy during the 2021-2022 period.

Result

Average monthly error rates decreased across day supply, refill-too-soon, and missing residency categories.

Conclusion

Identifying common errors and retraining staff was presented as useful and financially prudent.
