Evaluating Health System Time Demands and Challenges of Specialty Pharmacy Accreditation

A survey of health-system specialty pharmacies found accreditation maintenance clustered around scarce dedicated staffing, high policy and quality documentation workload, and reported effects on time available for patient care.

Objective

Map the operational load behind accreditation

The poster evaluated challenges, time commitments, and resource allocation required to achieve and maintain specialty pharmacy accreditation among health-system specialty pharmacies.

  • 5 ptLikert scales captured perceived time intensity and difficulty for accreditation tasks.
  • FTEStaffing resources, weekly hours, and personnel roles were collected to characterize resource models.
  • CareRespondents reported whether accreditation requirements affected time available for patient care.

Conclusion

Dedicated support may preserve clinical capacity

The survey highlights accreditation activities that are both time-consuming and challenging, especially policies and procedures, quality improvement preparation, data reporting, and patient management program work.

Because resource allocation varied substantially, the authors recommend that health-system specialty pharmacies evaluate accreditation strategies and consider dedicated personnel or alternative support models that allow clinical staff to focus on patient care.
