Objective

Validate AI support across the full SLR path, not just one task

The poster describes a methodology for using AI SLR tools with human-in-the-loop curation, then reports validations, time savings, and compliance-oriented safeguards for review best practices.

The central claim is that AI can accelerate major review steps while transparency, replicability, and expert oversight remain required for high-quality SLR findings.

Methods

Six SLR stages are mapped to AI or curated workflow components

1

Search strategy

Smart Search converts a research question into Boolean PubMed strings and maps PICO concepts.

2

Dual screening

Project-specific machine learning supports title, abstract, and full-text review.

3

Evidence extraction

Adaptive Smart Tags extract text, numbers, options, and tabular content with traceable highlights.

4

Critical appraisal

Manual appraisal remains part of the workflow for interpreting study quality.

5

Insight drafting

Curated findings can be shaped into transparent review insights.

6

Quant synthesis

Forest plots, funnel plots, I-squared, SUCRA rankings, odds ratios, and network diagrams are generated.

What is mature

Validated support for repeatable review tasks

  • Search strategy generation reports 76.8-79.6% recall from research-question-to-Boolean-string workflows.
  • Reviewer-level screening reports 82-97% recall with supervised machine learning.
  • PICO extraction reports F1 of 0.74, with study size accuracy at 91%.

What remains curated

Custom extraction is exploratory and needs human review

The poster distinguishes validated workflow acceleration from open-ended extraction of user-specified qualitative and quantitative tags. Those custom outputs remain exploratory and require human curation for SLR use.

AI can cover the full SLR workflow.

AutoLit is described across search, screening, extraction, appraisal, insight drafting, and quantitative synthesis.

Validation is stage-specific.

Performance varies by task, with recall emphasized for search and screening and F1 or accuracy used for extraction.

Oversight is the conclusion, not a footnote.

Transparency, replicability, and expert oversight are presented as central requirements for AI-enabled SLRs.
