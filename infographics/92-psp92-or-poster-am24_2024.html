Study Question

Can a multidisciplinary navigation program reduce access friction for the bleeding disorder community?

The poster examined 2023 outcomes from an integrated specialty pharmacy navigation program that provides education, financial-barrier support, insurance assistance, community-resource facilitation, and emotional support.

155 resolved 24 unresolved

Authors: Justin Lindhorst, Shelby Smoak, Bill Wilbert, Brenda Montgomery King, and Kimberly Epps.

Methods

Retrospective case review with primary and additional support needs captured

1

Cases completed

179 unique patient navigation cases were completed during January through December 2023.

2

Barriers categorized

Each record captured the challenge and outcome, then grouped challenges into eight operational categories.

3

Additional needs added

Secondary and tertiary support needs were identified in 60 cases and added as data points.

4

Outcomes tabulated

Resolution, insurance access, provider or product access, and financial-burden outcomes were summarized.

Conclusion

Integrated navigation addressed present barriers and anticipated future needs

34% of cases had additional points of support identified by navigators.

The poster concludes that a multidisciplinary patient navigation program was effective in helping members of the bleeding disorder community resolve health-system barriers and overcome nonclinical obstacles to care.

Because navigators identified secondary and tertiary needs, the intervention functioned as both a problem-resolution service and a way to preempt future access challenges.
