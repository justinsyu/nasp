Methods

Electronic record variables and a five-question SDOH screen fed regression models.

Medication support

Part 1 result

Secondary adherence was high overall, with smoking and BMI standing out.

  • Smokers had 3.95 higher odds of nonadherence compared with never-smokers.
  • Patients with BMI 30 or higher had lower odds of nonadherence than healthy-weight patients.
  • Hispanic/Latino ethnicity, nonmetropolitan geography, and non-English language without interpreter showed trends.

Background

Oral oncolytics offer convenience and autonomy, but adherence can vary widely across cancer type, measurement method, and setting.

Objective

Identify patient characteristics and SDOH factors associated with secondary and primary oral oncolytic nonadherence.

Methods

Part 1 used mixed-effect logistic regression; Part 2 used logistic regression after retrospective phone-based SDOH assessment.

Key numbers

Part 1 included 1,128 prescriptions from 979 patients; Part 2 included 340 SDOH respondents and 176 nonrespondents.

Intervention point

Smoking status and health literacy emerged as practical markers for targeted adherence support.
