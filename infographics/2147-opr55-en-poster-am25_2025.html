Home Infusion Specialty Drugs: Pharmacist Patient Care Tasks and Time

A National Home Infusion Foundation multicenter time study quantified how pharmacists spend professional time supporting specialty infusion and injection medications in home settings.

Methods

Study flow from referral through two dispensing cycles

1

Expert committee defined scope

Therapy types, dispensing cycles, and pharmacist task categories were determined before data collection.

2

Participating providers tracked time

Pharmacists at self-selected home infusion providers used standardized spreadsheets.

3

Two cycles observed

Work was tracked from patient referral through completion of two dispensing cycles.

4

Tasks categorized

Clinical care, coordination, documentation, monitoring, and preparation work were summarized.

Interpretation Clinical intensity, not just dispensing

The poster frames specialty home infusion pharmacist work as a substantial professional care commitment with staffing and reimbursement implications.

Conclusion

What the poster adds

This study systematically quantified and categorized professional work by home infusion pharmacists caring for specialty infusion medication patients.

The authors concluded that the results underscore the complexity of specialty medication management at home and the substantial pharmacist time committed to clinical care.
