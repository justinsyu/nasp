2023 NASP Annual Meeting | Poster 67

Tablet and capsule forms showed near-identical persistence in two oral oncology cohorts.

CVS Specialty researchers compared prescription-based proportion of days covered (PDC) and adherence for enzalutamide and palbociclib after manufacturers introduced tablet and capsule options.

Objective

Test whether formulation choice changes persistence

Because capsule and tablet products are expected to be bioequivalent, the study asked whether members prescribed different dosage forms had similar adherence after accounting for observable confounders.

Enzalutamide tablet and capsule groups had no insurance type or regional differences. Palbociclib groups differed by age, gender, insurance type, and region, making the regression adjustment especially important.

Methods

Retrospective cohort logic

Identify

Adult Caremark members with enzalutamide or palbociclib fills from Mar 1, 2021 through Mar 1, 2022.

Require coverage

Members needed 6 months continuous eligibility before index and 12 months after index.

Exclude switches

Members were removed for a gap greater than 60 days or switching from capsule to tablet, or vice versa.

Measure PDC

PDC was days with medication on hand divided by days between index fill and exhaust of last fill.

Compare

t-tests, Mann-U tests, chi-square tests, and logistic regression evaluated dosage-form differences.
