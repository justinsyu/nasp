Impact of Patient Assistance Programs on Out-of-Pocket Costs for Oral Oncolytics

A retrospective HSSP analysis found that patient assistance programs were frequently secured for oral oncology fills, helping keep most observed out-of-pocket costs below five dollars despite high average medication package prices.

Objective and methods

Describe the impact of patient assistance programs on OOP costs for cancer patients filling oral oncolytics through HSSPs.

Retrospective cohort Adult and pediatric patients with oncology clinic visits and at least three oral oncolytic fills.
HSSP dispensing lens Fills managed through integrated health system specialty pharmacies from 2020 to 2022.
Cost variables Patient OOP cost, financial assistance presence, primary insurance type, and drug package price.
Descriptive analysis Results summarized by copay range, insurance type, and brand versus generic medication group.

Access problem

Novel oral anticancer agents carry high costs that can increase financial stress for patients and caregivers.

HSSP role

Integrated specialty pharmacy teams are positioned to identify and secure PAP funding during therapy management.

Measured fills

The analysis summarized 33,697 fills and linked OOP cost to financial assistance status.

Observed impact

Most fills landed below $5 OOP, and overall 83% had financial assistance secured.

Policy nuance

Brand support and grant-based assistance appeared to matter differently across insurance and generic groups.

Conclusion: the observational analysis demonstrates the impact of the HSSP model on access to patient assistance program funding for oral oncolytics, with manufacturer support prominent for brand drugs and grant or other PAP funding especially important for government-insured patients receiving generics.
