Biologic Adherence: COVID-19 Vaccine Timing Dilemma

In a rheumatology specialty pharmacy cohort, pharmacist counseling corrected biologic DMARD timing decisions around COVID-19 vaccination and converted some vaccine decliners into new vaccine commitments.

Objective

Measure whether pharmacist education aligned biologic dosing with vaccine timing guidance.

The primary objective was to determine the impact of pharmacist education on patient alignment with American College of Rheumatology guidance for biologic DMARD dosing around COVID-19 vaccination.

The secondary objective was to measure pharmacist impact on COVID-19 vaccination rates.

The poster translates timing corrections into adherence, immune-response, and cost signals.

The cost estimate uses the poster's cited median COVID-19 infection cost of $34,662 per patient.

Guidance gap

Changing and drug-class-specific recommendations created inconsistent advice and patient confusion.

Pharmacist touchpoint

Routine specialty pharmacy assessments created a structured moment to ask about vaccine timing.

Primary correction

Thirty-seven of 62 vaccine-planning patients needed dose-timing counseling.

Vaccine conversion

Twelve previously declining patients committed to vaccination after intervention.

Adherence value

Education protected days on therapy while supporting vaccine response when a hold was appropriate.

Conclusion: specialty pharmacists helped autoimmune patients translate ACR vaccine timing guidance into actionable biologic DMARD plans, preventing inappropriate dosing decisions in 60% of vaccine-planning patients and increasing vaccine acceptance.
