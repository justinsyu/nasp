Oral oncology adverse event reporting via text messaging

Optum Specialty Pharmacy and Optum Care Cancer Center piloted a text-enabled PRO-CTCAE screening workflow to surface oral-oncology adverse events earlier, route higher-severity symptoms into the clinic EHR, and support pharmacist-led counseling and triage.

Objective

Reduce AE severity through earlier intervention.

The pilot replaced isolated phone/fax communication with a patient-reported text pathway and real-time EHR task communication between specialty pharmacy and the oncology clinic.

The intended clinical path was proactive AE screening, pharmacist counseling, and clinic triage before symptoms worsened into potentially preventable emergency or inpatient care.

Methods

A four-step pilot converted patient texts into risk-stratified pharmacy and clinic work.

Offer screening

New oral-oncolytic patients from Optum Care Cancer Center were offered AE screening by text, with phone available when texting was not feasible.

Survey biweekly

Participants completed PRO-CTCAE screening at baseline and every two weeks for a total of 10 weeks.

Score severity

Nausea, vomiting, constipation, diarrhea, pain, shortness of breath, cough, and fatigue were assessed and graded.

Triage response

Low-severity AEs prompted pharmacist consultation; moderate to high severity added clinic triage and an oncologist EHR task.

Discussion

Integrated communication turned symptom reports into action.

The pilot enrolled 24.5% of eligible patients and showed that specialty pharmacy triage plus EHR task communication can create opportunities for early clinic management of AEs.

Of the EHR tasks entered, 31.6% resulted in clinic actions, supporting the authors' conclusion that a larger and longer implementation could produce meaningful cost avoidance.

Conclusion: text-based PRO-CTCAE monitoring helped bridge specialty pharmacy and oncology clinic workflows, enabling earlier AE counseling, triage, and potential healthcare cost avoidance.

Limitations

Small, single-partnership pilot.

The short timeframe and sample size reflect one pharmacy and one oncology clinic; results may differ in broader settings.
