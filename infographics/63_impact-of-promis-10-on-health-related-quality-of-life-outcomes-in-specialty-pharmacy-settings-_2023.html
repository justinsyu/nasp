NASP 2023 | PROMIS-10 in specialty pharmacy

Impact of PROMIS-10 on Health-Related Quality-of-Life Outcomes in Specialty Pharmacy Settings

A retrospective observational study of specialty pharmacies using TherigySTM examined whether PROMIS-10 scores could document health-related quality-of-life outcomes and guide pharmacist interventions.

Objective

Document HRQoL outcomes and the interventions attached to them

The poster evaluated the implementation of PROMIS-10 in specialty pharmacy settings, using TherigySTM as the clinical care management and reporting platform for score capture and intervention documentation.

  • PROMIS-10 T-scores were first categorized as Poor, Fair, Good, Very Good, or Excellent.
  • Categories were consolidated into two analytic groups: Poor-Fair and Good-Excellent.
  • Change was measured from baseline to the last recorded score.

Intervention intensity

Lower baseline HRQoL aligned with more specialty pharmacy activity

In both PROMIS-10 domains, patients with Poor-Fair baseline scores averaged 2.3 interventions per patient. The poster reported higher intervention rates versus Good-Excellent baseline patients in mental and physical health analyses.

Methods flow

From score collection to patient-specific intervention tracking

Enroll eligible patients

Adults at least 18 years old with two or more collected PROMIS-10 scores were included.

Capture scores

TherigySTM was used to collect, manage, and report specialty pharmacy patient data.

Collapse categories

Five T-score labels were consolidated into Good-Excellent and Poor-Fair groups.

Count interventions

Descriptive analysis summarized intervention counts and percentages for the cohort.

Background

Accreditation standards emphasize performance systems that evaluate specialty pharmacy patient outcomes.

Tool

PROMIS-10 is a concise, validated 10-item questionnaire for physical and mental HRQoL.

Use case

Specialty pharmacies can identify low or worsening HRQoL and intervene around patient-specific needs.

Signal

Mental and physical domains showed improvement from Poor-Fair and maintenance within Good-Excellent.

Next question

The authors recommended studying the impact of PROMIS-10 implementation on healthcare utilization.

Conclusion: PROMIS-10 gave specialty pharmacies a structured way to detect HRQoL needs, document interventions, and show improvement in patient-reported mental and physical health status over time.
