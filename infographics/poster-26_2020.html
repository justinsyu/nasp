The Economic Burden of Hemophilia B: A Lifetime Decision Analytic Model

A decision analytic Markov model estimated adult lifetime costs for severe and moderately severe hemophilia B across standard half-life FIX prophylaxis, extended half-life FIX prophylaxis, and FIX on-demand treatment.

Objective

Estimate adult lifetime costs for HB treatment options and identify drivers of overall management cost.

Framework

An expert panel of clinicians, HTA specialists, and patient advocates reached consensus on the model structure.

Model

Four states: no bleed, joint bleed, non-joint bleed, and death, repeated across problem-joint sub-models.

Costs

Base case used a lifetime horizon, 2019 USD, and a 3% discount rate from societal and payer views.

Conclusion

Severe and moderately severe HB carried a modeled burden of more than $20 million per adult patient.

Interpretation

Unmet need framed through cumulative cost

The poster's conclusion was consistent across treatment strategy: modeled adult lifetime costs remained substantial for severe and moderately severe HB.

  • FIX treatment was the leading cost driver.
  • Sensitivity analyses generally supported the base-case direction.
  • Model limitations included economic assumptions and omission of caregiver impact.

The resulting policy signal is direct: reducing treatment burden and durable cost drivers remains an unmet medical need for people with hemophilia B.
