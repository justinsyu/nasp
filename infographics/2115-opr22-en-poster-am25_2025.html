Real-world dosing patterns of biologics in Crohn's disease and ulcerative colitis

A retrospective MarketScan study compared dose escalation, discontinuation, and switching across four biologics used by adults with Crohn's disease (CD) or ulcerative colitis (UC) in the United States.

  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Observational research
  • Commercial + Medicare claims
  • 2016-2023 study window

Biologic classes compared 4

Evidence flow

Claims were transformed into treatment-pattern signals

1

Identify adults

Patients were at least 18 years old with claims evidence for newly initiated adalimumab, infliximab, ustekinumab, or vedolizumab.

2

Anchor the index

The index date was the first claim for the study biologic, with 12 months of baseline and at least 12 months of follow-up required.

3

Define cohorts

CD and UC cohorts were separated by diagnosis claims and exclusions for overlapping IBD or selected autoimmune conditions.

4

Classify patterns

Dose escalation was a 20% or greater maintenance-dose increase or administration at least 7 days early; discontinuation was a 90-day supply gap.

Primary result

Dose escalation was common, but not uniform by biologic

Infliximab had the highest observed escalation in CD, while ustekinumab had the highest observed escalation in UC. Adalimumab had the lowest escalation in both cohorts.

Interpretive takeaway

Dose changes are a routine signal in real-world biologic management

The study frames dose escalation as common across biologics, with disease cohorts often showing similar directions but materially different rates by treatment.

  • Escalation ranged from 20.8% for adalimumab UC to 58.9% for infliximab CD.
  • CD and UC are distinct diseases, but escalation for each biologic was generally directionally similar across the two cohorts.
  • Switching among discontinuers varied by biologic and escalation status, spanning 24.2% to 66.7% in the reported table.

Objective

What the poster asked

Evaluate dosing patterns across biologics in CD and UC in the United States.

Conclusion

What the poster concluded

Dose escalation was common but varied between biologic treatments; discontinuation was higher among non-escalators, and switching was assessed among patients who discontinued index treatment.
