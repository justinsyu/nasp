Cost analysis of once-daily LCPT vs twice-daily IR-Tac for kidney transplant patients

This single-center retrospective claims review compared patient out-of-pocket costs for extended-release tacrolimus (LCPT) and immediate-release tacrolimus (IR-Tac), including the role of Vanderbilt's Medication Access Program (MAP).

Objective

Measure final patient cost after coverage and assistance

The analysis evaluated out-of-pocket cost, defined as the final amount charged to the patient, for LCPT versus IR-Tac in kidney transplant recipients filling prescriptions through the center's transplant pharmacy.

A secondary focus was how much patient cost was covered by the institution's Medication Access Program.

Conclusion

Assistance programs shaped the affordability story

  • Patients receiving LCPT had lower average OOP costs than patients receiving IR-Tac in this claims review.
  • MAP coverage reduced member-paid mean costs to zero in the overall Primary Insurance + MAP group.
  • Patients with high copay or deductible plans, no secondary coverage, and no grant access remain at risk for high OOP costs for both therapies.
