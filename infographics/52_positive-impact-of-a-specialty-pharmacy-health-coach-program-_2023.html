Positive Impact of a Specialty Pharmacy Health Coach Program

Fairview Pharmacy Services evaluated whether integrated health coaching could support specialty pharmacy patients through emotional, social, physical, and coping needs while improving patient-reported health and satisfaction.

Objective

Determine the impact of a health coach on specialty pharmacy patients using global physical and mental health scores, referrals to additional services, and patient satisfaction.

Methods

Coaching was built into therapy management touchpoints

1

Offer or trigger

Patients were offered coaching during an initial therapy management call or after distress, diagnosis, life transition, coping, or caregiver concerns.

2

Initial call

The coach explained the program, confirmed interest, selected focus areas, encouraged goals, and recorded baseline PROMIS scores.

3

Goal sessions

Follow-up calls centered on patient goals and changing social, physical, psychological, and resource needs.

4

Referrals

The coach connected patients to health care, non-health care, behavioral health, social work, and other services.

5

Fifth call

Patients completed follow-up two-item PROMIS global health scores and received a deidentified satisfaction survey.

Patient-reported health

Most measured patients held steady or improved after five sessions

  • 14% improvement
  • 67% no change
  • 19% deterioration

PROMIS two-item global physical and mental health scores were assessed at baseline and session five in 49 patients.

Table pattern

Focus areas were not mutually exclusive

Pattern What it shows
Multiple coaching foci Most patients selected more than one focus area across 1,528 assessments.
Emotional burden Emotional/spiritual and loss/grief categories were the top two focus areas.
Resource connection Non-healthcare resources were the largest referral category, followed by physicians and therapists.

Conclusion

The program's value was strongest at the intersection of coping support and resource navigation

  • Health coaching showed positive impact on physical and mental health measures.
  • The coach made referrals that extended specialty pharmacy support beyond medication management.
  • High satisfaction suggests patients accepted coaching as a useful addition to specialty pharmacy services.
  • The authors concluded that integration may improve outcomes and reduce long-term health care spending.
