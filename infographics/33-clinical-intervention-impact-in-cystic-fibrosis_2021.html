Objective

Determine overall cost avoidance from a pharmacy-based clinical services program.

The intervention targeted patients initiating ELX/TEZ/IVA, a CFTR modulator for patients with at least one F508del mutation. The poster frames the need around a high-relevance population: 84.7% of genotyped cystic fibrosis patients in the cited registry had at least one F508del copy.

Methods flow

Each therapy start moved through a multidisciplinary intervention pathway.

Initial dispense

Patients were included after an initial ELX/TEZ/IVA dispense between October 21, 2019 and April 21, 2020.

Chart review

A pharmacist reviewed the chart before outreach, looking for consultation, DDI, and reconciliation needs.

Counseling call

Pharmacist-led counseling supported therapy initiation, with very low decline rates among reached patients.

Medication reconciliation

Reconciliation requests were initiated when gaps or updates were identified during review.

Nurse follow-up

About one week after shipment, nurse calls assessed adverse drug events related to ELX/TEZ/IVA.

High-need start

ELX/TEZ/IVA initiation created a clear point for specialty pharmacy intervention.

Pharmacist review

Chart review and counseling produced 910 therapy consultations and 331 reconciliation requests.

Safety surveillance

Drug interaction detection was uncommon, while nurse follow-up identified 133 ADEs.

Economic translation

Published cost-avoidance estimates converted intervention types into avoided-cost totals.

Patient value signal

Low counseling-decline rates suggested perceived value of clinician counseling in pharmacy settings.

Conclusion: integrating pharmacist-led initiation support with nurse-led follow-up enhanced patient care during CFTR modulator starts and produced an estimated $91.6K in cost avoidance.
