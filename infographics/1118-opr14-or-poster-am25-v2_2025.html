Identifying and addressing nonadherence risk with a technology-supported pharmacist-led risk stratification process

Penn State Health Specialty Pharmacy piloted a TherigySTM-enabled risk model to help health-system specialty pharmacy teams target adherence support for rheumatoid arthritis patients most likely to need intervention.

Intervention model

Technology flagged risk; pharmacists converted the signal into outreach.

Objective: describe a technology-supported HSSP risk stratification model for targeted RA adherence support, then observe adherence barriers and outcomes.

1

Identify risk

RA patients in specialty services were classified as low, medium, or high risk of nonadherence inside the patient management platform.

2

Target outreach

Pharmacists proactively contacted high- and medium-risk patients, assessed barriers, and delivered adherence interventions.

3

Reassess outcomes

Patients were followed after three months and the nonadherence risk model was rerun to measure risk movement.

Clinical outcomes

Intervention follow-up showed resolution and risk movement.

56% Encountered adherence issues and connected with a pharmacist for clinical intervention.
80% Reported adherence problems and resolved them by three months after intervention.
59% Experienced a reduction in nonadherence risk level.

Risk reduction split: 24% improved by one level, 35% improved by two levels.

Conclusion

A model-assisted pharmacist workflow helped triage adherence support before adherence dropped further.

  • Risk stratification gave HSSP pharmacists a practical way to focus time on RA patients most likely to need support.
  • Proactive interventions reduced nonadherence risk and addressed patient-specific barriers.
  • The process may extend to other chronic specialty populations, including dermatology, inflammatory bowel disease, and inflammatory arthritis.
