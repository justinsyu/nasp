Medication Adherence Post-Implementation of a PMP

UF Health Jacksonville evaluated whether a Patient Management Program helped specialty pharmacy patients maintain strong proportion of days covered after PMP implementation.

Objective

Evaluate whether PMP implementation optimized care and PDC

Primary outcome

Nearly all included patients met the adherence threshold

PDC ≥ 80% was the poster's adherence definition; 155 patients reached that mark after PMP implementation.

PDC = days supply dispensed / total days Proportion of Days Covered

Methods

Single-center retrospective cohort

UF Health Jacksonville Ambulatory Pharmacy described specialty pharmacy PDC after PMP implementation from August 1, 2018 to March 30, 2020.

  • Included adults 18 years or older with gastroenterology, hepatology, neurology, or rheumatology specialty medications.
  • Excluded patients with concomitant cancer diagnosis or specialty pharmacy use for other disease states.
  • Secondary aims included pre/post PDC comparison, pharmacy contact counts, non-adherence categories, and compliance with PMP timing policy.

Interpretation

High adherence, but documentation needed reinforcement

Signal Poster pattern Action implied
PDC 95% met PDC ≥ 80% Continue PMP as adherence-support infrastructure.
Contacts Mean 3 per patient Maintain pharmacist touchpoints across refills.

Conclusion: the PMP was associated with a very high post-implementation share of specialty pharmacy patients meeting the PDC adherence threshold, while early documentation inconsistency highlighted a need for informatics support and staff re-education.
