NASP 2020 Poster 14

Technician-driven 7-day post-transplant discharge follow-up call

Wake Forest Baptist Health evaluated an integrated specialty pharmacy touchpoint for abdominal, heart, and bone marrow transplant recipients after discharge. The call reviewed pharmacy services, billing, refills, medication changes, and need for pharmacist counseling.

Objective

Measure whether an early specialty pharmacy call could connect, retain, and triage transplant patients.

The evaluation focused on average time to call completion, patient retention after the post-transplant discharge call, early medication changes, and pharmacist interventions needed during the call.

The poster frames the touchpoint as a relationship-building moment for patients newly exposed to lifelong specialty pharmacy services after transplant.

Methods

IRB-approved single-center retrospective chart review.

Included abdominal, heart, and bone marrow transplant patients with a completed post-transplant discharge follow-up call from November 1, 2019 through June 1, 2020.

The specialty pharmacy was embedded in an academic medical center and supported transplant discharge prescription services.

Technician-driven workflow

Three-step handoff from discharge delivery to refill planning.

Day1

Discharge prescription services

  • Medications delivered to patient.
  • Specialty pharmacy services and welcome packet reviewed.
Day7

Post-discharge follow-up call

  • Medication changes documented.
  • Updated prescriptions requested as needed.
  • Pharmacist connection offered as needed.
NeedRefill

Refill call

  • Medications refilled.
  • Additional changes documented.
  • Next refill call scheduled.

Discussion signals

What the implementation surfaced

  • Most patients were reached at 7 days post-discharge.
  • Relationship building may have contributed to retention.
  • Timely documentation enabled proactive updated-prescription requests.
  • Limitations included no comparator group, free-text documentation variation, and role rotation.

Conclusion

A timely checkpoint after transplant discharge

The 7-day call offered an early opportunity to reinforce specialty pharmacy information and identify therapy changes after discharge.

The process was considered successful, with most patients able and willing to continue filling with WFBH Specialty Pharmacy. Future opportunities included technology-enabled alternative contact methods.
