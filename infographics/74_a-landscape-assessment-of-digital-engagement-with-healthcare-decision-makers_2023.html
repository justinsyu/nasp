NASP 2023 / Poster 74

A landscape assessment of digital engagement with healthcare decision-makers.

A targeted web-based literature review found that healthcare decision-makers increasingly prefer digital resources for formulary review, especially when biopharma communication is early, tailored, interactive, and available asynchronously.

Objective

Analyze current research trends in digital communication between HCDMs and biopharma companies.

Specialty drug reviews require balancing clinical effectiveness, product cost, and patient access. The poster frames digital engagement as a way to make product information easier for HCDMs to find, review, and clarify during formulary decision-making.

Best-practices framework

Digital engagement works best as an operating model, not a file repository.

Platform first, strategy second, evidence always current. The poster's framework connects access infrastructure with timing, tailoring, interactivity, and bidirectional communication.
  1. Develop or identify a comprehensive digital platform. Secure, reliable, asynchronous access with a feedback path for missing or low-quality materials.
  2. Build a communication strategy and timeline. Plan when HCDMs may need specific evidence, such as phase 3 data before product launch.
  3. Communicate early with HCDMs. Provide unmet need, disease burden, pre-approval information exchange, and clinical trial evidence as available.
  4. Create interactive digital materials. Examples include interactive dossiers, disease modules, budget models, economic models, and short patient-experience videos.
  5. Tailor resources to the HCDM audience. Organization type and decision-making level influence what evidence is most useful.

Audience tailoring patterns

Different HCDM settings emphasized different digital content needs.

65%

Integrated delivery networks associated with health plans expressed high interest in disease trends.

50%

Integrated delivery networks not associated with a health plan showed higher interest in interactive budget models.

National vs regional

National HCDMs wanted clinical data and pipeline insights, while regional HCDMs wanted more locally relevant evidence.

Interactive

Digital materials should make representative meetings more engaging and meaningful.

Tailored

Resources should reflect HCDM organization type, decision-making level, and local evidence needs.

Accessible

Easy access and asynchronous review support the way HCDMs evaluate specialty products.

Bidirectional

HCDMs need ways to ask clarifying questions and provide feedback to biopharma companies.

Early

Earlier communication may help prevent formulary review delays that affect patient access.

Limitations

The evidence base was directional rather than definitive.

The qualitative assessment was restricted to sources returned by the selected search terms, so identified trends were not all-inclusive.

Primary research with structured methodologies was limited, leaving uncertainty about the full extent of digital communication's impact on HCDM-biopharma relationships.

Conclusion

Digital platforms are becoming a preferred route for comprehensive formulary evidence exchange.

The review found increased utilization of digital communication between HCDMs and biopharma companies, with a need for stronger market research to quantify how these trends affect specialty pharmacy and patient access.
