Improving employee engagement in a hybrid work setting at an integrated health system specialty pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health's outpatient specialty pharmacy tracked whether engagement could be maintained or improved as most staff moved into a hybrid work model.

Objective

Maintain or improve engagement while preserving workplace social support

The specialty pharmacy moved most staff into a hybrid model in 2023, creating a need to counter possible isolation and disengagement with structured recognition, team-building, wellness, and management support.

Twice-yearly engagement measurement paired with ongoing team support

Benchmark result

Every measured score was above benchmark

Compared with 2022, 35 scores increased, no scores decreased, and all 42 survey scores remained above the health-system benchmark after implementation of the hybrid staffing model.

Interpretation

Engagement gains likely reflected a broader support ecosystem

  • Enhanced recruitment and specialty training helped address staffing pressure and supported efficient work.
  • Peer support and wellness resources, including meditation, reiki, chair massage, and therapy dog visits, may have contributed.
  • Administrative support may limit direct transferability to smaller health systems.

Why it matters

Hybrid work did not dilute the employee experience signal

The poster links a proactive employee-engagement program with improved 2023 survey performance, suggesting that social support can be designed into a hybrid specialty pharmacy model rather than left to chance.
