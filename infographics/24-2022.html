Collaborative Cancer Nutrition Referral Program

Yale New Haven Health built a specialty pharmacy referral pathway so oncology patients receiving oral chemotherapy could be screened for nutrition support needs, referred to Smilow registered dieticians, and tracked through outreach and consultation.

Interpretation

What made the program work, and what threatened durability.

Objective

Develop and implement a referral program focused on nutrition counseling for health system oncology patients receiving oral chemotherapy.

Discussion signals

  • About 1,600 oncology patients were assessed by specialty pharmacists for nutrition support needs.
  • The standard of practice and interdisciplinary collaboration were cited as key contributors to program success.
  • Rapid OPS growth and falling Q4 referral volume created a need for new staff training and refresher education.

Bottom line

A collaborative nutrition referral pathway can extend HSSP SDoH response beyond identification.

The poster concludes that an interdisciplinary nutrition referral program for oral chemotherapy patients can empower health system specialty pharmacies to identify and refer social determinants of health issues for specialty patients.

Future work centered on training new staff, refreshing current staff, optimizing the workflow, expanding to other disease states, and adding clinical endpoint documentation.
