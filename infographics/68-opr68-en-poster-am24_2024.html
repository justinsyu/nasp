Efficacy and Safety of Lebrikizumab in Patients With Skin of Color and Moderate-to-Severe Atopic Dermatitis

Objective

Measure clinical response in a historically under-represented population

The poster reports interim 16-week safety and efficacy findings for lebrikizumab in patients with moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis and skin of color.

It also highlights objective assessment of pigment, erythema, and post-inflammatory hyper- or hypo-pigmentation, including PDCA-Derm™.

Why

Evidence gap

Moderate-to-severe AD data are limited for populations traditionally under-represented in clinical trials.

Who

Skin of color

The cohort required Fitzpatrick Phototype IV-VI and included 80% Black or African American patients.

How

Open-label treatment

Patients received lebrikizumab 250 mg Q2W through the Week 16 interim assessment.

What

Signs and symptoms

Responses were assessed by EASI, IGA, Pruritus NRS, patient measures, pigment tools, biomarkers, and microbiome endpoints.

So what

Week 16 signal

Lebrikizumab improved AD signs and symptoms, with no serious adverse events in this interim analysis.

Conclusion

Interim interpretation

ADmirable is described as the first Phase 3b clinical trial of lebrikizumab for moderate-to-severe AD in patients with skin of color and Fitzpatrick Phototype IV-VI.

After 16 weeks, lebrikizumab improved clinician- and patient-reported signs and symptoms, and PDCA-Derm™ detected improvement in hyperpigmented lesions.
