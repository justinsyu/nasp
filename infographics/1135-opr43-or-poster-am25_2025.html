Patient Reported Outcomes and Adherence among Patients with Multiple Sclerosis Treated with Disease Modifying Therapies

Yale New Haven Health reviewed specialty pharmacy clinical activities for people with multiple sclerosis receiving self-administered disease modifying therapies, linking high adherence with frequent pharmacist follow-up and favorable patient-reported outcomes.

Objective: evaluate patient-reported outcomes and adherence among people with multiple sclerosis on oral or injectable self-administered DMTs.

Key readout

High adherence stayed above benchmark while pharmacist touchpoints accumulated.

The poster describes an integrated health system specialty pharmacy using disease-specific patient management activities after a May 2023 platform migration. The analysis period was October 1, 2023 through September 30, 2024.

Platform context

The specialty pharmacy migrated to a disease-specific patient management platform in May 2023.

Program support

pwMS were enrolled in an MS disease program with targeted support tools and recurring activities.

Adherence signal

Observed MPR and PDC exceeded the stated benchmark and literature values cited in the discussion.

PRO signal

Most reached patients reported healthy days, preserved activities, and good or better quality of life.

Limitations

PROs reflect reached patients, and impairment may vary by MS type, age at diagnosis, and relapse history.

Conclusion

Specialty clinical pharmacists were positioned as a key support for adherence and outcomes in MS DMT therapy.

The poster concludes that specialty clinical pharmacists can improve patient outcomes and adherence among people with multiple sclerosis through close monitoring, follow-up, medication access support, and coordinated care.
