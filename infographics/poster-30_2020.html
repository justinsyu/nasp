Understanding biosimilar purchasing patterns within a home infusion and specialty pharmacy GPO

A case study tracked quarterly purchase growth for a contracted biosimilar against a non-contracted innovator biologic after competitive pricing, education, communication, and product support were offered to participating pharmacies.

Objective

Understand purchase behavior after a biosimilar agreement

The case study compared purchasing growth for a contracted biosimilar with a non-contracted innovator biologic used for inflammatory conditions among participating home infusion and specialty pharmacies.

Market Context

Why uptake was difficult

  • Biologics represented almost 40% of prescription drug spending and 70% of prescription spending growth from 2010 to 2015.
  • From 2014 to 2018, biologic drug spending was recorded at $125 billion.
  • Recent analyses cited by the poster estimated biosimilars had achieved only 9% of the projected $1 billion in savings.

Methods

A focused retrospective purchasing-data readout

1 Define cohort Include pharmacies with at least one contracted biosimilar purchase during the study period.
2 Set baseline Use 4Q17 as the baseline for both product groups.
3 Track quarterly sales Analyze growth each quarter from 1Q18 through 4Q19 versus baseline.
4 Layer support Provide education, communication, sales training, and manufacturer engagement at intervals.

Support Flow

Education and contracting activity preceded the growth curve

June 2016 Approval message included in a Clinical Practice Update.
October 2017 Contract announced; sales team training and clinical spotlight released.
Nov-Dec 2017 Member webinar plus targeted communication on savings, coverage, and support.
2018 Clinical Practice Update feature and live manufacturer education with MHA sales team.
Ongoing Continued clinical education, communication, and contracted product support.

Discussion

Growth despite structural barriers

The poster identified patent and legal action, payer rebate dynamics, prescriber inertia, and an evolving regulatory pathway as major obstacles to US biosimilar uptake. The purchasing trend could not control for those confounders, but it showed meaningful gains were possible in this GPO setting.

Conclusion

Pricing plus support may help drive adoption

Manufacturer agreements that combine competitive biosimilar purchase pricing with clinical education and operational support may help participating home infusion and specialty pharmacies increase biosimilar adoption and move toward expected cost savings.
