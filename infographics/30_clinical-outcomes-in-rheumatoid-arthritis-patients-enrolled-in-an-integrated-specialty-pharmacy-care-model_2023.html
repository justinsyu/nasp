2023 poster 30 | Rheumatoid arthritis outcomes

Clinical outcomes in rheumatoid arthritis patients enrolled in an integrated specialty pharmacy care model

A multisite retrospective analysis followed RA patients in health-system specialty pharmacy programs, using RAPID3 and patient-reported outcomes to show how integrated pharmacy care translated into symptom, flare, and activity-burden signals.

Outcome signal

Patient-reported outcomes point in the same direction as RAPID3.

855 patients had any negative change from baseline RAPID3, reported as overall improvement.
694 patients had a clinically meaningful RAPID3 response or met treat-to-target goals.
1,028 patients reported no more than one RA flare in the past year.
987 patients reported no missed school, work, activities, or planned events.

How the evidence was built

Three-step outcome capture across multiple health-system specialty pharmacies.

1

Select outcomes

Change in RAPID3 score, RA flares, pain score, and absenteeism were selected for RA reporting.

2

Deploy fields

Discrete clinical outcome fields were implemented in the patient management platform for multiple HSSPs.

3

Analyze eligible patients

Patients needed a pharmacist touchpoint, baseline and follow-up RAPID3, and at least 84 days between assessments.

RAPID3 interpretive frame

Treat-to-target meant low activity or near remission.

Near remission RAPID3 score <= 3
Low activity RAPID3 score 3.1-6
Moderate RAPID3 score 6.1-12
High RAPID3 score 12.1-30

Clinically meaningful improvement was defined as a RAPID3 change of -3.6 or greater and/or meeting treat-to-target goals.

Objective

Demonstrate clinical outcomes in RA patients enrolled within an integrated HSSP model.

Setting

Multisite retrospective analysis across 25 health systems during January to December 2022.

Measures

RAPID3 change, treat-to-target status, flare count, pain score, and missed activities.

Signal

60% improved on RAPID3, 49% had a clinically meaningful response, and 69% reported no missed days.

Future direction

Electronic PRO collection may simplify capture, improve response rates, and strengthen longitudinal monitoring.

Conclusion distilled

Integrated care creates a broader outcomes lens.

The poster concludes that patients enrolled in an integrated HSSP patient management program had clinically meaningful responses to therapy, reflected by disease severity scores and patient-reported measures.

  • RAPID3 and PROs help guide comprehensive disease management.
  • Absenteeism measures connect disease burden to quality of life.
  • Electronic PRO methods are a next step for stronger data capture.
