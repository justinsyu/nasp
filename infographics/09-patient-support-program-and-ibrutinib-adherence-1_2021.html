Patient Support Program and Ibrutinib Adherence

A retrospective specialty pharmacy claims study evaluated whether a bi-directional text-messaging patient support program improved ibrutinib refill adherence among adults with CLL/SLL.

Methods

A pharmacy claims cohort was followed from the first observed ibrutinib claim through fixed PDC windows.

Identify cohort

Adults aged at least 18 years with one or more ibrutinib claims indicating CLL/SLL use.

Define exposure

Enrollment in a bi-directional text PSP that delivered dosing and refill reminders.

Match groups

1:1 propensity score matching balanced observed sociodemographic and clinical characteristics.

Measure PDC

PDC was calculated in 3-month intervals from 1 to 9 months; PDC greater than 0.8 defined adherence.

Cohort flow

The analysis moved from a broad specialty pharmacy claims cohort to equal matched groups.

Table 1 pattern

Matching narrowed the original age and insurance differences.

Before matching, PSP enrollees were younger and more often privately insured. After matching, the comparison centered on two equal 200-patient groups with similar average age, sex mix, region, plan type, and prior ibrutinib use.

  • Mean age after matching: 66.9 years for non-enrollees and 66.7 years for PSP enrollees.
  • Male patients after matching: 68.0% for non-enrollees and 70.0% for PSP enrollees.
  • Private plans after matching: 65.0% for non-enrollees and 61.5% for PSP enrollees.

Care gap

Oral oncolytics depend on sustained patient adherence outside infusion-centered care.

Support model

Patients received timely dosing and refill reminders through a bi-directional text program.

Claims evidence

Specialty pharmacy data provided objective refill records from November 2016 through December 2018.

Matched comparison

Propensity matching compared 200 PSP enrollees with 200 non-enrollees.

Observed signal

PSP enrollees had significantly higher refill adherence through 9 months.

Objective and limitations

What the poster asks readers to take forward.

  • Objective: Assess the impact of a specialty pharmacy text-messaging PSP on ibrutinib refill adherence.
  • Conclusion: Enrollment was associated with significantly improved refill adherence among matched CLL/SLL patients.
  • Primary caution: Claims data did not include disease stage, line of therapy, prognosis factors, or reasons for nonadherence.
  • Next step: Larger and longer studies are needed to evaluate PSP effects more comprehensively.

Conclusion: in a 1:1 matched CLL/SLL cohort, a bi-directional text-based patient support program was associated with higher ibrutinib refill adherence at 3, 6, and 9 months, with the largest reported lift at 9 months.
