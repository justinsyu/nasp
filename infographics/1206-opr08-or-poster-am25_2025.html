Evidence Flow

Five high-touch phases create a controlled gene therapy pathway

Access & eligibility

3+ months before
  • Benefits investigation and medical benefit prior authorization.
  • Appeal, peer review, external review, and single-case agreement as needed.

Clinical readiness

Pre-infusion
  • Pathogenic DMD deletion confirmation and anti-AAVrh74 titers.
  • Baseline CMP, LFTs, GGT, bilirubin, troponin-I, and CBC.

Team activation

30 days before
  • Providers, nursing, pharmacy, revenue cycle, and supply chain align.
  • Specialty pharmacist documents initial assessment and family education.

Procurement & infusion

14 days to day 0
  • Dosing weight locked, drug order submitted, and steroid access confirmed.
  • Final education, inpatient preparation, IV administration, and reaction monitoring.

Post-infusion watch

Weeks 1 to 9 months
  • Weekly pharmacist follow-up in the first 4 weeks.
  • LFT/GGT/bilirubin weekly for 12 weeks and troponin-I weekly for 4 weeks.

Main Interpretation

High-risk gene therapy depends on operational pharmacy infrastructure, not just infusion capability.

The pattern is a sustained coordination load: access negotiation, medication procurement, protocol checks, steroid management, family education, and structured safety surveillance.

Conclusion

Replicable framework for high-touch, high-risk therapy

DELA implementation required intensive specialty pharmacy participation across acquisition and management. The authors position this model as a framework for other institutions implementing similarly complex therapies.

Next Steps

Supportive care remains a moving target

The poster notes that recent sentinel events have prompted reconsideration of supportive care protocols, with ongoing research focused on optimizing regimens to improve DELA infusion safety.
