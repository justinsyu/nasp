Extended adherence and persistence to HIV PrEP in a multidisciplinary PrEP clinic

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy evaluated adults initiating emtricitabine-tenofovir disoproxil fumarate for HIV PrEP through a multidisciplinary clinic, measuring medication coverage, persistence, side effects, and discontinuation reasons.

Objective

Describe PrEP medication adherence and persistence in a multidisciplinary clinic.

The analysis focused on patients initiating PrEP with prescriptions filled by Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy. Adherence was measured by proportion of days covered, and persistence was based on patient-reported discontinuation date or last fill plus days' supply.

Multidisciplinary care model

The pharmacist role spanned the full PrEP journey, not just dispensing.

PrEP assessment

Provider-led review covered drug interactions, risk factors, comorbidities, baseline labs, and screening.

Medication access

Technicians evaluated insurance, preferred pharmacy, prior authorization needs, and expected cost.

Access pathways

The team managed prior authorizations, appeals, and manufacturer assistance for uninsured or underinsured patients.

Counseling

Pharmacists addressed administration, adherence, side effects, storage, refill management, monitoring, and contact points.

Monitoring

Follow-up assessed adherence, efficacy, adverse effects, ongoing risk, need for therapy, and financial assistance.

Methods in brief

Single-center retrospective cohort.

  • Adult patients initiated emtricitabine-tenofovir disoproxil fumarate for PrEP.
  • Primary outcomes were PDC adherence and persistence over variable follow-up.
  • Secondary outcomes were side effects and reasons for discontinuation.

Low PDC context

Most lower PDC cases had interpretable causes.

  • Therapy holds related to IBS exacerbation, insurance reinstatement, or nausea.
  • One patient filled PrEP at two pharmacies, complicating coverage capture.
  • The poster frames discontinuations as appropriate in context.

Conclusion: in a multidisciplinary PrEP clinic with integrated specialty pharmacy support, PrEP users showed high adherence, sustained persistence through at least 18 months, few side effects, and discontinuations that largely reflected transfer of care or appropriate clinical reassessment.
