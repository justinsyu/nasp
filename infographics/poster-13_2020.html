Evaluating the Benefit of a Clinically-Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy Care Model in Treating Rheumatologic Diseases

A retrospective case-control review compared adults with rheumatoid arthritis or psoriatic arthritis who received health system specialty pharmacy clinical support alone versus clinical support plus HSSP dispensing.

  • Multi-center retrospective study
  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Psoriatic arthritis
  • RAPID3 patient-reported outcome

RAPID3 trajectory

Dispense opt-in patients started lower and improved across both follow-ups.

RAPID3 scores range from near remission to high disease severity. In this poster, both groups improved from baseline, with the HSSP-dispensed group showing a larger reported change at first follow-up and overall.

Outcome interpretation

Clinical integration plus dispensing is the poster's central signal.

No standout Drug class, payor, demographic, and monetary trend analyses did not identify one superior agent or class.

Patient management pathway

The HSSP model adds pharmacy touchpoints around the provider's rheumatology plan.

Provider starts or modifies therapy

Patient enrollment and prior authorization begin inside the health system workflow.

Financial access and pharmacist assessment

Need for financial assistance is assessed, and the pharmacist completes patient evaluation.

Same-day or next-day coordination

Opt-in patients receive synchronized fills, shipment, packaging, and accessible documentation.

Re-evaluation and team support

Patients can access face-to-face support plus 24/7 phone, text, and email contact.

Conclusion

The authors reported stronger RAPID3 improvement with HSSP dispensing added to clinical services.

The opt-in HSSP dispensing cohort produced 1.75 times better baseline-to-first-collection RAPID3 change and 1.33 times better overall change than opt-out outside-pharmacy patients. Head-to-head medication comparisons did not show evidence of one agent or class outperforming the others.

RAPID3 combines patient-reported function, pain, and global estimate of status.
