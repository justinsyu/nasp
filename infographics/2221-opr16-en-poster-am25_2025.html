Objective

Can elacestrant serve as the endocrine backbone for abemaciclib after CDK4/6 inhibitor exposure?

1

Resistance setting

ER+/HER2- metastatic disease eventually progresses after first-line endocrine therapy plus CDK4/6 inhibition.

2

Combination rationale

An oral SERD plus abemaciclib targets endocrine resistance and cell-cycle pathway dependence.

3

RP2D selected

ELECTRA phase 1b identified elacestrant 345 mg QD plus abemaciclib 150 mg BID as the phase 2 dose.

4

Pooled update

ELECTRA Cohort 3 and ELEVATE Arm C were pooled for updated safety and preliminary efficacy.

Methods

Two studies, one RP2D lens

  • Ongoing ELECTRA phase 1b portion and ELEVATE phase 2 Arm C were included in the pooled update.
  • Patients had prior endocrine therapy plus CDK4/6 inhibitor exposure, excluding prior abemaciclib in the advanced setting.
  • The pooled RP2D safety population was ELECTRA Cohort 3 plus ELEVATE Arm C (n=42); efficacy-evaluable pooled population was n=38.

Conclusion

Clinically important activity across ESR1 status

  • Elacestrant plus abemaciclib showed preliminary efficacy regardless of ESR1 mutation status.
  • The regimen is positioned as a potential all-oral option that could extend PFS and delay chemotherapy or ADC-based regimens.
  • The phase 2 portion of elacestrant 345 mg QD plus abemaciclib 150 mg BID is ongoing.
