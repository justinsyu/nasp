Clinical outcomes of migraine patients receiving CGRP antagonists from an integrated health system specialty pharmacy

A retrospective NYU Langone Health Specialty Pharmacy analysis linked patient-reported monthly migraine days with adherence, duration of therapy, diagnosis category, and CGRP treatment status.

Methods

Retrospective real-world analysis of CGRP antagonist patients enrolled in specialty pharmacy management.

Identify adults Episodic or chronic migraine patients receiving a CGRP antagonist from NYU Langone Health Specialty Pharmacy in 2023.
Apply criteria Included patients enrolled for at least 4 months with both baseline and follow-up migraine-day counts.
Extract data Electronic medical record and specialty pharmacy management system data supplied outcomes and covariates.
Measure adherence Medication adherence was summarized with proportion of days covered, with a cohort mean of 94.6%.
Model change Ordinal logistic regression evaluated improved, unchanged, and declined monthly migraine-day categories.

Cohort shape

Objective

Evaluate the percent reduction in patient-reported monthly migraine days among CGRP-treated patients managed by an integrated HSSP.

Limited data were available on real-world CGRP outcomes in integrated health system specialty pharmacies. This study tested whether results in pharmacy-managed care aligned with reductions reported in clinical trials.

Clinical gap

Real-world outcomes for CGRP antagonists in integrated HSSPs were limited.

Data capture

Baseline and follow-up monthly migraine-day counts anchored the primary endpoint.

Overall effect

The cohort showed a 35.7% average reduction in monthly migraine days.

Largest responder group

Patients new to CGRP medication had a 44.7% reduction from baseline.

Care-model signal

Findings support CGRP effectiveness when managed through an integrated HSSP model.

Conclusion: CGRP antagonist therapy managed by an integrated health system specialty pharmacy was associated with clinically meaningful real-world reductions in monthly migraine days, especially for patients new to therapy and those with higher baseline migraine frequency.
