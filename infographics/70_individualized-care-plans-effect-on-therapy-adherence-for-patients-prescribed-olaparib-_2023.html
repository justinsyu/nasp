Individualized Care Plans' Effect on Therapy Adherence for Patients Prescribed Olaparib

Biologics by McKesson and Arine evaluated whether nurse-led, symptom-focused Care Plans in a Risk-Based Care program were associated with longer time on olaparib therapy among patients at high risk for medication nonadherence.

Objective

Did personalized nurse-led Care Plans extend time on olaparib therapy?

The study compared time on therapy between high-risk olaparib patients who received a Care Plan and high-risk patients who did not. Within the Care Plan group, it also explored whether time on therapy differed by symptom resolution and dose reduction.

  • Data source: Biologics by McKesson specialty pharmacy records, including dispenses, indication, risk level, Care Plan status, symptoms, discontinuation, and adverse events documented by clinicians.
  • Follow-up: From first olaparib dispense until treatment discontinuation or the end of the study period.
  • Primary outcome: Time on therapy, defined as days between first fill and last fill plus the days' supply of the last fill.

Risk-Based Care program

High-risk patients were routed to a dedicated nurse and tailored Care Plans.

Day 0 intake

Risk assessment during counseling identifies barriers to adherence and symptom concerns.

High-risk flag

Patients at high risk for nonadherence are enrolled in the Risk-Based Care workflow.

Care Plan

Nurses create personalized, symptom-focused plans tailored to patient barriers and goals.

Outreach cadence

Frequency is based on patient need; a nurse can coordinate monthly refills and remain the single point of contact.

Reassessment

Risk is reassessed at the Day 14 check-in and as new Care Plan needs emerge.

Methods

Retrospective cohort design with nonparametric comparison of therapy duration.

  • Index date was the first olaparib dispense; patients were followed until discontinuation or the study end.
  • Time on therapy was compared using the Mann-Whitney U test between Care Plan and control groups.
  • Subgroup analyses compared Care Plan patients by symptom resolution and dose decrease status.

Conclusion

Nurse-led, personalized care was associated with longer therapy continuation.

The authors concluded that the Care Plan group had statistically significantly longer time on therapy and a lower risk of discontinuation than the control group, with the strongest effect among patients who experienced symptom resolution or dose modification.

The findings support a specialty pharmacy-led, personalized nursing model for helping patients maintain oral oncology treatment.

Reference frame

Why symptom management mattered

The background emphasized that under-detected and under-managed cancer symptoms can contribute to medication nonadherence, early discontinuation, and worse health outcomes.

  1. Patients with cancer often receive fragmented care across multiple professionals and settings.
  2. Approximately half of patients with cancer may experience fatigue, pain, or distress without communicating symptoms to providers.
  3. Specialty pharmacy case management may help coordinate complex oral oncology care at home.
