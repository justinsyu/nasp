Ambulatory Clinical Pharmacist Impact on Coverage Outcomes for Specialty Oncology Medications

A New York health-system specialty pharmacy evaluated whether remote ambulatory clinical pharmacist support improved third-party prior authorization and appeal outcomes for adult oncology patients starting specialty medications.

Objective

Measure how pharmacist review changed coverage decisions

The ACP program added remote pharmacist support alongside prescribers, pharmacy liaisons, and patient support advocates. The evaluation focused on PA and appeal approval rates, program volume, and prescription turnaround time.

Evidence flow

Where the ACP entered the coverage workflow

Prescription ordered

Provider sends a specialty oncology prescription to the health-system specialty pharmacy.

Benefits checked

Patient support advocate validates eligibility through benefits investigation.

PA or appeal prepared

PSA prepares documentation for payer submission and status tracking.

ACP review added

Pharmacist reviews all new appeals and validates PA recommendations before submission.

Outcome and fill

Liaison communicates the coverage result, then the medication is filled by HSSP or an external pharmacy.
