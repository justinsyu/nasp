Persistence of VMAT2 inhibitor therapy for Tourette syndrome and chronic tic disorders

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy reviewed newly initiated VMAT2 inhibitor therapy in adults with chronic tic disorders. The core signal was not initiation, but durability: half discontinued before 12 months, usually after adverse events early in therapy.

Evidence flow

The poster's logic in four moves

Clinical gap Traditional tic pharmacotherapy can be limited by tolerability and efficacy concerns.
Study question How often do adults newly started on deutetrabenazine, tetrabenazine, or valbenazine remain on therapy at 12 months?
Observed pattern Only 5 of 14 were persistent through the full study period; 7 discontinued before follow-up ended.
Interpretation Adverse events were the main driver, with most events reported within 100 days of initiation.

Setting

Academic medical center with an integrated specialty pharmacy.

Design

Retrospective cohort using data from January 1, 2018 through December 31, 2020.

Included

Adults at least 18 years old with chronic tic disorder diagnosis and VMAT2i prescribed during the study period.

Sources

Electronic health record, specialty pharmacy management system, and pharmacy claims.

Outcomes

Time to discontinuation, reason for discontinuation, and adverse events resulting from therapy.

Objective

What the authors set out to measure

Assess the persistence rate of newly initiated VMAT2 inhibitor therapy for chronic tic management at 12 months after initiation.

Agents captured

Deutetrabenazine, tetrabenazine, and valbenazine.

Conclusion

Persistence was limited by early tolerability problems

Half of patients discontinued VMAT2i therapy before the end of 12-month follow-up, and adverse events were the main driver of discontinuation. The authors called for further studies in patients with common comorbid conditions.
