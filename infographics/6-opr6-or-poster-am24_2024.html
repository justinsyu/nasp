Values and pitfalls of using RAPID3 aggregate scores to guide rheumatoid arthritis interventions

Clearway Health reviewed RAPID3 domain scores in patients with rheumatoid arthritis to test whether the total disease-activity score aligned with functional status, pain, and global health signals that may not all be RA-specific.

Main interpretation signal

76.4% of patients with moderate-to-severe disease activity had little to no physical or functional disability, indicating that aggregate RAPID3 scores need domain-level review before therapy optimization.

The poster's caution: domains for pain and global health may reflect comorbidities or overall well-being, not only active RA.

RAPID3 domain lens

Three patient-reported domains can point in different clinical directions

1

Functional status

Physical function questions are scored from 0 to 10 and showed low limitation for many moderate-to-severe patients.

2

Pain

Pain is scored on a 0 to 10 scale; more than half of higher-activity patients scored above 5 on pain/global assessment.

3

Global health

Overall well-being can be affected by non-RA conditions, so it can skew the cumulative score if read in isolation.

RAPID3 combines function, pain, and global health into a cumulative score ranging from 0 to 30.

Objective

Investigate whether the three RAPID3 domain scores align with the aggregate score and how the pattern can tailor interventions.

Design

Multi-center, retrospective, observational descriptive study using clinical and pharmacist intervention dashboard data.

Population

Patients with rheumatoid arthritis enrolled in Clearway Health specialty care management services from January 1 to May 25, 2024.

Result pattern

Moderate and severe aggregate activity did not always mean substantial functional difficulty.

Conclusion

RAPID3 is useful, but aggregate scores should be interpreted carefully when deciding on therapy optimization.

Method snapshot

How the analysis was framed

  • Patients were included if enrolled in multi-site specialty pharmacy services during the 2024 study window.
  • RAPID3 data were pulled from a clinical and pharmacist intervention dashboard.
  • Descriptive statistics summarized aggregate activity, function, and quality-of-life patterns.
