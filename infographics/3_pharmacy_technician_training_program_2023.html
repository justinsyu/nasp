Poster title

A comprehensive approach to alleviate the pharmacy technician shortage and the implementation of a structured specialty pharmacy training program in a classroom setting

The program responded to turnover, vacancies, overtime reliance, and the temporary use of pharmacists for technician tasks during rapid specialty pharmacy service expansion.

Intervention design

Three connected moves built the pipeline

1 Recruit beyond the usual pool

Onsite and remote open houses were organized with human resources, with outreach to local community colleges and high school job fairs.

>
2 Simulate specialty work

A temporary classroom was approved to mimic a live call center, with coordinator-led modules across oncology, transplant, infectious diseases, multiple sclerosis, and inflammatory conditions.

>
3 Transition under supervision

After the training rotation, new liaisons staffed the call center under supervision before moving into independent specialty pharmacy support.

Conclusion

Structured specialty training helped alleviate the shortage

A specialty pharmacy focused recruitment and training curriculum resulted in alleviating the specialty pharmacy technician staffing shortage in a health-system specialty pharmacy.

The approach reduced reliance on overtime and pharmacist coverage for technician work while creating a repeatable liaison onboarding model.

What still constrained scale

Demand for trained liaisons continued inside the system

  • Backfilling remained constant as call-center liaisons were recruited into prior authorization and medication assistance departments.
  • Expanded patient management required training in additional disease states.
  • Future directions included curriculum expansion to two retail locations and creation of a formal on-site classroom.
