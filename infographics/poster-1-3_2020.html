Access to Direct Acting Antiviral Therapy for Recipients of Solid Organs from Hepatitis C-Viremic Donors

A real-world retrospective cohort evaluated whether patients who developed donor-derived HCV after solid organ transplant could obtain direct acting antiviral therapy, how long access took, and where the delays occurred.

Conclusion

Outpatient DAA access was achievable and affordable, but the fill location mattered

In this standard-practice cohort, DAA therapy for donor-derived HCV after solid organ transplant reached all patients, with high sustained viral response and very low post-assistance out-of-pocket cost. The most actionable operational signal was pharmacy routing: use of the on-site specialty pharmacy for the first fill was associated with significantly shorter time to first dose.
