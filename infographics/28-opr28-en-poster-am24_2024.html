Mirikizumab improved fatigue, bowel urgency, and quality of life in active Crohn's disease

In the phase 3 VIVID-1 treat-through trial, patients with moderately to severely active Crohn's disease receiving mirikizumab showed larger patient-reported outcome gains than placebo through Week 52.

Patient-reported outcome signal

3 domains improved Fatigue, urgency, and HRQoL all moved favorably with mirikizumab versus placebo.

Objective

Measure whether symptom relief translated into patient-facing benefit

The poster evaluated mirikizumab versus placebo through 52 weeks in adults with moderately to severely active Crohn's disease, focusing on fatigue, bowel urgency, and health-related quality of life.

  • FACIT-Fatigue captured chronic-illness fatigue; higher change scores indicate improvement.
  • Urgency NRS captured severity of bowel-movement urgency; lower scores indicate improvement.
  • IBDQ measured bowel, systemic, emotional, and social quality-of-life domains.

Treat-through design

Induction response was carried into blinded maintenance through Week 52

W0randomize
Induction MIRI 900 mg IV every 4 weeks, placebo IV, or ustekinumab active comparator through Week 12.
W12response
Maintenance MIRI responders continued 300 mg SC every 4 weeks; placebo responders continued placebo SC.
W52endpoint

The efficacy comparisons highlighted here use the poster's placebo and mirikizumab groups; placebo patients switching to mirikizumab after Week 12 had baseline values carried forward for later change-from-baseline analyses.

Population

Adults with moderately to severely active Crohn's disease and objective endoscopic activity were included in the primary analysis set.

Eligibility

Entry criteria included stool frequency at least 4 and/or abdominal pain at least 2, plus SES-CD thresholds by disease location.

Analysis

Continuous outcomes used ANCOVA with modified baseline observation carried forward; binary outcomes used non-responder imputation.

Key result

Mirikizumab was significantly better than placebo for fatigue and IBDQ at Weeks 12 and 52 and for urgency from Weeks 8 to 52.

Conclusion

The poster concluded that mirikizumab improved fatigue, bowel urgency, and HRQoL through one year in active Crohn's disease.

Definitions

How the poster framed response

IBDQ response: at least a 16-point improvement from baseline.

IBDQ remission: total score at least 170.

Clinical response: at least 30% decrease in stool frequency and/or abdominal pain, with neither score worse than baseline.
