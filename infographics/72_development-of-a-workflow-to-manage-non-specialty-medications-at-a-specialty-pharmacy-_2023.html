NASP 2023 | Poster 72 | Workflow design

Development of a workflow to manage non-specialty medications at a specialty pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health created a parallel non-specialty medication workflow so patients receiving specialty pharmacy services could have routine medications filled without forcing every prescription through specialty-focused EHR documentation.

Why a separate path was needed

The poster reports this split from April 2023 dispense history, showing that comprehensive care required a non-specialty fulfillment model.

Objective

Use dispensing-software data to manage non-specialty medications without specialty-documentation overload.

The redesigned process reduced unnecessary EHR documentation built for specialty medication management and shifted operational work into reports, queues, SPL outreach, and pharmacist review.

Methods | Evidence flow

A tandem workflow connects reports, queues, outreach, refill review, and delivery.

1

Identify due refills

A report surfaces non-specialty prescriptions with active refills due within five days.

2

Start fulfillment

The pharmacy supervisor reviews work volume statistics and initiates processing in dispensing software.

3

Route by disease state

Software assigns work to five teams aligned to the patient's specialty medication disease state.

4

Outreach from queues

SPLs call from dispensing-software outreach queues, set up delivery, or document missed attempts.

5

Review inactive refills

A daily RPh review determines whether requesting a refill is appropriate when refills are inactive.

  • The new workflow may have contributed to improved turnaround time, although the poster notes concurrent quality improvement initiatives.
  • The operational design reduced documentation burden while preserving pharmacist review for inactive refill decisions.

Discussion

Design principles

  • Run non-specialty and specialty workflows in tandem to minimize repeated patient outreach.
  • Use IT collaboration to build reports and routing logic that match health-system specialty pharmacy operations.
  • Keep pharmacist review for decisions where inactive refills require clinical or operational judgment.

Conclusion

A replicable path for comprehensive care

The health system specialty pharmacy successfully manages patients on both specialty and non-specialty medications to support complete care.

The poster concludes that similar health system specialty pharmacies using comparable electronic records and dispensing software could adopt the workflow.
