Utilization of a Network Adherence Dashboard and Subsequent Quality Initiative to Enhance Adherence

Banner Health and Excelera used a scheduled PDC reporting dashboard to examine transplant and oncology adherence, then launched a 2019 quality initiative focused on workflow standardization, documentation consistency, side effect management, and pharmacist education.

Evidence flow

The dashboard turned quarterly adherence reporting into an operational quality initiative.

Scheduled PDC reporting

Network members submitted data used to calculate adherence through proportion of days covered.

Therapy-area review

Banner reviewed transplant and oncology PDC performance against network benchmarks and goals.

Workflow investigation

Clinical pharmacist workflow and documentation around interventions were assessed for 2018.

Quality initiative

Standard workflows, consistent intervention documentation, and ongoing adherence education were implemented in 2019.

Adherence support

Interventions emphasized therapy adherence, side effect resolution, inappropriate therapy removal, and interaction prevention.

PDC-linked interventions

One-third or more of documented interventions were associated with improving PDC.

39% of transplant interventions were linked to improving adherence.
35% of oncology interventions were linked to improving adherence.

Approximate counts implied by the poster: 36 of 92 transplant interventions and 215 of 613 oncology interventions.

Conclusion

Regular adherence reporting exposed documentation gaps and created a structured improvement path.

The poster concludes that systematic PDC reporting improved understanding of prescribing practices and clinical pharmacist intervention documentation.

The quality initiative also supported strategic side effect management, adherence education, and improved PDC reporting across the reviewed transplant and oncology programs.
