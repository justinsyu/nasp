Computer-based adherence monitoring for kidney transplant immunosuppressants

Vanderbilt Transplant Pharmacy built CBAM to combine outpatient pharmacy fill data with pharmacist review, turning low proportion of days covered into targeted monitoring and outreach decisions.

The six-quarter signal: 12-month PDC below 80% among high-risk patients moved from 64% in Q1 2022 to 47% in Q2 2023, while misidentification remained a major reason low PDC alone could mislead.

Conclusion

CBAM converted adherence surveillance into a pharmacist review workflow

The poster concludes that integrating clinical and outpatient pharmacy data can empower targeted adherence monitoring and outreach for kidney transplant patients, while the high rate of misidentified reasons shows why PDC should be interpreted with clinical and pharmacy context.
