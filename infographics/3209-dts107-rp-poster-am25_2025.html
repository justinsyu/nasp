Streamlining copay assistance for CIDP and myasthenia gravis

A neurology-clinic quality-improvement pilot evaluates whether a standalone AI search, match, and enroll module can move foundation grant work into the narrow window when disease-based funds open.

Objective

Test a drop-in foundation enrollment module

Evaluate whether a standalone AI-enabled search, match, and enroll workflow can coordinate with patients, complete foundation applications the same day a fund opens, and reduce therapy drop-offs without bundling manufacturer copay cards or patient assistance tools.

Methods as a service pattern

Three-part concierge workflow

Offload foundation tasks

The practice routes foundation assistance work to GetCopayHelp for patient interaction and enrollment completion.

Poll open funds

The platform monitors availability across disease-based foundations and fund-specific rules.

Match eligible patients

Simple intake feeds patient and therapy details into automated fund matching.

Gather documents

Patient coordination focuses on missing income or enrollment information before the window closes.

Submit same day

AI-enabled automations prepare and file applications with the goal of keeping treatment on schedule.

24h

Speed and workload

Track the share of eligible patients funded within 24 hours of fund opening, median hours to grant, and staff minutes per enrollment compared with manual work.

Rx

Brand flexibility

Assess whether real-time visibility into assistance-supported alternative brands helps maintain therapy persistence.

1p

Low-friction intake

Gauge whether a one-page checkbox form reduces staff digital overload by avoiding another portal.

Anticipated impact

What success would change

Earlier grants: same-day submissions are expected to capture openings before funds fill.

Fewer therapy interruptions: assistance-supported brand options may help patients stay on treatment.

Lower staff load: a lightweight intake path avoids new portals while coexisting with manufacturer cards and PAP tools.

Operational components

Program search and intake Polls funds, auto-matches patients, and accepts EHR exports, email, or the MMS checklist.
Same-day engagement Collects documents and files applications when a foundation fund opens.

Interpretive takeaway

Poster status

This poster describes a prospective quality-improvement evaluation rather than final outcome results. The evidence emphasis is on operational feasibility, measurement endpoints, and anticipated impact for CIDP and MG specialty care.

  • Study site: single neurology clinic with seven infusion chairs.
  • Study interval: February 2025 through July 2026.
  • Primary operational target: funding within 24 hours after fund opening.
