Concomitant Use of Apalutamide and Anticoagulants

A descriptive post hoc analysis of SPARTAN and TITAN assessed whether thrombotic and embolic adverse-event rates differed when oral anticoagulants were used with apalutamide plus androgen deprivation therapy.

Objective

Evaluate treatment-emergent thrombotic and embolic AEs among concomitant oral anticoagulant users.

The analysis compared patients receiving apalutamide plus ADT with those receiving placebo plus ADT, using the overall safety population and subgroups defined by concomitant oral anticoagulant use.

Methods flow

How the post hoc analysis converted trial data into the AE comparison

Start with trial safety sets Use patients receiving at least one dose in SPARTAN and TITAN.
Identify OAC exposure Capture direct factor Xa inhibitors, direct thrombin inhibitors, and vitamin K antagonists.
Code AE outcomes Classify thrombotic and embolic adverse events using MedDRA version 19.1.
Describe rates Compare overall safety sets and OAC-defined subgroups without inferential testing.

Trial exclusions included severe or unstable recent cardiovascular events within 6 months before randomization.

Clinical concern

Apalutamide can induce drug-metabolizing enzymes and transporters used by common oral anticoagulants.

Allowed OACs

Apixaban, rivaroxaban, edoxaban, dabigatran, and warfarin were allowed in SPARTAN and TITAN.

Trial lens

Safety populations were split by concomitant OAC use and by randomized treatment arm.

Observed pattern

Within OAC users, thrombotic and embolic AE rates were close between APA + ADT and placebo + ADT.

Practice message

When needed, OACs can be used with apalutamide, with appropriate monitoring.

Conclusion: among advanced or metastatic prostate cancer patients using concomitant oral anticoagulants, thrombotic and embolic adverse-event occurrence was similar for apalutamide plus ADT and placebo plus ADT in the SPARTAN and TITAN post hoc safety analysis.
