NASP 2022 poster 41

Patient-reported outcomes inside an integrated IBD specialty pharmacy clinic

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy analyzed monthly refill questionnaires for patients with inflammatory bowel disease to connect refill-touchpoint PROs with adherence, safety, effectiveness, and longitudinal disease-status signals.

Secondary outcomes

SIBDQ, PHQ-9, and CRP were tracked from baseline through 600 days

SIBDQ: 58 to 52 The poster states that the decrease indicated improved disease severity by patient response.
PHQ-9: stable at 5 Depression screening scores were relatively stable over time.
CRP: 5 to 10 mg/L Inflammatory lab values slightly increased over the same interval.

Conclusion: patients receiving IBD specialty medications through an integrated care model reported high medication effectiveness, low adverse-effect and missed-dose rates, and generally stable secondary outcomes versus baseline.

Objective

Question asked

Evaluate patient-reported outcomes in patients prescribed specialty medications by a health-system outpatient IBD clinic and dispensed by an integrated specialty pharmacy.

Next research step

What remains open

The authors call for additional research evaluating the relationship between refill-touchpoint PROs and long-term clinical outcomes.
