Specialty pharmacists close the DPYD testing loop before capecitabine starts

CPS Solutions described a health-system specialty pharmacy protocol that confirms DPYD pharmacogenomic testing, escalates missing results, and recommends CPIC-guided changes for patients at increased toxicity risk.

Clinical rationale

Preemptive testing before first fill DPYD variants can reduce fluoropyrimidine metabolism, raising the risk of severe adverse events. The protocol moved testing confirmation into the specialty pharmacy workflow.

Objective

Describe how HSSP pharmacists operationalized DPYD metabolizer testing

The initiative asked whether a specialty pharmacy team could reliably verify testing before capecitabine initiation, notify prescribers when testing was absent, and document recommendations for high-risk metabolizer results.

Evidence flow

A four-part protocol turned genetic testing into a medication-safety checkpoint

1

Educate providers

Share evidence-based DPYD testing recommendations when capecitabine is prescribed.

2

Confirm testing

Document available results in the pharmacy patient management system before initiation.

3

Recommend action

For intermediate or poor metabolizers, recommend CPIC-guided dose adjustment or therapy change.

4

Add decision support

Use EHR order-entry prompts to reinforce genetic testing after the protocol begins.

Interpretation

What the poster concludes

  • HSSP teams can embed pharmacogenomic protocols into routine specialty medication workflows.
  • Medication-management expertise helped connect DPYD results to CPIC-guided dose or therapy recommendations.
  • The next phase is adaptation and scale across other health systems that manage patients on capecitabine.
  • Future work may extend the model to other pharmacogenomic testing opportunities in specialty pharmacy.
