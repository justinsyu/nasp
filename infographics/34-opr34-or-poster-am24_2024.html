Clinical Outcomes in Cystic Fibrosis Patients Enrolled in an Integrated Hospital System Specialty Pharmacy Care Model

A multisite retrospective analysis compared cystic fibrosis outcomes for patients in health system specialty pharmacy patient management programs against CF Foundation Patient Registry benchmarks.

  • Cystic fibrosis
  • Health system specialty pharmacy
  • Retrospective analysis
  • 10/1/2022 to 9/30/2023
OPR34-OR

Integrated pharmacist monitoring, EMR-derived outcomes, and patient-reported disease burden signals.

Total patients 298
Health systems 12
Pediatric cohort 111 mean age 10
Adult cohort 187 mean age 33

Objective

Benchmark the HSSP care model against registry outcomes

Investigators evaluated cystic fibrosis patients enrolled in health system specialty pharmacy programs and compared primary clinical outcomes with CF Foundation Patient Registry benchmarks.

Care model signal: pediatric and adult patients had comparable ppFEV1 and lower rates of pulmonary exacerbations and IV-antibiotic-treated exacerbations than the benchmarks shown in the poster figures.

Evidence flow

How the poster framed disease management

1

Enroll CF patients

Patients came from multisite HSSP cystic fibrosis management programs.

2

Stratify cohorts

Results were separated into pediatric patients under 18 and adult patients 18 or older.

3

Collect clinical signals

ppFEV1, exacerbations, IV antibiotics, hospitalizations, and absenteeism were drawn from PRO and EMR data.

4

Compare benchmarks

Primary outcomes were compared with CF Foundation Patient Registry benchmarks.

Secondary outcomes

Hospitalization and absenteeism were low across cohorts

4% Pediatric CF-related hospitalization
7% Adult CF-related hospitalization
5% Pediatric absenteeism
3% Adult absenteeism

Conclusion

Integrated specialty pharmacy monitoring supported a broader view of CF outcomes

Clinical pharmacists monitored medication efficacy, administration, adherence, and related disease-management information. The authors concluded that similar lung-function results, fewer exacerbation events, and low disease-burden measures supported the effectiveness of the integrated HSSP care model.
