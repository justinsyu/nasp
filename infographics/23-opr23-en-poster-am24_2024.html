Ustekinumab infusion to subcutaneous transition: coordinating care and identifying potential gaps

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy examined how patients with Crohn's disease or ulcerative colitis moved from clinic-administered IV induction to self-administered subcutaneous maintenance dosing, with attention to prior authorization and shipment timing.

Conclusion

Transition timing can be disrupted by PA requirements; integrated specialty pharmacy coordination may improve goal-window shipment.

Patients moving from ustekinumab IV induction to subcutaneous maintenance dosing may experience delays related to prior authorization requirements.
Subcutaneous prescriptions filled through the integrated health-system specialty pharmacy were more likely to ship in the appropriate post-infusion timeframe.
