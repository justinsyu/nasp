Growth signal

Specialty pharmacy revenue rose from $162B in 2015 to $248B in 2020.

Operational weakness

Retail pharmacies reported incomplete real-time provider database updates and manual validation workflows.

Compliance exposure

Verification gaps included missing NPI, DEA, license, and sanctions checks.

Recommended response

The snapshot called for data-integrity, patient-matching, and prescriber-affiliation tools.

Conclusion and action path

Growth depends on cleaner data plus targeted workflows.

1

Strengthen compliance

Move prescriber verification into workflow and refresh NPI, DEA, license, sanctions, and credentials data.

2

Match patient records

Reduce duplicate records and improve patient numbering so reimbursement and service workflows remain stable.

3

Map high-value providers

Analyze prescribers, affiliations, referral patterns, facility type, disease state, and therapeutic category.
