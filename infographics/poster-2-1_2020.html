NASP 2020 Poster 2-1

Assessing pharmacist interventions in an outpatient renal transplant CPPA

At Vanderbilt University Medical Center, pharmacists working under a collaborative pharmacy practice agreement managed prescription orders, chart reviews, counseling, and medication access work for adult renal transplant clinic patients.

Objective

Measure what pharmacists actually did under the CPPA

The study evaluated interventions performed by pharmacists in a collaborative pharmacy practice agreement at an outpatient renal transplant clinic.

Methods

Retrospective review of adult clinic patients

Adult patients prescribed medication from the renal transplant clinic between January 1 and June 30, 2019 were included. Clinic notes and EMR documentation were reviewed for chart assessment, counseling, access, and medication continuation work.

Evidence flow

From referral to pharmacist intervention

Post-transplant follow-up

Patients discharged after renal transplant continued care in the transplant clinic.

Referral to pharmacist

Nurses, physicians, pharmacy, phone calls, EMR messages, or clinic visits triggered pharmacist work.

Chart review

Pharmacists checked CPPA referral, visit recency, medication changes, labs, and the medication list.

Order generation

Renewal or new non-controlled medication orders were generated under protocol support.

Therapy support

Interventions addressed assessment, counseling, medication access, and persistence barriers.

Implementation advice

How the poster framed CPPA setup

  • Obtain pharmacist liability insurance.
  • Seek legal review before initiating a CPPA.
  • Integrate a pharmacist in clinic first to build provider trust.
  • Maintain open, constructive pharmacist-physician communication.

Conclusion: Pharmacist prescription management under the renal transplant CPPA was reported as safe, common, and operationally important in a high-volume clinic with frequent medication changes.
