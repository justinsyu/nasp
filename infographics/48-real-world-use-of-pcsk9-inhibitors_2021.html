Real-World Use of PCSK9 Inhibitors and Adherence to Guideline-Directed Therapy

A Vanderbilt single-center retrospective review followed patients prescribed evolocumab or alirocumab, asking whether real-world use aligned with lipid guidelines and what happened to LDL after 24 months.

Objective and design

Assess real-world guideline-directed lipid management, PCSK9i use, and clinical outcomes.

Single-center review Conducted at Vanderbilt University Medical Center.
Prescription window Patients prescribed a PCSK9i from September 2015 to August 2018.
Exclusions Patient assistance program, no insurance approval, or therapy never started.
Outcomes Guideline adherence, LDL goal, LDL change, and new cardiovascular events or diagnoses.

Guideline context

PCSK9i therapy was framed as add-on treatment after maximally tolerated statin and ezetimibe.

≥70 mg/dL LDL for ASCVD patients on prior lipid-lowering therapy.
≥100 mg/dL LDL for familial hypercholesterolemia patients.

The poster cited 2018 AHA/ACC cholesterol guidance and noted scarce real-world evidence.

Guideline threshold

Real-world cohort

477 Vanderbilt patients began from a median LDL of 157 mg/dL.

Therapy pattern

The source conclusion reported less than half using recommended statin or ezetimibe therapy at initiation.

LDL response

Median LDL declined 59% among patients with paired 24-month LDL values.

Research need

More work is needed on PCSK9i use outside guideline-directed recommendations and future events.

Conclusion: real-world PCSK9i therapy at an integrated specialty pharmacy produced large LDL reductions among persistent patients, but initiation patterns showed a gap between guideline-directed lipid management and observed practice.
