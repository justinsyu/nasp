Evaluating the Impact of Health System Specialty Pharmacist Interventions on Rheumatologic Disease Clinical Outcomes

Trellis Rx evaluated how embedded health-system specialty pharmacists used worsening RAPID3 scores to trigger review, separate clinically relevant disease signals from unrelated score changes, and recommend timely care changes between office visits.

Objective

Measure whether pharmacist review turns digital score alerts into meaningful care actions

The poster's primary objective was to determine how many RAPID3-triggered alerts resulted in pharmacist-led intervention, then measure post-intervention RAPID3 scores to evaluate whether patient-reported outcomes improved.

RAPID3 combines three patient-reported domains accepted by the American College of Rheumatology for rheumatoid arthritis, psoriatic arthritis, and ankylosing spondylitis: function, pain, and patient global estimate of status.

Evidence flow

From patient-reported worsening to pharmacist interpretation

Collect RAPID3

Scores were collected digitally or by phone and documented in Arbor, Trellis Rx's specialty pharmacy platform.

Detect worsening

Adults with RA, PsA, or AS who had two consecutively worsening RAPID3 scores entered the triggered review pathway.

Alert pharmacist

Arbor auto-triggered pharmacist interventions using an algorithm for worsening score patterns.

Interpret context

Embedded pharmacists evaluated whether worsening represented clinically relevant disease activity or another cause.

Clinical interpretation

Why the pharmacist layer mattered

Signal Pharmacist role Care impact
Two worsening RAPID3 scores Review scores and patient context Earlier identification between routine visits
Clinically relevant worsening Recommend therapy change, follow-up, or adherence aid Action could occur before the next office appointment
Unrelated worsening Determine non-rheumatologic driver and notify provider Avoid overinterpreting RAPID3 as a standalone therapy measure

Conclusion: locally embedded health-system specialty pharmacists are positioned to collect and interpret RAPID3 scores routinely, intervene sooner when worsening is clinically meaningful, and prevent RAPID3 from being used as the sole measure of therapy status.
