Objective

Turn fragmented access data into comparable benchmarks

The poster evaluates whether real-time benchmarking combined with specialty channel and patient-service data can guide brands toward stronger fill rates, better care coordination, and more efficient HUB operations.

  • Compare a brand against similar therapies instead of interpreting performance in isolation.
  • Use fill-rate and time-to-fill signals to find patient-access barriers.
  • Translate comparative performance into payer, HCP, and patient-service actions.

Study Lens

Two benchmark views, one access question

The chronic skin disease basket shows meaningful spread: fill rates range from 34% to 65%, with Your Brand at 42%. That spread is the practical target for benchmarking: identify which channel steps separate the leader from the median performer.

Conclusion

Benchmarking reframes performance as a set of fixable gaps

Across the poster examples, benchmarks help brands see where fill rate, prior authorization, and shipment timing diverge from stronger performers. The intended result is not just reporting, but targeted alignment with high-performing HUB and channel patterns.

Rank the brand

Place current fill rate beside competitive brands to quantify the access gap.

Find the delay

Separate referral, authorization, triage, and shipment timing to locate bottlenecks.

Activate changes

Use top-performer patterns to refine HUB, HCP, payer, and patient-service strategy.
