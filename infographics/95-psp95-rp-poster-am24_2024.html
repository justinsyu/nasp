Analysis of appropriate storage and stability of oral anticancer agents

Cleveland Clinic specialty pharmacy built a systematic review pathway to decide when oral oncolytic bottles or packages can be opened for partial fills without compromising medication stability, storage requirements, or patient safety.

Objective

Create a repeatable opening decision workflow

The project translated scattered storage information into a pharmacy workflow for deciding whether an oral anticancer product may be opened, repackaged, or dispensed as a partial fill.

Partial fills can reduce waste

Scan timing, laboratory checks, dose changes, and regimen changes can make full fills clinically or financially inefficient.

Stability can be product-specific

Labeling may require original packaging, protection from light or moisture, or retention of a desiccant.

Methodology

Three-stage evidence pathway for opening decisions

1

Identify medications

  • Retrospective review of historically opened products.
  • Student, resident, and pharmacist data compilation.
  • Initial evidence from package inserts, medication guides, and patient guides.
2

Research and outreach

  • Request written manufacturer correspondence when available.
  • Document phone calls when written responses were not available.
  • Assess desiccants, open-dish studies, and data outside original containers.
3

Compile findings

  • Consolidate each product into a shared spreadsheet.
  • Compare storage language, package size, historical opening, and final recommendation.
  • Route selected drugs to legal, medication safety, and Drug Information stakeholders.

Data collection lenses

Each product was reviewed through five practical storage questions

1

Packaging type

Bottle, blister, package size, and whether a partial fill creates an uneven multiple.

2

Lexi-Comp storage

Tertiary drug reference guidance used as one structured evidence source.

3

Package insert

Label storage language, including original package, light, moisture, and temperature controls.

4

Desiccant status

Whether the medication contains a desiccant and whether it must remain with the drug.

5

Manufacturer input

Written correspondence or documented calls when labeling did not answer the operational question.

Background

Partial fills may prevent waste and reduce cost when treatment timing or dosing changes make full quantities undesirable.

Core barrier

Manufacturer labeling often has limited detail, making original-container requirements difficult to operationalize.

Method

The team combined tertiary references, package labeling, medication guides, and manufacturer outreach.

Result

The MUE identified more than 160 medications for review, with the first six approvals completed by February 2024.

Conclusion

A systematic workflow can help oncology pharmacists make product-specific opening decisions while review of all oral anticancer agents continues.
