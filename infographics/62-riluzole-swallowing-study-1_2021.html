NASP Annual Meeting & Expo 2021 | ALS dysphagia

Swallowing Safety With Riluzole Oral Film

A two-study poster links riluzole oral film bioavailability with videofluoroscopic swallowing safety in ALS, asking whether a tongue-dissolving film can offer an alternative to tablet or liquid swallowing burden.

  • Amyotrophic lateral sclerosis
  • Oral film
  • Pharmacokinetics
  • VFSS swallowing study

Objective: describe riluzole oral film bioavailability and pharmacokinetic data, with a focused swallowing safety study in ALS.

Poster conclusion

Oral film was presented as an alternative administration route for ALS patients with dysphagia.

The poster concludes that ROF was bioequivalent to Rilutek tablets and well tolerated in healthy adults, while a single 50 mg dose had no detrimental effect on swallowing in the small ALS VFSS study.
