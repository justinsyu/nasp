Clinical, Safety, and Quality Outcomes of a Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy Clinical Pharmacy Management Program

A pharmacist-led health system specialty pharmacy program embedded REMS refill screening, heart-failure symptom checks, interaction review, reassessment, and adherence monitoring for NYU Langone Health patients newly prescribed mavacamten.

Objective

Evaluate whether a pharmacist-led HCM management program supports clinical, safety, and quality outcomes.

The program translated REMS requirements into repeatable specialty pharmacy touchpoints: monthly refill screening, heart-failure symptom assessment, drug-interaction review, and periodic clinical reassessment.

Conclusion

Integrated specialty pharmacy management aligned real-world care with REMS-dependent mavacamten monitoring.

  • Real-world HSSP management demonstrated positive clinical outcomes and NYHA class reduction consistent with clinical trial results.
  • A pharmacist-led patient management program promoted clinical, safety, and quality outcomes for patients prescribed mavacamten.
