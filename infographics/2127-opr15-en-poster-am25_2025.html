Saturday delivery as a specialty pharmacy adherence intervention for CFTR modulators

Maxor Specialty Pharmacy retrospectively evaluated whether adding Saturday delivery was associated with better proportion of days covered among patients with cystic fibrosis receiving CFTR modulators.

Conclusion

Saturday delivery was associated with a measurable adherence gain, but causality remains limited by sample size and concurrent interventions.

The poster concludes that PDC averages increased by 6.2 percentage points, more than half of nonadherent patients achieved adherence, and staff phone efficiency improved as outbound calls decreased and click-to-refill use increased.

Objective

Evaluate change in PDC for nonadherent CFTR modulator patients who used Saturday delivery between May and October 2024.

Methods

Longitudinal retrospective chart review using pharmacy operating and clinical platforms across two specialty pharmacy locations.

Population

Patients with cystic fibrosis on standard-dose CFTR modulators, filled before Nov. 1, 2023, with complete PDC data.

Key pattern

Overall Saturday delivery utilization was 3% for CFTR modulator prescriptions, with 12 patients using it more than once.

Limitations

Small sample size, claims-based PDC limits, intentional therapy gaps, and concurrent adherence interventions limit causal interpretation.
