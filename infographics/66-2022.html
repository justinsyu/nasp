RAPID-3 metrics tracked quality-of-life gains after sDMARD therapy management.

Fairview Pharmacy Services followed rheumatoid arthritis patients starting specialty disease modifying antirheumatic drugs to understand how total RAPID-3 and domain scores changed by baseline disease severity.

Study architecture

RAPID-3 became a longitudinal signal for therapy management intensity.

Cohort

RA patients presenting to specialty pharmacy for sDMARD therapy from 8/2018 to 7/2021.

Baseline

Included patients had at least one RAPID-3 within 30 days of therapy management initiation.

Follow-up

At least one RAPID-3 was required 3 to 12 months after therapy management initiation.

Analysis

Mixed effects regression modeled 12-month trajectories; logistic regression tested improvement factors.

Regression signals

Drug therapy problems marked a lower chance of improvement.

In the early cohort model, patients with a documented drug therapy problem had lower odds of improved total RAPID-3 and pain scores, reinforcing the role of targeted pharmacist intervention.

0.22 Drug therapy problem: improved RAPID-3 OR
0.16 Drug therapy problem: improved pain OR

Conclusion

High and moderate baseline severity groups achieved meaningful 1-year reductions.

The poster concludes that RAPID-3 tracking can help therapy management pharmacists identify patients most likely to improve and patients who may need more clinical support, especially when drug therapy problems are present.

Future work was proposed to connect longer-term quality-of-life outcomes with clinical characteristics and to further separate the interplay of function, pain, and global domain scores.
