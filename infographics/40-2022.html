Patient-reported outcomes guide specialty pharmacy interventions in multiple sclerosis

A Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy retrospective analysis used monthly refill questionnaires to identify adverse effects, missed doses, perceived effectiveness, and intervention opportunities for patients receiving specialty MS medications.

Reported outcomes

Most MRQ responses showed stability on therapy

Across patients with at least two specialty pharmacy fills and at least two monthly refill questionnaires, adverse effects and missed doses were uncommon, while perceived effectiveness was overwhelmingly favorable.

Evidence flow

Three refill questions determined when pharmacists stepped in

1

Certified pharmacy technician assessment

At each monthly refill, technicians asked about side effects, missed doses, and perceived medication effectiveness.

2

Trigger signals

Side effects, missed doses, or poor/fair effectiveness responses routed the patient toward pharmacist review.

3

Specialty pharmacist intervention

Interventions focused on adherence counseling, therapeutic monitoring and safety, side-effect management, and care coordination.

4

Stable responses

Good or excellent effectiveness with no reported problems generally required no additional intervention at that refill touchpoint.

Action profile

What the intervention pattern suggests

39 adherence interventions Most often chart review or update plus patient counseling.
36 safety interventions Typically therapy or lab monitoring review and treatment recommendations.
20 side effect/toxicity interventions Even with only 2% reporting adverse effects, pharmacy touchpoints created a route for escalation.
175 total interventions The intervention mix was broader than symptom reporting alone, spanning access, coordination, medication lists, and drug information.

Conclusion: patient-reported outcomes collected during routine refills can direct specialty pharmacist interventions that support safe and effective MS medication use.

Objective

Question asked

Assess PROs and pharmacist interventions in patients prescribed specialty MS medications at a health-system specialty pharmacy.

Methods snapshot

Who was included

Patients prescribed an MS specialty medication by the outpatient MS clinic provider, with at least two fills through the center's specialty pharmacy and at least two MRQs from January to March 2020.
