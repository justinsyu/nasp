NASP 2024 / 77-DTS77-EN | Specialty pharmacy adherence

Impact of an alternative ordering method on medication adherence and persistence among patients at a large mail-order specialty pharmacy

Optum Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether Schedule My Fill, a refill scheduling program for patients engaged in clinical management, improved adherence and persistence across autoimmune, multiple sclerosis, and oncology specialty therapies.

Methods

A six-month matched comparison of scheduled versus traditional refills.

Eligible patients

Oncology, multiple sclerosis, or autoimmune patients on therapy for at least 3 months and engaged in the Clinical Management Program.

SMF exposure

Participants used Schedule My Fill to set up refills for the rest of the year.

Six months

Each patient contributed a unique 6-month period from April through December 2023.

Matched control

Traditional-order patients were selected with propensity score matching.

Endpoints

PDC, gap days, days on therapy, persistence over 6 fills, and participant ease of use.

Persistence curve

Persistence separated modestly by the sixth fill, strongest in multiple sclerosis.

Persistence was statistically significant by log-rank testing for the combined and disease-state curves. By fill 6, SMF participants remained on therapy at higher rates in every disease state.

  • Multiple sclerosis66% vs 60%
  • Oncology71% vs 68%
  • Autoimmune54% vs 52%
  • Combined61% vs 58%

Objective

Evaluate SMF impact on oncology, multiple sclerosis, and autoimmune patients versus traditional ordering methods.

Background

Nonadherence contributes to treatment failure, hospitalization, and mortality; specialty pharmacy ordering data remain limited.

Primary endpoints

PDC, gap days, days on therapy, and persistence curve over six fills.

Main signal

SMF participants had significantly higher adherence, fewer gap days, and more patients on therapy after six fills.

Next step

Continue clinician support and advocacy for Schedule My Fill enrollment.
