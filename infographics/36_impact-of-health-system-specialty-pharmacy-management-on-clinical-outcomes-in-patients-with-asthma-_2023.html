Impact of Health System Specialty Pharmacy Management on Clinical Outcomes in Patients with Asthma

A multicenter retrospective review found clinically meaningful ACT score improvement among patients receiving embedded HSSP pharmacist management, with intervention acceptance near universal.

  • Asthma Control Test

Objective: evaluate whether HSSP clinical management was associated with better clinical outcomes in patients with asthma.

Evidence flow

How the cohort was defined and analyzed

Eligible asthma diagnosis

Adults with ICD-10 asthma codes J45.0-J45.998 were considered for inclusion.

Biologic therapy

Included therapies were dupilumab, benralizumab, mepolizumab, omalizumab, or tezepelumab-ekko.

Embedded HSSP care

Patients were clinically managed by HSSP allergy and pulmonary specialists embedded in partner sites.

Two ACT scores

Patients needed at least two documented ACT scores; therapies on hold and patients under 18 were excluded.

Conclusion: embedded HSSP pulmonary specialist management was associated with clinically significant ACT improvement and a higher proportion of patients reaching asthma control.

Why it matters

Access work became clinical work

The poster frames medication access, adherence support, insurance approval, and therapy concern resolution as part of the same clinical management model.

High acceptance of pharmacist recommendations supports the value of embedding HSSP pharmacists directly within specialty clinics.
