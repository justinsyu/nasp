Question

What happened after branded adalimumab became a formulary exclusion?

The poster evaluated whether impacted members switched to the preferred biosimilar, switched to a different branded biologic, remained on branded adalimumab, or later switched back. It also examined reasons for unsuccessful biosimilar switches and disease states linked to product-hopping.

Design

PBM pharmacy claims plus prior authorization review.

Claims from 284 self-insured employers implementing an exclusionary formulary were extracted from PBM reporting tools. Key fields included product, member account ID, fill date, age, gender, diagnosis code, and pharmacy.

Interpretation

Product-hopping was visible, but the preferred biosimilar absorbed the majority of switches.

Large direct-switch base

Almost 80% of impacted branded adalimumab utilizers moved to the preferred biosimilar.

Diagnosis mattered

Alternative biologic choices varied by autoimmune condition and available indications.

Provider hesitancy was smaller

Provider-only requests represented 5% of switch-back reasoning in the prior authorization review.

Limitations

Interpret cautiously: claims and ICD-10 coding are imperfect proxies.

Indications depended on prescriber coding accuracy, one autoimmune condition per member was assumed, and included biologics did not share identical indications or dosing schedules. Natural disease progression and antibody development were not incorporated.

Exclude originator

Branded adalimumab was removed from the formulary effective April 2024.

Track claims

Pharmacy claims identified product fills, timing, diagnosis, and member-level switch pathways.

Separate pathways

Direct switch, indirect switch, nonswitch, step-through, and switch-back groups were quantified.

Read disease signal

Alternative biologic use clustered around PsO/PsA, RA, HS, CD, UC, and AS patterns.

Explain failures

Switch-back reviews most often cited trial failure or intolerable side effects.

Conclusion: the exclusionary formulary moved most branded adalimumab users to the preferred biosimilar, but disease state and post-switch tolerability shaped the smaller groups who product-hopped, stepped through, or returned to branded adalimumab.
