Development of a Palivizumab Specialty Pharmacy Management Program

Mizzou Specialty Pharmacy described a health-system specialty pharmacy program built with pediatric clinics to identify RSV-season palivizumab candidates, coordinate access, support families, and manage ongoing refills and clinic visits.

Objective

Build a clinic-linked management pathway before and during RSV season.

The poster framed palivizumab as a specialty-pharmacy fit because of cost, handling requirements, injection administration, and the importance of adherence. The program linked Mizzou Specialty Pharmacy with health-system teams and community pediatric clinics.

Clinical rationale

Palivizumab prevents serious lower respiratory tract disease caused by RSV in pediatric patients.

2

Specialty fit

High cost, handling needs, injection administration, and adherence risk supported specialty pharmacy management.

3

Relationship model

The program required a health-system specialty pharmacy to partner with privately owned community clinics.

4

Operational loop

Patient medication liaisons and pharmacists coordinated benefits, refills, visits, and RSV prevention education.

5

Expansion signal

The authors concluded that results supported expanding services to additional community providers.

Pattern across seasons

Program reach expanded while RSV hospitalizations remained low in reported counts.

+104% Referrals grew from 55 in 2016-2017 to 112 in 2018-2019.
+74% Mizzou-managed patients increased from 34 to 59.
2 -> 1 Reported RSV hospitalizations decreased after the first season and stayed at 1 in each later season.

Conclusion: Mizzou Specialty Pharmacy successfully built local pediatric clinic relationships for palivizumab management, and the poster linked the program to adherence support, low RSV hospitalization counts, and a rationale for broader community expansion.
