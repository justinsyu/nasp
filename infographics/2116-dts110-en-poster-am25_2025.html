Accelerating Evidence Generation to Implementation in Cardiometabolic Health

The LATTICE Consortium is framed as a first-of-its-kind implementation science community built to shorten the distance between cardiometabolic evidence and everyday clinical practice.

  • Cardiometabolic health
  • Implementation science
  • Evidence-based tools
  • Clinical practice translation

Authors include Laney K Jones, Ankeet S Bhatt, Seth S Martin, and collaborators from Amgen, academic centers, health systems, and patient advocacy.

Objective Research -> care

Improve collaboration across the healthcare ecosystem to translate evidence-based cardiometabolic advancements into clinical practice.

Evidence-to-implementation pathway

The poster positions LATTICE as a connective platform, not a single intervention.

Generate evidence Scientific advancements create new opportunities to reduce cardiometabolic events.
Share tools Evidence-based methodologies are collected for learning, ideation, and collaboration.
Adapt at scale Like-minded teams test implementation strategies across care settings.
Close care gaps Success is defined by faster adoption, broader awareness, and meaningful care-gap reduction.

Reducing the time between evidence generation in research settings and implementation in care settings is the central hypothesis for improving cardiovascular outcomes.

Outcomes framework

The poster separates implementation quality from downstream care outcomes.

Acceptability Adoption Appropriateness Feasibility Fidelity Penetration Sustainability

These seven measures define whether a strategy can move from promising concept to routine, durable care delivery.

Service outcomes

Three levels of guideline follow-through.

Patients Adherence to prescribed testing and treatment.
Clinicians Guideline adherence at the clinician level.
Systems Guideline adherence at a health-system level.

Program evaluation

Success is measured as a chain of acceleration, awareness, adoption, and care-gap closure.

1

Reduce lag time

Shorten the interval from evidence generation to implementation.

2

Improve awareness

Increase recognition of scalable implementation strategies.

3

Drive downstream action

Encourage individuals and teams to adopt sustainable care strategies.

4

Close care gaps

Measure meaningful improvement in cardiometabolic care gaps.

Conclusion

LATTICE is designed to make implementation science reusable.

The poster concludes that connecting teams around evidence-based tools, shared learning, and collaborative adaptation may help health systems adopt effective cardiometabolic strategies more efficiently.
