NASP 2023 poster 81

The Implementation of Agile Methodology and its Impact on Healthcare and Pharmacy Technology

A systematic review argues that Agile's iterative, user-centered model can help healthcare teams build patient portals, management dashboards, medical-device software, telemedicine tools, and clinical decision support systems with faster feedback and better fit for complex care needs.

Core thesis

Iterate closer to care Agile is framed as a better match for patient-centered technology because requirements change, users need a voice, and healthcare workflows rarely stay fixed.

Objective

Explain why Agile became a software standard, then test its fit for healthcare

The poster reviews Agile principles, strengths, weaknesses, and case-based examples in healthcare technology, with emphasis on pharmacy clinical and dispensing platforms.

Methods

22 Years of peer-reviewed literature screened for healthcare Agile use cases.

Evidence was filtered for healthcare technology relevance

  • 1Included peer-reviewed articles from 2001 through 2022 on Agile implementation in healthcare.
  • 2Excluded Agile articles outside healthcare and patient-care articles unrelated to technology development or access.
  • 3Combined key points into a composite overview rather than a quantitative meta-analysis.

Results

The review highlights three mature healthcare Agile patterns

1

Pandemic operations dashboards

A Rio Grande do Norte ICU-bed dashboard reportedly completed about 70% of requirements in a single one-week sprint and was used by more than 200 government agents and clinical unit staff by late October 2020.

2

Patient and caregiver portals

Agile co-design placed patients directly into the development process, including a multiple sclerosis portal example, improving fit to user experience and supporting patient participation.

3

Medical-device software

Despite regulatory concerns, the poster cites Agile medical-device software work associated with better code quality, shorter release cycles, fewer field defects, and more delivered features.

Additional domains

Evidence extended beyond the three anchor cases

The poster also points to telemedicine and clinical decision support systems as areas beginning to benefit from Agile development practices.

Its interpretation is that Agile can help teams work in safety-critical environments while strengthening collaboration between healthcare and software professionals.

Specialty pharmacy relevance

Rare and complex disease care needs adaptable tools

The conclusion emphasizes specialty pharmacy and complex disease populations, where patient circumstances are heterogeneous and technology must be able to respond to changing care needs.

Conclusion: Agile fits patient-centered systems

Based on the reviewed cases, the authors conclude that Agile is an ideal software development methodology for healthcare systems because it enables responsiveness, adaptability, and direct alignment with patient-centered care.
