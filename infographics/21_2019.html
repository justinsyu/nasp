NASP 2019 poster 21 | Metastatic breast cancer cost analytics

Cost of Leukopenia and Neutropenia in Metastatic Breast Cancer

A curated disease model was used to surface per-patient-per-month cost patterns across recent 12-, 36-, and 60-month views, with age-stratified attention to leukopenia and neutropenia as cost-intense side effects.

Conclusion

The tool turned claims data into immediate cost signals.

The poster concluded that curated, cloud-based disease models can make PPPM cost and outcome patterns available to business users without programming or data science support.

The substantive signal was directional: costs were generally higher for younger patients overall and among patients experiencing leukopenia or neutropenia, which the authors attributed to more aggressive treatment patterns in younger patients.
