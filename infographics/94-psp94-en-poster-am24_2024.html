Development and implementation of an innovative pulmonary arterial hypertension clinical management model

A health system specialty pharmacy team built a standardized PAH care model for patients receiving ambrisentan, aligning onboarding, REMS counseling, clinical assessment, adverse event screening, dispensing, and audit readiness.

Operating model

REMS-ready PAH care Dedicated pharmacists, nurses, and in-clinic liaisons connect benefits work, REMS authorization, assessments, dispensing, and shipment.

Why this model was needed

Rare disease therapy with dispensing controls

PAH is described in the poster as rare, progressive, and affecting fewer than 50,000 people in the United States. Ambrisentan requires a REMS protocol, making clinical coordination and dispensing governance central to pharmacy operations.

Care pathway

From benefits verification to time-limited shipment

1

Access setup

Liaison verifies benefits and completes prior authorization or financial assistance work.

2

REMS verification

Clinician confirms prescriber and patient enrollment, then counsels females of reproductive potential.

3

Clinical assessment

Team obtains the REMS authorization code and completes PAH clinical assessments and adverse event screening.

4

Dispense and ship

Pharmacy coordinates delivery, dispenses with medication guide, and ships within the authorization-code window.

Gap analysis

Reviewed ambrisentan REMS requirements and PAH literature, then selected a validated quality-of-life and severity assessment tool.

Workflow build

Created standardized processes for onboarding, REMS counseling, clinical assessment, and adverse event screening.

Training

Developed curriculum covering REMS, adverse event reporting, policies, procedures, and knowledge assessment.

Evaluation

Implemented the PAH clinical care model at four HSSPs and evaluated the program for REMS compliance.

Objective

Describe development and implementation of a PAH clinical management model for patients receiving ambrisentan from integrated HSSPs.

Clinical anchor

The care model links REMS requirements to disease monitoring, quality-of-life assessment, and adverse event screening.

Team design

Pharmacists, nurses, and in-clinic pharmacy liaisons divide operational work while preserving a standardized workflow.

Audit logic

Annual and post-dispense mock audits use the ten identified elements to sustain readiness.

Conclusion

The model enabled dispensing of ambrisentan under REMS controls; future QOL-score analysis could measure clinical impact.

Bottom line

A specialty pharmacy operating model, not a single intervention

The poster's central contribution is a repeatable infrastructure for high-control PAH therapy: literature-informed assessment, REMS-aware workflows, staff training, audit elements, and outcome tracking.
