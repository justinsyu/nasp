Neurology Specialty Pharmacist Interventions Across Integrated and External Fill Sites

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy compared pharmacist interventions for non-MS neurology patients filling through an integrated health system specialty pharmacy (IHSSP) versus non-integrated pharmacies. The signal: pharmacists intervened often regardless of fill site, but external fills shifted work toward access, transfers, and longer intervention time.

Workload Pattern

Access work concentrated outside the IHSSP. External pharmacy patients required more financial assistance referrals, more prescription transfers, and a higher median intervention time.

Objective and Methods

Compare what pharmacists do, how long it takes, and whether recommendations are accepted.

The study extracted interventions from the electronic health record and specialty pharmacy software for all eligible non-MS neurology patients with at least one pharmacist intervention.

Regression analysis showed VSP/IHSSP patients had lower odds of greater intervention time; Medicaid, Medicare, no insurance, Tricare/other insurance, and several clinic categories trended toward greater time.

Conclusion

Three operational takeaways

  • Non-IHSSP patients needed more financial assistance and prescription transfer support.
  • Non-IHSSP interventions required more pharmacist time, with a 10-minute median versus 8 minutes for IHSSP.
  • Provider acceptance was high: almost all intervention recommendations were accepted.
