Quality of Life Measures in Specialty Pharmacy

A retrospective descriptive analysis evaluated whether the PROMIS-10 quality-of-life survey could function as a feasible standardized outcomes measure across specialty pharmacy programs using TherigySTM patient management software.

Background

No single quality-of-life tool is the industry default.

Specialty pharmacy accreditation standards require programs to evaluate benefits, including quality of life. PROMIS-10 offers a brief validated instrument spanning mental and physical domains and can be used across disease states.

Opportunity

Make quality-of-life outcomes comparable over time.

The poster positions PROMIS-10 as a quantifiable tool that could help specialty pharmacies monitor patient quality-of-life change, benchmark clinical management, and identify where patient interactions should be modified.

Conclusion: PROMIS-10 was routinely used across multiple specialty pharmacy settings and disease states, supporting its feasibility as a standardized quality-of-life outcomes measure.
