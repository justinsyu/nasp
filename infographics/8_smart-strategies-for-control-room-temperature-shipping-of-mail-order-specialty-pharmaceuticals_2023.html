NASP 2023 Poster 8 | Controlled Room Temperature Shipping

Smart strategies for control room temperature shipping of mail order specialty pharmaceuticals

A TempAid case study tested soft pouch PCM18 mats against water-based gel packs to maintain 15-25°C under ISTA 7D Winter conditions. Across four cooler sizes, PCM mats reduced tare weight, preserved more payload volume, and met or exceeded time-in-range performance.

Objective

Test whether room-temperature PCMs can outperform conventional gel packs

Problem

Water-based gel packs are familiar and low cost, but they add weight and bulk when the target range is controlled room temperature rather than near 0°C.

Approach

Use properly tested phase change material in a mat format to create smaller, lighter CRT packaging.

Endpoint

Maintain 15-25°C against the ISTA 7D Winter profile while improving logistics economics.

Payload pattern

PCM mats shifted space from refrigerant to product

Available payload volume increased in every cooler size. The smallest containers showed the largest relative payload gains, which supports the poster's conclusion that smaller cooler payloads may carry greater savings potential.

Methods

Four coolers, two configurations, one winter stress profile

Build test set

TempAid began 2022 testing with soft pouch PCM mats and water-based gel packs.

Vary size

Coolers represented 8, 18, 26, and 50 L package classes.

Pair configs

Each cooler received one gel-pack configuration and one PCM18 mat configuration.

Stress route

Testing used the ISTA 7D Winter temperature profile.

Compare output

Weight, payload volume, and hours inside 15-25°C were compared.

Implication

Cost-of-ownership levers

Downsize

Smaller containers

PCM mats may allow smaller coolers without compromising payload capacity.

Consolidate

More product per shipper

Higher available volume can support larger shipments to one receiver or tighter shipment consolidation.

Reduce

Lower handling burden

Lighter, less bulky containers can reduce warehouse space, shipping energy, and recipient disposal burden.

Authors: Anthony Alleva, BA; Chantal Walker, MESc, CTP Level II; Ryan Sanders; Kevin Kohleriter, MBA; Jordan Bonomo, BS, BA.

Conclusion

For CRT mail-order specialty pharmaceuticals, PCM18 mats changed the economics of the package

The poster concludes that PCM mat configurations can reduce package size and weight while maintaining the required temperature band, creating potential cost savings, productivity gains, better logistics efficiency, and reduced environmental impact from manufacturing, warehousing, shipping, and disposal.
