Ambulatory pharmacist integration scaled oral oncology interventions

A retrospective single-center review compared six months before and after an ambulatory clinical pharmacist was embedded in a hematology/oncology clinic integrated with a health-system specialty pharmacy.

Objective

Characterize and quantify the impact of an ambulatory clinical pharmacist-provided service in a hematology/oncology clinic.

Design

Retrospective, observational, single-center analysis of adult oral oncolytic patients in a patient management program.

Collection

Demographics, malignancy, ICD10, oral oncolytic data, intervention type, intervention count, and intervention outcomes were abstracted.

Cost method

Intervention-specific cost avoidance benchmarks from literature were multiplied by the number of interventions.

Conclusion

Embedding the ACP added measurable value to the integrated HSSP model

$678,937

Estimated incremental cost avoidance after ACP integration. The poster concludes that the HSSP model plus ACP services can optimize oral oncolytic outcomes through more pharmacist-led interventions.

Caveat and next step

Benchmarks may underestimate oral oncology value

The authors noted that cost avoidance may be underestimated because intervention benchmarks came from mixed inpatient, outpatient, oncology, and non-oncology datasets rather than oral-oncolytic-specific evidence.

Recommended future analysis: provider satisfaction and patient experience scores.
