Pharmacy Integration into the Fertility Service Line

University Hospitals Specialty Pharmacy phased a pharmacy technician and clinic-embedded pharmacist into fertility care to support medication access, patient education, IVF coordination, and specialty pharmacy services.

Objective

Evaluate and strengthen a collaborative fertility pharmacy model

The poster evaluates the current impact of pharmacy integration and identifies ways to enhance comprehensive pharmaceutical care, disease-state management, and specialty pharmacy support within a fertility service line.

Fertility medications were framed as a high-touch category because they are costly, may have limited insurance coverage, require strict adherence, often change dose, may be self-mixed and self-administered, and need specific storage guidance.

Integrated service concept

Access, education, coordination, and continuity in one clinic loop

Methods | phased build

Three implementation milestones created the service line

May 17, 2021

Service line creation

Integration began with background research, stakeholder analysis, and assessment of clinician interest.

August 16, 2021

Pharmacy technician integration

A pharmacy support liaison was created to provide benefit investigation, prior authorization support, and patient care coordination.

March 28, 2022

Pharmacist integration

The clinical pharmacy specialist embedded in clinic, joined daily IVF meetings, followed treatment start to finish, and managed third-party patients.

Context for variation

Operational factors shaped the trend line

  • Andrology and embryology laboratory closure each December for quality improvement.
  • Provider retirement.
  • Phased rollout designed to maintain patient satisfaction.
  • Electronic medical record and technology availability.

Conclusion: since launch, new patient referrals and prescription volume increased, supporting the value of a clinic-based pharmacist in optimizing fertility treatment and medication safety for IVF-cycle completion.

Future opportunities

Next steps extend pharmacy scope

The poster points to a collaborative practice agreement, continuous improvement of the patient management program, broader access to women's health medications, appeal support for off-label medication use, pharmacist appointments, and clinical interventions.
