NASP 2023 poster 1

Health system owned specialty pharmacy provided laboratory monitoring for patients with multiple sclerosis receiving sphingosine 1-phosphate receptor modulators

Nebraska Medicine specialty pharmacists built a lab-monitoring protocol for patients with MS receiving S1P modulators, then compared six-month lab compliance before and after the January 2022 workflow launch.

Why it mattered

S1P therapy creates a safety-monitoring cadence

S1P modulators for MS may increase risk for hepatotoxicity and immunosuppression, making periodic LFT and CBC monitoring part of safe longitudinal care.

Composite outcome required both LFT and CBC completion at least every 6 months during the observed year.

Protocol enacted January 1, 2022

Specialty pharmacy inserted a repeatable lab-follow-up workflow

Flag S1P orders

MSOT queue orders identified patients receiving fingolimod, siponimod, ponesimod, or ozanimod.

Review labs

Specialty pharmacists reviewed lab orders and lab results, including standing lab availability.

Set next check

Outreach assessments were scheduled so the next monitoring event could be evaluated on time.

Close gaps

Patients or outside facilities were contacted when labs were missing or late.

Methods

Pre/post denominator anchored to active S1P care

2021 PRE No specialty pharmacist lab-monitoring protocol in place.

2022 POST Protocol allowed pharmacists to order and follow labs.

Included patients were adults with MS, at least one MS clinic office visit, and at least one S1P prescription during the period, with a 2020 lookback supporting the pre-period query.

Protocol population

Four S1P agents were captured

Patients entered the protocol after an MS clinic prescription for one of the included S1P modulators, regardless of where the medication was dispensed.

Conclusion

CBC monitoring was the clearest signal

The pharmacist-led protocol was associated with numerically higher composite and LFT compliance and a statistically significantly higher proportion of CBC compliance after one year.

Future work proposed in the poster: distinguish new starts from chronic regimens, quantify outside labs, assess patient factors, and evaluate clinical outcomes.

Problem recognized

Specialty pharmacists identified that patients with MS on S1P agents were not consistently receiving appropriate lab monitoring.

Protocol created

Pharmacists partnered with MS clinic providers and could order LFT and CBC labs at Nebraska Medicine or outside facilities.

Query repeated

Automated database queries supported a pre/post comparison without relying on manual chart pulls.

Signal observed

All three monitoring measures increased after implementation, with CBC only reaching p=0.018.

Limits noted

Small sample size, unquantified outside labs, possible discontinuations, and unmeasured confounders limit interpretation.
