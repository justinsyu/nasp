Clinical and financial impact of clinical pharmacist interventions in a rural specialty pharmacy

A rural, integrated health-system specialty pharmacy reviewed documented pharmacist interventions from January through April 2024 to quantify intervention patterns, provider response, and estimated adverse-event cost avoidance.

Objective

Make pharmacist value visible to clinical and financial stakeholders

  • Assess the clinical and financial impact of interventions documented by a clinical pharmacist at one rural Midwest specialty pharmacy site.
  • Quantify intervention categories, provider response, adverse-event severity, and cost avoidance using descriptive statistics.
  • Support specialty pharmacy metric consistency by adding an economic lens to intervention recommendations.

Evidence flow

From intervention documentation to cost-avoidance estimate

1

Find eligible patients

Any patient with at least one documented clinical pharmacist intervention from January 1 to April 30, 2024.

2

Exclude other sites

Patients without intervention-document entries and patients at other Clearway Health sites were excluded.

3

Categorize work

Interventions were grouped by primary category, therapeutic area, provider response, and adverse-event grade.

4

Assign ADE risk

Probability weights ranged from 60% for potentially lethal errors to 1% for minor errors and 0% for no error.

5

Estimate value

Cost avoidance was estimated from weighted ADE prevention, then adjusted for the proportion of pharmacist time.

Case example

A delayed adverse event became a quantified avoided cost

1Specialty medication was administered and adverse event symptoms began.
2Symptoms worsened; the patient reported shortness of breath despite albuterol use.
3The pharmacist identified a possible delayed, serious adverse event and directed urgent evaluation.
4The patient was treated for an allergic reaction in the emergency department.

The panel categorized this as lethal and calculated $5,201.21 of cost avoidance for the intervention.

Study design

Retrospective observational quality-improvement analysis using a clinical pharmacist intervention document.

Population

Patients at one Clearway Health Midwest client site with at least one documented pharmacist intervention.

Clinical signal

Top intervention categories involved drug-drug or disease interactions, medication administration, and adherence support.

Financial signal

Four months of documented interventions produced an estimated $176,872.09 in cost avoidance.

Conclusion

Pharmacist interventions were widely accepted and linked to improved clinical and financial outcomes.
