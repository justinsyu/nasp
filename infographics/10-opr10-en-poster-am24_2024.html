Quantifying Health-System Specialty Pharmacists' Interventions for Patients Utilizing External Pharmacies

A single-center quality improvement analysis from Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy measured the hidden pharmacist workload created when specialty medication fulfillment occurs outside the integrated health system specialty pharmacy.

Objective

Make invisible external-fill work countable

The analysis evaluated the number and type of pharmacist actions completed for patients who could not utilize the integrated health system specialty pharmacy for fulfillment, then estimated the labor cost of that time.

Conclusion

Integrated specialty pharmacists continued to provide education, access support, adherence work, and persistence support even when patients filled elsewhere.

The poster frames payer and manufacturer lockouts as a driver of reduced visibility in the patient journey and increased workload on providers and health systems.

1. Lockout context

Payer and manufacturer restrictions limited the IHSSP's ability to dispense specialty medications.

4. Workload tally
