Optimize Your Patient Services to Support the Patient Treatment Journey

Claritas Rx evaluated whether early insights and case coordination through a patient intervention platform could improve first-fill conversion and refill persistence across specialty therapy brands, with an immunology rheumatoid arthritis scenario used to translate lift into patient impact.

Objective

Turn journey visibility into targeted intervention

The poster examined brand baskets across specialty therapies to measure how a patient intervention solution affected core patient-services KPIs, especially conversion to first fill and the ability to remain on therapy after issues appeared.

  • 1 UnderstandAccess barriers affecting new starts and refills.
  • 2 OptimizeCoverage, enrollment, funding support, and coordination strategies.
  • 3 BenchmarkPatient services performance against comparator patterns.

Methodology

Matched A/B comparison across intervention exposure

Patients were assigned to groups with and without intervention exposure, matched on preset gender, age, and payer parameters. Claritas Rx's aggregation platform supplied faster visibility than traditional data sets.

Evidence flow

Where the platform is positioned to change the journey

1. Detect access friction

Identify new starts and refill patients encountering coverage, enrollment, delay, or funding barriers.

2. Coordinate early

Use case coordination and market alerts while the patient is still recoverable in the therapy journey.

3. Convert first fill

The observed average intervention effect was a 10% lift in patient fill rate.

4. Protect refill

Among patients with at least one more fill opportunity, refill rate increased by 11% on average.

5. Keep patients on brand

The conclusion emphasized more patients starting and staying on treatment by year two.

Reported result 10% + 11%

Rheumatoid arthritis brand basket translated lift into opportunity

Conclusion

Faster insight plus coordination supported improved access

Customers using the patient intervention solution improved throughput of first-fill conversions and refill rates for patients encountering journey issues. The poster summarizes this as a 10% first-fill lift and 11% refill uplift, equating to more than 300 patients starting and staying on treatment by year two.
