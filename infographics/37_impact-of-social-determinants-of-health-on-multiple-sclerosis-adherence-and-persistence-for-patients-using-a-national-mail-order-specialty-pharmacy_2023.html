Social determinants shape different signals for MS adherence and persistence.

Objective and design

Assess who is at risk for gaps in MS therapy use.

The study evaluated associations between demographic and socioeconomic factors and medication adherence or persistence among MS patients using a national mail-order specialty pharmacy.

  • 1Adult patients with MS diagnosis code G35 and DMT initial dispense from January 1 to December 31, 2021.
  • 2Transactional, demographic, and clinical data were collected from pharmacy prescription processing systems.
  • 3Logistic regression modeled optimal adherence and persistence using age, sex, disease burden, medication count, cost, and payer type.

Outcome definitions

Two endpoints, two different risk profiles.

PDC Optimal adherence

Primary outcome: proportion of days covered, with optimal adherence defined as PDC of at least 80%.

DOT Persistence

Secondary outcome: days on therapy, with discontinuation defined as a therapy gap of at least 60 days.

How to read the split signal

Adherence risk and persistence risk were not interchangeable.

Complexity: more concomitant medications were associated with lower adherence odds but higher persistence odds.
Age: older age was associated with lower optimal PDC odds but higher persistence odds.
Payer: Medicaid increased adherence odds versus external plans, while commercial and non-health PBM increased persistence odds.

Clinically meaningful findings

Medication-list complexity, age, and male sex may reduce the likelihood of remaining adherent, while age and medication-list complexity may increase the likelihood of remaining persistent.

Strength of evidence

The analysis used a large specialty pharmacy cohort and multivariable logistic regression to account for multiple demographic, clinical, and payer factors at the same time.

Boundaries

Findings were limited to Optum Specialty Pharmacy patients, available demographic data were limited, and drug switches were counted as separate subjects in regression analysis.

Next-step pathway

Use the model signals to target proactive clinical support.

1 Flag patient profiles linked with poor adherence or persistence.
2 Build predictive analytics into specialty pharmacy workflows.
3 Increase clinical touchpoints for patients at higher risk.
4 Adjust program design using adherence and persistence separately.
