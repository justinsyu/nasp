The poster's core result is a sequenced operating checklist for manufacturer and specialty pharmacy partnerships.

Select the right advisors

Use third-party subject matter experts with real-world access experience and market trend awareness.

Allow time for relationships

Build alignment deliberately, including shared understanding of market pain points and engagement opportunities.

Define contracting strategy

Research financial drivers, network design, drug class, and drug cost before locking contract language.

Establish performance KPIs

Align metrics to brand strategy, identify KPI risks, and benchmark against market expectations.

Limit change orders

Anticipate service evolution, align internally, and reduce avoidable conflict before direction is issued.

Set QBR expectations

Define communication cadence, industry-standard governance, and contract language that supports it.

Invite proactive solutions

Use account teams as operating partners through trend monitoring, workshops, and brainstorming.

Separate relationships from deliverables

Set expectations up front while respecting the business drivers on both sides of the partnership.

Objective and method

What the poster set out to do

Objective: describe practices that help specialty pharmacies and manufacturers structure relationships that improve access, adherence, and overall program performance.

Method/source basis: the poster synthesizes best practices, lessons learned, and real-world insights from supporting multiple manufacturers with specialty pharmacy network design.

Conclusion: partnership quality is an operational lever. Clear strategy, aligned KPIs, communication expectations, and proactive account management can improve both prescriber and patient experience.

Implementation checklist

Conflict prevention appears before conflict resolution

  • Align internally before sending contractual direction to a specialty pharmacy partner.
  • Anticipate change orders by planning around evolving service models and market trends.
  • Use contract language to support expected communications and QBR cadence.
  • Maintain professional deliverable accountability even when personal relationships are strong.
