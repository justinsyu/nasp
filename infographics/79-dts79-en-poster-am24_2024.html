Targeted pharmacist outreach for patients with MS at high risk for nonadherence

Optum Specialty Pharmacy tested whether a proprietary adherence-risk model plus a proactive pharmacist call could improve medication coverage, close refill gaps, and connect patients to refill and clinical support programs.

  • Clinical Management Program enrollment showed the strongest conversion signal: 49 enrolled after call among 53 offers.
  • Adherence texting and Schedule My Fill also added post-call enrollment, suggesting targeted outreach moved patients toward operational supports.
  • The pilot was limited by one clinician call, low volume, and only three months of dispense follow-up.

Objective

Evaluate proactive pharmacist intervention for MS patients predicted to be at high risk for suboptimal adherence.

Methods

Single-center, observational, retrospective cohort with propensity score matching and Wilcoxon signed rank testing.

Population

MS patients receiving fills 1-7 of a disease modifying therapy at Optum Specialty Pharmacy.

Signal

PDC increased by 6 percentage points and total gap days decreased by 5 days in the participant group.

Conclusion

Findings favored the intervention but were not statistically significant, likely because of low volume and short follow-up.
