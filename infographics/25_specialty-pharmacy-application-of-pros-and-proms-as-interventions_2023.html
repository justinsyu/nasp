Methods flow

From claims signal to patient-centered intervention loop

1

Find risk

Twelve months of retrospective claims and fills were pulled across 16 pharmacies.

2

Define cohort

HE patients concurrently prescribed lactulose and rifaximin were mapped for non-adherence patterns.

3

Enroll monthly

Patients were offered a monthly adherence program during referral intake.

4

Capture voice

Staff recorded PRO/PROM fields, activities, education, and patient or caregiver understanding.

5

Monitor data

Patient experience entries were plotted against claims data during the same period.

PRO/PROM capture architecture

Six structured fields turned conversations into analyzable interventions.

Disease understanding

Patient verbalized understanding of HE and the need for lifelong treatment adherence.

Teach-back

Staff documented whether the teach-back method was employed for patient education.

Therapy holiday

Reasons for self-imposed interruption or temporary abandonment were captured.

Acute care

All-cause hospitalizations or emergency room visits were tracked alongside fills.

Ability to converse

Patient ability or inability to converse was recorded as part of the encounter.

Interaction quality

Inappropriate or combative interactions were documented for clinical context.

Monthly monitoring map

  • Patient hospitalizations, emergency room visits, and transplant events.
  • Patient assistance program transfers and refill continuity indicators.

Learning objective

Validate whether DOT gains aligned with patient intervention.

The poster asked whether the increase in days on therapy among non-adherent prone patients could be attributed to pharmacist and nurse interventions that incorporated patient-reported outcomes and patient-reported outcome measures.

Conclusion

Specialty pharmacy can surface a care gap and implement a response.

The authors concluded that patient-interactive interventions aligned with higher days-on-therapy data, illustrating how specialty pharmacy teams can identify an adherence problem and operationalize patient-care solutions.
