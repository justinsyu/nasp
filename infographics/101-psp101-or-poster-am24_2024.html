Methods and QI model

Annual survey feedback became a five-part service improvement loop

1

Launch HSSP services

SRHS established specialty pharmacy services in August 2019 with liaisons embedded in oncology, HIV, hepatitis C, and one additional clinic.

2

Survey annually

A 33-question patient satisfaction survey included the standardized NPS recommendation question on a 0-10 scale.

3

Target experience gaps

Results informed QI efforts around customer service, delivery coordination, phone access, and care issue resolution.

4

Track NPS trend

Fall survey cohorts from 2019 through 2023 were compared with available NASP specialty pharmacy averages.

Focused QI efforts to improve patient experience
Building relationship Foster connections over time between patients and liaisons.
Enhanced trust Execute improvement work that strengthens patient confidence in the specialty pharmacy.
Provider buy-in Maintain communication with clinic staff, frequent check-ins, and resolution of patient care issues.
Continuity Keep a consistent liaison presence within clinics.
Communication Tailor conversations to individualize patient care.

Results

SRHS crossed into excellent NPS territory and stayed there

In the years with direct comparison after 2020, SRHS exceeded available NASP averages in 2021 and 2022; no 2023 NASP value was listed in the poster table.

Survey pattern

More patients reached, stable response rates

The survey pool expanded from 324 distributed surveys in Fall 2019 to 1,041 in Fall 2023. Responses increased from 50 to 129 while response rates remained in the low-to-mid teens after the 2020 peak.

Interpretation

What the rising score implies

  • The program moved from the high end of the great NPS range in 2019 into the excellent range by 2020.
  • Sustained scores above 80 coincided with continuity of clinic-based liaison support and targeted communication improvements.
  • The conclusion attributes the trend to focused quality improvement and positions the HSSP model as a lever for patient engagement.
Bottom line QI + continuity

Annual survey feedback, consistent liaisons, and service fixes created a measurable patient-satisfaction signal.

Conclusion

Focused improvement work tracked with sustained patient satisfaction gains

The poster concludes that continuous NPS increases from HSSP inception to 2023 highlight the impact of focused quality improvement on patient engagement and satisfaction. SRHS scores exceeded the national specialty pharmacy benchmark in available comparisons, supporting the potential value of the integrated HSSP model.

  • Customer service examples included reliable phone access and delivery coordination.
  • A financial assistance example showed a liaison obtaining approval for a high-cost medication within one day.
