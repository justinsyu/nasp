NASP 2023 / Poster 5

Education and evaluation strategies to implement a new care management documentation system.

Yale New Haven Health built a structured training model to onboard specialty pharmacy staff to a new EHR care management application, then used pre- and post-implementation surveys to tune education tools and track self-reported preparedness.

Objective

Build and test a robust onboarding plan for a new specialty pharmacy EHR workflow.

The poster focused on whether a deliberate education plan could prepare pharmacy personnel for a new EHR application and the clinical and operational workflows tied to care management documentation.

Why change was needed

Specialty medication growth and variable workflows created pressure for integrated, comprehensive documentation.

What made training resilient

Guides, recordings, terminology crosswalks, and superusers gave staff multiple ways to learn the same new workflow.

Where feedback changed action

Survey feedback that staff needed more training led to another session closer to go-live.

What complicated rollout

Go-live delay and a simultaneous shift from onsite to fully remote work added implementation noise.

What to improve

Communication needed tightening because some staff did not realize recorded walkthroughs were already available.

Conclusion

Recorded walkthroughs, live training, and remote training emerged as the most useful resources.

The poster concluded that these formats were the strongest education tools for onboarding specialty pharmacy staff to the new care management documentation software.

Voice of staff

Staff comments emphasized reusable materials and transition support.

"Extremely helpful" pre-recorded videos supported post-training review.

A comprehensive training document was useful during the transition.

Staff felt the department provided as much training as reasonably possible before use.
