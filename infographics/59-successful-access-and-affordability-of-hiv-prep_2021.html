Overcoming Prescriber Concerns Through Successful Access and Affordability of PrEP

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy described how a multidisciplinary PrEP clinic moved patients from prescription to treatment start while addressing insurance approval, prior authorization, copay card, and assistance-program barriers.

Objective

Describe the PrEP medication access process and its outcomes.

The poster focused on patients seen in a multidisciplinary PrEP clinic, where specialty pharmacists and pharmacy technicians reviewed coverage, pursued prior authorization when needed, coordinated assistance, and counseled patients before treatment start.

Evidence flow

The clinic separated clinical assessment, coverage work, assistance, counseling, and monitoring.

Assess

Prescribing provider and pharmacist reviewed risk factors, drug interactions, comorbidities, baseline labs, and screening.

Check coverage

Technicians confirmed insurance eligibility, preferred pharmacy, prior authorization requirements, and anticipated PrEP cost.

Clear access

Insured patients moved through PA paperwork and appeals if needed; uninsured or underinsured patients entered assistance pathways.

Counsel

Pharmacists covered administration, adherence, side effects, storage, refill management, monitoring, and contact information.

Monitor

On-treatment follow-up tracked adherence, efficacy, adverse effects, ongoing risk, and continued need for financial assistance.

Methods in brief

Retrospective review of starts through one specialty pharmacy.

Adult patients initiating emtricitabine-tenofovir disoproxil fumarate from the multidisciplinary clinic were included when prescriptions were filled by Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy. The primary outcome was time to treatment initiation; secondary outcomes were reasons for delay and patient out-of-pocket medication cost.

Conclusion

Access can be rapid when financial navigation is built in.

Less than half of patients required prior authorization, all PAs were approved, and most insured patients had low or no medication cost when copay cards and assistance programs were used.

Bottom line: a pharmacist-technician access pathway addressed prescriber concerns by pairing rapid coverage approval with affordability tools, allowing most patients in this insured PrEP cohort to start therapy quickly and with no medication out-of-pocket cost.
