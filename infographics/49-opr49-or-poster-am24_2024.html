Methods translated into a workflow

From expected refill to avoided waste

1

Expected fill flagged

The team documented situations where the full oncology medication amount was not dispensed at the expected refill time.

2

Inventory reason captured

Rationale included excess supply, therapy hold, likely medication change, likely dose change, split fill, or dose optimization.

3

Medication amount tracked

Medication, strength, and amount not dispensed were recorded to quantify avoided inventory movement.

4

AWP converted to value

Average wholesale price was used to estimate cost avoidance for the pharmacy, patients, and payers.

Interpretation

Inventory stewardship depended on live clinical context

Oral oncology regimens can be expensive, complex, and frequently changed because of adverse effects or disease status. The pharmacy process paired refill timing with patient and provider engagement so supply decisions reflected the current plan.

  • High-change-risk and excess-supply rationales drove the largest combined avoided-cost signal.
  • Dose optimization had the highest average category savings at $17,856 per intervention, but occurred only twice.
  • Access to medical record information was highlighted as a way to enable additional inventory interventions.

Conclusion: strict oral oncology inventory management reduced waste and generated more than $1.1 million in estimated cost avoidance over one year.

Objective

Describe interventions that reduced oral oncology medication waste and calculate cost avoidance from specialty pharmacy processes.

Study frame

Inventory management interventions by oncology pharmacists and liaisons from April 2023 through March 2024.

Table pattern

Interventions were grouped by role, category, rationale, frequency, total cost, and average cost per intervention.

Result

120 interventions generated $1,145,114 in estimated cost avoidance, averaging $9,543 per intervention.

Conclusion

Strong engagement with patients and providers was necessary to avoid excess cost or waste and maintain appropriate supply.
