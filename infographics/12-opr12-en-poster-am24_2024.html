Risankizumab dose escalation in patients with Crohn's disease

A retrospective single-center cohort describes who received more frequent risankizumab maintenance dosing, how access was managed, and early response after escalation in heavily treatment-experienced Crohn's disease patients.

Study question

Who needed escalation, and what happened after access was obtained?

The cohort focused on Crohn's disease patients who initiated risankizumab from June 2022 to July 2023, received at least one escalated dose, and had follow-up evaluation before October 3, 2023.

  • Reasons for escalation included persistent symptoms, endoscopic inflammation, elevated fecal calprotectin with pouch inflammation, hospitalization flare, and CTE inflammation with symptoms.
  • The poster noted 50 additional referrals for escalated dosing at the study site as of December 12, 2023.

Conclusion

Early signal supports more systematic study

In this single-center cohort, escalated risankizumab dosing was used in severe, treatment-experienced Crohn's disease, often requiring additional insurance authorization. The observed early outcomes were promising because most patients improved or remained stable.

  • The study is descriptive and retrospective, so it should be interpreted as an early practice signal rather than comparative efficacy evidence.
  • Access process complexity is part of the intervention context, not just an administrative footnote.
