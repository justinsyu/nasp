Leveraging technology for specialty refills

Penn Medicine Specialty Pharmacy replaced a phone-heavy refill workflow with a Way to Health texting platform that gathered refill, adherence, safety, and therapy-goal information while auto-populating the EHR refill note.

Methods as operating design

The measurement plan followed three lenses: program, patient, and staff.

Build the texting platform

Penn Medicine Specialty Pharmacy partnered with Way to Health in May 2020 to script chatbot logic and integrate refill questions with pharmacy workflows.

Measure program completion

Program impact was defined as successful check-ins that did not require staff telephone intervention.

Survey patient experience

Rolling NPS surveys began in November 2021 for patients who completed at least three refills, with annual surveying planned.

Analyze staff time

A February 2021 time study evaluated workflow steps among 670 patients enrolled in texting, including 25 W2H and 25 non-W2H refill comparisons.

Scale-up results

A 42-patient pilot became a multi-pharmacy refill channel.

Conclusion

Texting expanded capacity while preserving clinical safeguards.

The W2H platform positively impacted program, staff, and patient outcomes by shortening time to reach patients by two days, reducing process time by half, lowering administrative burden, and maintaining strong patient satisfaction.

Practical takeaway

The automation worked because it stayed inside the refill workflow.

The chatbot was scripted around pharmacy-defined parameters, integrated with pharmacy systems, and routed information directly to the EHR, so technology replaced repetitive coordination rather than removing clinical escalation.
