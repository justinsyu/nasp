NASP 2022 poster 02-2022

Impact of an adherence texting program in a large national specialty pharmacy

Optum Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether adherence texts - dose reminders, motivational prompts, and adherence feedback - improved medication coverage and time on therapy for patients using texting as a preferred communication channel.

Study architecture

From eligible texting patients to matched adherence outcomes

Population

Patients serviced by Optum Specialty Pharmacy with texting as a preferred communication method.

Groups

ATP enrollees who had not opted out were compared with non-participants.

Controls

Propensity score matching reduced measured differences between participant groups.

Analysis

PDC, days on therapy, fills, persistency, and gap days were tested with p < 0.05 significant.

Persistency pattern

Participant curves stayed higher in INF and ONC

The reported persistency curves separated after month 2, with participants retaining higher persistency through month 6 in both cohorts.

Interpretation

What the poster concluded

Conclusion

Text technology can be used to improve specialty medication adherence and reduce gap days, with the longer-term goal of improving health outcomes.

Secondary endpoints

Days on therapy and additional fills did not significantly increase in the MS, INF, or ONC cohorts.

Limitations

  • Baseline language, race, and insurance type were unavailable for deeper cohort definition.
  • The observation period was short: 6 months for INF and ONC, 8 months for MS.
  • Participants with at least one refill were included even if they later withdrew from ATP.
