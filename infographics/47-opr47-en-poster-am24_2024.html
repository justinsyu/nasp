Impact of Pharmacist Intervention on Herpes Zoster Vaccination Rates in JAKi Patients

A pharmacist phone intervention tested whether targeted counseling could move immunocompromised patients with inflammatory disorders from RZV-eligible to RZV-started within 30 days.

Why this mattered

ACIP recommends 2 RZV doses for immunocompromised adults age 19+

The poster framed the problem as a recommendation-awareness gap: WVUHSP prior data showed only 23.3% of patients taking JAK inhibitors were appropriately vaccinated against herpes zoster.

Conclusion

Education moved intent more than immediate uptake

Two of forty contacted patients started RZV within 30 days. The poster concludes that patient education was the most common reported barrier and recommends evaluating provider education as the next step.

Study limits

Interpret with a short follow-up window

Authors noted a small study population and limited time between pharmacist call and data collection, which may undercount later vaccination starts.
