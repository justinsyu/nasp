Evaluation of a Social Determinants of Health Screening and Intervention Framework within an Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy Care Model

A four-health-system pilot tested a standardized SDOH screening, pharmacist action planning, resource referral, and follow-up process for specialty pharmacy patients not meeting HIV, rheumatoid arthritis, or hyperlipidemia outcomes.

Core idea

Screen, refer, follow up. Pharmacists used a structured care loop to identify social barriers and connect patients with tailored resources.

Methods as workflow

The framework moved from standardized questions to action and reassessment

1

Identify eligible patients

Patients were selected when disease markers suggested unmet clinical goals in HIV, RA, or hyperlipidemia.

2

Ask standardized SDOH questions

Screening questions were based on the CMS Accountable Health Communities Screening Tool.

3

Create a personalized action plan

Pharmacists matched identified barriers with targeted mitigation steps for each patient.

4

Use a resource guide

Community and social service connections supported food, transportation, financial, and activity barriers.

5

Close the loop

Follow-up assessments occurred one to two months after screening to check progress and new needs.

Conclusion signal

Specialty pharmacists can convert social risk screening into actionable support.

The pilot linked tailored education, facilitated community connections, and follow-up to barriers that can affect medication use and outcomes.

What the poster adds

A practical model for HSSP-based SDOH intervention

  • Acceptance of pharmacist recommendations was high among screened patients, supporting feasibility of pharmacist-led SDOH intervention.
  • Food security and nutrition needs were the largest intervention category, followed by transportation and financial strain.
  • Follow-up remained important because nearly one-third needed additional assistance and one patient had a new barrier identified.
  • The authors call for future research on clinical outcomes, satisfaction, adherence, and absenteeism.
