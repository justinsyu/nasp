Impact of a structured approach to reengage patients lost to follow-up within a health system-based specialty pharmacy

A retrospective observational analysis tested whether a sequenced outreach workflow could bring specialty pharmacy patients who were unreachable or previously opted out back into clinical pharmacist services.

Study question

Can a standard workflow recover gaps in pharmacist follow-up?

The poster focused on patients appearing on a lost-to-follow-up report who were due for a refill within the next week. Exclusions removed patients whose medication did not qualify for specialty pharmacist outreach, those with outreach already underway, or those who had requested future outreach.

Re-engagement modalities

A three-touch workflow moved from pharmacist calls to refill-call handoff.

P

Two phone calls

Clinical pharmacist outreach started with direct calls to patients identified through the lost-to-follow-up report.

M

Portal message if available

If phone outreach did not reach the patient, the team sent an electronic portal message when that channel was available.

W

Warm transfer during refill

During refill calls, pharmacy technicians could warm transfer eligible patients back to the clinical pharmacist team.

Conclusion

Multi-method outreach can be a practical recovery pathway.

Patients previously lost to follow-up may be re-engaged with clinical pharmacist services using a structured workflow that coordinates clinical pharmacists and pharmacy technicians within a health system specialty pharmacy.

Interpretive caution

Not a head-to-head test of channels

  • The study did not compare outreach methods, so it cannot identify which channel was most effective.
  • Some modalities, such as phone calls, were adhered to at a higher rate than portal messaging.
  • Findings can support future workflow development and research on specialty pharmacy patient engagement.
