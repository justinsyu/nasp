Disease Activity Scores and Medication Adherence in University Hospitals' Rheumatoid Arthritis Patients

University Hospitals Specialty Pharmacy reviewed RAPID3 documentation in a rheumatology clinic to describe rheumatoid arthritis disease activity, medication adherence, and whether adherence aligned with disease activity.

  • Rheumatoid arthritis

  • DMARD therapy
  • Integrated specialty pharmacy

Secondary objective

Adherence data were available for only part of the UHSP cohort.

PDC ≥80% Among UHSP patients with calculable adherence
  • PDC distribution: 1 patient below 40%, 4 patients from 40% to 80%, and 17 patients at or above the usual good-adherence threshold.
  • Correlation test: Chi-square value was 0.01 and R2 was 0.0123, indicating lack of a strong disease activity/adherence relationship in this small sample.
  • Interpretive limit: PDC measured medication possession, not confirmed DMARD administration.

Conclusion

The finding points more to workflow improvement than a firm adherence conclusion.

The poster concluded that most included RAPID3 scores were high disease activity, while the low number of UHSP fills and RAPID3 captures made the secondary objectives difficult to interpret.

The operational next step was to build a sustainable RAPID3 documentation workflow and target high-severity patients for clinical pharmacist follow-up.

Demographic pattern

The analytic patient table showed an older, mostly female RA population.

186 Total patients in the demographic table.
63 Average age overall, with a 20 to 90 year range.
83% Female patients in the overall demographic table.
58% Conventional DMARD therapy overall; UHSP patients were mostly combination therapy.

Future directions

Turn RAPID3 capture into a repeatable specialty pharmacy trigger.

  • Share results with rheumatology providers included in the study.
  • Adjust EMR note templates to prompt necessary disease activity documentation.
  • Repeat the study after workflow changes to track patient-level disease activity over time.
  • Use high-severity RAPID3 scores and PDC below 80% as pharmacist outreach signals.
