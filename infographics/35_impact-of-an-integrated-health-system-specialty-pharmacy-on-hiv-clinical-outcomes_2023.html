Objective and methods

What the analysis tested

Objective: Describe the impact of an integrated health system specialty pharmacy model and socio-demographic factors on HIV viral load suppression.

Design: Single-center, retrospective, observational analysis of adult and pediatric patients with HIV on antiretroviral therapy filling with Proprium Specialty Pharmacy from January to December 2022.

Included patients: Enrolled in the patient management program for more than 6 months with reportable viral load, or on service less than 6 months with viral load below 200 copies/mL and a clinical assessment in the past year.

Regression signals

Most demographic and service variables were not significant; adherence and cost patterns carried the discussion.

Conclusion

Consistency across patient groups is the central clinical message.

  • The HSSP model showed high suppression despite variation in age, sex, service time, race/ethnicity, and insurance type.
  • The OOP-cost association should be interpreted cautiously because more than 94% of patients had no out-of-pocket cost.
  • The adherence signal reinforces the importance of monitoring and follow-up in HIV specialty pharmacy care.
