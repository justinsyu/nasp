Specialty pharmacists built a practical oral oncology education bridge for nurses.

Yale New Haven Health piloted a specialty pharmacist-led education series for outpatient oncology practice nurses, pairing pharmacy operations expertise with medication-specific teaching on oral oncolytics.

Evidence flow

A focused pilot moved from gap identification to measured staff feedback.

Identify the gap

Growth in targeted oral oncolytics created a need for oncology nurse education beyond traditional IV-focused training.

Build the series

A lead specialty pharmacist coordinated topics and recruited pharmacy residents and specialty pharmacists as speakers.

Evaluate at 10 months

An electronic survey assessed satisfaction, expected use in practice, and desired future education topics.

Staff voice

Conclusion

Specialty pharmacists were positioned to close the education gap among nursing and pharmacy staff in a large health system with an integrated specialty pharmacy.

Objective

Create, implement, and evaluate a pilot education series for outpatient oncology practice nurses on oral oncology medications.

Future direction

Use the pilot findings to guide development of an educational program offered to all cancer center staff.
