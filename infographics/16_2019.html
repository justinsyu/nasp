How the model was built

One week of work became a clinic staffing equation

1. Record

Pharmacist duties were logged across a week, including tasks time did not allow.

2. Classify

Each duty was judged as pharmacist-required or appropriate for technician delegation.

3. Categorize

Tasks were grouped as access/cost, clinical, communication, or unable to complete.

4. Convert

Reported hours were divided by 40 to express the workload as FTEs.

5. Staff

The resulting model pairs pharmacist capacity with a technician FTE requirement.

Poster context

Why this mattered

The Penn Specialty Pharmacy Program was created in 2014 to establish a pharmacy presence in clinics with a focus on specialty medication access and adherence. As the program expanded, the team needed a repeatable way to estimate pharmacist and technician staffing needs instead of relying on informal workload impressions.
