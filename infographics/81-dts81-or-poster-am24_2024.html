Implementing targeted adherence outreach for patients with rheumatoid arthritis identified as at risk of nonadherence

Penn State Health Specialty Pharmacy piloted a risk-stratification model that flagged rheumatoid arthritis patients at medium or high risk of nonadherence, then routed pharmacists to targeted clinical calls and recommended actions before adherence problems became reactive rescue work.

The poster's objective was to demonstrate implementation of targeted adherence outreach based on a model that predicted nonadherence risk in a specialty pharmacy setting.

Methods

A four-step implementation loop embedded model output into pharmacy workflow.

Develop model

Clinical care-management data from the specialty pharmacy platform were used to stratify RA patients by nonadherence risk.

Flag patients

Risk categories were added to patient profiles, making medium-risk and high-risk patients visible in the care workflow.

Tailor outreach

Pharmacists completed clinical calls using recommended actions tied to patient-specific risk drivers.

Reassess risk

The model was used again six months later to identify whether each patient's risk category changed.

Primary signal

66% of initially high-risk patients had a lower risk category at six-month follow-up.

Background

Uncontrolled RA increases disease activity and costs, while nonadherence remains a persistent treatment challenge.

Model use

Specialty pharmacy care data were used to predict nonadherence risk before waiting for missed therapy to occur.

Intervention

Pharmacists contacted at-risk patients and used model-linked recommendations to address risk drivers.

Signal

High-risk outreach reached 85% of targeted patients and produced a 97% successful recommendation rate.

Next step

The authors recommend evaluating the model in other disease states and directly measuring adherence impact.

Interpretation

Early identification created a practical outreach queue.

The poster positions the pilot as a workflow demonstration: model flags plus pharmacist calls can identify adherence barriers, monitoring gaps, adverse events, and regimen issues before reactive nonadherence management is needed.

Limitations and next steps

The implementation signal needs broader outcome testing.

The authors call for future studies in other disease states and for direct adherence analyses using inferential statistics.

Conclusion: a specialty pharmacy risk-stratification workflow for RA patients helped pharmacists target adherence outreach, complete calls for most high-risk patients, and shift two thirds of known high-risk patients to lower risk by six months.
