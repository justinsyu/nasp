NASP 2020 Annual Meeting & Expo

Health-system specialty pharmacy coordination moved GCSF injections from clinic to home during COVID-19

Atrium Health's Levine Cancer Institute and Specialty Pharmacy Service evaluated a rapid pilot that converted medically billed, on-site granulocyte-colony stimulating factor injections into pharmacy billed, at-home self-injection when access and affordability allowed.

Objective

Measure whether rapid pharmacy coordination could make at-home GCSF feasible

The evaluation tracked successful transition from on-site administration to pharmacy-provided at-home use, turnaround times for key access endpoints, and the value of financial coordination activities.

Methods: retrospective quality evaluation of electronic prescribing, EMR messaging, transaction, and dispensing records for prescriptions written March 23 through April 23, 2020.

Discussion pattern

Integrated access mattered

Because SPS had provider collaboration and EMR access, technicians could coordinate benefit investigation, authorization, appeals, financial assistance, patient outreach, delivery, external transfer, and clinic fallback without losing visibility into the patient's care path.

Conclusion

Health-system SP services can reduce avoidable clinic visits while preserving access

The pilot showed efficient benefit investigation and care coordination for at-home GCSF self-injection, with low patient costs when financial resources were available and appropriately coordinated.
