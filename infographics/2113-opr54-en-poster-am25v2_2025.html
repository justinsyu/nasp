Study Architecture

One open-label route, then response-guided maintenance

Screen

Eligible patients had chronic AD, age ≥12 years, adolescent weight ≥40 kg, and prior dupilumab exposure.

W0-W16

Lebrikizumab 250 mg Q2W after 500 mg loading doses at Weeks 0 and 2.

W16

IGA 0/1 or EASI 75 responders moved to Q4W; non-responders continued Q2W.

W24

Primary endpoint readout, followed by safety follow-up approximately 12 weeks after last dose.

Objective

Assess efficacy and safety after dupilumab

The analysis reports 24-week efficacy and safety of lebrikizumab in dupilumab-experienced patients with moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis.

It specifically asks whether patients with inadequate response to dupilumab respond to lebrikizumab, and whether patients who stopped dupilumab because of adverse events experience similar events again.

Safety Snapshot Through Week 24

Safety profile was described as consistent with other Phase 3 lebrikizumab trials

53.5% TEAE
2.3% SAE

Three participants reported conjunctivitis TEAEs; events were mild or moderate and did not lead to discontinuation.

Conclusion

Meaningful multi-domain improvement after prior dupilumab

Lebrikizumab improved skin clearance, facial disease, hand dermatitis, itch, and quality of life over 24 weeks in dupilumab-experienced patients with moderate-to-severe AD, with safety findings aligned with prior lebrikizumab Phase 3 experience.
