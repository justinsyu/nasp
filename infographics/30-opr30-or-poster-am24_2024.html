An integrated health system specialty pharmacy and HIV viral load suppression

Sentara Health evaluated whether patients with a high HIV viral load who ever used Proprium Specialty Pharmacy were more likely to reach and maintain undetectable viral load than patients using commercial specialty pharmacies.

Core finding

Higher suppression and better maintenance Proprium patients were 1.09 times more likely to achieve undetectable viral load and 1.28 times more likely to maintain suppression for six months after reaching undetectable status.

Adults aged 18-89 years with HIV, ARV therapy, and high viral load from July 1, 2017 through June 30, 2023.

Objective

Determine the impact of a health-system specialty pharmacy on HIV viral-load suppression and maintenance after high viral load.

Data source

Electronic medical record and specialty pharmacy management system data captured demographics, labs, utilization, and covariates.

Primary outcome

Achievement of undetectable viral load, defined by the poster as fewer than 20 copies/mL.

Secondary outcome

Maintenance of suppression for six months after reaching undetectable status.

Conclusion

Use of Proprium Specialty Pharmacy was associated with higher likelihood of suppression and more durable maintenance.

Clinical interpretation

Integrated care model as adherence infrastructure

  • Patients with HIV need sustained ARV adherence to achieve and maintain viral-load suppression.
  • The poster frames the HSSP model as a way to overcome barriers to optimal HIV outcomes.
  • Durability mattered: the survival curve favored Proprium after patients had already achieved suppression.
