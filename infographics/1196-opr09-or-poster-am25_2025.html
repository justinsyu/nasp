NASP 2025 poster 1196-OPR09-OR

Pharmacist-led patient management in inflammatory disease

Vivo Health Pharmacy evaluated a health-system specialty pharmacy patient management program for inflammatory disease. Across paired initial assessments and reassessments, patient-reported pain, flares, and RA RAPID3 scores all moved in the right direction.

Objective and interpretation

What the study asked.

Objective: Measure the effectiveness of the pharmacist-led PMP on pain and flares across inflammatory conditions and RAPID3 scores among RA patients.

  • The PMP paired initial telehealth assessments with reassessments, education, medication counseling, safety monitoring, and refill screening.
  • The conclusion links specialized clinical pharmacist integration with positive clinical outcomes in complex inflammatory disease medication management.
62.2% RA patients moved from severe to less-severe RAPID3 category.
34.7% Initial severe patients reassessed as moderate.
17.3% Initial severe patients reassessed near remission.

Methods flow

Retrospective pre-post review of patients with assessment pairs.

Identify

Patients with inflammatory disease and an initial assessment documented since 2021.

Enroll

Include patients who opted into the PMP and had at least two telehealth assessments.

Compare

Pair baseline initial assessment values with the latest reassessment completed in 2024.

Classify

Assess pain, flares, RAPID3, and RA severity from severe through near remission.

Included

  • Opted into the patient management program
  • At least 2 telehealth assessments
  • Diagnosis of inflammatory disease

Excluded

  • Declined or transitioned out of PMP
  • No reassessments documented
  • Fewer than 3 refills with the HSSP

Bottom line

The pharmacist-led PMP showed meaningful PRO improvement across inflammatory disease care.

In this HSSP population, reassessment values showed significantly lower pain and fewer flares across inflammatory disease conditions, plus a lower RAPID3 score among RA patients. The poster frames these findings as evidence that specialized pharmacist management can support positive clinical outcomes for complex medication regimens.
