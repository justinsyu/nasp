Evidence flow

Prior authorization moved from clinic bottleneck to pharmacy-owned pathway

New DMT start

Neurologist prescribes an infusible disease-modifying therapy for a patient with MS.

EMR trigger

A note routes the new start to the specialty pharmacy team to initiate PA work.

PA decision

Pharmacy tracks approval, denial, benefit-route issues, and appeal needs before scheduling.

Infusion ready

Once approved, nursing schedules infusion and the clinical pharmacist counsels the patient.

Objective and methods

What the program tested

  • Increase approved authorizations for infusible MS DMTs.
  • Improve authorization timeliness so patients could receive appropriate therapy without avoidable delay.
  • Include patients initiated on infusible DMT between September 2018 and April 2019.
  • Coordinate status updates between pharmacy and neurology before infusion scheduling.

Conclusion

Ownership created a repeatable access service

Embedding specialty pharmacy in the MS clinic produced a new service line for prior authorization completion, appeals, and handoffs. The poster concludes that multidisciplinary organization improved access to infusible DMT and supported optimized clinical outcomes for patients with MS.

Barrier

High-cost infusible DMTs require prior authorizations that can delay therapy access.

Intervention

NHSP took ownership of the PA process for new infusible DMT starts.

Communication

EMR notes and pharmacy-to-neurology updates kept scheduling tied to authorization status.

Outcome

Approval reached 90%, and 96.7% of patients received infusion.

Timeliness

Turnaround improved to 24-72 hours after pharmacy onboarding.
