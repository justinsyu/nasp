Poster 53 | NASP 2023 | PAH nursing support

A Comparison of Face-to-Face vs. Virtual Educational Nursing Support on Patient Compliance with Oral and Inhaled PAH Therapies

Claims-linked cohort analysis compared patients starting complex oral or inhaled pulmonary arterial hypertension therapies during a pre-pandemic live-visit period with patients in a later virtual-visit period.

  • Medication adherence
  • Pulmonary arterial hypertension
  • Nurse education program
  • Live vs virtual visits

Main message: overall medication compliance was similar across live and virtual nursing support, while recorded adverse drug events were notably higher in the virtual-period group.

Objective

Did virtual education preserve compliance?

Investigators compared adherence and compliance rates for oral and inhaled PAH therapies between patients receiving in-home, face-to-face educational nursing visits and patients receiving telephonic nurse visits.

50.96% Live cohort

June 2018 through March 31, 2020 was treated as the face-to-face nursing visit period.

49.04% Virtual cohort

April 1, 2020 through December 31, 2021 was treated as the virtual nursing visit period.

Interpretation

What the poster adds

Compliance parity

0.15 pts

Difference in optimal adherence between live and virtual groups was less than one percentage point.

Support still matters

86.4%

The authors cite prior evidence of higher MPR with nursing support vs unsupported oral PAH therapies: 86.4% vs 75%.

Modality caveat

10-40%

Some visits during the virtual-period assumption still occurred live, depending on therapy.

Conclusion

Similar compliance, different ADE burden

Patients receiving live nursing visits demonstrated similar overall medication compliance to those receiving virtual nursing visits. The notable difference was a higher incidence of recorded ADEs in the virtual visit setting.
