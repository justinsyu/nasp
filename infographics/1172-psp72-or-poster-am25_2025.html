HSSP support turns HCV-positive kidney offers into faster antiviral starts

Source poster: Utilization of a health-system specialty pharmacy to optimize management of HCV-positive kidney transplant recipients

This quality-improvement poster describes how a Portland health-system specialty pharmacy helped kidney transplant recipients receiving HCV-NAT+ allografts navigate DAA access, prior authorization, financial assistance, counseling, and therapy monitoring.

Why the pathway matters

HCV-NAT+ donors may expand the kidney donor pool

>100K Patients in the U.S. awaiting kidney transplant
3-5 yrs Approximate average kidney transplant wait time
  • Historically, HCV-positive donors were underutilized because of disease-transmission concerns.
  • Advances in HCV treatment have prompted centers to consider these donors to reduce wait times.

Objective

Describe an HSSP program for HCV-NAT+ kidney recipients

The poster centers on program development for recipients of kidney allografts from donors with a positive HCV nucleic acid test. Evaluation measures were operational: referral count, prior authorization approval, financial assistance, and time to treatment.

Program need

Transplant demand exceeds organ supply, making HCV-NAT+ donors a potential donor-pool expansion strategy.

HSSP role

Specialty pharmacy expertise addressed DAA access, cost barriers, medication education, and treatment follow-up.

Operational signal

Early data showed 100% prior authorization approval and a 7-day mean time to DAA initiation.

Conclusion

HSSP teams can be valuable assets in clinical management of HCV-NAT+ kidney transplant recipients.

Next step

Future studies should evaluate clinical outcomes and the effect of treatment delays after HCV-NAT+ allograft receipt.

Infographic synthesis

The poster's central pattern: access work is clinical infrastructure

In this early QI cohort, the HSSP did not simply dispense DAAs. It created an operational bridge between transplant decision-making and antiviral treatment initiation by coordinating benefits, approvals, assistance, delivery, counseling, refill touchpoints, and protocolized monitoring.
