The Impact of an Integrated Health-System Specialty Pharmacy on HIV ART Outcomes

Atrium Health embedded an HIV clinical pharmacist and pharmacy technician in an outpatient infectious disease clinic, then compared patients who opted into specialty pharmacy services with patients who opted out.

Embedded care model

Pharmacy support was placed at the point of HIV care

The service joined patient, provider, pharmacist, technician, and pharmacy operations inside the clinic workflow. Refill histories drove Medication Possession Ratio, while baseline and follow-up labs tracked viral load and CD4 outcomes.

Clinical interpretation

The poster reports significant within-group improvements for opt-in patients but no statistically significant between-group difference for reaching viral suppression or CD4 endpoints, likely limited by sample size.

Conclusion

High-touch refill and access support reinforced the HIV care continuum

The authors concluded that integrated health-system specialty pharmacy services can improve ART adherence and support viral suppression and immune function among HIV patients.

Core service components included proactive refills, drug-interaction management, side-effect support, financial assistance, prior authorizations, and barrier resolution.
