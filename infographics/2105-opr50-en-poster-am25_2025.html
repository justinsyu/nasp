Pharmacist involvement in specialty medication discontinuations and dose changes

Methods translated

From pharmacist recommendation to attributable cost avoidance

1

Find pharmacist-linked outcomes

Electronic medical record and specialty pharmacy management data identified interventions tied to discontinuation, switch, or dose change.

2

Define the medication outcome

Discontinuation meant no new specialty drug within 30 days; switch meant a different specialty drug; dose change kept the medication but changed the dose.

3

Score pharmacist probability

A 3-investigator panel assigned probabilities from absolutely not attributable to likely attributable, with values from 0 to 0.75.

4

Apply medication cost lenses

One avoided fill was priced using WAC, AWP, and AWP minus 20%, then multiplied by the assigned attribution probability.

Objective

Evaluate HSSP pharmacist involvement in specialty medication discontinuations, switches, and dose changes, plus resulting cost avoidance.

Design

Single-center, retrospective cohort analysis using electronic medical record and specialty pharmacy management data.

Panel scoring

Three investigators scored the probability that each medication outcome occurred because of the pharmacist recommendation.

Result signal

Pharmacists avoided an estimated $129,705 to $162,133 in direct monthly medication costs through interventions.

Conclusion

Findings support pharmacist roles in specialty pharmacy beyond prescription filling by showing clinical and financial value.

Interpretation

What the poster adds

  • Specialty pharmacists identified ineffective, unsafe, or otherwise suboptimal therapy and recommended medication outcome changes.
  • The cost model valued one avoided medication fill and adjusted by the panel-assigned probability of pharmacist attribution.
  • Results can help rationalize pharmacist employment and support within health-system specialty pharmacy practice.
