Methods distilled

Inclusion required medication exposure, ACP touchpoints, and paired A1C data

  • Adults at least 18 years old filling new or existing prescriptions for GLP-1RA or SGLT-2i for type 2 diabetes.
  • At least two clinical encounters with an ambulatory clinical pharmacist.
  • Baseline A1C collected no more than three months before enrollment plus at least one subsequent A1C during pharmacy services.
  • Outcomes were summarized with descriptive statistics.

Objective

Describe the impact of ACP-led medication management specializing in diabetes care within an integrated HSSP.

Background

Type 2 diabetes is not typically a specialty pharmacy disease state, but it has access, adherence, and outcome demands similar to traditional specialty care.

Primary endpoint

Change in A1C after clinical encounters with an ambulatory clinical pharmacist.

Signal

Total mean A1C fell by 0.55 percentage points, with a 0.85-point reduction among patients who began with uncontrolled T2D.

Conclusion

The poster supports integrating ACPs into HSSP diabetes management to improve clinical outcomes for patients with T2D.
