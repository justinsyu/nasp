The impact of multiple pharmacy use on medication adherence in individuals with colorectal cancer

A CVS Health retrospective PBM cohort assessed whether using more than one pharmacy for medication fills was associated with lower adherence among members receiving specialty therapies for colorectal cancer.

Methods

The analysis followed specialty pharmacy users from eligible CRC therapy fills to adherence and regression models.

Identify adults

PBM members with at least two fills of regorafenib, encorafenib, or trifluridine-tipiracil during 2022.

Confirm CRC codes

Eligible fills carried CRC-related diagnosis codes including C18.X, C19.X, C20.X, C21.8, C78.5, C78.6, D37.4, or D37.5.

Apply eligibility

Members without continuous eligibility for 180 days before therapy initiation were excluded.

Classify pharmacy use

Multiple pharmacy use meant using more than one pharmacy for medication fills, including non-CRC medications.

Model adherence

MPR >= 0.8 defined optimal adherence; logistic regression estimated adjusted associations with adherence.

Evidence flow

A broad eligible cohort split into single- and multiple-pharmacy exposure groups.

Objective

Assess multiple pharmacy use and adherence in a cohort of individuals with colorectal cancer.

Exposure

Multiple pharmacy use was defined as utilizing more than one pharmacy for medication fills.

Outcome

Optimal adherence was operationally defined as medication possession ratio of at least 0.8.

Primary signal

Multiple pharmacy use was associated with lower optimal adherence and lower mean MPR.

Conclusion

The poster reported a 29.5% decreased probability of optimal adherence for members using multiple pharmacies.
