Work productivity assessment for employed patients with migraine in a health-system specialty pharmacy

A single HSSP embedded the six-question WPAI:SHP instrument into migraine specialty therapy management to measure absenteeism, presenteeism, work productivity loss, and activity impairment over six months.

Conclusion

Useful measurement, uncertain workflow value

The WPAI:SHP helped quantify how HSSP clinical management for migraine related to work productivity and daily activities, with several scores improving by month 6.

Interpretation Guardrails

Promising pattern, limited generalizability

The small sample, single-center design, two losses to follow-up, and three-item pharmacist feasibility survey limit the strength of broad conclusions.

Need

Migraine produces missed work and reduced productivity, but standardized work-impact tools are not routinely embedded in HSSP workflows.

Intervention

WPAI:SHP added to an existing clinical management protocol for targeted migraine specialty therapies.

Analysis

Month 1 scores compared with month 3 and month 6 using Wilcoxon signed-rank tests.

Patient Signal

Presenteeism, productivity loss, and activity impairment showed statistically significant improvement by month 6.

Workflow Signal

Pharmacists found the tool easy to use but did not view it as adding value beyond existing clinical data.
