Improving asthma care through patient-reported outcome measure optimization

A CPS-managed health-system specialty pharmacy network integrated the Asthma Impairment and Risk Questionnaire (AIRQ) into routine asthma management to capture both symptom control and future exacerbation risk.

Objective

Integrate a better PROM into real-world specialty pharmacy care.

The poster describes how CPS selected, implemented, and evaluated AIRQ in an existing HSSP clinical management process for patients receiving asthma medication.

Implementation Flow

How the PROM moved from selection to actionable care

Evaluate PROMs

Clinical subcommittees reviewed asthma-specific patient-reported outcome measures.

Select AIRQ

AIRQ was chosen for administration ease and dual-domain asthma control assessment.

Embed in workflow

Patients completed AIRQ after enrollment and at routine 3- to 6-month intervals.

Collect scores

Baseline and follow-up scores were captured by phone, text, or in-person interaction.

Act on data

Pharmacists gained structured information to support therapy optimization decisions.

Conclusion

AIRQ added a more complete asthma-control signal for HSSP teams.

The authors conclude that HSSPs should periodically evaluate their PROMs and transition when alternatives better support patient management. In this implementation, AIRQ provided pharmacists with actionable data covering both current symptoms and future exacerbation risk.
