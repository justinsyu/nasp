Oral edaravone use in ALS: who was treated, and what care came before and after?

A US administrative claims analysis described demographics, treatment utilization, pre-index disease progression milestones, and preliminary healthcare resource utilization among patients with ALS treated with Radicava ORS® (oral edaravone).

Objective

Describe the treated ALS population and preliminary HCRU patterns after oral edaravone entry.

Methods in claims-data sequence

1 Identify ALS patients Continuous enrollment in Optum Clinformatics Data Mart from June 15, 2022 through June 30, 2023.
2 Set index date First dosing date of MTPA oral edaravone.
3 Split treatment pathway Prior IV edaravone switchers compared with patients initiating oral edaravone.
4 Summarize HCRU Descriptive counts, percentages, means, medians, and payer-specific Medicare/commercial views.

Evidence readout

What the tables collectively say

Two oral-treatment pathways were visible in claims. Most patients initiated oral edaravone directly, while a smaller group transitioned from IV edaravone.
Pre-index burden was not evenly distributed. Switchers had higher percentages for nearly every pre-index disease progression milestone.
Payer stratification mattered. Medicare-covered patients outnumbered commercially covered patients by more than two to one in the total cohort.

Conclusion & constraints

Preliminary real-world context for clinicians and payers.

The authors concluded that the ongoing study may help clinicians and payers understand demographics, ALS treatment utilization, disease progression milestones, and HCRU among oral edaravone-treated patients with Medicare or commercial coverage.

Interpretation should account for the claims-data design, coverage restrictions to commercial and Medicare Advantage plans, coding limitations, possible selection bias, and exclusion of patients not continuously enrolled post-index.
