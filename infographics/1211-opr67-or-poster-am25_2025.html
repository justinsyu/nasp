Ocrelizumab/Hyaluronidase Subcutaneous Injection in the Outpatient Clinic Setting

A real-world clinic protocol followed patient-reported comfort, side effects, and tolerability across three timepoints after clinic-administered subcutaneous ocrelizumab/hyaluronidase for multiple sclerosis.

  • Multiple sclerosis
  • Patient experience
  • Subcutaneous administration
  • Clinic workflow

Single-center cohort

88 patients received treatment through a standardized in-clinic administration process.

Format

Poster conclusion: clinic-administered subcutaneous therapy was feasible, efficient, and well tolerated in routine neurology workflow.

Objective

Measure the patient experience around a faster clinic-administered option

The evaluation focused on patient-reported pain or discomfort, visible injection-site reactions, ongoing or delayed effects, and overall tolerability in a real-world clinical setting.

Interpretation

Visible reactions rarely translated into meaningful patient discomfort

Across immediate and delayed reporting, most patients described minimal pain. The poster emphasizes that erythema, swelling, bruising, or other visible changes did not consistently correlate with patient-reported discomfort.
