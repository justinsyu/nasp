Packaging pattern

Two materials strategies under evaluation

Conventional packaging

Relies heavily on expanded polystyrene and plastic to protect temperature-sensitive medications.

Eco-friendly packaging

Cardboard-based cold-chain packaging is framed as a greener alternative requiring performance validation.

Study objective

Test sustainability without losing medication protection

The poster positions cold-chain medication delivery as high-stakes, high-cost, and high-volume for patients, pharmacies, and the environment.

The central question is whether eco-friendly packaging can preserve temperature performance while reducing non-biodegradable waste, total consumer waste, and downstream landfill burden.

Performance

Temperature excursion proportion and time to event.

Waste

Non-biodegradable, total consumer, and recyclable waste volumes.

Experience

Patient satisfaction, terminal destination, and recycling rate.

Evidence flow

Prospective trial blueprint

Population

Patients receiving medications delivered in cold-chain packaging from January 2024 onward.

Exclusion

Ambient-temperature medication packaging is excluded from the cold-chain comparison.

Primary outcome

Packaging performance measured by temperature excursion rate and time to excursion.

Secondary outcomes

Waste, costs, medication waste, satisfaction, landfill destination, and recycling behavior.

Analysis

Fisher's exact test, t-test, Mann-Whitney U, and multivariate regression are listed.
