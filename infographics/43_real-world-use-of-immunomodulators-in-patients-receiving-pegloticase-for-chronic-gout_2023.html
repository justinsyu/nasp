NASP 2023 Poster 43

Real-world use of immunomodulators in patients receiving pegloticase for chronic gout

A specialty pharmacy record review found that immunomodulator use was broader than the assessment initially captured: 76% of pegloticase patients had an immunomodulator documented after post hoc medication-profile and referral review.

Objective

Find real-world co-therapy patterns after a label-driven assessment change

1

Pegloticase prescribing information was updated in July 2022 to include methotrexate co-use, reflecting evidence for improved efficacy and fewer infusion reactions.

2

The organization modified its therapy-specific pegloticase clinical assessment to capture methotrexate use.

3

Patient records from 07/08/2022 through 03/31/2023 were retrospectively reviewed in SoleMetrics and cross-checked against referral documents and medication profiles.

Assessment lens

Post hoc review nearly doubled the detected co-therapy signal

Population

Adults receiving pegloticase for chronic gout through a complex specialty pharmacy.

Trigger

A July 2022 prescribing-information update elevated methotrexate as a key co-therapy to assess.

Detection gap

The assessment focused on methotrexate, while referral documents revealed other immunomodulators.

Practice signal

Clinicians can intervene when no immunomodulator is ordered alongside pegloticase.

Next iteration

The assessment could be expanded to capture all immunomodulators and support adverse-event analysis.

Conclusion

Assessment design shaped what the pharmacy could see

The poster concluded that a therapy-specific pegloticase assessment can identify immunomodulator prescribing trends, but the initial methotrexate-centered view missed substantial use of mycophenolate. Capturing all immunomodulators may improve intervention targeting, durability-of-response monitoring, adverse-event analysis, and future real-world data collection.
