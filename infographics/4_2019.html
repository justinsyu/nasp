Integrated specialty pharmacy services as a medication optimization strategy for rifaximin in hepatic encephalopathy

CHI Health integrated a specialty pharmacy medication access coordinator into the inpatient-to-clinic care pathway so eligible hepatic encephalopathy patients could start rifaximin at discharge instead of losing therapy to authorization or cost barriers.

Alicia Battershell, PharmD, BCPS; Elezabeth Mac, CPhT; Kami Nolan, PharmD, CSP; Haitam Buaisha, MD. CHI Health Specialty Pharmacy, Omaha, Nebraska.

Objective

Move medication access upstream, before discharge creates a gap.

The study objective was to integrate the CHI Health Specialty Pharmacy medication access coordinator into the care cascade for patients hospitalized with hepatic encephalopathy, optimizing rifaximin access and initiation when discharge occurred.

Fully symptomatic overt HE occurs in about 30-40% of patients with decompensated cirrhosis.

Risk of recurrence is described as 40% at 1 year, with an additional 40% risk at 6 months.

Nearly 110,000 U.S. HE hospitalizations occurred yearly during 2005-2009.

Clinical problem

HE recurrence and lactulose adherence challenges create a high-risk discharge moment for medication optimization.

Guideline link

Guidelines recommend adding rifaximin to lactulose after overt HE recurrence on lactulose alone.

Observed gap

Local clinical observations suggested indicated rifaximin was not always started at HE-related discharge.

Intervention

The MAC moved benefits, PA, copay, sample, and PAP work into the hospitalization-to-discharge sequence.

Result

All 9 included patients started rifaximin at discharge after MAC-supported access coordination.

Conclusion

Integrated MAC assistance optimized rifaximin access and initiation.

The authors concluded that embedding medication access support in the health-system gastroenterology clinic improved rifaximin initiation for patients following HE-related hospitalization.

Next evaluation

The access win still needs outcomes follow-up.

The poster called for further evaluation to determine whether medication optimization with rifaximin improves adherence and reduces readmissions in this population.
