NASP 2025 Annual Meeting & Expo / OPR30

Real-world elacestrant rwPFS signal was strongest in patients without prior fulvestrant.

Using US insurance claims, the poster followed adults with ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer who initiated elacestrant after FDA approval and summarized real-world progression-free survival overall and across clinically relevant treatment-history subgroups.

Objective and methods

Claims data translated elacestrant use into a time-to-event rwPFS readout.

Define population

Adults with ER+/HER2- metastatic breast cancer initiating elacestrant from January 2023 through May 2024.

Set index date

Time zero was first elacestrant administration in the Komodo Research Database.

Capture baseline

Metastatic sites, prior mBC therapies, prior endocrine therapy lines, and fulvestrant exposure were profiled.

Define rwPFS

rwPFS ran from index date to next line of therapy for mBC, disease progression, or death.

Censor follow-up

Patients were censored at end of data availability or end of health plan enrollment.

Main result

Median rwPFS was 2.6 months longer in patients without prior fulvestrant than in the overall cohort.

The 12-month rwPFS estimate was also higher in this subgroup: 44% versus 33% overall. Earlier-line use showed a similar directional pattern.

Conclusion

Elacestrant showed consistent rwPFS across clinically relevant subgroups.

The authors concluded that real-world rwPFS with elacestrant was longer in patients treated in earlier lines and exceeded the overall median PFS reported in EMERALD.

Future analyses were proposed for coexisting mutations, including PI3K, AKT1, and PTEN alterations, plus additional clinically relevant subgroups.

Context

Why fulvestrant exposure matters in the story.

Patients without prior fulvestrant represented 38% of the study population and had a median rwPFS of 9.4 months. In that same no-prior-fulvestrant group, the 1-2 prior endocrine therapy line subgroup also had median rwPFS of 9.4 months.
