Economic Impact Of Ustekinumab In The Treatment Of Psoriasis

A targeted literature review found that ustekinumab's reference product is clinically effective in psoriasis, but its current cost profile often weakens economic value across payer settings.

Objective

Understand the economic impact of reference ustekinumab in psoriasis treatment.

Ustekinumab is an IL-12/23 inhibitor biologic approved for multiple autoimmune and inflammatory conditions, including psoriasis. The review focused on whether its reference product offers economic value in psoriasis, given high drug costs and potential reimbursement restrictions.

Time horizons varied

Cost-effectiveness models ranged from one year to lifetime horizons; four used a 10-year horizon.

Some positive signals

Studies from the Netherlands and Spain found favorable economic benefit or efficiency for ustekinumab.

Responder cost concern

Recent Italy, France, and Germany cost-per-responder analyses placed ustekinumab at the highest cost per responder.

Cost-effectiveness gap

All cost-effectiveness analyses found ustekinumab was not cost-effective at current willingness-to-pay levels.

Biosimilar evidence absent

The included studies did not test how an ustekinumab biosimilar could change economic outcomes.

Result interpretation

The economic signal depended on perspective, but high acquisition cost was the recurring pressure point.

1

Dutch CUA: sequences beginning with ustekinumab had the lowest costs and highest quality-adjusted life years gained.

2

Philippine payer analysis: effectiveness was highly similar to cost-effective treatments, but higher unit drug costs worsened the ICER.

3

Broad CEM pattern: at current willingness-to-pay thresholds, the reference product did not clear cost-effectiveness criteria.

Conclusion

Clinical value did not consistently translate into economic value for the reference product.

The review concluded that although ustekinumab's reference product is effective for patients with psoriasis, its use may not provide an economic benefit under current pricing and payer conditions.

Access implication

Biosimilars were framed as the next economic lever.

Lower-cost ustekinumab biosimilars were expected to improve budget impact, cost-effectiveness profile, willingness-to-pay alignment, healthcare resource utilization, and patient access.
