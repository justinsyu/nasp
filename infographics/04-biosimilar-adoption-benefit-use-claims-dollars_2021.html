Objective

Use objective data to examine growth in specialty drugs, biosimilar use, and coverage benefits.

The analysis focused on marketed US biosimilars for reference products including infliximab, filgrastim, pegfilgrastim, bevacizumab, trastuzumab, rituximab, and epoetin alfa.

Methods

Claims were sorted across product type and benefit channel, then compared to the 2016 baseline.

Select population US beneficiaries in the Workpartners RRDb from 2016 through 2020.
Classify agent Specialty products categorized as innovator/reference agents or biosimilars.
Classify benefit Claims and costs assigned to prescription drug or medical benefit processing.
Compare baseline 2020 claims and dollars were compared with 2016 levels.

Interpretation

The conclusion links payer adoption to savings potential and infusion logistics.

  • Health plans had recognized value and savings opportunities and increased biosimilar use.
  • The shift from pharmacy to medical benefit may reflect the infusion route for many biosimilars.
  • Future work should evaluate individual innovator/biosimilar pairs and variation by therapy area, agent, insurance plan, and region.

Specialty costs rose

Across all specialty products, 2020 dollars were 30% higher than 2016.

Claims rose more modestly

Claims increased 17%, suggesting spend growth outpaced utilization growth.

Biosimilars accelerated

Biosimilar claims and dollars grew sharply from a small 2016 base.

Benefit channel shifted

Extracted tables show medical-benefit share increasing over time.

Next question narrows

Pair-level and region-level analyses are needed to explain variation.

Conclusion: in a large employer claims database, biosimilar use expanded rapidly between 2016 and 2020, while specialty-product costs continued to rise and more activity appeared under the medical benefit.
