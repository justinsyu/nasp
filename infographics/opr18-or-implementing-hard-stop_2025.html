NASP 2025 | OPR18-OR | Oncology medication safety

Implementing Hard Stop Alerts in Hospital Software

Saint Luke's Health System evaluated a best practice alert designed to interrupt inpatient continuation of oral oncolytics and prompt hematology/oncology review when non-oncology teams reorder therapy from the prior-to-admission medication list.

Objective

Make inpatient continuation an intentional decision

The intervention alerts non-oncology providers, nurses, and pharmacists when an oral oncolytic is ordered or reordered, requiring order justification and documenting whether hematology/oncology consultation occurred.

Safety mechanism

Hard-stop workflow built around consultation and documentation

1

Order detected

Oral oncolytic is ordered or reordered from the prior-to-admission medication list.

2

BPA interrupts

Non-oncology prescribers, nurses, and pharmacists receive the alert at a high-risk moment.

3

Justification required

The provider must document the rationale, including whether hematology/oncology was consulted.

4

Medication disposition

The order is continued, discontinued inpatient, removed for outpatient continuation, or inappropriately bypassed.

Risk context

Why a pause matters

Oral oncolytics are high-risk therapies in acutely ill hospitalized patients because clinical instability, organ dysfunction, treatment-related toxicity, and drug interactions can change the risk-benefit balance.

The poster specifically notes interaction risks with common inpatient medications such as antibiotics and anticoagulants.

Alert design

Scope narrowed to reduce alert fatigue

Methotrexate, hydroxyurea, and hormonal therapies were excluded because they are commonly used in non-oncology settings.

This keeps the hard stop pointed at inpatient oral oncolytic decisions where consultation and documentation are most relevant.

The reduction in potentially inappropriate inpatient oral oncolytic continuation suggests that hard-stop BPA design can improve medication-use safety by forcing review, documentation, and timely hematology/oncology consultation.
