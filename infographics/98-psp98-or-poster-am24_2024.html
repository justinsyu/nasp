Objective

Customize a new care management application for a high-friction oral oncology workflow.

The team needed documentation tools that matched treatment-plan releases, external pharmacy handoffs, signed-order requests, and consent requirements. The aim was not just to document work, but to keep every oral anticancer medication path clinically visible to the specialty pharmacy team.

Treatment plans

Automated release visibility

Orders were managed around treatment plans rather than standard refills.

  • Automated message when an oncology provider signs a specialty oral anticancer medication.
  • External Treatment release date added by SCP for next-release tracking.

Signed orders

Request work became trackable

The workflow needed a repeatable way to pursue unsigned treatment-plan orders.

  • Renewal Review task created for oral anticancer medication patients.
  • Manual checklist task developed to document treatment-plan signature attempts.

Consent review

Dispensing stop built into the path

Oral anticancer therapies required signed patient consent before dispensing.

  • Consent Required flag added to orders in the dispensing system.
  • SCP contacts clinic for consent before patient education and fulfillment proceed.

Decision logic

The core workflow branches around therapy status, signatures, consent, and dispensing location.

Therapy status

New therapy routes to cancer center pharmacist review and SCP clinical appropriateness review.

Ongoing therapy

SPL reviews patients due for oral anticancer medication delivery.

Missing signature

Checklist attempts trigger messages to provider, APP, or clinic nurses.

No consent

SCP flags the script to prevent dispensing and contacts clinic for anticancer consent.

Background

Specialty clinical pharmacists support both specialty pharmacy accreditation standards and cancer center accreditation requirements.

Methods

A multidisciplinary group of SCPs, SPLs, IT experts, and pharmacy clinical leadership mapped application gaps and modifications.

Results

External patient management tasks, checklist requests, consent flags, Renewal Review, and reporting were incorporated into routine work.

Conclusion

Oral anticancer medication-specific tasks were successfully adapted in the new care management application.

Next direction

Analyze checklist data for prescribing deviations and target reeducation for IHSSP and cancer center staff.
