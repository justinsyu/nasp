Evidence flow

From onboarding to dispense: the optimized REMS pathway

1

Onboard

Provider, pharmacy, and patient enroll in REMS; staff training and internal clinical guidance are completed.

2

Enroll patient

Patient is entered into the pharmacy system, benefits are investigated, and services are introduced.

3

Assess therapy

Clinical review includes brochure receipt, medical assessment, labwork, pregnancy screening, and treatment authorization.

4

Consult

Patient counseling, REMS portal submission, checklist authorization, delivery setup, and handoff are documented.

5

Dispense

The dispensing pharmacist verifies authorization, logs the dispense in REMS, removes hard stops, and ships medication.

6

Maintain

Records are maintained, adverse reactions are reported, reassessments continue, and therapy is adjusted with ongoing labwork.

Conclusion

Integrated checks supported access and patient safety

  • Extra workflow checks and pharmacist collaboration created an efficient, REMS-compliant dispensing process.
  • Reporting tools and hard-stop flags helped standardize refill visibility and process completion.
  • Maintained PDC above both the 0.85 goal and 0.90 stretch goal showed consistent medication access.

Background

Mavacamten treats hypertrophic obstructive cardiomyopathy and is subject to a stringent REMS.

Objective

Optimize and implement a dispensing process that complies with strict REMS requirements.

Methods

Pharmacists reviewed prescriptions, managed outreach, used refill reports, embedded flags, and logged dispenses in REMS.

Results

Drug interaction, counseling, and log-dispense checklist completion reached 100% compliance.

Next steps

Future work includes ambulatory pharmacist coordination, external provider collaboration, and access time studies.
