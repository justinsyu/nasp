Healthcare Resource Utilization and Cost Burden of Hemophilia B in the United States

A claims-based study used IBM MarketScan data to classify adult male patients with hemophilia B by clinical profile, then compared their one-year healthcare use and costs with demographically matched controls.

Objective

Build a claims-based clinical-profile algorithm and quantify hemophilia B HRU and costs overall and by profile.

Population

Adult male patients with repeated hemophilia B diagnosis claims and 1-year baseline plus follow-up enrollment.

Comparator

Demographically matched enrollees without hemophilia B, hemophilia A, or other coagulation disorder diagnoses.

Baseline signal

Charlson Comorbidity Index was higher in hemophilia B: 0.9 vs 0.3, p < 0.001.

Interpretation

The authors concluded substantial economic burden remained, with profile classification enabling richer claims analyses.

Conclusion

Economic burden and unmet need remained evident

Hemophilia B was associated with substantially greater healthcare resource use and costs in the United States.

The claims algorithm may help expand use of administrative data to understand burden by clinical profile, while the lack of laboratory data limits formal validation of severity assignment.
