Patient Satisfaction Survey in Specialty Pharmacy

Therigy implemented a short PSAT survey across specialty pharmacies to make patient satisfaction measurement more feasible, metric driven, and easier to analyze for quality improvement.

Survey architecture

A compact 7-item instrument covered three operationally useful satisfaction domains.

Satisfaction

Measured education and counseling on medications and health conditions, along with accuracy and condition of prescription fills.

Staff communication

Captured whether pharmacy interactions supported issue resolution and effective patient communication.

Patient advocacy

Included the pharmacy's ability to resolve issues and provide timely services in a patient-centered workflow.

Methods flow

The analysis filtered platform-generated surveys into a study-ready satisfaction dataset.

Build

Therigy developed the 7-item PSAT survey and scoring model for specialty pharmacy use.

Administer

Pharmacies completed surveys in office, over the phone, or by text to a patient's mobile device.

Score

Responses generated an aggregate satisfaction score from 20 to 100 and a satisfaction-level stratum.

Select

Included pharmacies had at least one year of activity in TherigySTM before the retrospective analysis.

Analyze

TherigyInsights data from March 2020 through June 2021 quantified completion and mobile survey use.

Objective

Optimize patient-satisfaction measurement.

The poster objective was to improve implementation and analysis of a patient satisfaction survey in specialty pharmacy, with a tool short enough for broad operational use.

Future work

Increase participation and study response timing.

The conclusion identified opportunities for wider pharmacy adoption, higher patient participation, and an additional pilot to study PSAT survey turnaround time.

Conclusion: the 7-item Therigy PSAT survey gave specialty pharmacies a practical, scored way to track patient satisfaction, while mobile texting emerged as an important channel for future participation gains.
