Once-daily valbenazine administration options for patients with dysphagia or pill aversion

This poster evaluates whether two approved valbenazine formulations can support alternative administration routes for people treated for tardive dyskinesia or Huntington disease chorea who may have difficulty swallowing intact capsules.

Evidence plan

Five linked tests, from benchtop handling to pharmacokinetics

  • Study 1: dissolution profile after crushing compacted powder capsule contents.
  • Study 2: compatibility of crushed contents with foods, buffers, and fed-state simulated gastric fluid.
  • Study 3: suitability of crushed contents for G-tube delivery.
  • Studies 4-5: granule food compatibility plus phase 1 bioequivalence and food-effect testing.

Administration map

Two formulations create different route options

Compacted powder capsule

Can be swallowed whole or opened and crushed for selected uses

  1. 1Swallow intact capsule with water, with or without food.
  2. 2Open capsule, crush contents, and mix with about 1 tablespoon of compatible soft food.
  3. 3Crush contents and stir into water or juice for immediate use or storage up to 2 hours.
  4. 4Crush, disperse in about 10 mL room-temperature water, and administer via feeding tube.
Granules sprinkle capsule

Designed for sprinkling on compatible soft foods without crushing

  1. 1Swallow whole capsule with water, with or without food.
  2. 2Open capsule and sprinkle granules onto about 1 tablespoon of soft food.
  3. 3Stir gently without crushing granules.
  4. 4Consume immediately or store for up to 2 hours after mixing.
Granules: do not mix into milk or water.
Granules: do not administer via feeding tube.

Results by question

What each test answered

Crushing changed handling, not release

Study 1 found rapid and complete release for crushed capsule contents and intact capsules at 40 mg and 80 mg.

Crushed contents mixed acceptably

Study 2 met the 90%-110% recovery definition after mixing with tested soft foods, buffer solutions, and FeSSGF.

G-tube delivery was supported

Study 3 found suitability when crushed contents were dispersed in room-temperature water, with storage in water up to 2.5 hours.

Granules fit acidic soft foods

Study 4 met the 95%-105% recovery definition for granules in applesauce, yogurt, pudding, selected buffers, and FeSSGF.

Phase 1 linked formulation exposure

Study 5 demonstrated bioequivalence of granules versus compacted powder and no food effect for granules sprinkled on applesauce.

Conclusion

Valbenazine offers once-daily administration flexibility for patients with swallowing difficulty

The poster concludes that compacted powder capsule contents can be crushed for mixing with compatible foods or liquids and for G-tube delivery, while the approved granule formulation is compatible with acidic to slightly acidic soft foods and demonstrated bioequivalence with no food effect in phase 1 testing.
