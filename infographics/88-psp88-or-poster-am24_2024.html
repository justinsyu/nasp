AM24 2024 / PSP88-OR / Oral oncology cost avoidance

The value of a medically integrated pharmacy to maximize cost avoidance and reduce waste of oral oncolytic medications.

AON Pharmacy describes a nurse-led reassessment process before oral oncology refills ship. The workflow uses EMR review, shipment holds, and provider intervention to prevent unnecessary fills and reduce waste.

Objective and method

Quantify avoidable oral oncology waste by reviewing every specialty oncology refill before shipment.

1

Refill due

Specialty oncology medications due for refill are queued for nurse review.

2

EMR review

Nurses review office notes, labs, scans, pathology, and upcoming appointments.

3

Care plan check

A reassessment care plan is completed before medication is shipped.

4

Hold and intervene

When a discrepancy is found, shipment is held and the provider receives a clinical intervention.

Problem

Unnecessary oral oncolytic refills can create high-cost medication waste.

Clinical lens

Oncology nurses can identify therapy holds, dose changes, disease progression, and end-of-life status before shipment.

Intervention

The pharmacy holds shipment when reassessment finds a discrepancy and communicates with the provider.

Measured result

AON documented 152 interventions and reported $1.9M in cost avoidance from February through September 2023.

Conclusion

Nurse-pharmacist collaboration through the cancer care continuum can improve care quality while reducing avoidable waste.
