Improving ponatinib therapy persistence through nurse-led specialty pharmacy outreach

AcariaHealth evaluated whether structured nurse-led counseling around first dispense and follow-up outreach was associated with better ponatinib refill persistence at days 30 and 90.

Objective

Measure whether counseling improves ponatinib persistence

1

Identify initiators

Patients starting ponatinib between May 19, 2023 and February 15, 2024 were included.

2

Offer nurse counseling

Calls reviewed diagnosis, regimen, role of therapy, adverse effects, side effect management, and financial risk for non-adherence.

3

Compare persistence

Day 30 and day 90 persistence were compared between counseled patients and those who declined or were unreachable.

Conclusion

Specialty pharmacy clinical programs can support persistence after ponatinib initiation

The authors conclude that incorporating clinical specialty pharmacy programs is an effective strategy to improve persistence after ponatinib initiation, while noting that further research should assess whether these interventions translate into clinical outcomes.

Poster context

Why this matters

Ponatinib requires careful education because patients and caregivers need to understand the regimen, expected results, adherence, common side effects, management strategies, and rare but serious toxicities.

As oncology fulfillment through specialty pharmacy expands, the poster frames nurse-led outreach as a practical adherence and persistence support model.
