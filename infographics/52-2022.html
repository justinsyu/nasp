Pharmacist Impact on Coverage Outcomes in Oncology

Shields Health Solutions evaluated whether remote ambulatory clinical pharmacist support inside oncology clinics changed third-party coverage decisions for specialty oncology medications managed by a health system specialty pharmacy.

Bottom line

Remote pharmacist support improved coverage outcomes in oncology clinics.

The poster concludes that ambulatory clinical pharmacists placed remotely alongside pharmacy liaisons improved approval rates for both prior authorizations and appeals for specialty oncology medications, even while the supported cohort submitted more requests.

The operational takeaway is direct: coverage work appears to benefit from a clinical pharmacist who can coordinate documentation, payer requirements, and clinic pharmacy workflows before requests become denials.
