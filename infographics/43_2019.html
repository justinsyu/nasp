Objective

Test the value of coordinated specialty pharmacy contact

The poster measured the impact of a high-touch specialty clinical program on completion of glecaprevir/pibrentasvir therapy in Hepatitis C patients.

Its broader aim was to demonstrate clinical value to patients, prescribers, payors, and other supply-chain stakeholders through a high-performing specialty pharmacy model.

Care design

A synchronized chain of calls and handoffs

5 Coordinated touchpoints spanning initiation, refill, shipment, and completion confirmation.

Methods

Patient-centric workflow from start to final refill

1

Technician welcome call

All patients received a welcome call, and the technician identified the best time for pharmacist outreach through shared decision making.

2

Pharmacist new-start counseling

A specialty clinical pharmacist completed initial counseling with every patient in the start cohort.

3

Nursing refill reminder

Nursing refill reminder calls were initiated and completed when applicable, supporting therapy continuity.

4

Shipment synchronization

A patient care coordinator synchronized prescription shipment with the nurse to align dispensing and patient contact.

5

Completion verification

After final refill, nurses and pharmacy interns confirmed completion with patients or ordering physician offices.

Conclusion

Coordinated specialty pharmacy processes supported value-based care claims

The poster concluded that a collaborative, high-touch specialty pharmacy managing Hepatitis C patients confirmed a high rate of completion, indicating better performance.

It also positioned coordinated specialty pharmacy care processes as evidence that specialty pharmacies can deliver measurable value in reimbursement conversations.

Background signal

Why completion mattered

The source poster framed Hepatitis C as a common chronic blood-borne infection in the United States, often curable with newer, shorter-duration antiviral therapy.

The PQA Specialty Core Measure Set defined Hep C treatment completion as completion of the intended antiviral therapy duration without significant gaps.
