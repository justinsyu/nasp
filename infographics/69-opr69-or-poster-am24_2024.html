Impact of pharmacy interns in medication therapy management at an integrated health system specialty pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health evaluated whether trained pharmacy interns could extend MTM capacity in a health system specialty pharmacy while pharmacists supervised, reviewed, and co-signed all patient and provider communications.

Interpretation

Interns extended MTM reach, but documentation underestimated impact

  • Only medication refills and CMRs were directly documented and attributed to interns, even though interns performed broader intervention work.
  • Interns used the Pharmacists' Patient Care Process across encounters, allowing pharmacists to focus on clinical review components.
  • The poster identifies time constraints and insufficient staffing as common MTM obstacles that intern integration can help address.

Objective

Demonstrate pharmacy intern value in providing MTM services within a health system specialty pharmacy.

Methods

Interns were trained, used the EMR, completed CMRs, communicated concerns, and worked under pharmacist review.

Result

Interns completed 129 MTM encounters and 25 CMRs after integration into the program.

Limitation

Patient health outcomes could not be directly measured, and several intervention types were not intern-attributed.

Conclusion

Pharmacy interns can play an integral role in MTM services within a health system specialty pharmacy.

Future direction

What the poster recommends next

  • Expand MTM services to include additional interns and pharmacy liaisons.
  • Develop a patient questionnaire on medication attitudes and satisfaction with MTM services.
  • Standardize documentation and patient follow-up methods across the health system.
