Why it matters

Prior authorization is a common access bottleneck.

The poster frames PA work as both a patient-access challenge and a provider burden. Published context cited in the background associated PAs with delayed care, abandoned treatment, and long specialty prescription authorization timelines.

Operational speed

Most PA requests were pushed to payers immediately.

The workflow emphasizes clinic-embedded or remote liaisons, electronic PA submission when possible, and automated follow-up if no payer response arrives within 48 hours.

Evidence flow

A liaison-led PA pathway moved from trigger to patient coordination.

Notification

Proprietary software alerts the liaison that a specialty medication PA is needed.

Benefits check

The team confirms coverage terms, payer requirements, and whether PA is required.

Submission

Electronic PA submission is used whenever possible with required payer content.

48-hour signal

Liaisons receive automated follow-up prompts when no response is received.

Decision route

Approvals are documented; denials trigger appeal coordination or therapy alternatives.

Access support

Claims, copay review, financial assistance, and patient coordination close the loop.

Methods extracted

Population-based retrospective cohort.

  • Data source: Clearway Health patient management platform PA submission data from September 2021 through February 2023.
  • Population: Specialty pharmacy patients across seven unique health systems with Clearway-supported PAs.
  • Exclusions: Resubmissions, PA timelines over 90 days, and records missing creation, submission, or decision dates.

Interpretation

The poster positions 2.1 days as a strong benchmark.

Clearway Health concluded that its integrated specialty pharmacy PA model produced expedited turnaround, especially relative to published commentary describing one- to two-week specialty therapy starts and prior authorization as a leading impediment.

The practical mechanism was not a single handoff: proactive benefits investigation, rapid submission, electronic PA use, 48-hour follow-up prompts, EHR documentation, and affordability coordination all supported the short interval.

Conclusion

Integrated specialty pharmacy support compressed PA turnaround into a few days.

Across 1,744 submissions, the average submission-to-decision turnaround was 2.1 days. The poster attributes the result to dedicated specialty pharmacy subject-matter experts, proactive benefits work, automated follow-up, and direct coordination with clinical teams and patients after approval or denial.
