Abemaciclib linkage to care through an embedded specialty pharmacy model

Health system specialty pharmacy pharmacists translated a new FDA indication into a structured clinic workflow: provider education, retrospective chart review, prospective patient identification, and prescriber-linked therapy decisions for high-risk early breast cancer.

Evidence flow

How the linkage-to-care intervention moved from label update to therapy decision

FDA indication update

Abemaciclib was approved for HR+, HER2-, node-positive EBC at high risk of recurrence as adjunct endocrine therapy.

Provider education

HSSP pharmacists educated prescribers on the new indication and clinical-trial data.

Retrospective review

Patients seen in clinic within the prior 12 months were evaluated for treatment eligibility.

Prospective screening

Newly diagnosed clinic patients were assessed for abemaciclib appropriateness.

Prescriber linkage

Pharmacists discussed therapy initiation and recorded a linkage intervention in Arbor.

Monitoring path

Patients initiated on therapy received counseling and monitoring; others remained under reevaluation.

Conclusion

Embedded pharmacists turned a new indication into identified treatment opportunities

HSSP pharmacists were positioned to find high-risk patients, connect eligibility findings to prescribers, and support treatment monitoring after abemaciclib initiation. The poster concludes that this pharmacist-led linkage-to-care model substantially increased appropriate abemaciclib prescribing.

Future directions

  • Standardize linkage-to-care initiatives for therapies with newly expanded indications.
  • Use this workflow as an implementation framework in HSSP settings.
  • Reevaluate patients not initiated on therapy for ongoing guideline-based appropriateness.
