Health-system specialty pharmacy services and medication adherence in pediatric cystic fibrosis

Yale New Haven Health implemented a clinic-to-pharmacy referral workflow for pediatric cystic fibrosis patients and compared medication adherence and pharmacy continuity before and after the launch.

Objective

Test whether an embedded specialty pharmacy could improve adherence

The project assessed the impact of Yale New Haven Health Outpatient Pharmacy Services on medication adherence in pediatric patients with cystic fibrosis.

Outside specialty pharmacy

  • May not have electronic medical record access
  • Limited patient education and financial assistance
  • Average turn-around time of 7-14 days
  • Limited direct coordination with clinic prescribers
7-14 day turn-around

Health-system specialty pharmacy

  • Direct EMR access for clinical assessment and monitoring
  • Continuous education, prior authorization, and assistance
  • Close access to prescribers, nurses, and clinic pharmacists
  • Local pickup plus same-day or next-day delivery options
<72 hour turn-around

Study design

Prospective 6-month review after implementation compared with a retrospective pre-implementation cohort.

Intervention

Patient education and an EMR-connected workflow moved eligible CF specialty prescriptions toward OPS.

Operational signal

OPS receipt increased from 5 to 100 prescriptions, while post-implementation OPS fill rate was 89%.

Adherence signal

MPR was sustained from 0.85 to 0.86, and PDC improved from 0.75 to 0.80.

Implementation barrier

Workflow integration, patient buy-in, and insurance lockouts remained practical challenges.

Conclusion

Embedding specialty pharmacy support preserved adherence while improving continuity

The poster concluded that implementation of health-system specialty pharmacy services was associated with improved and sustained medication adherence in pediatric cystic fibrosis patients.

The key operational lesson was collaboration: close coordination with prescribers, nurses, pharmacists, and the broader clinic team helped integrate the new referral process with minimal disruption.
