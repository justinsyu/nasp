NASP 2021 poster 63 | Phase 1 crossover PK study

Apalutamide Tablets in Different Food Vehicles

A single-center study tested whether 240 mg apalutamide dispersed in applesauce could provide comparable exposure to whole tablets, while in vitro work checked dose accuracy and stability across common food and drink vehicles.

Purpose

Support patients who have difficulty swallowing whole apalutamide tablets without requiring tablet crushing.

Preparation

Four 60 mg tablets were dispersed in applesauce, then mixed briefly at set intervals to form a uniform mixture.

PK finding

Early concentrations were higher with applesauce, but profiles were comparable after about 4 hours.

Bench finding

Food vehicles from 50-200 g or mL showed comparable purity and dose accuracy.

Conclusion

Applesauce dispersion was supported as a suitable alternative administration method for 240 mg apalutamide.

Poster conclusion

Practical takeaway

Apalutamide tablets dispersed in applesauce provided comparable total exposure to whole tablets, while yogurt, orange juice, and green tea produced similar compatibility results in dose accuracy and mixture purity assessments.
