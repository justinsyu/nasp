NASP 2023 Poster 80 | Specialty pharmacy adherence

Improving Medication Adherence Documentation for Specialty Pharmacy Patients

Dartmouth-Hitchcock Medical Center standardized a non-adherence SmartPhrase and refill outreach workflow to make missed specialty medication refills visible, actionable, and easier to escalate across pharmacy and clinic teams.

Core move: turn refill non-response into a standardized documented event.

The SmartPhrase gave the team a repeatable trigger for clinic alerts, follow-up, and specialty service decisions.

Objective

Better documentation as the lever for better adherence follow-up

The team targeted specialty patients with low MPR and built a workflow around a non-adherence SmartPhrase in the electronic medical record. The primary outcome was documentation uptake; the secondary outcome was MPR improvement.

MPR = sum of days supply days in time frame x 100

The project aimed for the specialty pharmacy benchmark of 95% MPR; post-implementation average MPR reached 93.15%, up 0.49 percentage points from 92.66%.

Evidence flow

What changed in the refill gap workflow

Identify low MPR

Patients with MPR below 95% were reviewed to locate adherence documentation gaps in the EMR.

Contact before due date

Patients were contacted starting 7 days before the estimated refill due date, then every 3 days.

Document when late

The non-adherence SmartPhrase was used when a patient was two weeks late to refill.

Alert clinic team

Clinic pharmacist and clinic staff were notified so the pharmacy team could make outreach.

Close the loop

Remote pharmacy followed up one week later; patients with no response were unenrolled from specialty services.

Interpretation

Standardization made non-adherence easier to act on

  • SmartPhrase use more than doubled from 57 to 122 documented uses over comparable five-month windows.
  • Patient follow-up and interdepartmental communication increased after the documentation workflow was standardized.
  • The procedure was successful enough to be implemented into departmental policy.

Conclusion

Small MPR movement, meaningful operating change

The 95% MPR goal was not reached within the study period, but average MPR increased from 92.66% to 93.15%. The poster concludes that standardized adherence documentation and workflow will continue to support patient follow-up for improved adherence and long-term cost savings.
