NASP 2021 poster 29

Barriers and facilitators of specialty pharmacy prescription turnaround time

A PQA cross-sectional survey mapped what specialty pharmacies see as slowing or shortening prescription turnaround time. The clearest pressure point was prior authorization, but the results also show that improvement levers vary by pharmacy type.

Objective and method

The study asked which factors influence turnaround time and which practices help shorten it

Focus group informed

The survey instrument was informed by prior focus groups on turnaround time.

Member distribution

Invitations went to NASP members and PQA specialty pharmacy members.

Fielded in 2021

Responses were collected from April through May 2021.

Pharmacy context

Respondents reported type, geography, rurality, and state location.

Descriptive analysis

Results summarized perceived barriers, facilitators, and measurement opinions.

Barrier versus facilitator contrast

Prior authorization dominated barriers; electronic PA and partnerships led facilitators

The same operational factor could be a barrier or a facilitator depending on local workflow. The largest gap was prior authorization: 72% barrier versus 6% facilitator.

Measurement signal

TAT

Important, but inconsistently measured

Respondents strongly valued turnaround time for patient satisfaction, patient care, and internal quality improvement, while disagreeing that SP-TAT is measured consistently across the industry.

Conclusion

Turnaround time improvement needs both common measurement and local barrier matching

Respondents indicated that SP-TAT is important for care and satisfaction but is measured inconsistently. Prior authorization was the most consistent barrier, while other barriers and facilitators varied within and across pharmacy types, supporting tailored quality improvement initiatives aligned to each pharmacy's actual bottlenecks.
