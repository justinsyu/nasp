Methods

Five implementation moves converted clinical guidelines into dashboard-ready outcomes reporting.

Define COMs

Clinical subcommittees selected disease-specific outcomes measures from guidelines, research, and specialty pharmacy recommendations.

Build protocols

Disease-state protocols encoded which measures to collect and when pharmacists or liaisons should act.

Implement in Arbor

The protocols were embedded into Trellis Rx specialty pharmacy technology across partner health systems.

Trigger alerts

Advanced algorithms generated automated alerts and interventions to standardize collection and response.

Report dashboards

Real-time dashboards monitored protocol compliance, COM collection, patient care needs, and stakeholder reporting.

Rheumatology PROM protocol

The protocol creates a closed loop: collect, compare, intervene, and keep monitoring.

The poster's rheumatology framework starts at specialty pharmacy enrollment, captures a baseline PROM, repeats collection every 3 to 6 months, and escalates to pharmacist intervention when scores worsen consecutively.

  • Baseline capture establishes the reference point.
  • Recurring PROM checks keep outcomes visible over time.
  • Worsening scores trigger pharmacist intervention before reporting continues.

Dashboards give COMs an operating model, not just a reporting destination.
