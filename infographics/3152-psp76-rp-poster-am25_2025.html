Clearer Skin, Smoother Care

A Geisinger dermatology workflow positions pharmacists as co-managers for isotretinoin therapy, shifting monitoring, iPLEDGE documentation, dose titration, prescription routing, and follow-up work away from already constrained provider visits.

The poster reports baseline and process measures, then defines planned evaluation endpoints and anticipated operational outcomes.

Objective

Move repeat isotretinoin management into a pharmacist-led lane

The intervention is designed to improve appointment access, maintain iPLEDGE adherence, increase health-system pharmacy prescription capture, and document the success of pharmacist-led dose adjustments and interventions.

Evidence flow

Care pathway: keep providers at decision points, route monthly work to pharmacists

Provider

Decision to start

Order labs, activate iPLEDGE, and refer the patient into pharmacist co-management.

Pharmacist

Initial touchpoint

Review baseline labs, plan future labs, complete iPLEDGE steps when needed, order to Geisinger Pharmacy, and coordinate authorization.

Pharmacist

Months 1 and 2

Assess tolerability, review labs, titrate dose, complete iPLEDGE, and route prescriptions to the health-system pharmacy.

Pharmacist

Months 3 and 4

Continue tolerability checks, adjust dose as needed, complete iPLEDGE, and maintain prescription routing.

Provider

Month 5

Determine whether treatment is complete or if provider follow-up is needed for month 6.

Pharmacist

Final visit

Sign off from patient care and complete final iPLEDGE documentation when applicable.

Evaluation methods

What the team planned to measure

Appointment access Provider access for patients referred into co-management.
PDC Medication adherence for isotretinoin patients managed through the process.
Experience Provider and patient survey feedback.
Capture rate Prescription capture into the health-system pharmacy.

Conclusion and next steps

Anticipated operational value

  • Reduced provider touchpoints are expected to improve dermatology access.
  • Pharmacist coordination and counseling are expected to support adherence and patient satisfaction.
  • Higher health-system pharmacy capture is expected to create operational and financial value.
