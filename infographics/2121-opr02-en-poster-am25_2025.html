Methods Snapshot

First-year post-approval cohort with at least 3 months of observable follow-up

Index date Each patient's abemaciclib start date.

Population

Adults with node-positive stage I-III early breast cancer initiating abemaciclib at 150 mg BID.

Exclusions

Prior CDK4/6 inhibitor use or another primary malignancy except defined low-risk exceptions.

Persistence rule

On treatment at 3 months, allowing up to a 60-day medication gap.

Dose modification

Dose hold or dose change, including increase or decrease, as recorded in the chart.

Dose Modification Patterns

Half stayed at 150 mg BID; half reduced dose, usually to 100 mg BID

Of 354 patients, 174 had no dose change or hold and 180 had a dose decrease. The first dose-change distribution was dominated by 100 mg BID, with a smaller shift to 50 mg BID and once-daily regimens.

The poster concludes that adjuvant abemaciclib discontinuation was low in US clinical practice, while patients with dose reductions had higher 3-month continuation.

Conclusion

Practical implication from the poster

Given early onset of diarrhea in abemaciclib treatment and evidence that dose reductions do not compromise efficacy, the authors emphasize anti-diarrheal medicine and timely dose modifications to improve tolerability and treatment persistence.

  • 88% continued beyond 3 months in the overall cohort.
  • 70% of patients who discontinued due to AEs did not attempt dose modifications.
  • Half of patients stayed on 150 mg BID and half had a dose reduction.
