Patient navigation resolved most health-system barriers for people with bleeding disorders.

BioMatrix Specialty Pharmacy evaluated a bleeding disorders-focused navigation program that coordinated nonclinical support for access, insurance, financial, disability, and social barriers during its first 6 months.

Objective

Test whether an integrated specialty-pharmacy navigation program can overcome access barriers.

The study examined a multidisciplinary, no-cost patient navigation service for members of the bleeding disorders community who were experiencing insurance, access, financial, disability, or social challenges.

Conclusion

Early data showed navigation helped patients gain access to care.

Patients responding to the follow-up survey indicated their problems were listened to and understood, issues were resolved, and they would use and recommend the services again. The poster concludes that patient navigation is an effective tool for helping patients overcome nonclinical health-system obstacles.

Evidence flow

How the program evaluated navigation effectiveness.

Referral

Patients and caregivers with access, insurance, or financial issues entered the bleeding disorders-focused navigation program.

Classify

Challenges were grouped into insurance, social, financial, premium assistance and enrollment, or disability categories.

Coordinate

Navigators connected patients to coverage options, community resources, assistance programs, and education.

Measure

Each case was recorded as resolved, in process, or unresolved; post-navigation satisfaction was collected voluntarily.
