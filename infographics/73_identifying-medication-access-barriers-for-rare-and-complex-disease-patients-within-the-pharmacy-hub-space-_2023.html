Medication access barriers inside the pharmacy hub journey

A retrospective analysis of four rare and complex disease support programs found that hub utilization shortened time to therapy, while missing information remained the dominant triage delay.

Conclusion

Missing information is the controllable choke point

Across the two-year analysis, missing information was the most prevalent barrier affecting time to triage. Reducing incomplete fields would improve the hub's already enhanced ability to move patients toward first commercial dispense.

Recommended action

Make completion a required digital step

The poster recommends a patient/provider portal or electronic enrollment process that manages required information sections before allowing progression, especially in a mandatory hub model.

Methods

Retrospective hub data review

Data generated from four rare and complex disease patient support programs between January 1, 2020 and December 1, 2022 were used to identify common barriers and the time associated with triage to filling pharmacy.

Limitations

Interpret results as operational evidence

Program population sizes, disease categories, medication costs, reporting methods, and outlier handling differed across programs, limiting direct generalization.
