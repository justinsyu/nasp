Understanding purchasing patterns and product access of newly launched specialty generics

MHA Specialty Pharmacy Solutions evaluated how quickly pharmacies shifted purchasing from specialty brands to newly launched generics, and whether generic entry widened the set of corporations able to purchase and dispense those therapies.

Table pattern

Quarterly tables showed a consistent crossover

Brand erosion Gleevec, Makena, and Zytiga unit purchases declined after generic availability. -63% to -80%
Generic climb Each generic rose from launch-quarter purchasing to higher 1Q19 volume. +134% to +2967%
Access signal Corporations purchasing generics generally matched or exceeded pre-launch brand access. 4 of 5
Limited distribution No study pharmacies purchased branded Xenazine or Ampyra through a wholesaler. 0 brand baseline

Evidence view

Brand-to-generic movement by 1Q19

Conclusion

Specialty generics were quickly adopted

The authors found rapid marketplace adoption after launch, with particularly steep uptake for more recent 2018 generic entries, suggesting growing comfort with specialty generic substitution.

Access implication

MHA agreements appeared to support broader pharmacy reach

For every product except imatinib, the number of corporations purchasing the generic by 1Q19 exceeded the pre-launch brand comparator or grew from a limited-distribution baseline.
