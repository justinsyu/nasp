Impact of a Health System Specialty Pharmacy Cascade of Care Referral Service for People Living with Hepatitis C

Cleveland Clinic specialty pharmacists used a collaborative practice agreement, patient tracking, and barrier removal workflows to move patients with positive HCV tests from referral to treatment and final cure documentation.

The project asked whether a specialty pharmacy referral service could reduce HCV treatment barriers at health-system scale.

  1. 1Describe pharmacist interventions made under a collaborative practice agreement.
  2. 2Quantify the referral service impact on HCV treatment rates.
  3. 3Engage stakeholders across a large health system to reduce HCV treatment barriers.

Evidence flow

From positive test to documented cure

Find

Positive HCV blood tests triggered specialty pharmacist review and chart documentation.

Route

Patients were triaged through CPA management, specialist prescribing, or pharmacist barrier intervention.

Remove

The team tracked access, financial, psychosocial, transport, and follow-up barriers in the patient management system.

Complete

Pharmacists followed treatment, refills, counseling, and final laboratory documentation of cure.

Caremodel reuse

Specialist use of the referral service accounted for many referrals, and the workflow created a foundation for additional specialty pharmacy care models.

Conclusion: specialty pharmacy services can remove treatment barriers, improve access to HCV care, and support high completion within a health-system cascade referral model.
