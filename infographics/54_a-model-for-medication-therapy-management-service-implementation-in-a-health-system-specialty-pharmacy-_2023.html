A Model for Medication Therapy Management Service Implementation in a Health System Specialty Pharmacy

Yale New Haven Health Outpatient Pharmacy Services built an MTM service to increase pharmacist interventions, close adherence gaps, and reduce exposure to Medicare Part D DIR fee pressure.

Implementation model

A specialty pharmacy team turned MTM into a structured intervention workflow.

Specialty clinical pharmacists were trained in an online MTM platform and a performance information management tool. Patients with PDC gaps in cholesterol, diabetes, and RASA therapy classes were targeted for adherence counseling.

  • Clinical interventions included therapy-gap closure, patient consults, cost-effective alternatives, and superior therapy alternatives.
  • Direct outcomes were targeted intervention program completion and comprehensive medication review completion.
  • Indirect measures included anticipated reduction in DIR fees, although fee impact was not yet known due to lag time in DIR fee collection.

Why it mattered

DIR pressure made MTM operationally relevant for specialty pharmacy.

The poster notes that pharmacy DIR fees increased sharply from 2010 to 2019 and that pharmacies can reduce fee exposure through MTM initiatives that also address nonadherence, readmissions, and patient-pharmacy relationships.

Conclusion

A health system specialty pharmacy implemented an MTM service within a few months.

The authors concluded that the model demonstrated practical implementation of MTM within a health system specialty pharmacy, with rapid gains in intervention completion and pharmacy performance score.

Future work focuses on development and expansion of MTM services to two retail locations and ambulatory sites across the health system.
