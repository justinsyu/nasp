Specialty pharmacy-driven stockpiling reduction program outcomes and savings

Walgreens evaluated a specialty pharmacy program that used dispense history, pharmacist EMR review, and refill rescheduling to prevent excess medication accumulation across 12 high-cost specialty therapies.

Evidence flow

Program logic: identify, verify, postpone, quantify.

1

Detect surplus risk

Computational logic screened 12 select high-cost specialty medications using the prior 12 months of central-fill dispense history.

2

Validate context

A clinical pharmacist reviewed the EMR for dose changes, lost prescriptions, or other explanations for apparent medication on hand.

3

Defer refill outreach

When at least 28 potential excess days were present, proactive refill outreach was postponed until roughly two weeks of supply remained.

4

Calculate avoided waste

Savings were based on the gap between original and actual refill dates, using medication cost per dose and number of doses saved.

Conclusion

Specialty pharmacies can reduce waste without blocking patient access.

The initiative met its objective by pairing utilization monitoring with individualized clinical oversight. Deferred outreach reduced unnecessary medication accumulation, produced payor cost avoidance, and could inform cumulative quantity-limit policy opportunities.

The intervention adjusted proactive refill timing only; patients could still request a refill when needed.
