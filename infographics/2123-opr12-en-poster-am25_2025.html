Comparing rates of primary medication nonadherence among patients at Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy to external specialty pharmacies

A retrospective cohort study compared new specialty prescriptions sent to a health-system specialty pharmacy with those sent to external specialty pharmacies, focusing on whether patients accessed and initiated therapy.

Objective

Compare PMN between health-system and external specialty pharmacies.

The study asked whether prescriptions routed through Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy were more likely to reach a confirmed therapy start than prescriptions sent to non-HSSP pharmacies.

The overall PMN difference was statistically significant, and the difference was also significant among MS clinic patients.

Purpose

Compare PMN rates between Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy and external specialty pharmacies.

Design

Retrospective cohort using HSSP software, Surescripts fill data, and chart review.

Eligibility

New specialty medication prescriptions, excluding recent prior fills, duplicates, alternate filling methods, and unresolved outcomes.

Result

PMN was higher for non-HSSP prescriptions overall and especially in the MS clinic.

Conclusion

HSSPs may help more patients access and initiate specialty therapy compared with non-HSSPs.
