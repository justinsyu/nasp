Objective

Can a tightly integrated specialty pharmacy workflow deliver both REMS rigor and patient outcomes?

The poster evaluated mavacamten clinical outcomes and REMS compliance one year after Yale New Haven Health implemented an optimized clinical and dispensing workflow.

  • Enroll obstructive hypertrophic cardiomyopathy patients in the Cardiomyopathy disease program.
  • Coordinate clinical management, delivery, monitoring, and drug-interaction safeguards through pharmacy workflows.
  • Track adherence through MPR and PDC, then compare against benchmark and stretch goals.

Methods snapshot

Retrospective review of first-year mavacamten dispensing.

Population

Patients receiving mavacamten from November 1, 2023 to September 30, 2024.

Diagnosis focus

Obstructive hypertrophic cardiomyopathy treated with mavacamten under REMS requirements.

Primary outcomes

MPR, PDC, yearly manufacturer REMS audit compliance, and patient-reported quality of life.

Clinical risk

Mavacamten can reduce left ventricular systolic function, making REMS monitoring central to safe therapy.

Workflow design

Pharmacists handled clinical management and delivery coordination inside an integrated HSSP model.

Adherence result

Average MPR and PDC were both 0.96, exceeding the 0.85 benchmark and 0.90 stretch goal.

Safety process

REMS audit compliance reached 100% in both 2023 and 2024.

Patient signal

83.85% of patients reported excellent, very good, or good quality of life.

Conclusion

Optimized clinical and dispensing workflows can keep REMS-heavy therapy operationally controlled.

The HSSP model combined pharmacist-led clinical continuity, delivery coordination, and REMS checklist discipline, producing high adherence and complete audit compliance during the first year of mavacamten dispensing.

Limits and next steps

What may affect transferability.

  • The integrated dispensing and clinical documentation platform may not be available at all health systems.
  • Future work proposes expanding mavacamten access to external patients managed by providers outside the HSSP.
  • The team also identifies a specialty pharmacy center of excellence for obstructive hypertrophic cardiomyopathy as a next direction.
