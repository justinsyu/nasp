PARPi Therapy: Patient Characteristics and Access

A Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy cohort analysis describes who initiated PARP inhibitor therapy and how prior authorization plus financial assistance shaped approval speed and first-fill out-of-pocket cost.

Approval time and OOP cost

Key access signal: 80% required prior authorization, 100% of PA requests were approved, and median PA approval time was 0 days.

Objective

Analyze disease characteristics, medication approval time, and first-fill out-of-pocket costs.

Cohort profile

Small oncology cohort, heavily ovarian cancer and olaparib centered.

Adult patients who initiated PARPi therapy through the outpatient oncology clinic were included; patients who did not initiate therapy or were enrolled in a clinical trial were excluded.

Define starters

Adult outpatient oncology patients who initiated PARPi therapy from November 2017 through December 2018.

Profile disease

Baseline characteristics, disease duration, prior regimens, mutation status, metastasis sites, and insurance type were summarized.

Track medication

Olaparib was the dominant agent, with ovarian cancer as the most frequent indication in the dosing table.

Measure access

Prior authorization frequency, approval success, and time to approval were evaluated for therapy starts.

Reduce OOP

Financial assistance patterns were linked to first-fill out-of-pocket cost and estimated patient savings.

Access economics

Financial assistance converted all assisted first fills to $0 out-of-pocket cost.

Approximately half of patients received financial assistance. In the source table, every assisted patient had a $0 first-fill OOP cost, while the non-assisted group still included two patients with $1000-$1500 first-fill costs.

Conclusion: PARPi therapy was used primarily in ovarian cancer and most often as olaparib; high PA approval, rapid median approval time, and low first-fill OOP costs support the integrated specialty pharmacist role in reducing financial toxicity.
