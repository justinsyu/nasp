One Shot at a Time: Pharmacy Team Driven Implementation of Long-Acting Antiretroviral Therapy for HIV Management and PrEP

At The Brooklyn Hospital Center, pharmacists and pharmacy liaisons compressed the LA-ART access pathway by coordinating benefits work, fulfillment, clinic delivery, administration support, and post-injection follow-up.

  • Specialty pharmacy model
  • HIV and PrEP
  • Retrospective observational study
  • January 2023 to December 2024

Siham Romahi, PharmD; Raven Heslop, CPhT; Jean Estime, PharmD

Clearway Health at The Brooklyn Hospital Center coordinated clinic, pharmacy, liaison, nurse, and pharmacist touchpoints.

Objective

Test whether a pharmacy-embedded team can remove access friction for LA-ART

The study examined how pharmacists and pharmacy liaisons facilitated adoption of long-acting antiretroviral therapy, improved medication access and adherence, and optimized outcomes for HIV management and PrEP.

Methods

Single-center retrospective review

  • Data came from EHR review and the Clearway Health specialty pharmacy patient management dashboard.
  • Included adults who started LA-ART from January 2023 through December 2024 at the Brooklyn client site.
  • Excluded patients who transitioned care to other clinics.
  • Results were summarized proportionally using descriptive analysis.

Interpretation

Why starts sometimes took longer

Patients initiated beyond two days primarily had clinically uncontrolled reasons, including transition-of-care timing, uncertainty about switching to LA-ART after multiple consultations, or insurance and employment changes.

The pattern points to access barriers the pharmacy team could actively manage and clinical timing factors that required individualized pacing.

Conclusion: integrating specialty pharmacy services into the LA-ART transition process improved medication access and patient support, enabling rapid initiation for many patients and contributing to a 99% viral load suppression rate by study end.
