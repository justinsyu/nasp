Enhancing specialty care through clinical pharmacist interventions

CHRISTUS Specialty Pharmacy piloted an intervention questionnaire across specialty clinical services to quantify intervention type, pharmacist time, enrollment status, and potential cost savings.

Objective

Measure clinical work that is easy to miss

The study evaluated the quantity and type of clinical interventions provided by specialty pharmacists, pharmacist time spent, and potential cost savings from those interventions.

  • A prospective intervention questionnaire was integrated into specialty clinical services.
  • Responses were summarized with descriptive statistics by specialty, intervention type, and HSSP enrollment status.
  • Potential savings were extrapolated from standardized values assigned to intervention types.

Evidence flow

Deploy tool

Intervention questionnaire integrated across specialty clinical services from April 1 to May 15, 2024.

Capture work

Pharmacists recorded type, specialty area, enrollment status, and time spent on each intervention.

Describe patterns

Counts and percentages were stratified by disease state, intervention category, and HSSP versus external patients.

Price impact

Standardized values by intervention type were used to extrapolate potential cost savings.

Prioritize change

High-volume access and therapy selection work pointed to opportunities for proactive pharmacist action.

Practice signal

Why the data collection mattered

The poster identifies a gap in consistent intervention data collection across health-system specialty pharmacies and specialty clinics.

  • More complete capture can clarify where specialty pharmacists create the most value.
  • Specialty distribution can reveal areas best suited for HSSP services.
  • Repeated patterns can identify where proactive pharmacist action may prevent later access or therapy problems.
