IL-1 pathway inhibition reshapes recurrent pericarditis treatment

A RESONANCE registry interval analysis found a temporal shift away from chronic corticosteroids and toward second-line IL-1 pathway inhibition after rilonacept became available for recurrent pericarditis.

1. Registry frame

Ambispective observation combined up to 1 year of retrospective data with prospective follow-up from March 2021 through the cutoff.

2. Failure point

Analyses focused on patients failing aspirin, NSAIDs, or colchicine and then adding or switching therapy.

3. Comparator

Treatment intensification categories included corticosteroids, csDMARDs, anakinra, and rilonacept.

4. Temporal signal

Rilonacept drove most IL-1 pathway inhibitor use as second-line treatment after availability in April 2021.

5. Clinical meaning

The authors interpret the pattern as growing adoption of a steroid-sparing paradigm beyond 2015 ESC guideline sequencing.

Conclusion

Steroid burden moved from default step to avoidable bridge

The poster concludes that RESONANCE demonstrates a temporal shift in RP management among pericarditis-focused cardiologists, with IL-1 pathway inhibition used more frequently than chronic corticosteroids after colchicine failure.

Limitations include the observational, nonrandomized design and use of interim data from an unlocked database.
