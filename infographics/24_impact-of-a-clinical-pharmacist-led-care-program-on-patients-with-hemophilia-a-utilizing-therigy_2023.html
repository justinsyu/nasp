NASP 2023 | Poster 24 | Hemophilia A care management

Impact of a Clinical Pharmacist-Led Care Program on Patients with Hemophilia A, Utilizing Therigy

A single-center hemophilia A patient care management program used Therigy to document pharmacist care, medication adherence, bleeding events, absenteeism, healthcare use, and intervention outcomes for patients prescribed emicizumab-kxwh.

Evidence flow

How the care program turned routine data into pharmacist action

Identify eligible patients

Included patients had a hemophilia A diagnosis in Therigy, at least one emicizumab-kxwh dispense, and pharmacist drug therapy management.

Track patient outcomes

Bleeding events, absenteeism, healthcare utilization, and PDC were extracted from documented clinical care data.

Evaluate therapy

Pharmacists assessed appropriateness, dosing, education needs, and access barriers during the PCMP workflow.

Resolve care gaps

Medication-related issues triggered documented interventions with outcomes categorized as successful or unable to reach/refused.

Methods snapshot

Single-center, retrospective, observational assessment

Clinical data were derived from Washington Center for Bleeding Disorders documentation in Therigy. Descriptive statistics summarized demographics, bleeding events, absenteeism, resource utilization, PDC, intervention reasons, and intervention outcomes.

Patients were excluded if they opted out of the PCMP or received factor therapy other than emicizumab-kxwh.

Conclusion

Program value centered on structured monitoring and timely pharmacist intervention.

The poster frames pharmacist integration, technology-supported reporting, and ongoing benchmarking as best practices for maximizing adherence while minimizing sickness absenteeism and healthcare resource use.

Future work called for understanding declined interventions, monitoring interaction timeliness, and exploring mobile messaging for patient engagement.
