Evidence flow

Monthly refill questionnaires converted patient reports into targeted pharmacist work.

Eligible patient

Prescribed a specialty rheumatology medication by an outpatient rheumatology clinic provider.

Refill touchpoint

Certified pharmacy technician completed the monthly refill questionnaire.

Trigger logic

Missed dose, adverse effect, or poor/fair effectiveness response generated a PharmD review need.

Clinical review

Pharmacist reviewed patient assessment responses and the electronic medical record.

Intervention

Pharmacist contacted the patient or provider when follow-up was needed.

Conclusion

PROs gave pharmacists a practical signal for next-step care.

  • Patients reported low rates of missed doses (5%) and adverse effects (1%).
  • Most patients rated perceived effectiveness as high, with 96% excellent or good.
  • Integrated specialty pharmacists used PROs to direct targeted medication-use interventions.

Objective and methods

What was assessed

The study assessed patient-reported outcomes and pharmacist interventions for patients prescribed specialty rheumatology medications at a health-system specialty pharmacy. The design was a single-center retrospective analysis of patients with at least two specialty pharmacy fills and at least two monthly refill questionnaire responses from January through March 2020.

PRO questions addressed adverse effects, missed doses, and perceived medication effectiveness.
