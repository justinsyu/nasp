Adherence of Patients to Oral Oncolytic and Neurologic Specialty Medications Provided by a Specialty Pharmacy

BioMatrix investigators reviewed one year of specialty-pharmacy patient-reported outcomes to quantify adherence to oncology and neurology specialty drugs and compare quality-of-life measures before and after therapy start.

Objective and methods

Measure adherence, then compare patient-reported QOL before and after therapy start.

  • Primary objective: measure adherence to oncology and neurology specialty medications dispensed by the specialty pharmacy.
  • Secondary objective: compare quality of life at Start of Care and follow-up assessments among SPMTM participants.
  • Study design: retrospective observational review of patient-reported outcomes from January 1 through December 31, 2018.
  • Assessment model: pharmacist education plus telephonic clinical assessments at SOC and seven days before each refill dispense.

Need

Complex specialty drugs require handling, shipping, clinical support, and adherence barrier reduction.

Program

SPMTM used pharmacist education and structured telephonic assessments before therapy and before refills.

Measures

PDC quantified medication coverage, while two PRO measures captured missed work or school and perceived feeling.

Analysis

Monthly means were annualized; Mann-Whitney U testing compared SOC and follow-up QOL means.

Takeaway

The poster linked high adherence and improved QOL signals with specialty-drug use during SPMTM participation.

Conclusion

High adherence aligned with favorable QOL movement, but the authors called for larger studies.

The one-year study concluded that annualized PDC was 0.96, missed work or school was lower after starting therapy, and patient feeling scores improved after specialty-drug start with SPMTM.

The authors interpreted these findings as encouraging and recommended additional larger studies to further evaluate the adherence and QOL signals.
