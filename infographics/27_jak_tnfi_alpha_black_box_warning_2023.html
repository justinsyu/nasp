Adherence patterns in rheumatoid arthritis patients receiving a JAK inhibitor or TNFi after a black box warning

CVS Specialty evaluated whether the 2021 FDA black box warning for Janus Kinase inhibitors changed prescribing, adherence, or persistence patterns compared with tumor necrosis factor α inhibitors in rheumatoid arthritis.

Study design

Three periods framed the warning signal

Pre-black box warning Apr 1, 2021 to Aug 31, 2021
Washout Sep 1, 2021 to Dec 1, 2021
Post-black box warning Dec 2, 2021 to Aug 31, 2022

Objective

Examine whether the warning changed prescribing patterns, adherence, or persistence in RA patients receiving JAK inhibitors or TNFi.

Inclusion

Patients needed at least 3 fills between April 2021 and August 2022 and maintained coverage through study end.

Adjustment

Propensity score matching used age, gender, socioeconomic status, location, and comorbid conditions.

Adherence

Proportion of days covered was calculated overall and within each warning-period window.

Persistence

Discontinuation was defined by last fill occurring more than 30 days before the end of the study.

Conclusion

Black box warning did not meaningfully disrupt adherence or persistence in the balanced cohort

The poster's main interpretation is that JAK inhibitor adherence and persistence were not significantly impacted by the warning once patient characteristics were balanced against the TNFi comparison group.
