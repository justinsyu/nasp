Migraine diagnoses, CGRPi use, and short-term disability among U.S. employees

A Workpartners Research Reference Database analysis followed employees with initial migraine diagnoses from 2017 to 2022, tracking CGRPi treatment access, direct pharmacy costs, and short-term disability claims into 2023.

Objective

Characterize migraine care and work absence after diagnosis

  • Employees had an initial migraine diagnosis using ICD-10 G43.x between 2017 and 2022.
  • All subjects had at least one year of post-diagnosis eligibility and continuous data into 2023.
  • Outcomes included CGRPi use, direct medical and prescription costs, STD costs, and lost work days.
  • Cost data were inflation adjusted to December 2023.

Methods as evidence flow

From diagnosis cohort to post-index work impact

1

Find initial migraine diagnosis

Employees with ICD-10 G43.x were indexed by first diagnosis year from 2017 through 2022.

2

Require follow-up data

Continuous eligibility into 2023 allowed post-diagnosis tracking and 2023 persistence checks.

3

Measure CGRPi claims

Analyses captured post-index CGRPi use, use in the first 12 months, and continued use in 2023.

4

Connect to disability

STD claims with a migraine diagnosis were summarized by claim frequency, paid cost, and lost work days.

Background

CGRPi therapies aim to reduce migraine burden but commonly require prior authorization tied to diagnosis and migraine frequency.

Database scale

The RRDb includes medical and pharmacy claims for more than 5.5 million employees and dependents across all U.S. states.

Access signal

Post-index CGRPi use rose to 88.4% for the 2022 diagnosis cohort, and average time to first claim fell to 0.4 years.

Burden signal

STD durations and costs were higher among those with a CGRPi, consistent with a more severe disease state.

Conclusion

CGRPi utilization increased, access improved, and STD claims per employee appeared to decrease with time.

Practice implications

Specialty pharmacy lens

The poster frames rising CGRPi use as an operational signal for specialty pharmacies serving persons with migraine.

  • Understand utilization trends for CGRPis and STD among persons with migraine.
  • Prepare for increased CGRPi utilization among patients with migraine diagnoses and frequent headaches.
  • Support patients who may also open STD claims, especially those with more severe disease burden.
