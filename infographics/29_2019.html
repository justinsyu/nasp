Measuring the impact of integrating PGY-2 ambulatory care residents into specialty clinics

University of Louisville Hospital assessed whether PGY-2 ambulatory care residents could extend specialty pharmacy services in Hepatitis C and Multiple Sclerosis clinics while meeting residency learning objectives.

Methods flow

Residents moved from training to supported independent service

Train

PGY-2 residents were prepared to provide ambulatory care pharmacy services in two specialty clinics.

Embed

Residents delivered clinic services over 6 months with peripheral support from a preceptor.

Measure

Hours covered, patients served, discontinuations, and provider satisfaction were collected.

Map learning

The experience was compared with ASHP residency objectives to document educational value.

Conclusion

A resident-supported model expanded reach for patients and providers

The authors concluded that patients, providers, and PGY-2 ambulatory care residents benefit when residents are integrated into specialty clinic settings.

The model increased access to individualized care for specialty conditions while giving residents a meaningful learning experience relevant to future pharmacy practice.
