Streamlining Hepatitis C Management

Geisinger describes a co-management model that links embedded Medication Therapy Disease Management pharmacists with the health-system specialty pharmacy to remove duplicate HCV monitoring work, preserve adherence, and accelerate treatment starts.

Poster Objective

Measure whether integration reduces friction without diluting outcomes.

  • 1Describe MTDM pharmacist co-management before and after specialty pharmacy integration.
  • 2Evaluate time to treatment initiation, patient adherence, and patient outreach volume.
  • 3Assess pharmacist engagement after duplicate clinical tasks are removed.

Baseline Signal

Strong outcomes, but nine average touchpoints per treatment.

The redesign targets operational redundancy: embedded pharmacists and specialty pharmacy teams were both assessing, reassessing, and closing therapy milestones.

Evidence Flow

From parallel work streams to a bidirectional handoff model

Methods

Operational data pull

Baseline metrics were collected from EPIC Hyperspace and Willow Ambulatory, then mapped against the MTDM and specialty pharmacy task sequences to identify duplication and define post-integration endpoints.

Initial assessmentShifted to joint in-person provider and pharmacist visit.
Start confirmationSpecialty pharmacy completes one-week follow-up call.
Final assessmentCompleted at SVR confirmation with end-of-treatment education.

Care model

Embedded MTDM pharmacists remain responsible for high-value clinical checkpoints.

Specialty pharmacy role

The health-system SP concentrates on medication access, dispensing, start verification, and refill calls.

Efficiency target

Fewer missed-communication delays are expected to shorten time to therapy initiation.

Adherence watchpoint

PDC will be compared before and after integration to test whether reduced outreach preserves adherence.

Broader use

The model may translate to other specialties with embedded pharmacists or emerging SP clinical programs.

Conclusion

Integration is positioned as a workflow intervention, with outcome evaluation next.

The poster anticipates that combining MTDM pharmacist workflows with specialty pharmacy patient-management programs will streamline care, reduce duplicative tasks, and support faster HCV treatment initiation.
