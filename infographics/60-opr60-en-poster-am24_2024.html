Real-world claims analysis characterizes tardive dyskinesia burden in long-term care

A US claims database study identified older long-term care patients with an ICD-10 code indicative of tardive dyskinesia and summarized the clinical burden surrounding their index LTC stay.

Objective

Describe who carries TD burden in LTC settings

The poster analyzed demographics, comorbidities, medication use, and healthcare visits among adults with any LTC stay and ICD-10 code G24.01 indicative of tardive dyskinesia.

Methods

Claims timeline centered on an index LTC stay

2016-2022 Study period

STATinMED Real-World Insights Database captured claims used to identify TD and LTC exposure.

2017-2021 Identification window

First LTC stay during this window was defined as the index LTC stay for the analysis subset.

1 year + 1 year Continuous capture

Medical and pharmacy benefits were required for 1 year pre-index and 1 year post-index.

Objective

Characterize demographic and clinical characteristics of patients with TD in LTC settings using US claims data.

Methods

Adults with LTC stays and ICD-10 G24.01 were identified; a continuous-benefit subset supported descriptive analysis.

Results

The analyzed cohort showed high comorbidity burden, common psychoactive medication use, and frequent repeat LTC stays.

Limitations

TD prevalence may be underestimated because of the ICD-code definition and continuous data capture requirement.

Conclusion

The data support special attention to TD clinical burdens in LTC, including practice guidance and treatment feasibility in dysphagia.

Interpretive frame

Clinical management implications

The poster connects TD burden in LTC to older age, comorbid conditions, anticholinergic exposure, dysphagia, and healthcare utilization.

  • Anticholinergic use was reported in 50.0% of patients, an important geriatric safety consideration.
  • Dysphagia affected 18.5%, making medication administration route and formulation relevant in LTC practice.
  • Further evaluation was recommended for falls, resource utilization, and appropriate VMAT2 inhibitor use.
