Persistence, discontinuation and specialty pharmacist interventions in patients receiving droxidopa

A Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy retrospective cohort study evaluated how long adults stayed persistent on droxidopa for orthostatic hypotension, why therapy stopped, and where specialty pharmacists intervened during ongoing monitoring.

Objective and methods

Measure persistence and map the pharmacist work around droxidopa therapy

Design

Single-center retrospective cohort analysis.

Population

Adults prescribed droxidopa by outpatient neurology or cardiology clinics from May 2017 to April 2020.

Entry criteria

At least 3 fills through the center's specialty pharmacy; excluded non-starters and deaths within 90 days.

Persistence definition

Time to first non-persistent event, defined as a coverage gap exceeding 60 days.

Conclusion

Integrated monitoring coincided with high persistence

This study found a high probability of persistence within an integrated care model, with discontinuation rates similar to prior literature.

Practice implication

Pharmacists supported ongoing treatment monitoring

The intervention mix highlights specialty pharmacists' role in reviewing dose titration, drug interactions, adherence, side effects and disease-related events.
