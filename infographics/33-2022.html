NASP 2022 / Poster 33-2022

Antipsychotics, GSKIP, and autophagy signaling under neurotoxicant stress.

In SH-SY5Y neuronal cell models, the poster frames GSKIP as a negative regulator of GSK3 and explores whether antipsychotics can trigger cytoprotective autophagy, with p62 and LC3 induction serving as the visible autophagy-marker readout.

Biologic premise

GSKIP sits at the crossroads of GSK3 regulation, mitochondrial dynamics, and stress protection.

Background claim extracted from the source: GSKIP is described as a naturally occurring negative regulator of GSK3 within a PKA/GSKIP/GSK3/Drp1 signaling axis.

Objective

Identify whether GSKIP and antipsychotics can promote cytoprotective autophagy in neuronal cells.

The poster asks whether antipsychotics induce autophagy-related markers and protect neuronal cells from oxidative or neurotoxicant stress, with possible implications for molecular drug development in psychopharmacology.

EGFP / SH-SY5Y Naive reporter cell line used for drug, inhibitor, and stressor experiments.
EGFP-GSKIP wt Wild-type GSKIP construct to test signaling-linked protection.
EGFP-L130P Variant construct collected after stable selection for downstream assays.

Methods to findings

Stable GFP-positive SH-SY5Y cultures were challenged with APDs, stressors, and autophagy inhibitors.

Drug set 3 APDs Olanzapine, paliperidone, and haloperidol are reported as inducing autophagy-marker expression.
Early marker response 6 h At 10 µM, p62 and LC3 stimulation was already visible in SH-SY5Y cells.
Dose test 10/50 µM Both concentrations are described as stimulating p62 and LC3 after 6 or 12 hours.
Analytic model GEE Tables model associated factors and interactions affecting APD neuroprotective action.

1. Build stable cultures

Cells were screened under G418 for more than one month until GFP expression exceeded 80%.

2. Apply APDs

Antipsychotics were administered at 10 and 50 µM across short treatment windows.

3. Add stressors

GEE tables include hydrogen peroxide, MPP+, and beta-amyloid stressor patterns.

4. Read autophagy

p62 and LC3 expression were the marker signals emphasized in the findings.

5. Extend mechanism

Future inhibitor work is proposed to locate the crucial autophagy pathway step.

Conclusion

The poster positions GSKIP as a candidate lever for psychiatric-disorder drug discovery.

Across the background, findings, and discussion, the core claim is that antipsychotic-triggered autophagy and GSKIP-linked signaling may protect neuronal cells from apoptotic or oxidative damage.

  • Autophagy inhibitors are proposed to identify which pathway step is critical.
  • The study links p62 and LC3 induction with potential neuroprotective action.
  • The author suggests possible downstream benefit for psychopharmacology development.
