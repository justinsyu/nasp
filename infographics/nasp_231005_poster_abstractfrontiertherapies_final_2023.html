Hidden in plain sight: finding Alpha-1 patients earlier

A claims-and-EHR analytics poster reframes the Alpha-1 antitrypsin deficiency diagnostic odyssey as a searchable signal problem: use millions of linked records, known AATD patterns, and prescriber relationships to identify likely undiagnosed patients and educate clinicians sooner.

Methods

Build a rare-disease signal library, then cluster likely undiagnosed patients.

  • 1Review deidentified pharmacy, medical, prescriber, and EHR records from 2016 through 2022.
  • 2Use 11,954 diagnosed AATD patients to identify the top 50 associated symptoms and comorbidities.
  • 3Map common medications, standard lab tests, and durable medical equipment used in AATD care.
  • 4Validate medication NDC categories against treatment guidance and known signals such as COPD and granulomatosis.
  • 5Apply k-mode clustering to surface undiagnosed potential patients and match them to prescribers for education.

Conclusion

A reproducible process for rare-disease case finding.

The poster concludes that the workflow can identify potential patients and prescribers for AATD and can be adapted to other rare diseases. The next evidence point will be how many previously undiagnosed patients are found and how many educational interventions occur.

The near-term operating insight is pragmatic: the strongest first tranche was not every possible provider, but clinicians with multiple probable patients and no known AATD patients already in practice.
