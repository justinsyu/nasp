Specialty Clinic Pharmacist Collaborative Model

A decentralized ambulatory care pharmacist model moved specialty pharmacy services closer to disease-state clinics, using a new collaborative practice agreement, EHR workflow, and marketing package to increase prescription continuity and clinician referrals.

Implementation logic

From clinic opportunity to pharmacy-linked clinical continuity

1

Map opportunity

Environmental analysis identified specialty clinics where medication complexity required closer coordination.

2

Align stakeholders

Clinic, pharmacy, compliance, credentialing, and marketing stakeholders shaped the model.

3

Build CPA

Disease state, therapy class, initiation, modification, refill, discontinuation, labs, and guidelines were defined.

4

Optimize EHR

Standardized documentation and referral workflows were added to support decentralized practice.

5

Market services

Patient brochures, clinician brochures, website copy, scripting, and pharmacist flyers introduced the service.

6

Measure impact

Primary endpoints tracked clinical continuity and signed referrals, with disease outcomes in development.

Endpoint architecture

Disease outcomes

RAPID 3, flare reduction, and sustained viral suppression were planned secondary measures.

Implementation constraints

What limited interpretation and scale

  • Clinicians needed fuller understanding of the ambulatory care pharmacist role.
  • Existing CPA regulations required monthly documentation.
  • The model was developed in a limited number of adult ambulatory care clinics.
  • Outcome data were limited by short time since implementation.

Clinician reception

"Best patient care improvement in my 30 years in practice."

Across testimonials, clinicians described the pharmacist as critical for medication approvals, insurance navigation, monitoring, Epic ordering, and overall confidence in patient care.

Conclusion

Poster takeaway

Expanding integrated decentralized pharmacists into target practices increased clinical continuity and drove pharmacist referrals beyond target.

The authors positioned the Medication Management Clinic model as adaptable to other health systems with specialty pharmacies, with future expansion into additional disease-state clinics and advanced technician roles.
