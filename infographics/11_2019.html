NASP 2019 Annual Meeting & Expo

Variation in health plan specialty drug coverage

Tufts CEVR compared large commercial health plans' specialty drug policies with FDA labels and found that access rules were rarely uniform: 95% of drug-indication pairs varied across plans.

Evidence flow

How the analysis classified access rules

1

Collect plan policies

Public specialty drug coverage policies were drawn from 17 large commercial health plans in SPEC.

2

Map to indications

The database contained 484 drug-indication pairs and 6,139 coverage decisions as of December 2018.

3

Compare to labels

Each policy was compared with the FDA label to determine whether it narrowed access.

4

Classify restrictiveness

Decisions were coded as unrestricted coverage, restricted coverage, or not covered.

Objective

Question

To examine variation in how commercial health plans cover specialty drugs.

Conclusion

Takeaway

Coverage restrictions appeared in roughly half of specialty drug coverage decisions, and both the frequency and type of restrictions varied notably across plans.

Interpretive summary

Access depended on the plan as much as the product.

What was consistent

Noncoverage was uncommon overall at 3%, and every plan had a small not-covered share in Figure 2.

What changed by plan

Plans differed sharply in restriction frequency, and only 5% of drug-indication pairs were covered the same way by all included plans.
