Exploring the impact of alternative funding programs on medication access and clinical outcomes

A single health-system specialty pharmacy compared patients routed externally through Alternative Funding Programs with patients able to start specialty therapy through the HSSP, focusing on time to treat and hospitalization rates.

Patient access flow

Where the AFP pathway introduces delay

1

Referral

The HSSP submits prior authorization and determines coverage or external-routing requirements.

3

Coverage split

Covered prescriptions can be dispensed by HSSP or triaged to another pharmacy by preference or payor rule.

4

AFP contact

When no specialty medication coverage exists, the patient must engage the AFP process.

5

Enrollment

The patient completes AFP enrollment while therapy start is pending.

6

PAP application

The AFP applies to a charitable patient assistance program for medication access.

7

Approval enables medication receipt; denial sends the case for exception review.

Methods

Retrospective, single-HSSP observational comparison

Inclusion

  • Adult patients older than 18 years.
  • Newly prescribed one of nine listed specialty therapies.
  • Study period from January 1, 2022 to May 31, 2023.

Exclusion

  • Infusion therapy or not eligible to fill medication during the study period.
  • Patients who did not start medication.

Objective

Compare time to medication access for specialty therapies obtained through HSSP versus external AFP routing.

Outcome lens

Assess specialty disease-related and all-cause hospitalization rates between AFP and HSSP groups.

Primary signal

Median time to treat was 21 days for AFP patients and 9 days for HSSP patients.

Clinical signal

Disease-related hospitalization rate was higher for AFP patients and statistically significant in the poster analysis.

Conclusion

External AFP requirements were associated with delayed therapy initiation and higher disease-related hospitalization rates.

Interpretation caveat

Estimated AFP delay may be conservative

The poster lists difficulty accurately determining medication start dates for externally filled AFP patients as a limitation, and notes that AFP median time to treat was likely higher than calculated.

  • Future work was recommended in larger samples and additional disease states.
  • The authors also called for examining patients who never start medication because of AFP issues.
