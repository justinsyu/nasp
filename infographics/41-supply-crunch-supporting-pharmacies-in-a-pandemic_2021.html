Supply Crunch: Supporting Pharmacies in a Pandemic

Managed Health Care Associates describes how its medical products and services team rapidly expanded contracting pathways so post-acute care pharmacies could obtain PPE, environmental service supplies, COVID-19 tests, and vaccine-administration equipment during a disrupted supply chain.

Objective

Keep pharmacies supplied when normal channels faltered

The case study reviewed MHA's role in providing timely access to necessary supplies and equipment at competitive pricing through new and existing business partners and manufacturers, enabling member pharmacies to continue patient care and meet COVID-19 testing and vaccination demands.

Environmental service supplies

  • Cleaning products

  • Antibody tests

  • Low dead space needles and syringes

Methods

Retrospective review of contracting actions

MHA analyzed actions taken from March 2020 through May 2021 to secure member pharmacy access to critical PPE, supplies, and equipment sourced from new and existing business partners and manufacturers.

1

Identify gaps

Track products that became difficult or impossible for pharmacies to source through traditional channels.

2

Widen sourcing

Engage regional distributors, existing partners, manufacturers, and emerging companies with relevant inventory.

3

Vet quality

Use medical products and services expertise with clinical review to evaluate samples and contracted products.

4

Contract access

Build new contracts and expand existing agreements to preserve competitive pricing and sufficient volume.

5

Refresh needs

Use advisory boards and field intelligence as pharmacy needs changed through testing and vaccination phases.

Conclusion

Procurement became a clinical continuity strategy

Traditional distributors and manufacturers could not consistently meet pharmacy needs. MHA's response centered on alternative contracting, regional distributor relationships, manufacturing-line shifts by existing partners, and quality vetting of newly contracted products.

Regional sourcing

Regional distributors helped source high-quality products at competitive pricing and adequate volume.

Partner flexibility

Existing partners shifted manufacturing lines to supplement product shortages.

Field intelligence

Advisory boards and field input kept procurement priorities relevant as COVID-19 needs changed.

MHA's medical products and services contracting efforts helped member pharmacies respond across the post-acute care continuum by accessing additional contracted items and suppliers for environmental services, PPE, testing, and vaccination response.
