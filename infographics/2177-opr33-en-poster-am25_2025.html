Analysis of Oral Oncolytic Waste for Patients Filling at a Health System Specialty Pharmacy

A retrospective HSSP evaluation found that oral oncology waste was uncommon across targeted clinic fills, but nearly half of waste events were potentially preventable and accounted for most of the estimated waste cost.

Methods

Retrospective fill-record review with preventability classification

Population

All oral oncology medication fill records from four targeted oncology clinics associated with a HSSP.

Window

January 1 through December 31, 2024.

Trigger

Medication discontinued before the next planned refill date, typically 30 to 90 days from the prior fill.

Preventability

Events were categorized as preventable when the dispense could have been delayed, reduced, or cancelled due to predictable circumstances.

Cost Estimate

Conclusion

A targeted intervention opportunity

Waste events represented a small proportion of total fills, but preventable waste remained material. The poster concludes that the findings support targeted interventions to minimize oral oncology medication waste and promote cost avoidance.

Practical leverage points include refill timing review, progression-risk assessment before dispense, side-effect follow-up, planned-start-date confirmation, and dose-modification checks.
