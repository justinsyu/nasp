Clinical Outcomes Evaluation of a Pharmacist-Led Social Determinants of Health Screening and Intervention Pilot

A health-system specialty pharmacy pilot used pharmacists to screen high disease-activity rheumatoid arthritis patients for SDOH barriers, connect them to resources, and track post-intervention RAPID3 outcomes.

Objective

Evaluate whether pharmacist-led SDOH support moves RA outcomes

The pilot focused on actively filling RA patients whose RAPID3 scores indicated high disease activity. Pharmacists screened for social needs, offered interventions, documented categories using Z-codes, followed up, and collected post-intervention RAPID3 scores.

Evidence flow

How the pilot translated social needs into pharmacist action

Find high-risk RA patients

Patients actively filling RA medication and showing high disease activity were enrolled.

Screen for SDOH barriers

Pharmacists used a defined workflow for screening, documentation, and follow-up.

Offer resources

Patients with needs could accept pharmacist intervention and resource navigation.

Track support status

Follow-up showed most patients still required ongoing support after the initial intervention.

Measure RA response

Post-intervention RAPID3 scores were collected when available to assess clinical signal.

Methods extracted

Workflow and measures

  • Literature review and gap analysis informed the pharmacist workflow.
  • Screening, intervention, documentation, and follow-up were standardized.
  • Metrics included screening status, intervention acceptance, pharmacist time, SDOH category, follow-up need, and post-intervention RAPID3.

Study caveat

Promising signal, limited scale

The pilot suggests benefit, but the poster notes that larger populations and longer follow-up are needed, especially because 80% of patients required ongoing support.

Conclusion: A pharmacist-led SDOH model in a health-system specialty pharmacy can uncover patient barriers, achieve high intervention acceptance, and may improve patient-reported RA outcomes when paired with sustained follow-up.
