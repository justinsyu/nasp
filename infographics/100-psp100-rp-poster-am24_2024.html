Research question Should pharmacies be ready to classify some biologics as BioHazardous drugs?

The poster argues that inconsistent external handling guidance makes internal risk assessment necessary when biologics have biohazardous properties.

Biologic source pattern

Most reviewed examples came from viral platforms.

The source table grouped novel biologics used in FDA-approved drugs or human clinical trials into viruses, bacteria, and other gene, RNA, DNA, cellular, chimeric, and bacteriophage therapies.

Objective

Translate hazardous-drug thinking to biohazardous biologics.

The assessment asks pharmacy teams to identify biologics with hazardous properties, evaluate exposure and health consequences, classify qualifying products, train at-risk personnel, and mitigate exposure through feasible controls.

Its central premise is pragmatic: as novel biologics move from investigational use toward standard care, specialty, outpatient, and retail pharmacy settings need a repeatable way to protect personnel, patients, visitors, and the community.

Method

Modified NIOSH exposure-assessment logic became a four-lens screen.

1

Hazard Assessment

  • Human pathogen origin
  • Genetic modification
  • Replication competence
  • Adverse outcomes or shedding
2

Exposure Assessment

  • Disease potential in humans
  • Manipulation pathways
  • Natural routes of exposure
  • Blood, excretion, aerosol risk
3

Population Assessment

  • Healthy-person disease risk
  • Pregnancy or immunocompromise
  • Transmission to caregivers, family, or community
4

Risk Characterization

  • Healthcare personnel risk
  • Altered hazards from modification
  • Shedding and transmission
  • At-risk populations

Interpretive caveat

The simple screen intentionally did not settle every biological variable.

The results did not account for genetic modification, bacterial or viral attenuation, selective tissue replication, or tropism. That caveat explains why several biologics remain in the further-study bucket and why periodic reassessment is part of the poster's recommended posture.

Conclusion

Pharmacies need a durable internal process before investigational biologics become routine care.

Clinical pipeline pressure

The poster cites seven new FDA cell and gene therapy approvals in 2023 as a signal that exposure questions will increasingly reach routine pharmacy settings.

Risk is not only product-based

Handling, manipulation, shedding, transmissibility, and vulnerable populations all influence whether a biologic creates workplace or community risk.

Controls follow classification

When criteria are met, the recommended response is policy, identification, controls, training, and surveillance rather than ad hoc precautions.
