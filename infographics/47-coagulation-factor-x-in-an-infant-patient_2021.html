Successful prophylactic use of coagulation factor X in an infant patient at home

Objective

Show whether long-term prophylactic factor X concentrate could be managed effectively at home

The poster focuses on a rare bleeding disorder requiring long-term management. After initial twice-weekly prophylactic infusions at a local pediatric Hemophilia Treatment Center, Patient A's therapy moved into the home environment at one month of age.

Methods

Clinical monitoring formed a repeatable home-care feedback loop

A multidisciplinary team retrospectively reviewed medical records, SoleMetrics assessments, progress notes, nursing visit assessments, and hospital and physician office notes.

Assessment at each dispense Patient interaction and clinical status were checked routinely.
Bleed logs Bleeding activity was tracked as a core therapy outcome.
Catheter events Access complications were recorded and managed.
Teaching barriers Caregiver training and barriers to care were monitored.
Dose / weight adjustments Dosing was revisited as the infant grew.
Inventory management Supply oversight helped prevent treatment interruptions.
0

Bleeds, side effects, hospitalizations, ER visits, and service interruptions

The poster reports no adherence issues or interruption of services during the 30-month factor X concentrate treatment period, with the patient also able to travel with family.

Conclusion

Structured specialty-infusion monitoring supported safe continuity of rare-disease prophylaxis at home

The authors conclude that customized therapy-specific assessments and regular outcome collection supported correct infusion use, helped prevent adverse events, and promoted positive outcomes for an infant receiving factor X concentrate in the home environment.
