Evaluating the Impact of a Licensed Social Worker in Specialty Pharmacy Practice

CenterWell Specialty Pharmacy reviewed 2024 referrals to a licensed social worker to understand which specialty pharmacy patients engaged with social work support and which social determinants of health were most often identified.

Program model

Clinical care plus social support Clinicians referred patients with identified need; the LSW conducted outreach and documented social-health barriers.

Objective

Quantify referrals and find high-engagement patient groups

The evaluation asked how often clinicians referred patients to the CWSP licensed social worker, which social needs appeared most often, and which disease-state groups may warrant future proactive support.

Interpretation

Specialty pharmacy support moved beyond adherence check-ins

  • LSW integration extended support into food access, home assistance, transportation, mental health, financial support, housing, caregiver needs, isolation, and durable medical equipment.
  • High-need groups such as cancer and MS may benefit from proactive screening for food insecurity, transportation barriers, and home support needs.
  • Future research should measure whether LSW interventions resolve or improve social-health needs and whether they affect adherence or patient-reported outcomes.

Conclusion

Social work filled a practical gap in specialty pharmacy care

Incorporating a licensed social worker helped address patient barriers that extend beyond side-effect management and medication adherence. The pattern suggests a role for proactive social-needs support in high-need specialty pharmacy populations.

Future research

Move from referral counts to outcome resolution

Next analyses should evaluate post-intervention results, including whether social-health needs were resolved or improved, and whether support influences adherence, symptom-free days, quality of life, or caregiver outcomes.
