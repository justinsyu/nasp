Impact of Multidisciplinary Patient Education Sessions on Expectations and Understanding of New CGRP Treatments

Wake Forest Baptist Health evaluated whether a neurologist plus specialty pharmacist education model could help patients understand new CGRP migraine prevention therapies while reducing repetitive provider education burden.

Objective

Turn a new drug class into a shared patient learning experience

New calcitonin gene-related peptide antagonist therapies created demand for efficient patient education around expectations, administration, access, and safety.

The team developed a patient-oriented multidisciplinary presentation led by headache medicine and specialty pharmacy clinicians.

41 patients attended one of three live 1-hour sessions in Oct-Nov 2018
43 patients viewed the recorded session online or in clinic beginning Feb 2019

Multidisciplinary format

The curriculum paired clinical context with access logistics

Patients could fill at the institution's specialty pharmacy, which helped with benefits investigation and prior authorization.

Education content

Five practical topics moved patients from mechanism to medication access

1

Treatment options

Migraine prevention choices and the overall approach to therapy selection.

2

CGRP biology

How CGRP pathophysiology connects to the new medication class.

3

Benefits and risks

Expected benefit, adverse effects, and pregnancy-related precautions.

4

Injection technique

Subcutaneous administration training to build self-injection comfort.

5

Cost and access

Coverage, prior authorization, medication access, and fulfillment logistics.

Access finding

Online viewing performed comparably to live sessions

The poster reported no statistically significant difference between in-person and online sessions for post-session comfort with administration technique or confidence in CGRP understanding.

This made the recorded session a scalable access strategy for patients who could not attend live sessions.

Need

New CGRP treatments required efficient patient education around a novel migraine prevention class.

Build

A neurologist and specialty pharmacist co-led one-hour informational sessions.

Extend

The third live session was recorded for online or in-clinic viewing.

Measure

Patients completed surveys before and after viewing the education session.

Signal

Post-session understanding and administration comfort both reached 97%.

Conclusion

Multidisciplinary education improved patient knowledge while preserving access through an online option.

The authors concluded the education session was effective and efficient, patients felt well-informed, and future studies could evaluate effects on adherence and clinical response.
