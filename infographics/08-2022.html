Patient-Tailored Pharmacist Interventions to Improve Specialty Medication Adherence

Vanderbilt investigators tested whether pharmacist review, baseline assessment, and patient-specific adherence support could outperform usual care among specialty pharmacy patients with low prior PDC.

Study methods

A high-noise adherence screen was narrowed by pharmacist review before randomization.

1,300

Intervention architecture

Assessment translated barriers into practical support.

1

Baseline assessment

Pharmacists asked how patients took medication, remembered doses, understood indication, and handled missed doses or side effects.

2

Reason coding

Nonadherence was categorized into memory, access, clinical, social, financial, health literacy, and system determinants.

3

Tailored response

Support included smartphone reminders, pill boxes, action plans, follow-up recommendations, error resolution, encouragement, and financial assistance.

Objective and conclusion

Target nonadherent patients, then match the support to the reason.

  • Objective: Evaluate patient-tailored complex interventions against usual care for specialty medication adherence.
  • Setting: Single-center, pragmatic randomized trial at an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy.
  • Conclusion: Patient-tailored interventions significantly improved adherence compared with usual care.
  • Practice signal: Specialty pharmacies should target nonadherent patients for adherence interventions.
