Objective

Create a reproducible way to compare clinical outcomes across HSSPs.

The poster describes a process for reporting clinical outcomes across multiple health-system specialty pharmacies, with the goal of creating standard benchmarks for performance comparison.

Methods Pattern

A care model plus data discipline

Capture

Discrete fields in the PMP aligned documentation to selected outcomes.

Train

Pharmacists completed standardized training to improve consistency.

Extract

Common criteria supported reproducible cross-site analysis.

Outcome Definitions

Each metric had a specific measurement rule.

Viral suppression HIV viral load < 200 copies/mL.
SVR Undetected HCV viral load at 12 weeks or more after HCV treatment completion.
RAPID3 improvement RAPID3 change of ≥ -3.8 from baseline and/or treat-to-target score of ≤ 6.
Hospitalization EMR or patient-reported emergency room or hospital utilization.

Interpretation

Standard criteria made outcomes comparable across specialty pharmacies.

A clinical outcome reporting framework was implemented across a network of HSSPs. The authors concluded that standardized criteria allowed each specialty pharmacy to compare performance and validate quality clinical care.

Reported results in HCV, HIV, RA, and oncology can help shape benchmarks for clinical outcome performance in health-system specialty pharmacy.
