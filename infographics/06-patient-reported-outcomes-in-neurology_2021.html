Assessing Patient-reported Outcomes and Pharmacist Interventions in Neurology Specialty Disease States

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy used monthly refill questionnaires to surface missed doses, adverse events, perceived effectiveness, and pharmacist intervention needs for patients receiving neurology specialty medications.

MRQs captured PROs

Monthly refill questionnaires asked about missed doses, adverse effects, and medication effectiveness.

Adherence looked high

Ninety-one percent of patients reported no missed doses during the study window.

AEs were uncommon

Only 5.4% of patients reported at least one adverse event in the extracted results.

Effectiveness was favorable

The extracted effectiveness ratings were overwhelmingly good or excellent.

Pharmacists targeted risk

The largest intervention type addressed recent emergency department or hospital visits.

Conclusion: Neurology integrated specialty pharmacists used patient-reported refill questionnaire data to monitor adherence, adverse events, and effectiveness, then focused interventions on safe and effective medication use, especially after ED or hospital visits.
