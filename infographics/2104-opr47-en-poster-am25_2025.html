Safety screening completion before immune response mediator therapy

Across 12 health-system specialty pharmacy sites, most adults starting immune response mediator therapy had tuberculosis and hepatitis B screening documented before treatment, with incomplete screening most often traced to provider-driven decisions.

Clinical trigger

Patients initiating immune response mediator therapy should be screened for TB and HBV reactivation risk.

Study lens

Electronic health record documentation and pharmacy fill data were reviewed across 12 HSSP sites.

Primary signal

Most referrals had documented screening, with 95% complete screening when any prior timing was accepted.

Timing pattern

Screening clustered near referral: 62% for TB and 56% for HBV within 30 days before or after referral.

Gap driver

When screening was absent, provider-driven reasons were the most common documented cause.

Conclusion

Health-system specialty pharmacy referrals were usually screened before therapy start.

The analysis concludes that most patients initiating immune response mediator therapy through a health-system specialty pharmacy referral completed TB and HBV safety screening before treatment, while missed screening was most commonly associated with prescribing-provider decisions.
