Impact of Implementing a Pharmacist-Led System-Wide Hepatitis C Screening and Test-To-Treat Program

Comanche County Memorial Hospital and Clearway Health evaluated a rural community health center model in which pharmacists used weekly HCV lab dashboards to identify infection, notify providers, connect patients to care, support access, and follow treatment milestones through cure confirmation.

Operational workflow

A pharmacist-managed pathway compressed screening, referral, treatment support, and cure verification into one monitored sequence.

1

Screen

Patients received HCV testing through the CCMH lab.

2

Identify

Pharmacists monitored a weekly lab dashboard for HCV-infected patients.

3

Link

Ordering providers or the Family Medicine Residency clinic connected patients to HCV care.

4

Start

Additional labs, follow-up visits, and HCV treatment prescriptions were initiated.

5

Support

The pharmacy team handled benefits investigation, prior authorization support, dispensing, and teaching.

6

Confirm

Follow-ups at 4 weeks, 8 weeks, EOT, and SVR12 supported cure confirmation.

Methods snapshot

Retrospective, descriptive, lab-data driven.

  • Design: retrospective observational descriptive study.
  • Data source: HCV lab data pulled from VigiLanz, the CCMH lab software.
  • Population: patients with HCV tests conducted at the CCMH lab from April 1, 2023 through April 30, 2024.
  • Exclusion: patients receiving HCV treatment who were not tested at the CCMH lab.

Need

National goals call for large reductions in HCV infection and major increases in diagnosis by 2030.

Objective

Evaluate the impact of a pharmacist-led, system-wide HCV screening and linkage-to-care workflow in a rural community health center.

Intervention

Pharmacists reviewed refreshed lab dashboards, notified providers, and helped establish a care pathway for patients with or without a primary care provider.

Result

Among 1,416 screened patients, 39 had detectable RNA; 67% were linked to HCV care and started treatment.

Conclusion

A dashboard-supported test-to-treat protocol can reduce attrition in the HCV treatment cascade and improve linkage, medication access, and adherence.

Bottom line

Pharmacist ownership turned a fragmented HCV cascade into a monitored care pathway.

The poster's core signal is operational: a pharmacist-led model that combines lab surveillance, provider outreach, clinic linkage, access support, medication teaching, and SVR12 follow-up was associated with a 116% increase in linkage to HCV care during the four-month study period.
