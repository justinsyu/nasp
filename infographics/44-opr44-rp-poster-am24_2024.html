Evaluating the role of social determinants of health and patient demographics on CDK 4/6 inhibitor adherence for breast cancer

A MaxorPlus retrospective cohort study linked pharmacy claims and publicly available SDOH indicators to ask whether specific risk factors align with lower adherence to breast cancer CDK 4/6 inhibitors.

Objective

Find adherence patterns that can guide targeted specialty pharmacy outreach

The poster describes the association between SDOH and CDK 4/6 inhibitor adherence in a MaxorPlus commercial breast cancer population. The practical implication is direct: identify patient groups where education, follow-up, and personalized support may improve medication coverage.

Clinical focus

Palbociclib, ribociclib, and abemaciclib therapy with at least two specialty pharmacy claims.

Adherence measure

Proportion of Days Covered with an 80% clinical threshold and a 6-month discontinuation gap.

SDOH lens

Access, literacy, isolation, sedentary lifestyle, economic stability, and transportation indicators.

Methods

Claims-to-SDOH evidence flow

1

Identify new therapy

Commercial MaxorPlus members newly treated after Jan 1, 2022.

2

Require repeat specialty claims

At least two claims for Ibrance, Kisqali, or Verzenio during the study period.

3

Calculate PDC

Coverage was assessed from the first fill or 180 days before last fill exhaust date through last fill plus 28 days supplied.

4

Layer public SDOH data

Consumer and public data sources were used to assign SDOH risk factor groups.

Conclusion

Education and outreach are the most actionable next steps

Forty percent of members in the <80% adherence cohort were at risk for a digital or health literacy SDOH factor, suggesting that tailored education, personalized support, and follow-up may help improve adherence.

Specialty pharmacy clinical programs can use these findings to focus outreach on patients who may need additional help understanding CDK 4/6 treatment.

Limitations

Interpret with cohort and data limits in mind

  • Medical claims were not included, limiting clinical context.
  • PDC does not capture missed doses due to neutropenia or side effects.
  • Some members may have had additional coverage such as Medicare.
  • The small cohort limits robust statistical analysis.
