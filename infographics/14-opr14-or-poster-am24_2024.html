Objective

Make the case for a Connecticut oral oncology drug repository program

The poster quantified oral oncology waste and opportunity within an integrated health system specialty pharmacy to support advocacy for a state oral oncology drug repository.

Connecticut residents can donate unused oral anticancer medications to national repositories, but the state did not have an active oral oncology repository program from which residents could receive donated therapies.

Methods

How waste was identified and valued

1

Retrieve fills

Oral oncology medications filled by the HSSP from Sept 2022 through Sept 2023 were pulled.

2

Review charts

Patient charts were screened for dose changes and clinically required therapy switches.

3

Exclude manufacturer switches

Manufacturer-driven formulation changes were removed from the opportunity estimate.

4

Calculate waste

Waste equaled last dispensed quantity minus quantity used at first fill of the new prescription.

5

Price and track assistance

Wasted dollars used average wholesale price while grant and free-drug support were tracked in parallel.

Interpretation

Waste is also a patient access signal

Frequent dose or therapy changes created unused oral anticancer medication while patients continued to face affordability challenges.

The poster frames the HSSP as a practical hub: it already sees dispensing, clinical documentation, therapy changes, and assistance outcomes in one workflow.

Conclusion

Turn oral oncology waste into a redistribution pathway

Health system specialty pharmacies can collect unused oral anticancer medications for redistribution to Connecticut patients experiencing financial toxicity through an oral oncology drug repository program.
