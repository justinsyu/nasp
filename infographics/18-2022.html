NASP 2022 | Workflow automation | Hepatitis C clinic

Harnessing the Electronic Health Record to Improve Workflow & Reporting in a Hepatitis C Clinic

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy converted fragmented HCV clinic documentation into discrete Epic SmartForm fields, reusable note text, and near real-time Tableau dashboards for workload, workflow, and outcomes reporting.

  • Quality improvement
  • Hepatitis C
  • EHR SmartForms
  • Tableau dashboards
  • Specialty pharmacy
18-2022
Authors
Cori L Edmonds; Kristen Whelchel
Institution
Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy

Academic medical center
System
Epic SmartForms to Tableau

Pre-treatment, treatment details, medication reconciliation and drug interactions, and on-treatment monitoring.

Objective

Turn routine clinical documentation into reporting infrastructure

The project aimed to gain real-time data collection and reporting, save staff time, and improve morale by using underutilized Epic documentation functionality inside the specialty pharmacy workflow.

1

Assess

Map daily systems, duplicative documentation, discrete data needs, and report elements.

2

Harness

Use EHR SmartForm functionality as the structured-data layer.

3

Create

Build SmartForms with an EHR SuperUser for clinic-relevant documentation.

4

Implement

Have PharmDs document in SmartForms as part of the care workflow.

5

Extract

Feed EHR data into Tableau dashboards that aggregate, tabulate, and chart activity.

Results

Four SmartForms organized the work that had been scattered across systems

01Pre-Treatment

  • Baseline characteristics
  • HCV type and genotype
  • Liver staging and fibrosis

02Treatment Details

  • Medication regimen
  • Start and end dates
  • SVR date tracking

03Med Rec & Interactions

  • Medication reconciliation
  • Drug interaction management
  • PharmD intervention record

04On Treatment Monitoring

  • Adherence monitoring
  • Side effect documentation
  • Current treatment status

Before

Reporting burden was manual, delayed, and duplicative

  • Data lived across multiple systems and could be outdated by presentation time.
  • Clinical staff spent time on duplicated documentation, chart review, and aggregation.
  • Complex reporting work threatened morale and increased burnout risk.

Evidence flow

What changed operationally

Documentation

Duplicative documentation and data recording were eliminated by moving key HCV fields into SmartForms.

Reporting

Data extraction, aggregation, and tabulation became automated rather than dependent on post-hoc chart review.

Team experience

The poster reports reduced delays and improved morale after clinic data became easier to collect and display.

Conclusion

A clinical documentation rebuild became a workload and outcomes reporting system

By pairing Epic SmartForms with Tableau extraction, the HCV clinic created near real-time dashboards while simplifying the day-to-day work of clinical pharmacy staff.

  • Eliminated duplicative documentation and data recording.
  • Simplified clinic reporting through automated extraction, aggregation, and tabulation.
  • Created near real-time workload, workflow, and outcomes dashboards.
  • Reduced reporting delays and improved team morale.
