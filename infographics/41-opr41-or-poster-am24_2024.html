Integrated specialty pharmacy services tracked with better IBD outcome signals.

This multi-center retrospective analysis evaluated adult and pediatric inflammatory bowel disease patients receiving biologic or small molecule therapy through integrated health system specialty pharmacies from January 2022 through December 2023.

  • Inflammatory bowel disease
  • Health system specialty pharmacy
  • Retrospective observational study
  • N = 1,373

Objective

Measure whether embedded specialty pharmacy services are associated with IBD control.

The poster assessed the impact of integrated health system specialty pharmacy services on corticosteroid use, IBD flares, and average pain score after at least four months of HSSP enrollment.

Associated factors

Baseline control was the strongest visual signal.

Steroid-free patients at baseline were more likely to maintain positive steroid outcomes, while symptom-free patients at baseline were more likely to maintain positive flare and pain outcomes at follow-up.

Conclusion Signals of sustained disease control

Reduced corticosteroid use, fewer flares, lower average pain, and high adherence support the potential value of integrated HSSP care for IBD.

Interpretation

The study frames HSSP care as both access support and longitudinal disease monitoring.

  • Patients enrolled in HSSP services demonstrated clinically meaningful reductions in corticosteroid use, IBD flares, and average pain score.
  • The 94.5% mean PDC suggests a consistent adherence signal for specialty therapies delivered through the HSSP model.
  • Baseline steroid-free and symptom-free status were associated with positive follow-up outcomes, but the poster notes that additional analysis is needed to identify contributing factors.
  • Lower steroid exposure may help mitigate risks associated with long-term corticosteroid use while supporting improved quality of life.
