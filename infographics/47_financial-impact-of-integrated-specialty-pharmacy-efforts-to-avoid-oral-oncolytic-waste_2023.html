2023 poster 47 | Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy

Financial Impact of Integrated Specialty Pharmacy Efforts to Avoid Oral Oncolytic Waste

Specialty pharmacists delayed renewal requests when a patient had enough medication and an upcoming follow-up where therapy might change. The review prevented unnecessary fills for patients whose oral oncolytic was later discontinued, held, changed, or dose-adjusted.

Evidence flow

How the waste avoidance screen worked

1

Renewal assessment triggered

A refill renewal came due for a patient filling an oral oncolytic through Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy.

2

Clinical context checked

Pharmacists looked for upcoming visits, labs, or imaging and confirmed the patient had enough medication.

3

Request postponed

When therapy change was plausible, the renewal request waited until after the scheduled follow-up.

4

Follow-up outcome reviewed

After the follow-up, pharmacists reassessed whether the prescription should continue, change, hold, or stop.

5

Unneeded fills avoided

Cost avoidance was calculated using average wholesale price for fills that no longer matched the care plan.

Objective

Evaluate waste and cost avoidance.

The poster examined whether postponing renewal requests for oral oncolytics could avoid medication waste and unnecessary cost when patients had near-term follow-up and sufficient medication supply.

Conclusion

Pharmacist review before renewal was effective.

Specialty pharmacist review prior to renewal request proved effective in avoiding waste: 98% of postponed renewals resulted in a therapy discontinuation, dose change, medication change, or hold, with $967,821.74 in total avoided cost.
