Exploring Rates of PCSK9 Inhibitor Persistence

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy evaluated PCSK9 inhibitor persistence in an integrated specialty pharmacy model, tracking patients from therapy start through 24 months and pairing persistence with LDL response and discontinuation reasons.

Integrated pharmacy model

The poster frames persistence as a care-process outcome, not only a medication outcome.

Prescription and referral

A patient is prescribed a PCSK9 inhibitor and referred to Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy.

Approval and assistance

The pharmacist manages insurance approval and financial assistance as needed.

Therapy start education

Patients receive counseling on administration and potential side effects.

Monthly refill monitoring

Refill calls are reviewed to monitor side effects and continued adherence.

Annual reassessment

Pharmacists reassess beneficial response and ongoing appropriateness of therapy.

Objective

Quantify PCSK9 inhibitor persistence, reasons for non-persistence, and LDL values in an integrated specialty pharmacy model.

Methods

Retrospective cohort of patients initiating therapy through a VUMC provider from September 2015 through August 2018.

Persistence

Patients persistent at 3, 12, and 24 months: 447, 383, and 326, respectively.

LDL response

Median LDL reached the low-60 mg/dL range by 3 months and remained similar through 24 months.

Conclusion

Persistence exceeded previously cited PCSK9 inhibitor reports, highlighting potential benefits of integrated pharmacist support.

Conclusion: sixty-eight percent of patients persisted with PCSK9 inhibitor therapy at 24 months, and persistent patients with available LDL data had a 59% median LDL reduction from baseline.
