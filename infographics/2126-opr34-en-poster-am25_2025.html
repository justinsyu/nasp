The poster reported no significant race-based differences in clinical outcomes during the first year of biologic therapy.

Objective

Describe biologic-era asthma outcomes and evaluate AQI exposure and clinic engagement by race.

Method

Retrospective cohort, Vanderbilt Allergy/Immunology prescribing, geocoded AQI linkage, 12-month follow-up.

Exposure

NO2 and PM2.5 were higher for Black/African American patients in the AQI table.

Engagement

Black/African American patients had a higher missed-appointment percentage without rescheduling.

Conclusion

Disparities were visible in exposure and engagement, while clinical response outcomes did not differ by race.
