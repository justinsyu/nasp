Impact of Health System Specialty Pharmacist-Liaison Teams on Patient Outcomes

Trellis Rx evaluated how onsite pharmacy liaisons, using standardized monthly Arbor questionnaires, triggered pharmacist-reviewed interventions across a multisite health system specialty pharmacy model.

Objective

Quantify what liaison-collected touchpoint data triggered for pharmacist follow-up.

The primary goal was to assess the number and types of interventions generated when pharmacy liaisons used a standard Arbor questionnaire during monthly patient touchpoints, then routed protocol-driven issues to their pharmacist counterparts.

Methods

A high-touch care model converted routine liaison contact into structured pharmacist intervention work.

Initial pharmacist counseling

Patients received education and counseling from the pharmacist at therapy start.

Liaison monthly touchpoint

Onsite pharmacy liaisons collected patient safety and efficacy data at recurring touchpoints.

Arbor questionnaire

A standard questionnaire in the Arbor platform created structured intervention triggers.

Protocol-driven follow-up

Triggered issues were reviewed and completed by the liaison's onsite pharmacist counterpart.

Outcome-focused support

Interventions were sorted into subtypes and assessed for patient-care outcomes.

Result signal

4,200 interventions triggered by liaison touchpoints, then reviewed and completed by pharmacists

Conclusion pattern

Embedded team model

Locally embedded model -> liaison engagement -> improved outcomes

  • Embedding the pharmacist-liaison relationship inside health systems supported comprehensive, high-touch care.
  • Expanding the liaison role gave pharmacists additional support for long-term outcome work.
  • Continuous monitoring helped identify and resolve patient needs or medication-related issues faster.

Liaison touchpoints

Onsite liaisons gathered structured safety and efficacy signals during monthly patient contact.

Protocol triggers

Arbor questionnaires turned those signals into actionable intervention triggers.

Pharmacist review

Clinical pharmacists reviewed and completed the triggered interventions.

Care actions

Interventions included education, regimen changes, adherence aids, lab orders, and provider follow-up.

Workflow outcome

The authors concluded that liaison-patient touchpoints help drive clinical outcomes.

Conclusion: a locally embedded pharmacist-liaison model used monthly touchpoint data to trigger 4,200 pharmacist-completed interventions, supporting faster identification and resolution of medication-related patient needs.
