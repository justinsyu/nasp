Policy context

Medicare Part D provisions created a staged affordability shift

2023

Out-of-pocket spending threshold around $3,100; catastrophic coverage still included 5% coinsurance; low-income subsidy extended to 135% to 150% of the federal poverty level.

2024

Catastrophic copays were eliminated and low-income subsidy eligibility expanded to 150% of the federal poverty level, changing the assistance need profile.

2025

Medicare Part D out-of-pocket drug spending cap of $2,000 and monthly payment smoothing may further reduce PAP enrollment need.

Specialty pharmacy workflow

Benefits investigation funnels prescriptions toward cost assessment and assistance

1

Prescription received

Technician gathers insurance information and completes initial system review.

2

Benefits investigation

Coverage requirements are assessed for prior authorization, appeal, peer-to-peer, or external appeal.

3

Cost assessment

Out-of-pocket exposure and assistance eligibility are reviewed after coverage status is known.

4

Assistance screen

Staff check copay assistance, PAP availability, program criteria, and alternate pharmacy triage needs.

5

PAP enrollment

For eligible uninsured or underinsured patients, the HSSP helps coordinate first enrollment applications.

Objective

Describe the impact of the Inflation Reduction Act on PAP enrollment for specialty oral hematology and oncology medications through an HSSP.

Methods

Retrospective operational analysis of Q1 2023 and Q1 2024 PAP enrollment applications coordinated for prescriptions after benefits investigation.

Cost method

Thirty-day PAP savings were estimated using average wholesale price values from Lexicomp.

Result pattern

PAP enrollments, total PAP savings, and total out-of-pocket cost all declined in Q1 2024.

Conclusion

IRA changes may have helped patients afford therapy, but future work should assess therapy abandonment from ongoing financial toxicity.
