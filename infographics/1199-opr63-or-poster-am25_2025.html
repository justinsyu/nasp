Social determinants shape how patients perceive home infusion.

A nurse-administered survey of home infusion patients mapped financial, access, safety, cultural, mood, and perception-change themes onto the HHS Social Determinants of Health framework.

Objective

Read home infusion through the SDoH lens.

The study evaluated perceived benefits and challenges of home infusion therapy across Ig and biologic therapies, with attention to access, finances, safety, culture, mood, and changing awareness.

Methods

From a 10% random sample to patient-reported SDoH signals.

Sample frame

A 10% random sample was drawn from more than 3,000 home infusion patients, yielding an intended n=300 survey group.

Survey completion

Nurses completed surveys with 268 patients from April 1 through July 31, 2024.

Eligibility boundary

Included company-employed nurse infusion or self-administered SC therapy training; excluded agency-managed nursing.

Instrument

A 30-question survey used yes/no items and 1-10 scales to summarize financial, access, safety, culture, mood, and perception outcomes.

Conclusion

Home infusion showed non-clinical value beyond medication delivery.

The poster concludes that home infusion can improve perceived quality of life, reduce caregiver and travel burden, and support a patient-centered model when financial, access, cultural, emotional, and safety factors are considered together.

  • Education opportunity: only 45% had considered these survey topics before home infusion, yet 97% said others should be informed about potential financial or social benefits.
  • Access gap: 53% were not aware of the closest infusion center, and caregiver support was inconsistent despite strong confidence in home therapy logistics.
  • Future research: compare home versus traditional sites, quantify indirect costs, mitigate rural or underserved access barriers, and test education campaigns.
