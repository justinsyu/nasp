Objective

Turn learner capacity into specialty pharmacy capacity

The poster aims to describe how UI Health SPS uses pharmacy externs to enhance patient care, manage essential projects, and provide a model other specialty pharmacies can adapt.

Evidence flow

How the extern model works

1. Gather practice input

Current externs and the operational manager summarized active practices, roles, and staffing patterns.

2. Train longitudinally

Training continues throughout the year, with pharmacist-guided projects transferred before senior externs begin APPE rotations.

3. Staff high-need hours

Externs cover weekends, early or late weekday hours, and full-time summer and winter break schedules.

4. Anchor delivery work

Students manage medication delivery service tasks, including temperature-controlled packaging and delivery-company handoff.

5. Extend patient care

Externs support fulfillment, monthly clinical assessments, refills, supervised counseling, prescription transfers, and medication reconciliation.

Operational roles

Three complementary lanes

Rx

Fulfillment support

Fill specialty and non-specialty medications and coordinate medication refills.

Pt

Patient touchpoints

Conduct monthly assessments, support supervised counseling, and assist medication reconciliation.

Ops

On-site operations

Package temperature-controlled medications and complete delivery handoffs.

Project portfolio

Individual and group projects matched to extern interests

  • Temperature-controlled shipping validation
  • Welcome packet material management
  • Prior authorization and reauthorization assistance
  • Outreach for SPS enrollment
  • Drug cards for new approvals and indications
  • Standard Operating Procedure updates
  • Monthly phone data for accreditation reporting

Methods

Practice-based summary

Investigators gathered input from current UI Health SPS pharmacy externs and their operational manager to summarize current extern practices.

Key result

COVID-era operations increased reliance on externs

Although many full-time staff tasks could shift remote during the pandemic, pharmacy still required on-site contributions; since March 2020, UI Health SPS reported greater reliance on externs for in-person operations.

Conclusion: fully leveraging pharmacy externs helps UI Health SPS innovate within specialty pharmacy, prioritize burdensome work, support accreditation and clinical management, and complete essential daily operations.
