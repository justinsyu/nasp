Multiple Switching Between Biosimilar Adalimumab and High-Concentration Reference Adalimumab

A randomized phase 3 study tested whether three switches between ADL-afzb and reference adalimumab changed pharmacokinetics, immunogenicity, or safety in adults with moderately to severely active rheumatoid arthritis receiving methotrexate.

Central interpretation No PK penalty

Multiple switching did not produce clinically meaningful differences in steady-state PK compared with continuous reference adalimumab.

Evidence flow

Regulatory question

Would switching between biosimilar and reference product increase safety risk or reduce effectiveness?

Controlled comparison

Patients were randomized 1:1 after a shared reference-adalimumab lead-in.

PK surrogate

Cmax and AUCtau were measured during intensive Week 30-32 sampling.

Similar biology

PK profiles, ADA incidence, NAb titers, and persistence patterns were comparable.

Safety context

Serious AEs and discontinuations due to AE were balanced; no deaths were reported.

Conclusion: in this first controlled multi-switch RA study, the risk of switching between citrate-free ADL-afzb and high-concentration ADL-REF was no greater than continuous reference use for immunogenicity, safety, or possible diminished efficacy assessed by PK.
