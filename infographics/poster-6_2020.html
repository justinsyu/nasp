NASP 2020 | Poster 6 | Rheumatology patient management

Development of a Rheumatoid Arthritis Patient Management Program

Intermountain Specialty Pharmacy built an RA-specific follow-up program around RAPID3, a telephonic patient-reported disease activity measure, to track disease severity, guide education, and identify patients needing rheumatology follow-up.

Program logic

Build a disease-specific loop around a telephonic RA score

01 Identify RA patients

Enterprise Rx diagnosis codes plus active RA medication in the prior 3 months.

02 Notify and contact

Patients received a letter before clinical staff called for baseline RAPID3.

03 Capture RAPID3

Patient-reported disease activity, dates, comorbidities, education, and counseling time.

04 Triage follow-up

More severe activity was intended for monthly calls; milder activity for 3 to 6 months.

05 Escalate when needed

Clinical staff shared resources, non-drug options, referrals, and recommendations to providers.

Interpretation

What the table patterns suggest

  • Patient-reported assessment was feasible by phone and gave the pharmacy a repeatable care-management signal.
  • Comorbid chronic pain, fibromyalgia, and osteoarthritis may influence RAPID3 because the score includes patient-reported pain.
  • Rheumatologist reception was mixed, especially around pharmacy-to-office outreach versus patient-directed contact.

Conclusion

Baseline severity data became an operating asset

The program established baseline RAPID3 values for this specialty pharmacy population and created a structure for education, resource sharing, realistic medication expectations, and longitudinal trend monitoring as the PMP matured.
