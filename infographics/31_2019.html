Impact of Using Real-World Outcomes Data Versus Clinical Evidence and Published Prices in Value Assessments

IBM Watson Health compared ICER psoriasis value-assessment inputs with fully adjudicated MarketScan claims to show how net price and real-world exposure assumptions can materially change therapy-level value signals.

Objective

Test whether value models change when assumptions are replaced with claims reality.

The study asked whether real-world financial performance data, including adjudicated prices and treatment exposure, would significantly shift incremental cost-per-QALY estimates versus pricing assumptions compiled from secondary sources.

Evidence flow

From claims cohort to value-assessment stress test

1 Identify psoriasis patients Adults with psoriasis-related treatment and at least two diagnosis claims around treatment initiation.
2 Exclude confounders Pregnancy, HIV/AIDS, Crohn's disease, rheumatologic diagnoses, and ankylosing spondylitis were excluded.
3 Measure real-world price and exposure IBM Access and Value Connect estimated net costs and mean duration of therapy in MarketScan claims.
4 Recalculate value signals ICER drug-cost and QALY assumptions were reviewed, then compared with claims-based inputs where possible.

Conclusion

Net price plus real-world outcomes can make value assessment more transparent and accurate.

Using fully adjudicated net prices allowed faster direct comparison across therapies and produced a different view of price versus value than list-price and discount assumptions alone. The authors recommend incorporating available real-world data sets and tools into value assessments whenever possible.
