NASP 2021 | Poster 30 | Relapsing multiple sclerosis

Persistence to disease-modifying therapy in patients with relapsing MS

A 3-year retrospective review found that long-term persistence to a single index disease-modifying therapy was low, with treatment switches, transfers of care, and discontinuations accumulating steadily after initiation.

Miranda Z. Kozlicki, PharmD; Brandon Markley, PharmD, BCPS; Nisha B. Shah, PharmD; Josh DeClercq, MS; Leena Choi, PhD; Autumn D. Zuckerman, PharmD, BCPS, AAHIVP, CSP. Vanderbilt University Medical Center.

Specialty pharmacist involvement

Integrated pharmacists supported discontinuation decisions and transitions

Identify

Initiate discontinuation needs for lymphopenia, liver enzyme elevation, or pregnancy.

Communicate

Coordinate conversations between the patient and prescriber.

Coordinate

Arrange follow-up care, including additional lab monitoring.

Recommend

Suggest new DMT options when a therapy change was needed.

Conclusion

DMT treatment changes were common across 3 years.

What the poster adds

  • Long-term persistence to one specific DMT over 3 years was low.
  • Patients commonly discontinued because of side effects or stable disease.
  • Integrated specialty pharmacists played a role in identifying, communicating, coordinating, and recommending next steps during DMT transitions.
