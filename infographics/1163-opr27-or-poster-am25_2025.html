2025 poster 1163-OPR27-OR | Specialty pharmacy operations

Integrated specialty pharmacy compressed prescription request-to-verification time

Clearway Health and Signature Health reviewed 948 specialty medication prescriptions over seven months. The integrated health system specialty pharmacy model produced a 1.5-day average turnaround time, below published retail specialty pharmacy benchmarks for both clean and intervention-heavy prescriptions.

Operational workflow

The model pairs access work with pharmacist review before verification.

Provider sends prescription

Specialty medication order enters the integrated pharmacy workflow.

Benefits and PA

Liaison team performs benefit investigation, submits prior authorization, and creates care plans when needed.

Denial response

Clinical pharmacist reviews PA denials, resubmits, coordinates peer-to-peer review, or appeals.

Patient coordination

Team notifies patient, provider, and pharmacist, then coordinates teaching and delivery.

Verify and deliver

Prescription is processed, verified, and delivered to the patient's preferred address.

Objective and methods

What was tested

Objective: Investigate the impact of a health system specialty pharmacy on time to prescription fill.

Design: Single-center, retrospective, observational study using RXQ Liberty pharmacy software data.

Study data: Specialty medication prescriptions sent to Signature Healthcare pharmacy from June 1, 2024 through December 31, 2024.

Conclusion

What the authors concluded

Average fill times were lower than published retail pharmacy turnaround times. The integrated specialty pharmacy workflow and clinical support were positioned as mechanisms for faster access to critical therapy and fewer delays in care.

Pharmacy staff planned to continue aiming for shorter fill times as a marker of operational efficiency and timely patient access.
