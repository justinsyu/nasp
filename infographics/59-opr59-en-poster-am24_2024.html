Comparative Effectiveness of Denosumab versus Zoledronic Acid among Postmenopausal Women with Osteoporosis in the U.S. Medicare Program

A retrospective Medicare fee-for-service cohort compared fracture outcomes after new initiation of denosumab versus zoledronic acid in treatment-naive women with postmenopausal osteoporosis.

Objective

Estimate real-world fracture risk after treatment initiation

This study evaluated whether denosumab reduced fracture risk compared with zoledronic acid among U.S. women with postmenopausal osteoporosis who had no prior osteoporosis treatment.

Denosumab 89,990 ZA 36,861
New initiators from Jan 1, 2012 to Dec 31, 2018 Followed through Dec 31, 2019 or 5 years

Methods

Doubly robust weighting across treatment and censoring

1

Identify

Women age 66+ in Medicare fee-for-service with PMO and new denosumab or ZA use.

2

Weight

Inverse-probability treatment weights from multivariate logistic regression.

3

Censor

Follow until fracture, discontinuation or switch, disenrollment, death, data end, or 5 years.

4

Compare

Risk ratios for major osteoporotic, hip, nonvertebral, NHNV, and hospitalized vertebral fractures.

Conclusion

Clinically meaningful reductions were observed in a cohort of more than 125,000 treatment-naive women.

The poster concluded that denosumab was associated with robust reductions in MOP, hip, NV, and NHNV fracture risk compared with ZA, with larger reductions as exposure duration increased.

Outcome definitions

Fracture families analyzed

  • MOP: nonvertebral and hospitalized vertebral fractures.
  • NV: hip, humerus, pelvis, radius/ulna, and other femur fractures.
  • NHNV: non-hip, nonvertebral fractures.
  • HV: hospitalized vertebral fractures.
