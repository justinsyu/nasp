Integrated Specialty Model Lowers Pharmacy Expense

A Medicare Advantage claims analysis compared non-oncology specialty patients managed in an integrated health system specialty pharmacy care model with network pharmacy members in the same geographies. The strongest signal was lower risk-adjusted pharmacy expense and fewer ER visits.

Objective and methods

What was tested

Objective: Evaluate pharmacy and medical expenses among non-oncology specialty pharmacy patients in an HSSP group care model versus a national Network.

Risk adjustment: CMS-HCC risk scores were used to normalize cost and utilization by patient months and disease burden.

Utilization result

ER visits were lower in the HSSP group.

Interpretation

Cost movement was not uniform across categories.

  • The integrated HSSP model was associated with significantly less pharmacy expense in the non-oncology specialty population.
  • Total medical expense was higher for HSSP patients, but the poster described this as small and non-significant.
  • The authors contrasted this pattern with a prior oncology analysis where the HSSP model showed lower total medical expense and slightly higher pharmacy expense.
