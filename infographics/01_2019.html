Patiromer and Maintenance of RAASi Therapy in Hyperkalemic Medicare Patients

A retrospective claims analysis asked whether Medicare Advantage patients with hyperkalemia who received a potassium binder continued renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system inhibitor therapy during the 6 months after index.

Objective

Measure RAASi persistence after hyperkalemia

The study evaluated RAASi utilization among Medicare Advantage patients with pre-index continuous RAASi exposure and serum potassium at or above 5.0 mEq/L.

CE = continuous exposure; ITT = intent to treat. RAASi included ACE inhibitors, ARBs, MRAs, and direct renin inhibitors.

Population

Medicare Advantage patients with hyperkalemia and pre-index RAASi use were identified in de-identified national claims.

Exposure arms

Patients were grouped as patiromer, sodium polystyrene sulfonate, or no potassium binder based on dispensing or diagnosis rules.

Baseline pattern

The patiromer cohort had more chronic kidney disease and diabetes, and the lowest mean baseline eGFR.

Outcome window

Continuation and down-titration were assessed during the 6 months after index, with separate ITT and CE views.

Interpretation

The authors framed the results as descriptive only, with no causal or comparative claims.

Baseline signals

Clinical complexity clustered in the patiromer arm

Conclusion

Continuation remained the central signal

The poster concluded that the patiromer cohort showed the highest percentage of patients continuing RAASi therapy over 6 months.

Down-titration of lisinopril, losartan, and valsartan was low across groups, roughly around 10% overall, but interpretation is limited by the descriptive observational design and small CE binder cohorts.
