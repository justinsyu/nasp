Objective

Move from pooled call handling to dedicated call lanes.

The poster evaluated whether a dedicated staffing model for inbound and outbound calls could improve specialty pharmacy call center metrics, including speed of answer and abandonment rate.

  • Pressure point: call center metrics were highly variable during COVID-19 staffing disruptions.
  • Design input: anticipated call volume and refill-coordination demand informed staffing targets.
  • Adjustment loop: daily metrics and monthly staff satisfaction surveys guided PDSA changes.

Staff signal

Separate teams were strongly preferred.

Among survey respondents, 46.15% were very satisfied and 46.15% were somewhat satisfied with separate inbound and outbound teams. All respondents preferred the separated model over one team handling both call types.

Operational interpretation

Dedicated lanes helped, but staffing remained constrained.

  • One FTE per 40 incoming calls was described as sufficient for daily call center volume.
  • Available agents still lagged required agents because of ongoing pandemic-related staffing gaps.
  • Supervisor monitoring of the call center board remained necessary when agents were unavailable.

Next steps

Future work focused on durability and monitoring.

  • Continue implementation of separate inbound and outbound teams.
  • Extend the staffing model with remote work options.
  • Use a new phone system to monitor separate teams.
