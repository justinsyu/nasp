Interpretation

What the poster adds

The MRQ workflow produced a compact surveillance loop: refill outreach captured patient-reported effectiveness, adherence, and adverse effects; specialty pharmacists then used those signals for targeted interventions.

  • Reported medication effectiveness was high across responses.
  • Missed-dose and adverse-effect reports were uncommon.
  • Pharmacist interventions remained clinically varied, spanning adherence, education, toxicity, care coordination, and acute-care follow-up.

Conclusion

Patients with inflammatory conditions cared for by an integrated health-system specialty pharmacy reported high medication effectiveness with low rates of missed doses and adverse effects. The poster positions specialty pharmacists as an active layer for safe and effective medication use, while calling for additional research on long-term clinical outcomes.

Care model signal

Monthly refill questionnaires gave pharmacists repeated, patient-centered inputs beyond dispensing records.

Future work

Longitudinal outcomes research is needed to connect these PRO and intervention patterns to durable clinical benefit.
