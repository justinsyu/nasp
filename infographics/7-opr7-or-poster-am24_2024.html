Methods snapshot

Retrospective dashboard review across multiple health systems

  • Study design: multisite, retrospective, observational, descriptive study.
  • Data source: patient-reported outcomes and the pharmacist intervention dashboard.
  • Inclusion: adults with at least one documented MIDAS score and at least one migraine medication.
  • Analysis: descriptive statistics calculated the proportion of patients in each disease-activity category.

Treatment outcome monitoring

Conclusion

PRO-guided pharmacist intervention showed clinical value

The poster concludes that patient-reported outcomes based clinical interventions by specialty pharmacists can improve migraine treatment outcomes, especially when pharmacists use frequent touch points to identify adherence, access, and response barriers.

A key limitation was that pharmacist interventions could not be presented as discrete EMR-derived data.

Objective

Describe the value of MIDAS-based targeted pharmacist interventions in migraine treatment outcomes.

Population

132 adults with migraine medication therapy followed by Clearway Health Specialty team.

Intervention logic

Dashboarded MIDAS scores routed outreach for medication adherence assessment and clinical intervention.

Result

48% improved MIDAS scores; only 8% worsened from moderate to severe disability.

Practice signal

More frequent pharmacist touch points can screen suboptimal outcomes between provider visits.

Poster context

Why MIDAS fit the workflow

Migraine management requires coordination across providers, while specialty pharmacists have repeated patient touch points to assess adherence, access barriers, and therapy outcomes.

  • MIDAS is a validated questionnaire for migraine-related disability.
  • Dashboard access turned patient-reported scores into targeted outreach queues.
  • Patients declining MIDAS were routed to prescribers and clinical pharmacists for engagement.
