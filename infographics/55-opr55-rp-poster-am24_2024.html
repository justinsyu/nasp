Evidence flow

Telephonic assessments become comparative outcome snapshots at months 3 and 6.

1

Define new starts

Adults with an FDA-approved indication, new to any adalimumab product, and enrolled in Lumicera's patient management workflow.

2

Exclude switchers

Patients restarting, continuing, pregnant or breastfeeding, under 18, or switching between products are excluded.

3

Compare groups

Reference-product patients are compared with patients prescribed one of ten FDA-approved adalimumab biosimilars.

4

Measure outcomes

Satisfaction, symptom trajectory, reported side effects, and proportion of days covered are assessed at 3 and 6 months.

Outcome architecture

Primary and secondary endpoints separate patient experience from refill behavior.

1-10 Patient satisfaction scale, grouped as not satisfied, neutral, or highly satisfied.
Better / same / worse Self-assessed symptom change compared with symptoms 3 months prior.
Yes / no Side effect reporting, with common categories including injection site reaction, infection, headache, fatigue, GI, and other.
PDC Adherence measured through first 3 months, first 6 months, and months 4 through 6.

Objective

Increase patient and provider confidence by comparing real-world outcomes for reference adalimumab and biosimilars.

Setting

Lumicera is a mail-order specialty pharmacy serving more than 30,000 patients across the United States.

Design

Retrospective cohort study using telephonically completed assessments embedded in standard pharmacy workflow.

Baseline signal

Reference adalimumab patients outnumber biosimilar patients nearly four to one in the reported eligible cohort.

Conclusion frame

The poster is still in progress, so the current evidence package is primarily cohort readiness and endpoint definition.

Working conclusion: this poster establishes a real-world specialty pharmacy cohort and follow-up framework for comparing adalimumab reference-product and biosimilar experience, with final clinical outcome interpretation pending study completion.
