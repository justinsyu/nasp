Pharmacist-led infusion management moved rheumatology orders upstream

Boston Medical Center evaluated a clinic-embedded pharmacist protocol for non-oncology infusion therapy management in rheumatic disorders, focusing on operational delays, provider capacity, and financial impact.

Interpretation

Integrated pharmacist services translated coordination work into measurable capacity

Objective

Assess the operational and financial impact of a clinical pharmacist-led non-oncology infusion therapy management protocol in patients with rheumatic disorders.

Methods

Single-center retrospective descriptive analysis comparing pre- and post-implementation EMR data, including order ownership, time from order to appointment, provider time spared, and associated revenue impact.

Conclusion

Embedding clinical pharmacist services improved clinical, operational, and financial outcomes by reducing infusion delays, shifting order management to pharmacists, and freeing provider capacity.

Operational gap

Infusion therapy requires medication access, screening, lab monitoring, order signing, scheduling, and follow-up coordination.

Protocol lever

Collaborative Drug Therapy Management enabled pharmacist ownership of ordering and reporting tools.

Access result

Post-protocol delayed infusions were eliminated in the reported analysis.

Capacity result

Provider time saved averaged about 30 hours per month.

System result

The health system estimated $0.4 million in annual revenue or cost-saving opportunity.
