Measuring Prescription Turnaround Times in an Integrated Health System Specialty Pharmacy Setting

3,532 initial prescriptions across two integrated health systems showed a mean written-to-filled turnaround time of 2.9 days, with most prescriptions filled within three days.

Objective

Quantify how quickly specialty prescriptions move from order to fill

Patients often face administrative barriers before specialty therapy can begin. The poster measured turnaround time from prescription written date to fill date in integrated health system specialty pharmacies.

  • Data source: Clearway Health patient management platform plus claims adjudication data.
  • Population: 1,817 unique specialty pharmacy patients at two health systems.
  • Exclusions: refills and scripts with written-to-fill dates greater than 30 days.
  • Analysis: descriptive statistics for prescription turnaround times.

Fill speed

Same-day and three-day fulfillment dominated the distribution

Across all specialty drug categories, more than half of prescriptions were filled on the same day and nearly three quarters within one day. Hematology/oncology was slower, but still reached 78.4% within three days.

Evidence flow

Liaison coordination turns administrative barriers into parallel workstreams

Trigger

Software identifies a new specialty prescription or an established patient at a specialty clinic visit.

Investigate

A dedicated liaison completes benefits investigation before pharmacy fulfillment.

Resolve barriers

Prior authorization, limited distribution access, contracted pharmacy limits, and high copays are addressed.

Coordinate

The liaison keeps the patient, provider, and pharmacy connected through each access step.

Fill or redirect

The prescription is processed, shipped or picked up, or redirected to a contracted specialty pharmacy when required.

Key result: 73.0% of all prescriptions were filled within one day, and 80.5% were filled within three days despite common specialty-pharmacy access barriers.

Conclusion

Integrated pharmacy coordination may compress time to therapy

The authors concluded that close coordination among liaisons, care teams, patients, and pharmacy staff helps remove addressable barriers and supports rapid initiation of specialty medications.
