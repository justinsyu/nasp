Analysis of Hepatitis B Screening with Oral Anticancer Therapy Initiation

Cleveland Clinic Specialty Pharmacy assessed whether patients starting oral anticancer therapy received hepatitis B virus screening and whether pharmacist recommendations improved alignment with ASCO's three-test screening guidance.

Most oral anticancer therapy starts lacked HBV screening before treatment, creating a clear workflow opportunity for specialty pharmacists.

Primary and secondary results

Pharmacist intervention converted missing screens into completed panels.

Screened before treatment 17.4%
No prior screening 82.6%
RPh rec among unscreened 57.4%
Provider acceptance 73.4%

After accepted pharmacist recommendations, 168 additional patients completed HBV screening; median time from recommendation to panel was 29 days [11.25-54].

Why screen?

Patients receiving anticancer therapy are immunocompromised, and HBV reactivation can cause serious hepatic outcomes.

Guideline target

ASCO recommends HBsAg, HBcAb, and HBsAb screening at the start of anticancer therapy.

Study frame

Retrospective review of Cleveland Clinic oncology patients with oral anticancer medication dispensed by CCSP.

Pharmacist action

Specialty pharmacists recommended HBV screening when therapy was initiated without documented screening.

Safety signal

Acceptance was common, but incomplete panels remained a key optimization target.

Among screened patients, HBcAb positivity was uncommon but actionable.

  • Objective: quantify HBV screening before oral anticancer therapy initiation.

Bottom line

The strongest workflow signal was not test positivity; it was missed screening.

In this 483-patient cohort, only 84 patients were screened before oral anticancer therapy, while pharmacist recommendations led to 168 additional screens after provider acceptance. The remaining opportunity is earlier, complete three-test HBV screening before treatment begins.
