NASP 2022 / Poster 17-2022 | AI-supported specialty pharmacy outreach

AI Chatbot Provides Efficiency and Convenience

UPMC Enterprises and the UPMC Retail Pharmacy Network piloted an SMS chatbot that turned routine seven-day specialty medication follow-ups into automated screenings, reserving staff calls for side effects, regimen changes, or low remaining supply.

Objective

7-day follow-ups became automated screenings after medication sold dates, with staff attention redirected only to patients who triggered a clinical or refill need.

Methods as automation design

Dispensing data created the ticket; patient answers decided whether staff needed to call.

Query fills

Hepatitis C and transplant GCN fills were queried daily from pharmacy dispensing software.

Create CRM tickets

Tickets were automatically opened from the sold date and set to recur every seven days.

Send SMS screen

Patients answered side effect, adherence, regimen-change, and days-on-hand questions.

Route exceptions

Only flagged responses changed tickets to pharmacist requested or refill requested status.

Operational interpretation

What the numbers changed for staff

1,014 follow-up moments initiated without spreadsheet or calendar tracking
~300 manual calls offloaded over the four-month pilot
3 ticket-changing triggers: side effects, regimen change, low supply

One source row reports 2,357 transplant patients with no regimen change, which exceeds dialogues started; the infographic preserves the directional finding while using internally consistent counts for totals and rates.

Conclusion

Automation narrowed manual outreach to patients who needed human follow-up.

By integrating dispensing software with CRM ticket creation and SMS screening, UPMC retail pharmacies automatically tracked patient-reported data and shifted pharmacy staff effort toward side effects, regimen changes, and impending refills.

Practical takeaway

The chatbot worked because it was a routing layer, not a replacement for pharmacy judgment.

Routine answers suppressed unnecessary calls, while clinically meaningful responses changed ticket status and prompted manual pharmacist or refill outreach.
