CF Refill Utilization Trends with CFTR Modulators

Maxor Specialty Pharmacy evaluated whether supportive cystic fibrosis therapy refill patterns shifted during the six months after Trikafta® initiation. The clearest signal was fewer inhaled-antibiotic fills, while mucolytic and PERT trends were flat to higher.

Objective

Measure whether Trikafta® changed refill behavior

The poster compared cystic fibrosis supportive therapy utilization in patients before and after starting Trikafta®, using percent change in refill counts as the main outcome.

Evidence flow

From CFTR modulation to refill trends

Context High CF treatment burden

Patients average five medications and about three hours of daily therapy.

Hypothesis Modulators may reduce symptoms

Reduced mucus buildup could lower perceived need for supportive therapies.

Data Specialty pharmacy claims

Claims were collected for patients taking Trikafta® through Maxor pharmacies.

Comparison Six-month windows

Fill counts were compared before and after Trikafta® initiation.

Interpretation Payor-relevant signal

Fewer antibiotic refills may suggest lower infection-related burden and cost.

Results

Observed refill changes

  • Inhaled antibiotic claims decreased by 14.79% after Trikafta® initiation.
  • Mucolytic fills were effectively unchanged, increasing by 1.04%.
  • PERT fills increased by 5.61%, but the specialty-channel-only data may not fully capture enzyme utilization.

Conclusion

Preliminary signal for real-world monitoring

  • The authors interpreted the inhaled antibiotic decline as potentially meaningful because pulmonary infections drive CF morbidity and mortality.
  • Reduced antibiotic refill demand may translate into lower transactional burden for patients and payors.
  • The poster calls for larger cohorts and longer follow-up to clarify the real-world effects of next-generation CFTR agents.
