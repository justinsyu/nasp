NASP 2020 Poster 19

Impact of Specialty Pharmacies on Stomatitis Prophylaxis with Dexamethasone at Everolimus Therapy Initiation

A retrospective Therigy platform review asked whether specialty pharmacy screening could improve access to dexamethasone mouthwash when patients started everolimus, using access to prophylaxis as a marker of higher-quality onboarding.

Intervention logic

Prompt, educate, then re-check

Specialty pharmacy teams were prompted during onboarding and follow-up assessments to verify whether patients had dexamethasone mouthwash at everolimus initiation.

Study mechanics

Retrospective specialty pharmacy observation

1 Select SPs

Data came from selected specialty pharmacies using the Therigy platform.

2 Screen 206 patients

Patients initiating everolimus were screened for a mouthwash prescription.

3 Support negative status

When no prescription was documented, teams supplied benefit resources and voucher access.

4 Compare follow-up

Negative statuses were tracked at later clinical follow-ups for conversion to yes.

Objective: observe the impact of specialty pharmacies on access to dexamethasone and quality of care at everolimus initiation.

Conclusion

Access to prophylaxis looked like a practical quality marker

Patients starting everolimus without dexamethasone had a higher observed chance of early therapy discontinuation. The authors interpret eventual access to dexamethasone as a surrogate marker of successful specialty pharmacy services.

Limitations included missing data on stomatitis incidence, severity, resolution, and reasons for negative dexamethasone status; future prospective studies were recommended.
