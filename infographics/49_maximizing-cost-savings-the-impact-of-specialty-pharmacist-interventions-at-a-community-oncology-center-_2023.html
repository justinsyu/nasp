Maximizing Cost Savings: The Impact of Specialty Pharmacist Interventions at a Community Oncology Center

A retrospective review of embedded health-system specialty pharmacy pharmacist interventions found that accepted oral oncolytic changes generated substantial drug cost savings while reinforcing appropriate medication use.

Objective

What the study asked

Determine the impact of health-system specialty pharmacy pharmacist interventions on cost savings for oral oncolytics at a community oncology center.

Conclusion

What the authors concluded

Embedded oncology specialty pharmacists play a pivotal role in appropriate oral oncolytic use, and structured intervention tracking can help demonstrate HSSP value in value-based care models.

Interpretation: this poster frames specialty pharmacist work as both a medication-management service and a measurable cost-savings mechanism, with the largest signal coming from accepted dose modifications and temporary holds.
