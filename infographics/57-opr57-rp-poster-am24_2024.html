The impact of a pharmacist-led patient management program on inflammatory disease

Vivo Health, a health-system-owned specialty pharmacy, planned a retrospective pre-post analysis of patient-reported outcomes collected during pharmacist telehealth assessments for patients with inflammatory disease.

Objective Measure whether pharmacist telehealth management improves patient-reported outcomes.

Initial assessment values will be compared with the latest reassessment through the end of 2023.

Eligible ID patient

Diagnosis of inflammatory disease plus at least 3 anti-inflammatory medication fills.

PMP assessment

Pharmacists provide education, counseling, monitoring, and refill screening by telehealth.

Reassessment

Patients with a completed 2023 reassessment form the analytic cohort.

Pre-post test

Initial outcomes are compared with latest outcomes using t-test or nonparametric alternatives.

Predictor model

Regression models examine age, sex, months in program, and RA severity.

Program model

Pharmacist contact turns refill encounters into structured outcome monitoring.

Baseline result

The cohort is broad but RA-heavy.

Diagnosis distribution supports separate primary-diagnosis analysis, with RA large enough for RAPID3 severity stratification.

Expected conclusion

PMP benefit is hypothesized, not yet proven here.

The poster anticipates positive effects, with outcomes predicted to vary by initial severity, sex, months in program, and age.

Interpretation limits

Selection and data quality matter.

Patients may opt out, retrospective records may be inconsistent, and medications are limited to the PMP formulary.
