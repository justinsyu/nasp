Implementation of a flowsheet to track IBD patients initiating biologics with complex dosing regimens

Vanderbilt Specialty Pharmacy built an EHR flowsheet to coordinate patients moving from induction IV biologic dosing to SubQ maintenance injections, creating a transparent process across infusion and specialty pharmacy teams.

Objective

Make complex biologic starts visible across teams

The poster describes a transparent EHR process for IBD therapies that require one or more IV induction doses before subcutaneous maintenance injections. The flowsheet was designed to reduce handoff ambiguity between infusion staff, specialty pharmacists, providers, and HealthIT stakeholders.

Evidence Flow

Workflow design: one shared source of truth, two operational lanes

Infusion staff lane

  1. Infusion counseling
  2. Review IV orders
  3. Monitor IV approval and scheduling
  4. Use BPA prompt to confirm IV completion and update the flowsheet

Specialty pharmacy lane

  1. SubQ counseling
  2. Begin SubQ prior authorization
  3. Coordinate SubQ prescription order to specialty pharmacy
  4. Confirm delivery and update the flowsheet

Best Practice Alerts

Timed prompts convert documentation events into follow-up work

+2 wk

Infusion tolerability check

After the first infusion is documented, an infusion pharmacist alert prompts follow-up with the patient about tolerability.

8 wk

SubQ PA runway

Medication-specific triggers fire after the first or second infusion when the first SubQ dose is about eight weeks away.

4 wk

SubQ Rx request

When the third infusion is documented, a specialty pharmacist prompt supports timely prescription coordination before the first injection is due.

A later six-week alert fires only when a SubQ prescription has not been ordered, prompting PA status investigation and Rx request follow-up.

Flowsheet Pattern

Fields bridge referral, infusion, and injection milestones

Section Representative fields Why it matters
Infusion Referral date, counseling date, approval date, scheduled/administered dates Tracks induction progress and exposes delayed approvals or missed doses.
SubQ PA approval/expiration, Rx sent date, first injection due date, copay card status Connects coverage work to the first maintenance dose timeline.

Conclusion

A purpose-built EHR flowsheet streamlined management across infusion and specialty pharmacy teams

For IBD patients starting biologics with IV induction followed by SubQ maintenance, the flowsheet gave staff a shared view of approvals, doses, prescription status, delivery, and due dates, supporting care coordination and reducing gaps in the handoff from infusion to injection.
