Addressing Social Determinants of Health: A Health System Specialty Pharmacy Initiative

A multi-center health system specialty pharmacy team used an embedded, high-touch model to identify health-related social needs, create service referrals, speed medication access, and support adherence.

Objective

Measure how embedded pharmacists and liaisons improve healthcare access and quality.

The study evaluated referral of service interventions, medication access, and medication adherence in patients enrolled in clinical or dispensing specialty pharmacy services.

Identify

Pharmacists and liaisons surfaced individual health-related social needs through a high-touch communication model.

Intervene

Referral of service interventions were created in the Arbor specialty pharmacy technology platform.

Refer

Patients were connected to social, medical, behavioral, nutrition, education, and support services.

Barrier domain

SDoH and individual HRSNs can limit healthcare access, medication starts, and uninterrupted therapy.

Embedded model

HSSP pharmacists and liaisons worked within outpatient clinics and specialty pharmacy workflows.

Referral reach

Service referrals covered case management, specialists, primary care, mental health, social work, and education.

Access support

The team secured more than $500 million in financial assistance from June 2019 to March 2023.

Conclusion

High-touch specialty pharmacy teams were positioned to identify SDoH-related factors and improve access to quality care.

Discussion

Referral, access, and adherence functioned as one support system.

Referral interventions connected patients to services that address underlying HRSNs; medication access work reduced delays between prescribing and therapy start; adherence support tracked insurance barriers that could otherwise cause gaps.

Future directions

Standardize SDoH screening inside specialty pharmacy workflows.

The authors proposed disease-state questionnaires and standardized HRSN screening at therapy initiation and throughout treatment, with an expanded liaison role in identifying needs and making referrals.
