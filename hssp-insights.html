How To Read This

What was reviewed

Public PDFs, research/outcomes HTML pages, public case studies, white papers, issue briefs, reports, and market-facing evidence pages from each HSSP organization.

Analysis workflow

Each organization was assigned a separate analysis lane, with sub-workstreams for PDFs, HTML pages, evidence themes, and HSSP audience strategy. Findings were then synthesized into a page-ready summary.

Interpretation approach

Findings are reported as observations about each set of public materials, including the audiences addressed, measures reported, analytic detail available, and remaining uncertainty in the public materials.

Shields Health Solutions

Shields has the broadest and most mature public evidence footprint in this set. The public materials position integrated specialty pharmacy as a health-system operating model that can support access, adherence, disease outcomes, patient experience, equity, cost of care, and limited-distribution drug access.

Research posture

Shields appears to be building a complete value platform rather than isolated marketing assets. The strongest signal is a progression from disease-specific outcomes to enterprise value themes such as total cost of care, payer/LDD access, population health, SDOH, clinician utilization, and network-scale benchmarks.

Observed positioning

The public materials present the integrated HSSP partnership model for health-system executives, finance leaders, payer and network stakeholders, and service lines that require patient-care outcomes and operating evidence.

Evidence limitations and analytic considerations

  • The public library is extensive, but claims, evidence strength, and source traceability are not presented in a single standardized framework.
  • Many assets are descriptive or observational. Public materials do not consistently show comparators, risk adjustment, site-mix assessment, or sensitivity analyses.
  • Economic measures are reported across assets, but cost to serve, staffing, avoided leakage, margin, and budget impact are not consistently separated.
  • Adherence, access, clinical, and experience measures appear across disease states, but cross-disease measure definitions are not always visible.
  • Payer and limited-distribution access materials connect accreditation, clinical management, and health-system reach, but endpoint definitions vary by asset.
  • The diabetes, GLP-1, oncology, CF, MS, HIV/HCV, and rheumatology assets show a recurring disease-state evidence structure.

Evidence observations

  1. Shields has the broadest public HSSP evidence set among the four organizations reviewed.
  2. The public materials address multiple health-system audiences, including executives, payer and network stakeholders, service-line leaders, and specialty pharmacy operators.
  3. Cost-of-care and population-health assets extend beyond pharmacy operations to health-system leadership questions.
  4. SDOH and patient-experience content are present, although public materials do not consistently show denominators, follow-up windows, intervention costs, or stratified outcomes.
  5. Clinician-utilization and access assets frame HSSP performance in terms of administrative burden, service-line capacity, and prescription capture.

CPS Solutions

CPS has the largest public web materials, with outcome-study pages, Patient Impact Reports, perspectives, issue briefs, Therigy-related content, 340B and revenue-cycle commentary, and specialty pharmacy operations material. The evidence posture is broad, frequent, and practical, but dispersed across many public pages.

Research posture

CPS publishes as a content engine with a visible outcomes-measurement program. The public materials emphasize Patient Impact Reports, Therigy-enabled dashboards, financial performance, nonadherence risk, SDOH/HRSNs, payer access, and specialty pharmacy infrastructure. Some of the most useful evidence is in HTML outcome-study pages rather than downloadable PDFs.

Observed positioning

The public materials present CPS as a partner for pharmacy complexity across specialty pharmacy, outpatient pharmacy, infusion, 340B, revenue, technology, and clinical outcomes.

Evidence limitations and analytic considerations

  • The web materials are large and heterogeneous. Audience, evidence type, service line, and business question are not consistently indexed in the public materials.
  • Outcome-study pages often describe objectives and results, but denominators, inclusion criteria, intervention exposure, and follow-up are not always visible.
  • Dashboard and risk-stratification content indicates a structured measurement program, although public materials do not always show a common outcomes framework.
  • Educational thought leadership and measured outcome evidence appear together across the public materials. This can make it difficult to distinguish market context from performance data.
  • 340B, revenue-cycle, infusion, and specialty pharmacy content are linked thematically, but operational and patient-care tradeoffs are not always quantified.
  • Patient Impact Reports provide a recurring annual evidence product. Public pages vary in the level of methods detail available for year-over-year comparison.
  • Therigy-related assets imply benchmark governance needs, including measure definitions, numerator and denominator logic, sample-size thresholds, site-mix adjustment, refresh cadence, and interpretation guidance.
  • Diabetes risk stratification and dashboard studies include clinical and operational measures. Economic endpoints such as avoided utilization, labor cost, cost per intervention, and budget impact are less consistently visible.

Evidence observations

  1. CPS has a large public evidence set, although content is dispersed across many pages.
  2. The public materials include frequent content that can be classified by audience, service line, evidence type, and reported outcome.
  3. Clinical dashboards and nonadherence risk content create a credible bridge between software-enabled operations and patient outcomes.
  4. Methods summaries are not consistently visible across outcome-study pages, limiting comparison across studies.
  5. Annual patient-impact content turns internal quality work into external evidence, but the public pages provide variable detail on metric governance.
  6. Therigy is central to the strategy because it connects HSSP operations with measurement, benchmarking, and publication-oriented evidence.
  7. Payer access is repeatedly framed as an evidence question, with emphasis on outcomes, transparency, and specialty pharmacy performance.

Clearway Health

Clearway is the most case-study-led organization in the set. The public materials focus on recognizable health-system partners, implementation narratives, access, revenue capture, affordability, labor relief, and mission-centric specialty pharmacy value rather than a broad publication library.

Research posture

Clearway's assets are built for executive persuasion. They are practical and commercially useful, with partner case studies that make the HSSP model tangible for leaders considering whether to build, expand, or outsource specialty pharmacy capabilities.

Observed positioning

The public materials use named health-system examples to describe changes in mission alignment, medication access, operational performance, and financial sustainability after specialty pharmacy implementation.

Evidence limitations and analytic considerations

  • Case studies generally report outcomes without a detailed public methods annex describing baseline period, implementation timing, eligible-patient denominator, attribution logic, and follow-up window.
  • Financial findings emphasize revenue and budget performance. Start-up investment, operating expense, avoided leakage, margin definitions, and patient-access gains are not consistently separated.
  • Partner case studies use a similar narrative structure, but operational, financial, clinical, and patient-experience measures are not reported uniformly across assets.
  • Revenue narratives are prominent. Quality, access, equity, and service-line outcomes are reported with variable depth across case studies.
  • Implementation details are described narratively, but milestones and transferability considerations are not standardized.
  • Patient liaison and medication-access stories describe access barriers. Time to therapy, abandonment, affordability, and patient burden are not consistently reported as standardized endpoints.

Evidence observations

  1. Clearway's public evidence is concentrated in executive-facing case studies rather than comparative outcomes studies.
  2. The BMC case study is the anchor proof point: it links specialty pharmacy revenue growth from $20 million to $600 million with 95% PDC, patient assistance, PA turnaround, and HIV/HCV/IBD outcomes.
  3. Children's National extends the story into pediatric access, accreditation, 340B resilience, and cell/gene therapy readiness.
  4. Signature and OU Health show the community-hospital and service-line versions of the value story, with evidence around PA turnaround, revenue contribution, patient growth, and fertility medication access.
  5. Methods and outcome definitions are not consistently visible across case studies.
  6. Financial outcomes are frequently paired with access, mission, and service-line narratives.
  7. The public evidence set is small enough for direct comparison of reported measures across partner case studies.

VytlOne / Maxor

VytlOne/Maxor combines market intelligence, pipeline reports, 340B and access strategy, case studies, and adherence content. The public materials read like a strategic advisory platform for health systems navigating specialty pharmacy, revenue pressure, access complexity, and emerging therapies.

Research posture

The evidence mix is more strategic than narrowly academic. Pipeline reports and insights papers help leaders anticipate therapy, access, and operating implications, while case studies and adherence material show how the organization wants to connect strategy to execution.

Observed positioning

The public materials present VytlOne/Maxor as a pharmacy strategy and operations partner focused on market change, specialty pharmacy economics, access operations, and pipeline readiness.

Evidence limitations and analytic considerations

  • Pipeline and insights reports identify market and therapy trends. Local implications for patient volume, access friction, staffing, payer risk, and service-line readiness are not consistently quantified.
  • Insights reports summarize trends and implications, but public methods notes do not consistently describe data sources, assumptions, or local applicability.
  • Prescription access liaison and high-touch adherence materials include access, adherence, and satisfaction measures, but endpoint definitions vary across assets.
  • 340B, rare disease, and specialty pipeline materials discuss financial exposure and operations. Scenario assumptions and budget-impact methods are not consistently visible.
  • Strategy content is prominent. Measured implementation outcomes are reported in selected case studies and are less consistent across insight-oriented assets.
  • VytlAIQ and analytics materials describe operational use cases. Public validation evidence tied to audit risk, capture, denials, work reduction, prior authorization turnaround, and patient access is limited.

Evidence observations

  1. VytlOne/Maxor's public materials emphasizes strategic intelligence in addition to outcomes reporting.
  2. Market insight is a central content category, particularly for pipeline, 340B, access, and pharmacy economics.
  3. Pipeline content becomes more valuable to HSSPs when linked to capacity, access operations, service-line impact, and budget risk.
  4. Adherence and prescription access liaison assets report patient-flow and economic endpoints with variable detail.
  5. The county health-system and prescription access liaison case studies include executive-facing proof points, although attribution and accounting logic are not fully visible in the public summaries.
