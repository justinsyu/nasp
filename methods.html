Overview

Extraction rule

Methods are counted only when rule-based text evidence appears in the parsed poster markdown. Records without explicit method language are classified as no explicit statistical method reported.

Traceability rule

Each result links to the saved PDF and markdown. Evidence snippets are stored on the individual poster record so counts can be inspected rather than treated as untraceable summaries.

Sample-size rule

The sample-size bucket uses the largest detected analytic denominator in the parsed text. The original snippet is retained because posters often report multiple denominators.

Outcome-variable rule

Outcome variables are extracted only from explicit outcome, endpoint, measure, evaluation, methods, or results language. Background claims and future/not-assessed outcomes are excluded.

Highest-Quality Methodology Signals

Review basis

All 487 poster-level records were reviewed across independent year-band passes before selecting this list. The rating favors completed studies with explicit design, denominator, data source, outcome definition, and quantitative analysis plan.

What counted most

Highest-quality ratings were assigned to randomized trials, comparative effectiveness studies, matched or adjusted real-world cohorts, longitudinal models, time-to-event analyses, and studies with patient-level clinical, adherence, utilization, or cost outcomes.

What was downgraded

Descriptive implementation reports, small uncontrolled pilots, missing denominators, research-in-progress posters, vague method language, and records with outcomes planned but not measured were not included even when the topic was important.
